Waqfs Ordinance (I of 1962)

Section 56

If the transfer is made by Mutawalli such transfer shall be declared void and any transfer made by the heirs of waqif shall be void ab initio and is not required to be declared void by filing suit as is required for the transfer made by Mutawalli. There is no scope for reading the words, “heirs of the waqif” for the word “Mutawalli” as mentioned in sub-section (3) of section 56 of the Waqfs Ordinance. The transferees of the heirs of waqif shall be deemed to be trespassers if they are found in possession of the waqf property. Sub-section (3) of section 56 of the Ordinance is not applicable to the transferees from the heirs of the waqif. The High Court Division was wrong in holding that the suit having not been filed within 3 years from the date of knowledge or from the date of such transfer, as the case may be, was barred and contrary to the provision of sub-section (3) of section 56 of the Ordinance.           . . . JUDGMENT

Syed Mahmud Hossain, J: Both the appeals by leave are directed against the judgment and order dated 18.08.2009 passed by the High Court Division in Writ Petition No.2358 of 2009 making the Rule absolute in part with direction.    

2.            Both the appeals involving similar questions of laws and facts having been heard together are now disposed of by this single judgment.

3.            The relevant facts for the purpose of disposal of these appeals, in a nutshell, are:

         One Md. Faizur Rahman dedicated 89.06 acres of land by way of registered waqf deed No.5765 dated 31.06.1945 (Annexure-A to the petition) and it was duly enrolled being E.C. No.10926. Faizur Rahman remained Mutawalli till his death. After his death, the office of Mutwalli was being maintained as per the guidelines made in the waqf deed. The writ-petitioner-appellant was appointed as official Mutwalli who submitted an application before the Administrator of Waqf praying for eviction of some unauthorized possessor (Annexure-B). Ultimately, the Administrator of Waqf by an order dated 16.05.2002 allowed the prayer for eviction upon hearing the Mutwalli as well as the persons sought to be evicted. Against the said order, added-respondents moved the High Court Division by filing a writ petition and obtained Rule Nisi in Writ Petition No.3067 of 2002. The learned Judges of the High Court Division by the judgment and order dated 07.08.2007 discharged the Rule. After that, on 24.01.2008, the Administrator of Waqfs issued a letter to the Deputy Commissioner, Gaibandha, vide memo No.105 dated 24.01.2008 requesting him to evict the illegal possessors who were possessing the waqf property illegally. The Waqfs Administrator again requested the Deputy Commissioner, Gaibandha, by a letter dated 02.07.2008 but the Deputy Commissioner did not take any step to evict the illegal possessors. After that, on 12.08.2008, the Waqfs Administrator again issued a letter to the Deputy Commissioner, Gaibandha, requesting him to evict the illegal possessors form the Faizur Rahman Waqf Estate but to no avail.

4.            Therefore, the writ-petitioner filed the writ petition in which Rule Nisi was issued.

5.            Added-writ-respondent Nos.2-5 contested the Rules by filing affidavit-in-opposition controverting the material statements made in the writ petition. Their case, in short, is that their predecessors were not aware of the fact of dedication of the properties by way of waqf as mentioned in the said affidavit-in-opposition. Their further case is that after the death of waqif, his heirs possessed the property as of their secular and ancestral property and they have transferred their interest by registered deeds. The heirs of mutwalli transferred 2.67 acres of land by a kabala dated 23.04.1977 in favour of Shah Azizul Haque, the predecessor of respondent Nos.2-5. One of the heirs named Zafar Mahmud Amirul Islam sold 2.00 acres of land by a kabala dated 01.04.1969 in favour of Mohiuddin who again sold it to Azizul Haque. Amirul Islam sold 2.00 acres of land by kabala dated 26.12.1968 in favour of Mohiuddin who subsequently transferred the same in favour of respondent Nos.2-5. The respondents’ father Azizul Haque purchased .25 acres of land from Abu Zafar Mohammad Hafizul Islam by a kabala dated 15.10.1985 and thus the respondents have acquired title to 13.82 acres of land. The khatinas have been prepared in their names (Annexure-X series). It is further stated that previously several civil suits were instituted by the petitioner and her brother but without result. It is stated that they will be ruined, if they are evicted pursuant to the order of the Administrator of Waqfs. It is further stated that the respondents have filed a suit being No.15 of 2008 before the Court of Subordinate Judge, First Court, Gaibandha, praying for declaration of title to the suit land and the suit is pending (Annexure-O series). It is asserted that the writ-petitioner has not come with clean hands.  

6.            The High Court Division, upon hearing the parties, by the judgment and order dated 18.08.2009 made the Rule absolute in part with direction.

7.            Feeling aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the impugned judgment and order passed by the High Court Division, writ-respondent, Deputy Commissioner, Gaihandha, filed Civil Petitions for Leave to Appeal No.1155 of 2012 and the writ-petitioner has filed Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No.30 of 2010 before this Division and leave was granted in these civil petitions on 20.01.2014 resulting in Civil Appeal Nos.63-64 of 2014.    

8.            Mr. Goutam Kumar Roy, learned Deputy Attorney General, appearing on behalf of the appellant of Civil Appeal No.63 of 2014, submits that by the impugned judgment, the High Court Division had tried to establish the title and ownership of unauthorized occupants on the plea of possession which cannot be the subject matter of the writ-petition. He further submits that the High Court Division cannot exclude the added-respondents from the hand of eviction which is to be done in compliance with the provision laid down in section 64(1) of the Waqfs Ordinance,1962.

9.            Mr. Habibul Islam Bhuiyan, learned Senior Advocate, appearing on behalf of the appellant of Civil Appeal No.64 of 2014, submits that in writ petition No.3067 of 2002 filed by trespassers Ezazul Haq and others, it has been resolved that the disputed property along with 6.92/13.82 acres of land is waqf property and that the judgment was affirmed by the Appellate Division in Civil Petition No.289 of 2008 and as such, the High Court Division was not justified in making the Rule absolute in part. He further submits that section 64(1) of the Waqfs Ordinance,1962 provides that on an application of the Administrator, the Deputy Commissioner shall evict the trespassers and that the Deputy Commissioner has no authority to act otherwise and that he cannot challenge the order of the Administrator and as such, the impugned judgment should be set aside.

10.        Mr. Qumrul Huq Siddique, learned Advocate, appearing on behalf of respondent No.1 of Civil Appeal No.63 of 2014 and Mr. Gias Uddin, learned Advocate-on-Record, appearing on behalf of respondent Nos.2-5 of Civil Appeal No.63 of 2014 and respondent Nos.2-5 of Civil Appeal No.64 of 2014, support the impugned judgment delivered by the High Court Division.

11.        We have considered the submissions of the learned Deputy Attorney General, learned Senior Advocate, learned Advocate and learned Advocate-on-Record of both the sides of both the appeals, perused the impugned judgment and the materials on record.

12.        Before entering into the merit of the appeal, it would be pertinent to go through the grounds, for which, leave was granted to consider. The grounds are as follows:  

“The decision of the High Court Division is absolutely illegal which is required to be interfered with by the Apex Court on the face of the provision laid down in section 64(1) and (2) of the Waqfs Ordinance, 1962. Moreover, the impugned judgment of the High Court Division has tried to establish the title and ownership of the unauthorized occupants on the plea of possession which cannot be the subject matter of the writ-petition.

In no circumstances, the High Court Division can exclude the respondents from the hand of eviction which has been done in compliance with the provision laid down in section 64(1) of the Waqfs Ordinance, 1962.”

13.        Admittedly, Faizur Rahman Waqf Estate was created vide waqf deed No.5765 dated 31.06.1945 with several hundred acres of land including 89.06 acres of land under litigation in Gaibandha. The waqf was registered as E.C. No.10926. Shafinaz Choudhry was appointed official Mutwalli of Faizur Rahman Waqf Estate on 30.08.1995. On 02.10.2000, the official Mutwalli filed a petition to the Administrator of Waqfs to evict the trespassers from 89.06 acres of waqf property. The trespassers claimed that their predecessors have purchased 6.92/13.82 acres of land in 1968, 1969, 1977 and 1986 from the heirs of the waqif. 16.        Official Mutwalli, Safina Choudhury filed Civil Petition No.30 of 2010 (Civil Appeal No.64 of 2014) against the judgment and order of the High Court Division holding that writ-respondent Nos.2-5 are not liable to be evicted. Deputy Commissioner, Gaibandha also filed Civil Petition No.1155 of 2005 corresponding to Civil Appeal No.64 of 2014 challenging the judgment as a whole.

17.        What is important to note here is that in the earlier writ-petition filed by the trespassers Ezajul Haq and others challenging the order of eviction dated 16.05.2002 of the Administrator of Waqfs, the High Court Division discharged the Rule holding that the writ-petitioners of Writ Petition No.3067 of 2002 were trespassers and illegal possessors. The said judgment of the High Court Division was affirmed by this Division in Civil Petition No.298 of 2009. Therefore, it has been resolved finally that the disputed property including 6.92/13.82 acres of land was also waqf property. Therefore, the High Court Division, in the subsequent Writ Petition No.2385 of 2009 should not have held that respondent Nos.2-5 acquired title to 6.92/13.82 acres of land and that they could not be evicted therefrom.

18.        Now it is to be seen whether writ-respondent Nos.2 to 5 acquired any title on the basis of their registered kabalas dated 23.04.1977, 01.04.1969, 26.12.1968 and 15.10.1985 purchasing 13.82 acres of land out of the waqf properties. In this connection it is pertinent to quote section 56 of the Waqfs Ordinance,1962 to resolve the issue. Section 56 is quoted below:

56.(1) No transfer by a mutawalli of any immovable property of a waqf by way of sale, gift, mortgage or exchange, or by way of lease for a term exceeding 5 years shall be valid without the previous sanction of the Administrator:

Provided that the sanction by the Administrator shall not validate any transfer which is in contravention of any other law for the time being in force or is otherwise invalid.

(2) No Court shall grant permission to any Receiver appointed by the Court for the transfer of any waqf property unless the prior sanction of the Administrator has been obtained.

(3) In the absence of the sanction required by sub-section(1), any transfer made by a mutawalli shall be declared void, if the Administrator, within 4 months of his coming to know of such transfer, or within 3 years from the date of such transfer, whichever is later, applies to the Civil Court in this behalf.(emphasis supplied)

(4) Where a mutawalli transfers a waqf property in contravention of sub-section (1) and afterwards himself becomes the owner of the property, the mutawalli shall, on the direction of the Administrator, re-convey the property to the waqf.  

(5) Any transfer made in contravention of the provisions of sub-section (1) shall be deemed to be an act of malfeasance and breach of trust for the purpose of sub-section (1) of section 32.”

19.        The High Court Division mentioned about section 56 of the Ordinance but did not discuss in detail the provision of section 56 of the Ordinance. Sub-section (3) of section 56 of the Ordinance provides that in the absence of sanction required by sub-section (1) of section 56 any transfer made by Mutawalli shall be declared void, if the Administrator within 4 months of his coming to know of such transfer or within 3 years from the date of such transfer whichever is later, applies to the Civil Court in this behalf.

20.        This sub-section provides that the transfer made by Mutawalli shall be declared void. For the word ‘Mutawalli’, the words, “heirs of waqif” cannot be read in sub-section (3) of section 56 of the Waqfs Ordinance. If the transfer is made by Mutawalli such transfer shall be declared void and any transfer made by the heirs of waqif shall be void ab initio and is not required to be declared void by filing suit as is required for the transfer made by Mutawalli. It has already been stated above that there is no scope for reading the words, “heirs of the waqif” for the word “Mutawalli” as mentioned in sub-section (3) of section 56 of the Waqfs Ordinance. The transferees of the heirs of waqif shall be deemed to be trespassers if they are found in possession of the waqf property. Sub-section (3) of section 56 of the Ordinance is not applicable to the transferees from the heirs of the waqif. The High Court Division was wrong in holding that the suit having not been filed within 3 years from the date of knowledge or from the date of such transfer, as the case may be, was barred and contrary to the provision of sub-section (3) of section 56 of the Ordinance.

21.        The Deputy Commissioner, Gaibandha was under the obligation to act as per direction of the Waqfs Administrator but he has failed to discharge his statutory power as required by section 64 of the Ordinance. Such indifference of the Deputy Commissioner in carrying out the order of the Administrator of Waqfs is deplorable.
