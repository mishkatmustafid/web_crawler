Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898)

Section 498

There is no doubt that the power to grant bail under section 498 of the Code is given both to the High Court Division as well as the Court of Sessions. In the decision reported in 10 DLR 541 it was held that a revision application direct to the High Court Division is not ordinarily entertainable. A different view appears in the case reported in 24 BLD (AD) 168. However, in the instant case this issue of the maintainability has not been finally adjudicated by the High Court Division. The ends of justice would be best served if the High Court Division is directed to adjudicate upon the issue of maintainability while disposing of the Rule issued in respect of the bail of accused-respondent.                     ...(6 and 7)

Shah Zillur Rahman Mutawalli Vs. The State, 10 DLR 541 and The State Vs. Md. Nurul Islam @ Babul, 24 BLD (AD) 168 ref.

JUDGMENT

Muhammad Imman Ali, J: This criminal petition for leave to appeal is directed against the order dated 28.05.2018 passed by a Division Bench of the High Court Division in Criminal Miscellaneous Case No.25897 of 2018 granting ad-interim bail for a period of 6(six) months in GR Case No.51 of 2015 arising out of Choddogram PS Case No.04 dated 03.02.2015 under sections 149/302/ 326/324 /427/109/34 of the Penal Code, now pending before the Court of Chief Judicial Magistrate, Cumilla.

2.             The respondent, Begum Khaleda Zia was shown arrested in the abovementioned case on 08.04.2018 and on 10.04.2018, an application for bail filed before the cognizance Court, Cumilla was rejected, whereupon the accused-respondent filed Criminal Miscellaneous Case No.2518 of 2018 before the Session Judge, Cumilla on 16.04.2018. By order dated 23.04.2018, the learned Sessions Judge fixed 07.06.2018 for further hearing of the bail matter. On 07.06.2018, the application for bail was dismissed as not pressed.

3.             In the meantime, the accused-respondent filed Criminal Miscellaneous Case No.25897 of 2018 before the High Court Division praying for bail. By the impugned Order, the High Court Division issued Rule and granted ad-interim bail to the accused-respondent for a period of 6(six) months. Hence, the State has filed the instant criminal petition for leave to appeal.

4.             Mr. Mahbubey Alam, learned Attorney General, appearing on behalf of the petitioner submitted that the bail application filed before the Sessions Judge in Criminal Miscellaneous Case No.2518 of 2018 was dismissed as not pressed and hence, there is no application for bail before the Sessions Judge. The accused-respondent filed a criminal miscellaneous Case for bail before the High Court Division, which has concurrent jurisdiction in such matter under section 498 of the Code of Criminal Procedure (the Code). However, he submitted, by reference to the Full Bench decision in the case of Shah Zillur Rahman Mutawlli –VS- The State 10 DLR 541, that a revisional application must be moved before the Sessions Judge and “a revision application direct to the High Court Division is not ordinarily entertainable.” He submitted that the decision in the case of the State -Vs- Md. Nurul Islam @ Babul, 24, BLD (AD) 168 is distinguishable in the facts and circumstances of the instant case, inasmuch as in that case this Division found that keeping the bail matter pending for hearing in spite of persistent prayers for bail tantamount to refusal of bail, whereas in the instant case the bail application was dismissed as not pressed. There was no final decision on merit. He further submitted that since the High Court Division has not finally disposed of the Rule, the question of maintainability of the miscellaneous case filed before the High Court Division will be decided there.

5.             Mr. Khandakar Mahbub Hossain, learned Senior Advocate with Mr. A.J. Mohammad Ali, learned Senior Advocate with Mr. Jainul Abedin, learned Senior Advocate with Mr. A.M. Mahbubuddin, learned Advocate and Mr. Sogir Hossain, learned Advocate instructed by Mr. Zainul Abedin, learned Advocate-on-Record, appearing on behalf of the respondent submitted that the accused-respondent is entitled to get the benefit of the provision to section 497 of the Code being a woman aged 73 years and also being a sick and infirm person. He further submitted that the jurisdiction of the High Court Division to hear an application for bail under section 498 of the Code is concurrent to that of the Court of Sessions. He further submitted that in the case of the State -Vs- Md. Nurul Islam @ Babul, 24, BLD (AD) 168, it was held that the lower Court should be moved first, but it is not an inviolable biblical rule. In exceptional circumstances, the Higher Court can also be moved, and hence, the High Court Division did not commit any error in entertaining the application and granting ad-interim bail to the accused-respondent.

6.             There is no doubt that the power to grant bail under section 498 of the Code is given both to the High Court Division as well as the Court of Sessions. the decision reported in 10 DLR cited above has been brought to our notice, where it was held that a revision application direct to the High Court Division is not ordinarily entertainable. A different view appears in the case reported in 24 BLD. However, in the instant case this issue of the maintainability has not been finally adjudicated by the High Court Division/

7.             In such facts and circumstances, we are of the view that the ends of justice would be best served if the High Court Division is directed to adjudicate upon the issue of maintainability while disposing of the Rule issued in respect of the bail of the accused-respondent.

8.             In view of the discussion above, the instant criminal petition for leave to appeal is disposed of. The Division Bench presided over by AKM Asaduzzaman, J is directed to dispose of the Rule within 4 (four) weeks from date. The order of stay granted by the learned Judge-in-Chamber shall continue till disposal of the Rule.
