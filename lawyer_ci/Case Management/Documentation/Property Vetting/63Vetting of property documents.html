Opinion

 : Upon perusal of the documents/papers we are of the opinion that Company 2, Mr. M and Mr. N (the ‘Landowners’) have acquired prima facie ownership over the land in question.

However, we have perused the Memorandum of Association of Company 2 and found that the Company does not have the power to give third party mortgages.

Therefore, Company 2 cannot execute Deed of Mortgage and Irrevocable General Power of Attorney (“IGPA”) in order to secure the liability of Company 1 without amending their Memorandum of Association.

Bank can only accept the property of Company 2 after Company 2 has amended its Memorandum of Association in accordance with Companies Act 1994.

Bank may accept the properties of Mr. M and Mr. N as security of loan to be given to Company 1 subject to ascertaining the physical possession of the present owners over the land and upon obtaining documents/papers mentioned in clause -13 above.

We have provided this opinion on the basis of the documents/papers referred to us. We can not guarantee the genuineness of the documents. You may verify the genuineness of submitted documents with the concerned Sub-registrar office or concerned authorities.

In view of the fact that Company 2 (owner of 2.67 acres out of total 3.43 acres of the proposed mortgage properties) is not authorised to execute Deed of Mortgage and IGPA in order to secure the liability of Company 1 without amending their Memorandum of Association, we are awaiting your further instruction regarding whether we should draft the Deed of Mortgage and IGPA.
