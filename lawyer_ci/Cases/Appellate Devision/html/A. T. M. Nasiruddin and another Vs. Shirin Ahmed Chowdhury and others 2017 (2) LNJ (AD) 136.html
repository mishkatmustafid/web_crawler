JUDGMENT

Md. Abdul Wahhab Miah, J; This petition for leave to appeal has been filed by writ-respondent Nos.3 and 4 against the judgment and order dated the 18th day of August, 2015 passed by a Division Bench of the High Court Division in Writ Petition No. 9369 of 2013 making the Rule Nisi absolute.

2.              Respondent No. 1 as the petitioner filed the writ petition before the High Court Division challenging the order dated 16.03.2011 passed by writ-respondent No.2, Additional Deputy Commissioner (Revenue), Dhaka admitting Miscellaneous Appeal No.68 of 2011 filed against the order dated 20.06.2010 passed by the Assistant Commissioner(Land), Keraniganj in Miscellaneous Case No.320 of 2009 condoning the delay of 268 days. The main ground on which the order of the Additional Deputy Commissioner (Revenue), Dhaka was challenged was that the limitation for filing an appeal under section 147 of the State Acquisition and Tenancy Act, 1950(the Act, 1950), as provided in section 148 thereof was 30 days, but the appeal was filed beyond 30 days with an application for condonation of delay. Therefore, the order passed by the Additional Deputy Commissioner (Revenue) admitting the appeal condoning the delay was without jurisdiction.

3.              From the impugned judgment and order, it appears that the High Court Division accepted the said contention of the writ-petitioner and made the Rule Nisi absolute.

4.              We find nothing wrong with the view taken by the High Court Division inasmuch as limitation provided in the Act, 1950 was a special limitation. Therefore, there was no scope of condoning the delay in filing the appeal after the expiry of the said statutory period of limitation in view of the provisions of section 29 of the Limitation Act.

5.              We find no merit in this petition and accordingly, the same is dismissed.
