Waqfs Ordinance (I of 1962)

Sections 56 and 57

Property belonging to a Waqf may only be transferred by its Mutawalli after getting sanction from the Administrator of Waqfs, as provided by section 57 read with section 56 of the Waqfs Ordinance, 1962. 16

 

Waqfs Ordinance (I of 1962)

Sections 56(3)

Any transfer made by a Mutawalli without the sanction from the Administrator of Waqfs shall be declared void, if the Administrator, within 4 months of his coming to know of such transfer, or within 3 years from the date of such transfer, whichever is later, applies to the Civil Court in this behalf. Thus, it is solely in the domain of the Administrator of Waqfs to challenge any transfer of Waqf property by a Mutawalli.       16

 

Waqfs Ordinance (I of 1962)

Sections 102

The finding of the Administrator of WEaqfs of the effect that the predecessor of the appellant-plaintiff in the suit had consented to the transfer of the property in question is a finding of fact which could have been agitated by the plaintiff in a properly constituted case challenge before the concerned District Judge. But he did not do so. Hence, the finding that the transfer was valid cannot be challenged by him since there is a bar under section 102 of the Ordinance.                                                17

JUDGMENT

MUHAMMAD IMMAN ALI, J: This civil appeal, by leave, is directed against the judgement and order dated 14.05.2008 passed by a Single Bench of the High Court Division in Civil Revision No.443 of 2001 discharging the Rule.

2.                  The facts of the case, in short, are that Zaker Ali Sowdagar was the owner of the land described in the schedule to the plaint and he made a waqf in respect of the property together with his other properties by executing and registering a waqf deed dated 10.05.1928 as Waqf Alal Aulad conferring benefits to the cause of religion, charity and his heirs. The Waqf Estate was duly registered with the Administrator of Waqfs-defendant No.10 bearing E.C. No.7035 and the schedule-1 property has been mentioned in item No.40 of the said waqf deed. The waqif Zaker Ali Sowdagar constituted himself as the first Mutawalli and he acted as such and managed and possessed the waqf properties within the terms and conditions of the said waqf deed till his death. After his death his first son late Imam Sharif Chowdhury became the Mutawalli of his Waqf Estate and he managed the same till his death. After his death the wakif’s second son Md. Sharif Chowdhury became the Mutawalli and his grandson the plaintiff Alhaj Nurul Islam Chowdhury son of late Imam Sharif Chowdhury became the Naib Mutawalli of the said Waqf Estate. Both of them used to manage the Waqf Estate jointly but by amicable partition Mutawalli Md. Sharif Chowdhury used to possess half of the entire Waqf Estate and the rest used to be possessed by the plaintiff. As per amicable partition, schedule-1(a) property was in possession of Mutawalli Md. Sharif Chowdhury and the rest of the schedule property was in possession of the plaintiff. The administration and management of the entire Waqf Estate was entrusted to the joint management of the plaintiff and Md. Sharif Chowdhury. The said Mutawalli Md. Sharif Chowdhury having died in June, 1979 the plaintiff became the sole Mutawalli of the said Waqf Estate and defendant No.10-the Administrator of Waqfs also has appointed the plaintiff as sole Mutawalli by his Memo No.1413 dated 10.12.1979 and since then the plaintiff has been managing the waqf property. Md. Sharif Chowdhury and the plaintiff were also the beneficiaries of the entire Waqf Estate and P.S. and B.S. Khatians have been recorded in their names for the purpose of enjoyment of the usufruct of the Waqf Estate as beneficiaries. Md. Sharif Chowdhury was in possession of the schedule-1(a) property in the eastern part of the schedule-1 property covered by municipal holding No.92 old and 95 new, house No.294 and the plaintiff was in possession of the remaining western part of the schedule property covered by municipal holding No.91 old and 94 new, house No.295. Md. Sharif Chowdhury as Mutawalli illegally transferred the schedule-1(a) property to defendant Nos.1 to 9 by 3 registered kabalas dated 19.03.1977, 20.03.1977 and 22.03.1977 bearing deed Nos.3063, 3012 and 3036. The  plaintiff having come to know of the fact of the transfers made a complaint before defendant No.10-the Administrator of Waqfs requesting him to recover possession of the schedule-1(a) property from defendant Nos.1 to 9 by a petition, whereupon the Administrator of Waqfs filed Miscellaneous Case No.208 of 1981 in the First Court of Subordinate Judge, Chattogram under section 56(3) of the Waqf Ordinance, 1962 (the Ordinance) on 03.06.1981 against defendant Nos.1 to 9 for recovery of possession. In that miscellaneous case the Administrator of Waqfs clearly stated that Mutawalli Md. Sharif Chowdhury did not take permission from the Administrator of Waqfs for transferring the schedule 1(a) property to defendant Nos.1 to 9 and the alleged kabalas are fraudulent and illegal and the transferees did not acquire any title and interest in the schedule-1(a) property and the kabalas are liable to be declared void and illegal and the schedule-1(a) property is liable to be recovered from defendant Nos.1 to 9.

3.                  Defendant Nos.1 to 9 without appearing in the miscellaneous case filed a petition before the Administrator of Waqfs on 26.09.1981 and prevailed upon him illegally to recognise their purchase and to pass illegal order on 04.02.1982 according permission to the aforesaid transfers with retrospective effect and to direct the local Waqfs Inspector to withdraw the Miscellaneous Case No.208 of 1981 from the Court of Subordinate Judge, First Court, Chattogram. On the petition filed by the Administrator of Waqfs for withdrawal of the Miscellaneous Case No.208 of 1981 the learned Subordinate Judge, Second Court-in-charge of the First Court rejected the objection of the plaintiff who was opposite party No.10 in the miscellaneous case and allowed the withdrawal of the petition in Miscellaneous Case No.208 of 1981 by order dated 18.02.1982. On the same date the plaintiff’s application to be added as co-petitioner in the said miscellaneous case was also rejected. Hence, the plaintiff filed the suit for declaration and recovery of possession.

4.                  Defendant No.l contested the suit by filing written statement denying the allegations of the plaintiff contending, inter alia, that defendant No.1 had to file a petition before the Administrator of Waqfs-defendant No.10 praying for withdrawal of the Miscellaneous Case No.208 of 1981 when defendants were convinced that the plaintiff had manipulated the Administrator of Waqfs to file the miscellaneous case knowing full well that the defendants obtained kabalas from the Mutawalli who obtained permission from defendant No.10 for such transfers with the consent of the plaintiff. The plaintiff filed a petition before defendant No.10 after the death of the Mutawalli Md. Sharif Chowdhury, who transferred the schedule-1(a) property along with other properties in total measuring 25 decimals land, in order to make illegal gain, but by order dated 04.02.1982 defendant No.10 rejected the plaintiff’s prayer and passed order conferring the permission to transfer with retrospective effect and got the Miscellaneous Case No.208 of 1981 withdrawn. Defendant No.1 purchased 25 decimals land out of schedule-1 property in his name as well as in the names of his sons defendant Nos.2-9 to the knowledge of all concerned and possession was delivered to him. In fact he was already in possession from before as bharatia under the Mutawalli and is a bona fide purchaser for valuable consideration. Defendant No.10 was pleased to accord permission with retrospective effect for the transfer to meet the ends of justice directing withdrawal of the Miscellaneous Case No.208 of 1981. The suit of the plaintiff is not maintainable and it is liable to be dismissed with cost.

5.                  On conclusion of the trial, the learned Subordinate Judge, First Court, Chattogram by his judgement and order dated 30.09.1989 dismissed the suit. Then the plaintiff preferred Other Class Appeal No.581 of 1989 before the District Judge, Chattogram. On transfer the appeal was heard by the learned Additional District Judge, First Court, Chattogram, who by his judgement and order dated 28.09.2000 dismissed the appeal affirming the judgement and order passed by the trial Court. Being aggrieved, the plaintiff filed Civil Revision No.443 of 2001 before the High Court Division and obtained Rule, which upon hearing the parties was discharged. Hence, the plaintiff filed Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No.2310 of 2008 and leave was granted to consider the following grounds:

“I. That the sale of the waqf property on 22.03.1977 by the predecessor Mutawalli without obtaining previous sanction of the Administrator of Waqfs under the mandatory provision of section 56(1) of the Ordinance was invalid in law and that the Administrator of Waqfs having filed the Miscellaneous Case No.208 of 1981 under section 56(3) of the Ordinance, for declaring the said sale as void and having thereafter passed order on 04.02.1982 giving sanction with retrospective effect to the said sale and withdrawing the Miscellaneous Case No.208 of 1981 acted arbitrarily and without jurisdiction and as such, the civil Court has jurisdiction to entertain the plaintiff’s suit to examine whether the said sale of the waqf property has been made upon following  the provisions of the   Ordinance and whether the Administrator of Waqfs has been vested with any power to pass any order giving retrospective sanction validating an invalid transfer of waqf property under the provisions of the Ordinance, 1962.

II. That Miscellaneous Case No.208 of 1981 under section 56(3) of the Ordinance beyond time was not maintainable as such the plaintiff’s suit is not barred under section 102 of the Waqfs Ordinance as such the High Court Division erred in law in considering this aspect of this matter.”

6.                  Mr. Abdul Wadud Bhuiyan, learned Senior Advocate appearing on behalf of the appellant made submissions in line with the grounds upon which leave was granted. He further submitted that the High Court Division committed gross error of law in not holding that the Administrator of Waqfs has not been vested with any jurisdiction or power under the Ordinance to pass any order giving retrospective sanction validating an invalid transfer of waqf property under section 56 of the Ordinance.

7.                  Mr. Abdul Quayum, learned Senior Advocate appearing for respondent Nos.2-9 made submissions in support of the impugned judgement and order of the High Court Division. He also submitted that the plaintiff-appellant consented to the sale by the erstwhile Mutawalli as such now he is estopped from challenging the sale and hence the Courts below as well as the High Court Division rightly dismissed the suit. He also submitted that the plaintiff having not challenged the order rejecting his prayer for being added as co-petitioner in Miscellaneous Case No.208 of 1981 as such the present suit at his instance is not maintainable since he was party in the suit. The learned Advocate submitted that the Administrator of Waqfs gave permission to sell with retrospective effect on 03.08.1982 for reasons mentioned in Exhibit-B(1) and since Exhibit-B gives impression of sale permission to a man of ordinary prudence, and accordingly withdrawal of Miscellaneous Case No.208 of 1981 filed under section 56(3) of the Waqfs Ordinance on false representation of the plaintiff was lawful and as such the Courts below and the High Court Division rightly dismissed the suit. He submitted that under section 56(3) of the Waqfs Ordinance it is the Administrator of Waqfs and not of any private person who may challenge the transfer of any Waqf property and the Administrator of Waqfs has exercised his right by filing Miscellaneous Case No.208 of 1981 and all are bound by the result and legal effect of the said case. He submitted that the plaintiff in the context of the case has no locus standi to file the suit as such the Courts below rightly dismissed the suit. The learned Advocate lastly submitted that the High Court Division rightly held that the suit is barred under section 102 of the Ordinance. 

8.                  We have considered the submissions of the learned Advocates appearing for the parties concerned, perused the impugned judgement and order of the High Court Division and other connected papers on record.

9.                  It is an admitted fact that the suit land belonged to a duly registered Waqf Estate, EC No.7035 created by late Zaker Ali Sowdagar by a deed of Waqf dated 10.5.1928. Late Alhaj Nurul Islam, the predecessor of the present appellant was a co-Mutawalli along with Md. Sharif Chowdhury. Each Mutawalli was in possession of half of the Waqf Estate. Md. Sharif Chowdhury sold 25 decimals land of his share of the Waqf Estate by three registered deeds dated 19.03.77, 20.03.77 and 22.03.77 in favour of respondent Nos.1–9. The predecessor of the present appellant filed Other Suit No.60 of 1982 praying for a declaration that the said three deeds were null and void, without proper consideration, inoperative, ineffective, sham paper transaction, without any legal consequence and not binding either upon the schedule-1(a) properties or the plaintiff. In addition he prayed for recovery of possession of the schedule-1(a) property.

10.              The aforementioned suit was dismissed by the trial court upon finding that the plaintiff did not have locus standi to file the suit as there is a bar under section 102 of the Waqfs Ordinance. The learned Judge of the trial Court noted that the plaint shows cause of action on 21.03.1977 when the deeds were registered, and subsequently when defendant No.10 by order dated 04.02.1982 accorded permission with retrospective effect for sale of the schedule-1(a) property and on 18.02.1982 when the Miscellaneous Case No.208 of 1981 was withdrawn. It appeared to the learned Judge of the trial Court that the plaintiff’s challenge was against the orders of defendant No.10 (the Administrator of Waqfs) dated 4.2.82 and 18.2.82, which he could have challenged before the learned District Judge. It was further observed, by reference to exhibit-B(1) that the plaintiff consented to transfer such land in favour of defendant Nos.1-9.

11.              The appellate Court dismissed the appeal taking a similar view as the trial Court. 13.              The language of the said letter in the parts underlined tends to imply that permission had already been given and details were being sought from the Mutawalli as to how the sale proceeds would be used. On the strength of this letter, the Mutawalli proceeded to sell the property in question to the respondent Nos.1-9. This aspect has been clearly explained in the order of the Administrator of Waqfs dated 04.02.82. We also note that in the said order the Administrator of Waqfs noted as follows:

“The matter was duly enquired upon through the local inspector as per order D/14.3.77 and the then Mutawalli Md. Sharif Chowdhury was asked to submit plan and estimate and the then Naib-Mutawalli was asked to give his comment vide this office memo No. 226-27D/14.03.77. The then Naib Mutawalli and the Mutawalli submitted a plan & estimate with a separate letter by the Naib-Mutawalli Haji Nurul Islam Chowdhury according consent to the proposed transfer. Vide Dairy No.8174D/21.04.77.”

14.              The Administrator of Waqfs observed as follows:

“On perusal of the records it appears that the present Mutawalli Haji Nurul Islam Chowdhury although given his consent in writing to accord permission to sell 25 decimals of land as per petition of the late Mutawalli Md. Sharif Chowdhury, he i.e. the present Mutawalli Haji Nurul Islam Chowdhury by suppression and mis-representation of the facts got the Miscellaneous Case No.208/81 filed by the Administrator of Waqfs in the Court of the Sub-ordinate Judge, 1st Court, Chittagong.”

15.              As observed by the trial Court, appellate Court and the High Court Division, the appellant did not challenge the said order of the Administrator of Waqfs, nor did he challenge the order by which the miscellaneous case was withdrawn and his application for addition of party as co-plaintiff was rejected on 18.2.82.

16.              Property belonging to a Waqf may only be transferred by its Mutawalli after getting sanction from the Administrator of Waqfs, as provided by section 57 read with section 56 of the Waqfs Ordinance, 1962. Section 56(3) provides that any transfer made by a Mutawalli without the sanction from the Administrator of Waqfs shall be declared void, if the Administrator, within 4 months of his coming to know of such transfer, or within 3 years from the date of such transfer, whichever is later, applies to the Civil Court in this behalf. Thus, it is solely in the domain of the Administrator of Waqfs to challenge any transfer of Waqf property by a Mutawalli. It transpires that at the instigation of the plaintiff-predecessor of the appellant, the Administrator of Waqfs filed Miscellaneous Case No.208 of 1981, which was subsequently withdrawn on the direction of the Administrator of Waqfs, who in the meantime scrutinised the records and found that the Naib Mutawalli (plaintiff in the suit) had consented to the transfer.

17.              The finding of the Administrator of Waqfs to the effect that the predecessor of the appellant-plaintiff in the suit had consented to the transfer of the property in question is a finding of fact which could have been agitated by the plaintiff in a properly constituted challenge before the concerned District Judge. But he did not do so. Hence, the finding that the transfer was valid cannot be challenged by him since there is a bar under section 102 of the Ordinance.

18.              In view of the above discussion, we are of the opinion that the instant appeal is devoid of merit and accordingly it is dismissed, without, however, any order as to costs.
