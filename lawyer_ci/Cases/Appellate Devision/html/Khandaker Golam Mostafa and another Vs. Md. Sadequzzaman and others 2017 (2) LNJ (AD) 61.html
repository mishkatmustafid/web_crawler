JUDGMENT

Muhammad Imman Ali, J: This civil petition for leave to appeal is directed against the judgement and order dated 10.06.2014 passed by a Division Bench of the High Court Division in Writ Petition No. 3564 of 2013 making the Rule absolute. 

2.            The facts relevant for disposal of the instant civil petition for leave to appeal are that the writ petitioner was appointed on 11.04.1998 as Headmaster of the Polytechnic Bilateral High School, Zummapara, Sadar, Rangpur (the School). By order dated 08.11.2007 the Managing Committee of the school suspended the writ petitioner temporarily from service and asked him to show cause as to why action should not be taken against him in respect of certain allegations as mentioned in the said notice. The writ petitioner replied on 27.03.2008 denying the allegations brought against him. Later on the writ petitioner was not allowed to attend the school and on several occasions the writ petitioner requested the managing committee for allowing him to sign on the attendance register and also to pay him subsistence allowance against the said order of temporary suspension, but to no effect.

3.            Challenging the order of temporary suspension the writ petitioner instituted Other Class Suit Nos. 777 of 2007 before the Court of Assistant Judge, Taragonj, Rangpur and simultaneously the writ petitioner also filed writ petition No. 10798 of 2007 before the High Court Division. Eventually the suit was dismissed by the judgement and decree dated 21.03.2012 and the Rule issued in the writ petition was also discharged by the judgement and order dated 11.11.2008 and that both the proceedings failed on point of maintainability, the actions being found premature.

4.            Thereafter, the writ petitioner received the impugned memo bearing No.2/Hp/ 1256/183/(10) dated 21.01.2013 issued by the Secondary and Higher Secondary Education Board, Dinajpur (the Board) approving dismissal order passed in respect of service of the writ petitioner. Challenging the said memo the respondent herein as petitioner filed Writ Petition No. 3565 of 2013 before the High Court Division and obtained Rule. 

5.            The Headmaster (in charge) of the school as writ respondent No. 13 filed an affidavit-in-opposition controverting the statements as made in the writ petition. He contended, inter alia, that bringing certain allegations as to misappropriation of fund and other irregularities the managing committee of the school by their resolution decided to suspend the petitioner temporarily to initiate a departmental proceeding. Accordingly, by the letter dated 08.11.2007 the writ petitioner was suspended temporarily from service and was also asked to show cause against the allegations as mentioned in the show cause notice and the writ petitioner replied against the show cause notice.

6.            Thereafter, a three member enquiry committee was constituted pursuant to “the Recognized Non-Government Secondary School Teachers (Board of Intermediate and Secondary Education, Rajshahi) Terms and Conditions of Service Regulations, 1979” (the Regulations, 1979). The enquiry committee submitted report on 12.12.2007 recommending to dismiss the petitioner from service as the allegations brought against him were found true and genuine. On the basis of the said enquiry report, the managing committee of the school by their resolution dated 13.12.2007 dismissed the petitioner from service pursuant to regulation 12 of the Regulations, 1979. Thereafter, the Appeal and Arbitration Committee of the Board by their resolution dated 05.01.2013 recommended to approve the order of dismissal. Accordingly, the Board in its meeting dated 08.01.2013 approved the order of dismissal pursuant to the regulation 12 of the Regulations, 1979 and it was communicated to the writ petitioner by the memo dated 21.01.2013. In view of the facts, the writ petitioner was dismissed from service following the procedures as laid down in the Regulations, 1979. Further contentions of this writ respondent are that the writ petitioner unsuccessfully filed Title Suit Nos. 777 of 2007 and the writ petition No. 10768 of 2007 challenging the action dismissing his service. 

7.            In due course after hearing both the parties by the impugned judgement and order the said Rule was made absolute. Hence, the writ respondent Nos. 6 and 13 as petitioners have filed the instant civil petition for leave to appeal before this Division.    

8.            Mr. Shahidul Islam, learned Advocate appearing on behalf of the petitioners submitted that the impugned Memo dated 21.01.2013 was legally issued by the proper authority approving the dismissal of the writ petitioner in accordance with the recommendation of the Appeal and Arbitration Board dated 05.01.2013. He further submitted that the High Court Division erred in basing its decision upon consideration that the dismissal of the writ petitioner was based on surmise and conjecture since against the order of suspension dated 08.11.2007 the writ petitioner filed Other Suit No. 777 of 2007 against the Managing Committee of the School and that suit was dismissed. He also submitted that the High Court Division misconstrued and misunderstood the materials on record in considering that the Rule issued in Writ Petition No. 10768 of 2007 had been discharged as premature and that was the reason behind the decision to approve his dismissal. He submitted that the High Court Division failed to appreciate the facts and circumstances of the case in their true perspective and did not consider the fact that, prior to his dismissal, notice was served upon the writ petitioner to show cause on 18.12.2012 with regard to the allegations brought against him and he also appeared before the Appeal and Arbitration Committee in its 5th meeting on 05.01.2013, and after hearing the writ petitioner the committee recommended to approve the final dismissal from service of the writ petitioner and on the strength of that resolution the President, Board Committee and Chairman of the Secondary and Higher Secondary Education Board, Denajpur on 08.01.2013 unanimously  approved the decisions of the Appeal and Arbitration Committee. He submitted that the High Court Division misdirected itself in considering that the dismissal of the writ petitioner was based on the findings of the Courts in the suit as well as the earlier writ petition.

9.            Mr. Sharif Uddin Chaklader, learned Advocate appearing for respondent No. 1 made submissions in support of the impugned judgement and order of the High Court Division.

10.        We have considered the submissions of the learned Advocates for the parties concerned, perused the impugned judgement and order as well as the evidence and materials on record.

11.        It is a fact that challenging the temporary suspension of the writ petitioner Other Class Suit No. 777 of 2007 was instituted by him before the Court of the Assistant Judge, Taragonj, Rangpur and at the same time he filed Writ Petition No. 10768 of 2007 before the High Court Division. Both the actions by the writ petitioner-respondent No. 1 herein resulted in failure due to the fact that his actions were premature. Subsequently, an Inquiry Committee submitted its report on 12.12.2007 which recommended the writ petitioner’s dismissal from service and on the basis of the said report the Managing Committee by resolution dated 13.12.2007 dismissed the petitioner from service. That dismissal was seemingly approved by the Appeal and Arbitration Committee by resolution dated 05.01.2013 and the Education Board approved the order of dismissal in its meeting dated 08.01.2013, which was communicated to the writ petitioner by the impugned Memo dated 21.01.2013. But a close scrutiny of the resolution of the Appeal and Arbitration Committee is required. 

12.        We find from the impugned judgement that the High Court Division noted that the Appeal and Arbitration Committee in their meeting dated 05.01.2013 took the following resolution:

13.        The High Court Division observed that the two proceedings, namely the suit filed before the Assistant Judge and the earlier writ petition, were initiated challenging the order of temporary suspension and the writ petitioner lost the same on the ground that the proceedings were initiated against a premature action.

14.        The resolution of the Appeal and Arbitration Committee clearly shows that the writ petitioner’s dismissal was approved because the decision of the Courts were in favour of the authority “A¢ik¤š² fËd¡e ¢nrL LaÑªL c¡­ulLªa j¡jm¡l l¡u fË¢aù¡­el f­r  qJu¡u a¡­L Q¡L¥l£ q­a hlM¡­Ù¹l ¢hou¢V Ae¤­j¡ce Ll¡l SeÉ p¤f¡¢ln Ll¡ qmz” Hence, it is clear that the approval given by the Committee was influenced by the fact that the cases filed by the writ petitioner were decided against him.

15.        We also find from the impugned judgement that the High Court Division considered that the inquiry report shows that the Inquiry Committee did not examine any witness to come to their decision, and therefore, the inquiry was not held in accordance with the requirement of Regulations, 1979.

16.        The High Court Division concluded as follows:

         “Therefore, it is apparent that in the aforesaid judgements of the civil suit and the writ petition were related to temporary suspension order and that the issue as to propriety of dismissal order through a departmental proceeding was not adjudicated in those proceedings. Despite the Appeal and Arbitration Committee justified the dismissal order referring to those judgements and as such we are of the view that the decision of the Appeal and Arbitration Committee was not based on record and proper exercise of their jurisdiction.” 

17.        In view of the above, we are of the opinion that the impugned notice served by the Managing Committee was based on the approval of the Board, which was in turn based on the approval of the Appeal and Arbitration Committee, which was given on account of the decisions reached by the Courts in favour of the authority. As we noted earlier, the decisions of the Courts were against the Head Teacher because the challenge was against his temporary suspension order, and the decision were not on merit of the allegations brought against him. 

18.        On scrutiny it is apparent that the approval of the Appeal and Arbitration Committee was not based on the consideration of the report of the Inquiry Committee. In any event, we find that the High Court Division has noted that the Inquiry Committee did not examine any witness to come to their decision and, therefore, the inquiry was not held in accordance with the requirement of Regulations, 1979.

19.        In the facts and circumstances detailed above, we do not find any illegality or infirmity in the impugned judgement and order of the High Court Division. In our view, the judgement does not call for any interference by this Division.

         Accordingly, the civil petition for leave to appeal is dismissed.
