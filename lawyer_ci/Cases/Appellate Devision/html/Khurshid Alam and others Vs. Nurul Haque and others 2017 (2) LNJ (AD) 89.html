JUDGMENT

Muhammad Imman Ali, J: This civil petition for leave to appeal is directed against the judgement and order dated 05.04.2011 passed by a Division Bench of the High Court Division in Civil Revision No. 3471 of 2003 making the Rule absolute.

2.            The facts, relevant for disposal of the instant civil petition for leave to appeal, are that the petitioner as plaintiff instituted Title Suit No.204 of 1997 in the Court of the Assistant Judge, Sadar, Noakhali for declaration of title in the ‘Kha’ schedule land of an area of .46 acre contained within ‘Ka’ schedule of .51 acre appertaining to plots Nos. 1516, 1517 and 1518 of D.S. Khatian No. 267, M.R.R. Khatian No. 238 under 98 Maijdee within police station-Sudharam. They contended, inter alia, that the land of D.S. Khatian No. 267 while it was owned by Shamsher Ali Hazi in his Sikari Taluki right the same was auction sold in Pattani Sale No. 294 of 1942-43 and Har Govinda Sil auction purchased the same by Miscellaneous Case No. 10 and then Hagendra Kumar Chakraborty while he was owner by Sikmi Taluki Settlement he settled .38½ acre of land with Kobbad Mia and Abdul Khaleque by registered patta dated 31.01.1947 and by another registered patta dated 29.04.1949 settled .12½ acre of land in favour of Kabbad Mia, Abdul Khaleque and Majibal Haque. Thus Kobbad Mia after getting (.19½+04).23 acre of land in his share sold the same to his wife Mafia Khatun by registered kabala No. 3160 dated 18.05.1950 and after death of Kobbad Mia his wife Mafia Khatun married Abdul Khaleque(brother of Kobbad Mia) and then she died issueless leaving Abdul Khaleque as husband and Mahmuda Khatun as sister. Mahmuda Khatun sold .06 acre of her share by registered kabala No. 5545 dated 21.04.1964 in favour of Abdul Khaleque who subsequently sold .09 acre of land to Tafura Begum, the sister of defendant No. 62. Abdul Khaleque thus while he had been owner and possessor of total .37 acre of land in the disputed khatian died leaving the plaintiffs as his heirs to enjoy the same in their title and possession. On the other hand, the predecessor of defendant Nos. 1-11 had got only .04 acre by dint of the settlement by registered patta No. 2236 dated 29.06.1949. During last S.A. operation the land of pattas dated 31.09.1947 and 29.06.1949 was recorded in M.R.R. Khatian No. 238, but wrongly showing the share of Majibul Haque 4 annas share instead of 1 anna 6 gondas 3 karas, and share of Abdul Khaleque as 20 annas instead of 14 annas 13 gondas 1 kara and the defendants also got Mutation Khatian No. 2472 for .29 ¼ acre on the basis of illegal order dated 22.06.1997 in Mutation Case No. 885 of 1996-97 beyond the knowledge of the plaintiffs and they have homestead, pond, Bhiti, garden etc. in the suit land and since the defendants denied their title in the ‘Kha’ schedule land, the plaintiffs filed the suit. 

3.            The defendants, respondent Nos. 1-11 herein contested the suit by filing joint written statement denying all the material averments made in the plaint. They stated, inter alia, that the three brothers, Abdul Khaleq, Kobbad Miah and Majibul Haque while living in joint mess, with the sale proceeds of their paternal property, acquired the ‘Ka’ schedule property by registered pattas dated 31.01.1947 and 29.4.1949 and as Majibul Haque was minor at that time he was not competent to execute Kabuliyat and personal properties were sold in the name of two brothers and one sister, and Kabuliyat was given in the name of two brothers and in fact the land under patta dated 31.01.1947 was acquired by the heirs of all the three brothers. The land of plot No. 1516 is a pond and plot Nos. 1517/1518 are bhiti and after taking settlement Majibul Haque had been possessing the same as homestead after constructing house. The story of sale to Mafia Khatun or sale by Mahmuda Khatun is false, ineffective and without consideration. Their predecessor Majibul Haque was owner and possessor of the share of .17 acre by settlement and .07½ acre by inheritance from Kobbad Mia and .06 acre by purchase from Abdul Khaleque by registered kabala dated 19.01.1954. Thus the M.R.R. record reveals that Abdul Khaleque recorded more than his share with lesser share of Majibul Haque. On the other hand, Abdul Khaleque sold .06 acre to Mominullah by registered kabala dated 29.01.1975 and Abdul Khaleque created a fictitious deed dated 17.02.1972 in favour of Tapura Begum for .09 acre of land and Abdul Khaleque gifted .03¾ acre of plot No. 1517 in favour of Majibul Haque’s wife Mafia Khatun by registered kabala dated 14.09.1991. So, the plaintiffs’ claim of title in the ‘Kha’ schedule unspecified land is baseless and unfounded and the defendants have title and possession in land of mutated khatian No. 2472 by paying up to date rents, and the defendants deny any cause of action for filing this suit and prayed for dismissal of the suit with costs.      

4.            After hearing the parties and considering the evidence and materials on record the Senior Assistant Judge, Noakhali by his judgement and decree dated 22.11.2000 decreed the suit. Then the defendants preferred Title Appeal No. 204 of 2000 before the District Judge, Noakhali. On transfer, the appeal was heard by the Additional District Judge, First Court, Noakhali, who by his judgement and decree dated 09.07.2003 dismissed the appeal thereby affirming the judgment and decree passed by the trial Court.

5.            Being aggrieved by the judgement and decree passed by the appellate Court, the defendants filed Civil Revision No. 3471 of 2003 before the High Court Division and obtained Rule, which upon hearing the parties was made absolute. Hence, the plaintiffs are now before us having filed the instant civil petition for leave to appeal. 

6.            Mr. Mahbub Ali, learned Senior Advocate appearing on behalf of the petitioner submitted that the High Court Division in its revisional jurisdiction erred in reversing the concurrent finding of facts arrived at by the Courts below. He further submitted that the High Court Division committed error of law finding that the suit land was unspecified although both the Courts below held that the suit for declaration of title was filed by the plaintiffs in respect of specified land measuring 28 decimals out of 51 decimals under M.R.R. Khatian No. 238 described in the schedule of the plaint. He finally submitted that the High Court Division made the Rule absolute without considering and discussing the elaborate judgements of the trial Court and the appellate Court and in holding that the plaintiffs being co-sharers had to file a suit for partition and that a suit for mere declaration of title without specification was not maintainable although the Courts below found that for specified portion of land the plaintiffs have filed the suit for declaration of title and had rightly decreed the suit.  

7.            No one has appeared for the respondents. 

8.            We have considered the submissions of the learned Advocate for the petitioners, perused the impugned judgement and order of the High Court Division and other connected papers on record.    

9.            We find that the High Court Division noted that admittedly the parties are co-sharer but the plaintiffs failed to make out any case of their exclusive possession in the schedule ‘Kha’ land to the plaint. Upon quoting the provision as contained in Order VII Rule 3 of the Code of Civil Procedure, the High Court Division observed that it appears from the plaint that the suit property has not been specifically described to identify the same sufficiently and that a suit for mere declaration of title without specification is not maintainable, being hit by the proviso to section 42 of the Specific Relief Act.

10.        Upon perusal of the plaint we find that the suit land has been described as schedule ‘Ka’-. The quantum of land does not appear to be mathematically accurate. Nevertheless, it is reproduced below:

11.        When the plaintiffs claim a specified quantum of land described in one schedule, which is part of land contained in another schedule not identified by giving boundaries or sketch map, and when the disputed land is only a part of total in a plot, then the disputed land must be clearly identified by stating boundary and giving sketch map to fulfill the requirement of Order VII Rule 3. We find that in  the description of the land as detailed in the schedule, which is quoted above, there is no identification of the disputed land. Moreover, it is an impossibility to have land of plot No. 1517 which is stated to be situated in the west-east portion. The land could be either in the west or the east but not in the west-east portion. “f¢ÕQj f§hÑ¡wn”.  

12.        We find from the evidence of P.W. 4 as stated in his cross-examination that the homestead of the defendants is located on plot No. 1517. This clearly indicates that the plaintiffs are not in exclusive possession of the suit land. 

13.        In view of the discussion above, we do not find any illegality or infirmity in the impugned judgement.

         Accordingly, the civil petition for leave to appeal is dismissed.
