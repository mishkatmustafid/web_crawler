JUDGMENT

Md. Abdul wahhab miah, j: These two civil appeals (C.A), by leave are from a common Judgment and order dated 15-04-13 passed by the High Court Division in petition Nos.17056 and 17216 of 2012 making the Rule Nisi issued in the respective Writ petition absolute.

2.            Respondent Nos.1-4 of C.A. No.228 and  Responden Nos.1 and 2 of C.A. No.229 of 2015 as the petitioners respectively filed Writ petition Nos.17056 and 17216 of 2012 before the High Court Division challenging Memo No.B.R–Dhaka 98/98/792 dated 01.10.2012, and Memo No. B.R-Dhaka 98/98/799 dated 02.10.2012, both issued by writ Respondent No.4, Manager Court of Wards, Bhawal Raj Estate Dhaka requesting respondent No.5, the Registar Dhaka not to  register any deed of transfer / sale of any property of C. S. Plot Nos. 2497, and 2582 of C.S Khation No. 118 of Mouza  Baunia J. L. No.211 as found at page 510 of projabili property Gazette (Annexure C to the Writ petition) corresponding to S .A. Khation Nos. 118/ 76, 183, 184, 188, 198, 212. 220, 1747, 179, 118/2, and 234 corresponding to R.S. plot Nos. 6108, 6109, 6112 and 6116, of R. S. Khation Nos. 63, 116, 117, 181, 240, 811, 818, 819, 962, 1083, 1371, 1490 and 1589 and C. S. Plot Nos. 2035, 2106, 2137, 2142, 2151, 2176, 2972, 2976, 2977, 2987, 3280, 3290, and 2977/3459 recorded in C.S. Khation Nos.554, 555 and C.S . Plot Nos. 2748 and 2970 of C. S. Khation Nos. 118, C. S. Khation Nos. 3280/3438 of C.S. Khation No.646 and C.S. plot No.3279 of C.S. Khation Nos.12 of Mouza Baunia of J. L. No.211 along with some other properties of other plots of the aforesaid Khations corresponding to as many as 66 R. S. plots respectively (Plots are not mentioned as those Would not be necessary in disposing the appeals) to have been issued without lawful authority and were of no legal effect and accordingly the Rule Nisi Was issued in the respective writ petition.

3.            The writ petitions were contested by writ respondent Nos.3 and 4 by filing affidavit-in-opposition.

4.            The High Court Division hearing the parties by the impugned judgment and order made the rule Nisi Issued in The respective writ petition absolute and then made certain observations  With caution to the Land Reform Board and Court of Wards (writ respondent Nos. 3 and 4).

5.            Being aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the impugned judgment and order passed in the respective writ petition Civil petition for Leave to appeal Nos. 2200 and 2201 of 2013 resection, were filed before this Division by writ respondent Nos.3 and 4 and leave was granted giving rise to these two C.As.

6.            Mr. Hasan Arif, Learned Advocate for the appellants at the very outset Submits that he would not press the grounds on which leave was granted, But he would press very much for expunging the observation made by the High Court Division in the impugned   judgment to the effect:

“If in future any such illegal and uncalled for claim is sought for by the court of wards or if any litigation is continuing or started afresh because of court of wards action by claiming title over the said “projabili property “by the Court of Wards and the land reform Board then they would be liable to be dealt with for contempt of Court, apart from such litigations been (sic) abated under P.O.90of 1972.

Under such circumstances we think it would proper to call the Manger court of wards and the land Administrator to explain their position in the respect because the government officials   under no circumstances can play with the property of the general peoples. This court thus thought that exercising the power conferred under the law we   should   call the said officials to explain their conduct as to these two cases in particular.  But considering that it will be nothing but a future exercise of this court on the matter,   because when the   respondents could not come forward with a proper affidavit giving proper explanations supported by the papers, we simply can express our anxiety and great concern that the Government official are doing this only to harass the general people of the country and thereby they are trying to have some personal gain over it.  As   such the respondents No.3 and   4 are cautioned not to deal with such property of the general people in such mannter which can be subject matter of harassment of the general people”

7.            He submits that the quoted observations were made unnecessarily out of the context after the Rules were made absolute.

8.            Mr. Khalilur Rahman, learned Advocate for respondent Nos.1-4 in both the appeals found it difficult to resist the submission of Mr. Hassan Arif.

From the impugned judgment, it appears that the observations quoted hereinbefore, were made absolute finding in clear terms the impugned Memos to have been issued without lawful authority and were of no legal effect. We are of the view that whatever observations the High Court. Division wanted to make in the context, it could make so before its final decision if those were necessary for the disposal of the writ petition or in giving relief to the writ petitioners in the facts and circumstances of the case, but not after the disposal of the rule. And then again no observation could be made pre-empting the future action (s) or future behavior of the writ respondents.  A  Court has right to make observation  (s)  about the behavior or action of the writ respondent (s) in future, particularly, about the possible disobedience or violation of the order/direction/observation of the Court, the reason being that when  a Court presumed that such order/direction /observation shall be obeyed and complied with in its true purport and spirit. And if the Court itself nurishes an element of doubt in its mind as to the obedience or the compliance with its order/direction/ observation, then there cannot have any meaning in passing such order/giving such direction /making such observation. And such kind of doubt by a court shall make the people’s confidence in the administration of justice shaky. We would like to add further that a Court must always pass an order/give/ direction/and make observation in a positive way to ensure fair justice and not in a manner quoted hereinbefore.

9.            In view of the   above, our considered view is that the High Court Division was not justified in making the quoted observations after making the Rule Nisi absolute declaring the impugned Memos to have been issued without lawful authority and were of no legal effect. We are of the further view that justice would be best served if the quoted observations are expunged accordingly those are expunged.

         The appeals are disposed of with the above expunction
