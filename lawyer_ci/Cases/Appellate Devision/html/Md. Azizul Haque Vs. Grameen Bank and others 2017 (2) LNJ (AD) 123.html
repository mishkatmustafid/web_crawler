JUDGMENT

Muhammad Imman Ali, J: This civil appeal, by leave, is directed against the order dated 06.08.2009 passed by the Administrative Appellate Tribunal, Dhaka in A.A.T. Appeal No.218 of 2005 allowing the appeal, thereby setting aside the order dated 30.10.2005 passed by the Administrative Tribunal, Bogra, in A.T. Case No. 84 of 2004.

2.            Facts of the case, in brief, are that the appellant herein filed Administrative Tribunal Case No.84 of 2004 in the Administrative Tribunal, Bogra, challenging the order of his termination  from service. The appellant stated, inter alia, that he was appointed as a ‘Bank Worker’ in the Grameen Bank on 28.02.1990 and his appointment was made permanent on 19.09.1991. Subsequently, he was promoted to the post of Upper Division Assistant on 04.10.1994. On his satisfactory performance in service he was promoted to Upper Division Assistant (Senior) on 06.10.1997. Eventually on 04.11.2001, he was promoted to the rank of officer in the bank. On 06.05.2002, he filed an application to go on voluntary retirement, but the said application remained un-disposed for a long time. On 11.05.2004, the bank authority illegally terminated his service without any cause. He preferred a departmental appeal praying for giving him termination benefits. Subsequently, he made two more representations to the authority for reinstating him in service, but to no avail. Thus, he was compelled to file the case before the Administrative Tribunal, Bogra under section 4(2) of the Administrative Tribunals Act, 1980.

3.            Respondents No.2 and 3 contested the administrative tribunal case by filing written statement denying the material allegations of the appellant contending, inter alia, that the authority rightly terminated his service under Rule 13 of the Grameen Bank Service Rules, 1993 and the said order suffers from no illegality, and as such the case was liable to be rejected.

4.            After hearing the parties, the Administrative Tribunal, Bogra, by the order dated 30.10.2005 allowed A.T. Case No.84 of 2004 holding that the termination of the appellant from service without giving him either three months' notice or three months' salary and other financial benefits as per Rule 13 of the Grameen Bank Employees Service Rules was illegal.

5.            Being aggrieved, the present respondents preferred Administrative Appeal No.218 of 2005 before the Administrative Appellate Tribunal, Dhaka, and  after hearing the parties and considering the facts and circumstances of the case, the Administrative Appellate Tribunal allowed the appeal in part setting aside the judgement and order of the Administrative Tribunal, Bogra maintaining the termination order dated 11.05.2004 and directing the Grameen Bank authority to pay the petitioner  Md.  Azizul  Haque 3 months' salary and other attendant financial benefits within 2(two) months, as admissible under the provisions of Rule 13 of the Grameen Bank  Employees Service Regulations, 1993 together with a sum of Tk.10,000/- as cost of the litigation and Tk. 10,000/- as compensation to the petitioner, in total Tk.20,000/-only.

6.            Being aggrieved, the appellant filed Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No.1833 of 2009 and leave was granted to consider the following submissions of the learned advocate appearing for the petitioner:

   “I. That the Administrative Appellate  Tribunal was wrong in maintaining the impugned order of termination on making observation to the effect that no useful purpose will be served by reinstating the petitioner on an unwilling employer, as this rule is applicable in cases of master and servant relationship, but in the instant case, Grameen Bank having been established by a statute namely, Grameen Bank Ordinance, 1983 and the terms and conditions of the service of its employees being governed by statutory rules namely, Grameen Bank Employees Service Regulations, the relationship between Grameen Bank and its employees is not one of master and servant.

II. That Rule 13 of the Grameen Bank Employees Service Regulations, 1993 clearly provides that an employee can be terminated from service either on giving him three months' notice or on payment of three months' salary and other attendant financial benefits, but in this case, admittedly the authority did not either give the petitioner three months' notice or three months' salary or other attendant financial benefits before terminating the petitioner from service and even did not disclose anything about payment of three months' salary and other attendant financial benefits before filing of this case by the petitioner before the Administrative Tribunal and in the circumstances the Administrative Tribunal rightly declared the termination order illegal.

III. That the Administrative Appellate Tribunal though found the impugned termination order illegal for the reasons of non-service of three months' notice or non-payment of three months' salary and other attendant financial benefits as per Rule 13 of the Grameen Bank Employees Service Regulations, 1993, but wrongly maintained the impugned termination order on an erroneous notion.

IV. That the Administrative Appellate Tribunal committed illegality in maintaining the impugned termination order while it itself found the impugned termination order illegal.

V. That the impugned termination order being illegal there was no scope to maintain that order by the Administrative Appellate Tribunal.”

7.            Mr. Sk. Mohammad Morshed appearing for the appellant made submissions in line with the grounds upon which leave was granted. He also submitted that the appellant submitted an application for retirement from service on 06.5.2002 after having served Grameen Bank for more than 12 years and he is entitled to get pension. That application has not been disposed of with mala fide intention and Grameen Bank served notice of termination on 11.5.2004. The learned Advocate submitted that the Administrative Tribunal rightly directed the employer, Grameen Bank to reinstate the appellant and Grameen Bank has terminated the appellant only with intention to deprive him from getting pension and other benefits.    

8.            Mr. Abdur Razzak Khan, learned Senior Advocate appearing on behalf of the Grameen Bank submitted that the allegations brought against the appellant are of a serious and intimate nature and because of the delicate nature of the allegations involving a female employee, Grameen Bank decided to terminate the service of the appellant instead of starting departmental proceedings against him. The learned Advocate further submitted that the employer was willing to pay the requisite 3 months’ salary in lieu of notice and attendant financial benefits to the appellant.

9.            We have considered the submissions of the learned Advocates for the parties concerned, perused the impugned order as well as the evidence and materials on record.

10.        We find that the termination of the appellant was a “termination simpliciter” without attaching any stigma and that in fact no departmental proceedings were brought against the appellant in spite of serious allegations against him. In such a situation it would be illogical to sustain the order of the Administrative Tribunal thereby reinstating the appellant in service on an unwilling employer. Admittedly, the appellant was entitled to get 3 months’ salary in lieu of notice. The Administrative Appellate Tribunal, therefore, rightly allowed the appeal in part setting aside the judgement of the Administrative Tribunal.

11.        In the facts and circumstances of the case, at our instance, Mr. Abdur Razzak Khan has agreed to instruct the Grameen Bank to pay the appellant 2 years’ salary in order to bring this matter to a close.

12.        In view of the discussion above, we are of the opinion that justice will best be served if the appeal is disposed of in the following terms:

13.        The respondent Grameen Bank is hereby directed to pay the appellant Md. Azizul Haque salary for two years at the rate which was paid to him as on the date of his termination of service.

         Accordingly, with the above direction, the appeal is disposed of, without, however, any order as to costs. The impugned order of the Administrative Appellate Tribunal is modified, as stated above.
