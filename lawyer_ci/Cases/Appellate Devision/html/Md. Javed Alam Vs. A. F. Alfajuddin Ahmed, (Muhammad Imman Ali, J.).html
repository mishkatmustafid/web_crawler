Abandoned Buildings (Supplementary Provisions) Ordinance, (LIV of 1985)

Section 7

The Court of Settlement, being competent to decide the matter of the abandoned character of the property, after due consideration of all evidence and facts presented in the case decided that the property in question was not abandoned. The Government was the competent party to challenge that decision. Any occupier of the property as tenant under the Government, within the scheme authorising the Government to manage the said property, has no locus standi to challenge the decision of the Court of Settlement. If the Government succeeds in establishing that the property was lawfully included in the list of abandoned properties, then only the question of transferring the property to the occupier will become relevant.           . . .(20)

Code of Civil Procedure (V of 1908)

Order VII, Rule 11

Abandoned Buildings (Supplementary Provisions) Ordinance, (LIV of 1985)

Section 7

The inclusion of the property in the abandoned property list by gazette notification dated 23.9.1986 is negated and that decision that the property was not abandoned relates back to the date on which it was included in the gazette. In other words, the effect of the finding that it is not abandoned means that it was not abandoned on 23.9.1986. Hence, transfer of the property by way of sale dated 18.10.1990 to respondent No.1 is nullified since the property was not abandoned and, therefore, the Government had no authority to transfer the property. The decision of the Government to release the property from the “Ka” list of abandoned properties by Gazette dated 02.12.2004 has put a final seal on the matter. The abandoned character of the property having been established all the way to this Division and the challenges of the Government as well as the present respondent having been unsuccessful, there can be no doubt about the legality of the gazette notification dated 02.12.2004 and hence we find that the trial Court rightly rejected the plaint of writ-respondent No.1 under Order VII, Rule 11 of the Code of Civil Procedure. The judgement and order of the High Court Division is not in accordance with law and accordingly the impugned judgement and order is set aside and the judgement and decree dated 24.03.2006 passed by the learned Joint District Judge, First Court, Mymensingh in Other Class Suit No.03 of 2004 is upheld.                              . . .(21)

JUDGMENT

Muhammad Imman Ali, J:- This civil appeal, by leave, is directed against the judgement and order dated 15.03.2010 passed by a Division Bench of the High Court Division in First Appeal No.161 of 2006 allowing the appeal.

2.             The facts of the case, in short, are that respondent No.1 as the plaintiff instituted Other Class Suit No.3 of 2004 in the First Court of Joint District Judge, Mymensingh impleading the appellant and others as the defendants for declaration of his title to the land detailed in the second schedule to the plaint and for further declaration that the notice dated 24.12.2001 issued by Deputy Commissioner, Mymensingh (defendant No.1) in respect of the land detailed in the second schedule was void, illegal, collusive, without jurisdiction and not binding upon the plaintiff. The plaintiff stated, inter alia, that the suit land belonged to S.M. Rahmatullah and during Liberation War of 1971, he deserted the property and as such, the same was declared abandoned property under President’s Order 16 of 1972. The suit property was included in the ‘Ka’ list of the abandoned properties prepared on 23.09.1986. The present appellant filed an application under Ordinance No.54 of 1985 before the Court of Settlement for exclusion of the property from the abandoned properties list and the said application was registered as Case No.1019 of 1992 before the Court of Settlement and the case was heard by the  Court of Settlement, but the respondent-Government did not disclose the fact that the father of the applicant by practicing fraud made a claim that he got the property by way of auction purchase. Thereafter, the appellant on 10.12.1998 obtained an order from the Court of Settlement for excluding the property from the abandoned list. The appellant thereafter moved the High Court Division by filing writ petition for a direction upon the respondent-Government to restore the vacant possession of the suit property in his favour and obtained Rule Nisi in Writ Petition No.5021 of 1999. The High Court Division by judgement and order dated 04.03.2001 made the Rule Nisi absolute and directed the respondent-Government to hand over the vacant possession of the suit property to the appellant. Pursuant to the said judgement and order, the Government on 24.12.2001 directed the plaintiff respondent to hand over possession of the suit property within 7 days from the date of receipt of the said notice. Subsequently, the plaintiff-respondent herein was evicted from the suit property.

3.             The plaintiff-respondent stated that the Government took decision to dispose of abandoned properties and accordingly, the plaintiff applied for purchasing the suit property, and the appellant did not take any step to resist the sale of the suit property and the Government transferred the suit property to the plaintiff by a registered deed of sale dated 08.11.1990 for a consideration of Tk.1,01,091.00. Thereafter, the plaintiff took loan from respondent No.5, National Bank Limited and constructed a semi pucca structure on the suit land by spending about taka 20 Lac. The respondent-plaintiff on going through abandoned property list and records of the Court of Settlement discovered that by judgement and order dated 10.12.1998 it released the suit property from the abandoned properties list. In Writ Petition No.5021 of 1999, the High Court Division passed a direction upon the respondent-Government to hand over vacant possession of the suit property in favour of the appellant. The Deputy Commissioner, Mymensingh thereafter, on 24.12.2001 issued a notice upon the plaintiff directing him to hand over vacant possession and on receipt of the notice of eviction the plaintiff preferred Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No.226 of 2002 before this Division against the judgement and order dated 04.03.2001 passed in Writ Petition No.5021 of 1999. The Government also preferred Civil Petition for leave to Appeal No.2186 of 2001 before this Division against the said judgement and order dated 04.03.2001. In addition the plaintiff-respondent filed Writ Petition No.3817 of 2002 challenging the decision of the Court of Settlement in Case No.1019 of 1992 which had declared the suit property as not abandoned. The Rule issued in the said writ petition was discharged on contest on 14.06.2003, against which the plaintiff filed Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No.963 of 2003 before this Division. By its judgement and order dated 27.08.2003 this Division dismissed both the petitions. The Government, was made party in the case before the Court of Settlement as well as in the writ petition filed by the petitioner and the Government had taken all necessary steps in support of the plaintiff’s case, but the Court found that the property was not abandoned property and accordingly, released the same in favour of the appellant.

4.             The appellant herein, as defendant in the suit claimed that after disposal of the plaintiff’s leave petitions by this Division, the plaintiff-respondent lost all sorts of claim over the suit property and the present suit was barred by the aforesaid judgement and, as such, the plaint was liable to be rejected.

5.             The plaintiff-respondent filed written objection against the application for rejection of the plaint, stating that, the suit property was released illegally by the Court of Settlement in favour of appellant as he obtained the said order on the basis of some false and forged documents and that the Appellate Division did not dispose of the leave petitions on merit. The plaintiff further stated that the points which have been raised in the present suit were not decided in the writ petitions filed by the appellant and the respondents.

6.             The trial Court by its judgement and decree dated 23.4.2006 allowed the application and rejected the plaint of Other Class Suit No. 3 of 2004.

7.             Being aggrieved, the plaintiff preferred First Appeal No. 161 of 2006 before the High Court Division and upon hearing the parties the High Court Division by its judgement and order dated 15.03.2010 allowed the appeal.

8.             Feeling aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the judgement and order passed by the High Court Division, the appellant as petitioner filed civil petition for leave to appeal No.09 of 2011.

9.             Leave was granted on the following submissions of the learned Advocate appearing for the petitioner:

“That the transferee (Plaintiff) from the Government had purchased the suit property during the pendency of the claim by the petitioner before the Court of Settlement and the judgement and order passed by the Court of Settlement having been upheld up to the Appellate Division, the transferee would rise and fall with the Government and as such, he has no cause of action to bring the suit. She further submits that the Government has no right to transfer the suit property as the Court of Settlement had determined the character of the suit property to be not an abandoned property.

10.         Mr. M.I. Faruqui, learned Senior Advocate appearing on behalf of the appellant made submissions in line with the grounds upon which leave was granted. He further submitted that the plaintiff is seeking a declaration of title of property which has already been adjudicated up to this Division and hence the trial Court rightly rejected the plaint. He submitted that the High Court Division fell into error in finding that there were issues which would be raised in the present suit that had not been decided by the Court of Settlement or by the High Court Division in the writ petition. He submitted that the only issue in the suit is the plaintiff’s title which he got by way of purchase of the property from the Government. But this issue is already decided inasmuch as the Government had no title in the property because it had been decided up to this Division that the property was not abandoned and, hence, the Government had no title to sell.

11.         Mr. Mahbub Ali, learned Senior Advocate appearing for the respondent submitted that that neither the Court of Settlement nor the High Court Division sitting in its writ jurisdiction can decide/ adjudicate the disputed question of facts as have been raised by the plaintiff therefore, the Civil Court is the only forum to redress grievances of the plaintiff as such, the appeal is liable to be dismissed. He further submitted that since, the case before the Court of Settlement was not filed within the stipulated period of limitation nor the original judgement of the Court of Settlement was found in the record therefore, judicial record was tampered with and fraud was practiced upon the Court of Settlement in having the property excluded from ‘Ka’ list and as such, without holding inquiry and trial and without thrashing out the allegations in the plaint it cannot be rejected. He further submitted that neither the High Court Division nor the Appellate Division decided the issues raised in the plaint on merit and the jurisdiction of the Civil Court is still open to thrash out the allegations. He submitted that the principles of res-judicata in writ matters, where decisions are made in a summary proceedings mostly on affidavits, it should not be applied with the same strictness as in disputes in suits or proceedings determined in Civil Courts. He submitted that since, the defendant-appellant obtained the judgement from the Court of Settlement suppressing the fact of the title and possession of the plaintiff in the suit property and wilfully and deliberately did not make the plaintiff a party in the proceedings nor the Government disclosed the fact of selling the property to the plaintiff by registered deed dated 08.11.1990 and delivered possession to him therefore, in any way the judgement of the Court of Settlement was not binding upon the respondents. He submitted that Government action in contesting the writ petition as well as filing of Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No.2186 of 2001 feeling aggrieved only to the extent of imposing penalty at the rate of 20% interest on the rent in no way can be said to have been decided the matter finally rather, the conduct of the Government as well as the appellant in contesting the proceedings is clearly collusive one which are liable to be adjudicated in the Civil Court.

12.         We have considered the submissions of the learned Advocates for the parties concerned, perused the impugned judgement and order of the High Court Division and other connected papers on record.

13.    The property in question was listed as an abandoned property in the Gazette (Extra Ordinary) dated 23.9.1986 at page 9762 (70) Sl. No.9 in the ‘Ka’ list. Javed Alam son of the original owner, namely S.M. Rahmatullah (deceased) filed a case before the First Court of Settlement on 08.01.1987 for releasing the property from the list of abandoned properties. The case was subsequently transferred to the Second Court of Settlement and numbered as Settlement Case No.1019 of 1992. In due course after hearing, the abandoned property case was decided in favour of Md. Javed Alam by order dated 10.12.1998. Due to the inaction of the concerned authority in releasing the property and handing over possession to him, Md. Javed Alam filed Writ Petition No.5021 of 1999. The Rule Nisi obtained by the writ petitioner was made absolute by judgement and order dated 04.03.2001 by which the Government of Bangladesh-respondent No.3 was directed to comply with the judgement and order dated 10.12.1998 passed in Settlement Case No. 1019 of 1992 by evicting the unauthorised occupants and also to pay rent to the owner of the property with a direction to pay interest at 20% of the rent actually received by the Government in respect of the property in question from the date of unauthorised occupation till the date of restoration of possession thereof.

14.         The Government respondent, by filing Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No.2186 of 2001, did not challenge the legality of the decision of the Court of Settlement which had found that the property was not abandoned, but challenged the direction of the High Court Division to pay interest at 20% on the rent actually received by the Government from the date of unauthorised occupation till the date of restoration of possession. This Division disposed of the said civil petition maintaining the judgement of the High Court Division except the part wherein direction had been given to the Government to pay interest @20% per annum on the rent actually received from the date of unauthorised occupation till the date of restoration of possession, which was deleted. However, it was made clear that “the rent actually received by the Government from the date of unauthorized occupation till the date of restoration of possession should be paid to the respondent. The other part of the judgement i.e. making direction to restore vacant possession of the property in question to the respondent is maintained.”

15.         Thereafter, the Government released the property in question from the “Ka” list of the abandoned properties list by Gazette dated 02.12.2004.

16.         It appears from the records that the present respondent A.F. Alfajuddin Ahmed filed Writ Petition No. 3817 of 2002 challenging the decision of the Court of Settlement dated 10.12.1998 passed in Settlement Case No. 1019 of 1992 claiming that he having been put into possession of the property in question by the Government was given the option to purchase the property, which he did by registered deed of sale dated 18.11.1990 and that all of a sudden he received notice dated 24.11.2001 directing him to hand over vacant possession of the property to Md. Javed Alam.

17.         In due course after hearing the parties the Rule was discharged by reference to the judgement and order of this Division in Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No. 2186 of 2001 where it was held:

“The other part of the judgement, i.e. in making direction to restore vacant possession of the property in question to the respondents is maintained.” The High Court Division also observed that it cannot interfere with the decision of the Court of Settlement passed in Settlement Case No. 1019 of 1992 since civil petition for leave to appeals filed by the writ-petitioner is pending before the Appellate Division.

18.         We find from the records that Writ respondent A.F. Alfajuddin Ahmed had filed Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No.226 of 2002 and No.963 of 2003 challenging the judgement and order dated 04.03.2001 and 14.06.2003 passed by the High Court Division in writ petition Nos. 5021 of 1999 and 3817 of 2002. By order dated 27.08.2003 both leave petitions were dismissed.

19.         From the above facts and circumstances it transpires that the decision of the Court of Settlement in Settlement Case No. 1019 of 1992 was challenged both by the present respondent No.1, A.F. Alfajuddin Ahmed as well as by the Government and the challenges have been unsuccessful up to this Division and subsequently the property was released by the Government vide gazette notification dated 02.12.2004.

20.         It must be borne in mind that the claim of the writ respondent No.1 depends entirely on the success of the claim put forward by the Government with respect to the abandoned character of the property in question. We find from the records that the Court of Settlement, being competent to decide the matter of the abandoned character of the property, after due consideration of all evidence and facts presented in the case decided that the property in question was not abandoned. The Government was the competent party to challenge that decision. Any occupier of the property as tenant under the Government, within the scheme authorising the Government to manage the said property, has no locus standi to challenge the decision of the Court of Settlement. If the Government succeeds in establishing that the property was lawfully included in the list of abandoned properties, then only the question of transferring the property to the occupier will become relevant.

21.         The effect of the decision of the Court of Settlement that the property was not abandoned is that the inclusion of the property in the abandoned property list by gazette notification dated 23.9.1986 is negated and that decision that the property was not abandoned relates back to the date on which it was included in the gazette. In other words, the effect of the finding that it is not abandoned means that it was not abandoned on 23.9.1986. Hence, transfer of the property by way of sale dated 18.10.1990 to respondent No.1 is nullified since the property was not abandoned and, therefore, the Government had no authority to transfer the property. It is curious that the Government did not mention the sale of the property at any point in time during the proceedings before the Court of Settlement or indeed when the matter was pending before the High Court Division. When the Government came before this Division, only the direction of the High Court Division with regard to interest to be paid on the rental amount was challenged. In our view the decision of the Government to release the property from the “Ka” list of abandoned properties by Gazette dated 02.12.2004 has put a final seal on the matter. The abandoned character of the property having been established all the way to this Division and the challenges of the Government as well as the present respondent having been unsuccessful, there can be no doubt about the legality of the gazette notification dated 02.12.2004 and hence we find that the trial Court rightly rejected the plaint of writ-respondent No.1 under Order VII, Rule 11 of the Code of Civil Procedure.

22.         In view of the above discussion, we find that the judgement and order of the High Court Division is not in accordance with law and accordingly the impugned judgement and order is set aside and the judgement and decree dated 24.03.2006 passed by the learned Joint District Judge, First Court, Mymensingh in Other Class Suit No.03 of 2004 is upheld.

23.         In the result the appeal is allowed, without however, any order as to costs.
