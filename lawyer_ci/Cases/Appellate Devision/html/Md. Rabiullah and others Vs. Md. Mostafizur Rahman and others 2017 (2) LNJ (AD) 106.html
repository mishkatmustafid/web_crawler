JUDGMENT

Md. Abdul Wahhab Miah,J: This Appeal, by leave, is from the judgment and order dated the 13th day of January, 2009 passed by a Single Bench of the High Court Division in Civil Revision No. 4843 of 2000 making the Rule absolute.

2.            Short facts essential to dispose the appeal are that one late Elahi Box Sarker made a Waqf of some of his properties under the name “Elahi Box Sarker Waqf Estate” (hereinafter referred to as the Waqf Estate) and the Waqf Estate duly registered on 17.10.1321 B.S. As per terms of the Waqf deed, the Waqif became the first Mutawalli of the Waqf Estate. Thereafter Fakir Ullah Miah , son of Nidam Nessa, the first daughter of the Waqif, Sabirullah Shah, son of Chetan Nesa, another daughter of the Waqif and Mostafur Rahman Chowdhury, son of tayabatun Nessa, the youngest daughter of the Waqif, were appointed as Mutawallis in succession. Threafter, the Administrator of Waqfs appointed respondent Nos.1-3 herein as the Joint Mutwallis of the Waqf Estate on 1st April, 1998 for the alleged better administration of the Waqf property. The Waqf Estate owns landed properties in Districts, Rajshahi and Nawabgonj. Accordingly, the Administrator of Waqfs directed the Additional Deputy Commissioners (Revenue) of both the Districts to hand over the charge of the properties of the Waqf Estate to the Joint Mutwallis. Being aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the said order of the Administrator of Waqfs, the appellants filed Miscellaneous Appeal No. 40 of 1998 before the District Judge, Nawabgonj. The learned District Judge upon hearing the parties by the judgment and order dated 19 November, 2000 allowed the appeal and set aside the order of the Administrator of Waqfs holding that respondent No.1 obtained the order appointing him and his two brothers (respondents Nos.1-3 herein) as the joint Mutwallis of the Waqf Estate by practicing fraud upon the Administrator of Waqfs inasmuch as he suppressed the fact that the solenama filed on 14.09.1996 in Miscellaneous Appeal No.23 of 1991 was not proved and was not also accepted by the Court and the said appeal  was withdrawn before any order could be passed on the solenama.

3.            Against this order of the learned District Judge, respondent Nos.1-3 filed the above mentioned civil revision before the High Court Division under section 115(1) of the Code of Civil Procedure (the Code). A single Bench by the impugned judgment and order made the Rule absolute and set aside the judgment and order of the learned District Judge in Miscellaneous Appeal No. 40 of 1998 and thereby the order of the Administrator of Waqfs stood revived.

4.            The High Court Division while interfering with the judgment and order of the learned District Judge observed that “For Fair administration of the property in my view there must be common mutawalli”. The High Court Division also observed that the administrator of Waqfs “also did so on 01.04.1998 by appointing the petitioners who were still Mutawalli of the portion of the aforesaid Waqf Estate fallen in the Rajshahi district and as such there should not be different administrators for the other part of the same Waqf Estate. " The High Court Division did not also found the impugned order legal, "because the District Judge, Nawabgonj has not brought in to his consideration that the petitioners are the mutawalli of the Waqf Estate fallen in the Rajshahi district. So. for the benefit of the Administration of the aforesaid Waqf Estate the impugned order should be set aside and the Additional Deputy Commissioner (Rev.), Nawabgonj be directed to hand over the charge of the Waqf Estate fallen in his district to the petitioners who are newly appointed Mutawalli of the entire Waqf Estate by the Waqf' Administrator on 01-04-1998".

5.            Against the impugned judgment and order, Petition for Leave Appeal No, 490 of 2009 was filed before this Court and leave was granted to consider the additional ground to the effect:

“the High Court Division fell in error in reversing the judgment and order dated 19.06.2000 passed in Miscellaneous Appeal No. 40 of 1998 being based on findings of fact arrived at by the Appellate Court to the effect that the respondent by practicing fraud and by suppressing the order number 54 dated 04.02.1995 by which the Miscellaneous Appeal No.23 of 1991 was allowed to be withdrawn before accepting the solenama dated 14.06.1996.”

6.            Mr. Probir Neogi, learned Counsel for the appellants having placed the order of the Administrator of Waqfs, judgment and order of the learned District Judge and the impugned judgment and order, has submitted that in view of the provisions of section 17 of the Code , the properties of the Waqf Estate in question having been situated in two different districts, i.e. Nawabgonj and Rajshahi, appeal under section 43 of the Waqfs Ordinance, 1962 (the Ordinance) could be filed before any of the two District Judges, i.e. Nawabgonj and Rajshahi. Therefore, miscellaneous appeal in question was rightly filed in the Court of District Judge, Nawabgonj. Mr. Probir Neogi has further submitted that since no procedure has been provided for in "the "Ordinance for disposal of appeal under section 43 thereof, the provisions of Order XLI of the Code shall be applicable in view of the provisions of section 141 of the Code. But the High Court Division failed to consider the above provisions of law in passing the impugned judgment and order. In support of his contention, he has relied upon the cases of Aminul Haque Shah Chowdhury-Vs-Abdul Wahab Shah Chowdhury and others 4 MLR(AD)367 and Amir Sultan Ali  Hyder-Vs-Md. K. Alam alias S. Alam and others reported in 29 DLR(SC) 295.  He has further submitted that the High Court Division did not at all consider the finding of the Appellate Court that respondent No.l herein obtained the order of joint mutawalliship from the Administrator of Waqfs by practicing fraud suppressing the fact that the solenama dated 14.09.1996 filed in Miscellaneous Appeal No.23 of 1991, was not proved and that before the same could be accepted by the Court, the appeal itself had been withdrawn.

7.            Mr. A. J. Mohammad Ali, learned Counsel, for respondent Nos.2-7, on the other hand, has supported the impugned judgment and order.

8.            Admittedly the appeal was filed before the District Judge under section 43 of the Ordinance and in the Ordinance, no procedure has been provided for as to the disposal of an appeal. In view of the provisions of section 141 read with section 108(b) of the Code, the procedure provided therein in Order XLI was applicable in the case of disposal of the appeal in question. In the context, Mr. Probir Neogi rightly placed reliance upon thecases of Aminul Haque Shah Chowdhury (supra) and Amir Sultan Ali Hyder (supra). And we also find substance in the submission of Mr. Probir Neogi that in view of the provisions of section 17 of the Code, the appeal under section 43 of the Ordinance Could be filed before the District Judge of either of the District, Rajshahi or Nawabgonj. So there was nothing wrong in filing the appeal before the District Judge, Nawabgonj against the order of the Administrator of Waqfs by the present appellant and the District Judge rightly decided the same.

9.            So far as the merit of the appeal is concerned, from the judgment of the District Judge, it is clear that Miscellaneous Appeal No.23 of 1991 was filed by respondent No.1 against the order of the Administrator of Waqfs dated 16.03.1999 and when that appeal was pending in the Court of the Additional District Judge, respondent No. I as the appellant and two of his brothers who were respondent Nos.8 and 21 filed a solenama on 14.06-1996 with the prayer for disposal of the appeal (Miscellaneous Appeal No. 31 of 1991) in terms of the solenama. The learned Additional District Judge by order No.43 dated 14.09.1996 kept the same with the record and fixed the next date on 28.09.1996 for hearing the appeal along with the solenama. Then upto 09.11.1997 adjournment was taken for hearing the appeal and the solenamaa and lastly the appeal was fixed for hearing on 04.02.1998 and on this date though the appellant and the respondents were present in Court, the appellant filed application for withdrawal of the  appeal and the prayer for withdrawal of the appeal was allowed on that very date, i.e. on 04.02.1998; thus it is clear that the appeal stood withdrawn before the solcnama dated 14.09.1996 could be accepted. But by suppressing the last order of withdrawal of the appeal (Miscellaneous Appeal No.23 of 1991) the order of appointment of the joint mutawalliship was obtained from the Administrator of Waqfs on the basis of the so-called solenama. The Appellate Court gave clear finding that the solenama filed on 14.09.1996 was not proved,  and the same was not accepted by the Court and that subsequently the appeal (Miscellaneous Appeal No.23 of 1991) having been withdrawn, the  solenama filed on 14.09.1996 had no legal consequence and on the basis of the said solenama, respondent Nos.1-3 had no locus standi  to file application on 15.03.1998 and as such, the appointment of the said respondents as the joint mutawallis by the Administrator of Waqfs on 01.04.1998 was obtained by practising fraud upon him; therefore, the order was liable to be set aside.

10.        Mr. A. J. Mohammad Ali, found it difficult to dispute the above factual aspect of the case.

11.        In making the Rule absolute, the High Court Division did at all advert the finding of the Appellate Court that the order of appointment of respondent Nos.1-3 as the joint mutawallis was obtained from the Administrator of Waqfs by practicing fraud upon him. The High Court Division also failed to consider the age old legal principle that fraud vitiates everything. The High Court Division also failed to consider that in view of the provisions  of section 17 of the Code, the District Judge, Nawabgonj had the jurisdiction to hear the appeal under section 43 of the Ordinance though some of the properties Waqf Estate were situated in District-Rajshahi.  In view of the above, the impugned judgment and order cannot be maintained and must be set aside.

12.        For the reasons stated hereinbefore, the appeal is allowed. The impugned judgment and order is set aside and those of the District Judge are restored. The Administrator of Waqfs is directed to appoint Mutwalli in respect of the Waqf Estate in question as expeditiously as possible, not beyond 3(three) months from the date of receipt of this judgment, keeping in view the terms of the Waqf deed and the provisions of law notifying the concerned parties and hearing them.

13.        Office is directed to send a copy of this judgment to the Administrator of Waqfs immediately.
