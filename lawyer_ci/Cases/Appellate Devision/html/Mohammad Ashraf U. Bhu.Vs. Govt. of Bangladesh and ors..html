JUDGMENT

MUHAMMAD IMMAN ALI, J: These two civil petitions for leave to appeal are directed against the judgement and order dated 19.08.2015 passed by a Division Bench of the High Court Division in Writ Petition No. 12161 of 2013 making the Rule absolute in part directing the respondents to issue Nikah Registration Certificate (sic.)(Nikah Registrar Licence) in favour of the writ petitioner A.S. Md. Siddiqur Rahman for Ward No. 1 under Narayangonj City Corporation within 2(two) months from the date of receipt of the copy of the Judgment and order.

2.             The facts, relevant for disposal of the civil petitions for leave to appeal, are that the writ petitioner A.S. Md. Siddiqur Rahman was appointed as Nikah Registrar on 13.03.1973 by the Ministry of Law, Justice and Parliamentary Affairs and he was given a licence (mb`) authorizing him to register Mohammedan Marriage and Divorce and perform function as a Quazi within the jurisdiction of Simulpara and Siddhirgonj Union Parishads under P.S. Siddhirgonj, which was at that time within the district of Dhaka. In 2003 one Md. Masihur Rahman was appointed as Nikah Registrar for Simulpara Union Parishad curtailing the jurisdiction of the writ petitioner. In the year 2005 Siddhirgonj Union Parishad was converted into Siddhirgonj Pourasova, comprising five Wards and in the year 2011 Siddhirgonj Pourasova was merged into Narayangonj City Corporation. The writ petitioner was performing his duties as the Nikah Registrar of Ward Nos.1 to 5 under the Narayangonj City Corporation. It is also stated, that on 20.11.2011 respondent No. 5, the Senior Assistant Secretary, Ministry of Law, Justice and Parliamentary Affairs issued a letter directing the writ respondent No. 4, the District Registrar, Narayangonj for sending a panel of Nikah Registrars for Ward Nos. 2 and 7 under the City Corporation. On 24.01.2012 respondent No. 5 directed respondent No. 4 to send panel of Nikah Registrars for Ward No. 3 under the Corporation. Similarly on 26.06.2012 respondent No.5 issued letter directing respondent No. 4 for sending a panel of Nikah Registrars for Ward No. 3.

3.             Challenging the said letters the petitioner filed the Writ Petition No. 3138 of 2012 before the High Court Division and obtained Rule, but that was discharged for default on 23.01.2013.

4.             The writ petitioner’s further case is that by letter dated 17.11.2013 the Government appointed Mohammad Ashraf Uddin Bhuiyan (writ respondent No. 7) as Nikah Registrar for Ward No 1; by letter dated 10.11.2013 Md. Abdur Rob (writ respondent Nos. 8) was appointed as Nikah Registrar for Ward No. 2; by letter dated 10.11.2013 Md. Nazrul Islam was appointed as Nikah Registrar for Ward No. 3 and by letter dated 11.11.2013 Md. Abu Taleb was appointed as Nikah Registrar for Ward No. 4, all within Narayangonj City Corporation.

5.             Being aggrieved, the writ petitioner filed writ petition No. 12161 of 2013 before the High Court Division and obtained Rule.

6.             The Rule was opposed by writ respondent Nos. 1 to 5 without filing any affidavit-in-opposition.

7.             After hearing the parties, upon considering the materials on record, the Rule was made absolute in part holding that the appointments made in respect of Ward Nos. 2, 3 and 4 were not illegal. However, with regard to Ward No. 1, the appointment of Md. Asraf Uddin Bhuiyan was considered to have been passed without lawful authority.

8.             Writ respondent Md. Ashraf Uddin Bhuiyan is now before us having filed Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No. 2901 of 2015. The Government has also challenged the impugned judgement in Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No. 3126 of 2015.

9.             Mr. Probir Neogi, learned Senior Advocate appearing on behalf of the leave petitioner in Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No. 2901 of 2015 submitted that the authority concerned after following the mandatory provisions of existing law regarding appointment of temporary Nikah Registrar, drawing up a Panel of competent Nikah Registrars appointed the petitioner as temporary Nikah Registrar for Ward No. 1 of Narayangonj City Corporation, and after his appointment he has been performing his functions as Nikah Registrar to the satisfaction of the concerned authority. He further submitted that when Shiddhirgonj was merged into Narayangonj City Corporation, 5 Wards were created, and for Ward No. 1 the petitioner (Md. Ashraf Uddin Bhuiyan)was appointed and also Md. Abdur Rob, Md. Nazrul Islam and Md. Abu Taleb were appointed as temporary Nikah Registrars respectively for Ward Nos. 2, 3 and 4. He further submitted that as such, Ward No. 5 remains with the writ petitioner-respondent No. 1 herein, but the High Court Division without considering that aspect erred in declaring as unlawful the appointment of the petitioner as Nikah Registrar for Ward No. 1 under Narayangonj City Corporation. The learned Advocate further submitted that the petitioner is a resident of Mijmiji which falls within the jurisdiction of Ward No. 1, and the petitioner has been rightly appointed for that Ward. On the other hand, the records show that respondent No. 1 is a resident of Ajibpur, which is within Ward No. 5.

10.         Mr. Biswajit Deb Nath, learned Deputy Attorney General appearing on behalf of the petitioner in Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No. 3126 of 2015 submitted that it is within the power of the Government under the relevant law to curtail the jurisdiction of Nikah Registrars, and accordingly, Ward Nos. 1, 2, 3 and 4 of Narayangonj City Corporation have been taken away from A.S.M. Siddiqur Rahman, who was previously Kazi for Ward Nos. 1 to 5 of Narayangonj City Corporation. By furnishing a list of the incumbent Nikah Registrars of Ward Nos. 1 to 6 of Narayangonj City Corporation, he submitted that A.S.M. Siddiqur Rahman is listed as the Nikah Registrar for Ward No. 5 of Narayangonj City Corporation. He also pointed out that Md. Masihur Rahman of Sumilpara is the incumbent Nikah Registrar for Ward No. 6 of Narayangonj City Corporation.

11.         Mr. Kamal–ul-Alam, learned Senior Advocate appearing on behalf of respondent No. 1 in both the cases made submissions in support of the impugned judgement and order of the High Court Division. He further submitted that although it is not denied that respondent No. 1 was Kazi for Ward Nos. 1 to 5 of Siddhirgonj Pourasava which merged with Narayangonj City Corporation, it is evident from the record that he is left with no jurisdiction as Nikah Registrar in any of the Wards of Narayangonj City Corporation. The learned Advocate pointed out that one Kazi Md. Masihur Rahman is the appointed Nikah Registrar for Ward Nos. 5 and 6 by appointment letter dated 22.07.2003 and he remains in that position by dint of an order of stay from the High Court Division in Writ Petition Nos. 7361 of 2011 and 620 of 2012, which is apparent from Annexure-‘K’ to the writ petition.

12.         We have considered the submissions of the learned Advocates appearing for the parties concerned and perused the impugned judgement of the High Court Division and other connected papers on record.

13.         At the outset, we should make it clear that when any person is appointed as a Nikah Registrar for any local area, his service will continue as such until and unless he is disqualified under the law. In a recent judgement by this Division in Civil Appeal Nos. 177 and 178 of 2005 (judgement delivered on 31st of May, 2016) it was observed that “upon conversion of the Union Parishad to a Pourasava only the character of the administrative unit changes.” In our view the same applies to the situation where a Pourasava becomes or is merged with a City Corporation. In the aforementioned case this Division held that after conversion the Kazi(Nikah Registrar) continues as Kazi for all the Wards of the newly created administrative unit (in this case City Corporation). He would remain the Nikah Registrar having jurisdiction over the whole of the territory for which he had been appointed as such until his jurisdiction is lawfully curtailed in respect of some or any of the Wards. Such curtailment is a power given to the Government by law, and cannot, therefore, be said to be unlawful. At the same time it must be stated that curtailment does not entail taking away all the Wards of the incumbent Nikah Registrar, thereby negating his licence altogether.

14.         In the impugned judgement the High Court Division observed that “as soon as the law regarding local jurisdiction has been changed those [posts] fallen (sic.) vacant and respondent No. 5 has authority to issue the impugned orders.” In our view such observation is not entirely correct, inasmuch as upon getting a new administrative character (City Corporation) the posts of Nikah Registrar for the relevant Wards do not ipso facto become vacant. The position of Nikah Registrar for Ward Nos. 1-5 of Narayangonj City Corporation never became vacant and until the appointment of writ respondents 7-10 in Ward Nos. 1-4 of Narayangonj City Corporation, respondent No. 1 remained Nikah Registrar for all the former Wards of Siddirgonj Pourasova, i.e. Ward Nos. 1-5 of Narayangonj City Corporation. Consequently, respondent Nos. 1 never lost his position as Nikah Registrar of Ward No. 5 of Narayangonj City Corporation.

15.         We reiterate that a Nikah Registrar, who had been appointed as such for a number of Wards, cannot have all the wards taken away from him. The power of the Government is to curtail his jurisdiction, in other words to appoint a Nikah Registrar for any of the Wards but that power does not extend to totally eliminating his licence as Nikah Registrar, unless there is some other legitimate ground.

16.               From the facts of the instant case, it appears that Kazi A.S.M. Siddiqur Rahman is a resident of Ajibpur, which is within Ward No. 5 of Narayangonj City Corporation. This fact is apparent from his National I.D. Card, which was produced before us. The learned Deputy Attorney General has supplied us with a list of the incumbent Nikah Registrars of Ward Nos. 1 to 6 of Narayangonj City Corporation. It is apparent from this list that A.S.M. Siddiqur Rahman is listed as Nikah Registrar for Ward No. 5 of Narayangonj City Corporation. We also note that Md. Masihur Rahman is listed as the Nikah Registrar of Ward No. 6 of Narayangonj City Corporation, which includes Sumilpara where he lives.

17.               In view of the above facts and circumstances, keeping in mind that according to the Government records, the writ petitioner-respondent No. 1 herein is listed as the incumbent Nikah Registrar for Ward No. 5 of Narayangonj City Corporation, there appears to be no illegality in the appointment of the Nikah Registrars  for  Ward  Nos. 1, 2, 3 and 4. It appears from the impugned judgement that the appointment of Md. Ashraf Uddin Bhuiyan as Nikah Registrar for Ward No. 1 of Narayangonj City Corporation was declared illegal as it was claimed that he was not a resident of that Ward. However, from the records we find that said Md. Ashraf Uddin Bhuiyan, petitioner herein lives in Mijmiji, Batanpara which is within Ward No. 1 of Narayangonj City Corporation and hence, his appointment as Nikah Registrar for that Ward is not unlawful.

18.               We note from the additional paper book dated 05.06.2016 filed in Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No. 2901 of 2015 that that Md. Masihur Rahman was listed as M.M.R.(Nikah Registrar) for Ward Nos. 5 and 6 under Narayangonj City Corporation. That list is dated 28.06.2015. However, that position appears to have been superseded by the list provided by the District Registrar, Narayangonj dated 12.10.2015 which is incorporated in the additional paper book dated 11.11.2015 at page 24, where Md. Masihur Rahman is listed as the Nikah Registrar of Ward No. 6 of Narayangonj City Corporation. At page 23 of the said paper book we find the name of A.S.M. Siddiqur Rahman, who is listed as Nikah Registrar for Ward No. 5 of Narayangonj City Corporation.

19.               Thus it appears clear to us that the respondent No. 1 continues to be Nikah Registrar of Ward No. 5 of Narayangonj City Corporation. His jurisdiction in Ward Nos. 1-4 has been curtailed. Md. Masihur Rahman is the incumbent Nikah Registrar for Ward No. 6 of Narayangonj City Corporation. There is, therefore, no impediment in appointing writ respondent Nos. 7 to 10 as Nikah Registrars for Ward Nos. 1 to 4 of Narayangonj City Corporation.

20.               In the light of the above observations, the judgement and order of the High Court Division is modified. Since Md. Ashraf Uddin Bhuiyan is a resident of Ward No. 1 his appointment as temporary Nikah Registrar for Ward No. 1 is found to be lawful and the position of respondent No. 1 A.S.M. Siddiqur Rahman as the Nikah Registrar of Ward No. 5 of Narayangonj City Corporation, is found to be continuing.

21.               Accordingly, with the above observations the civil petitions for leave appeal are disposed of.
