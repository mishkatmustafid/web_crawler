Words and Phrases

Equality before law.

The term ‘Equality before law’ should not be interpreted in its absolute sense to hold that all persons are equal in all respect disregarding different conditions and circumstances in which they are placed or with special quality and characteristics which some of them may possess, but which are lacking in others. In fact the term ‘equality before law’ means all persons are not equal in all respects but the persons similarly situated should be treated alike. Equal protection of law is a guarantee, that similar people should be dealt with in a similar way/manner and that people placed on different circumstances cannot be treated uniformly to all persons disregarding the basic differences among them. If these differences are identified, then the persons may be classified into different groups or categories according to those distinctions. . . . (14)

Administrative Tribunals Act (VII of 1981)

Section 4(2)

‘Promotion is not a matter of right’. It is to be earned by meritorious service along with efficiency and good conduct, character and integrity. Seniority, though not the sole criteria for promotion but it is one of the primary requirements for promotion. Seniority alone does not create right to promotion. Along with it some other factors, like merit, sincerity, honesty, integrity, efficiency, sense of responsibilities and values etc. are also to be taken into consideration for promotion.  Keeping such factors in mind, it is for the authority to make decision as regards the matter of promotion of an employee concerned. The petitioner being not in service and having not raised any such question any time during his tenure in the service, before his retirement, he cannot, under any circumstances, equate himself with respondents No. 5 and 6 and as such he cannot claim promotion and benefit after two and half years of his retirement on receipt of the full pension benefits without any objection on the basis of equating him with the said two respondents. His case can never be considered to be at per or of equal status with respondents No. 5 and 6. The petitioner is not at per or is not in a similarly placed position/category. Nor he can be termed as equal amongst the equals. Thus he cannot claim that he has been discriminated and thereby he has been deprived of getting the same benefit or treatment as has been given to respondents No. 5 and 6.

JUDGMENT

Mirza Hussain Haider, J:  This civil petition for leave to appeal is directed against the order dated 04.04.2016, passed by the Administrative Appellate Tribunal, Dhaka, in Administrative Appellate Tribunal Appeal No. 256 of 2013, allowing the appeal and thereby setting aside the order dated 26.09.2013, passed by the  Administrative Tribunal-2 Dhaka, in A.T Case No. 61 of 2012(Original A.T. Case No. 53 of 2010) allowing the case and thereby directing the authority  to give promotion to the petitioner with retrospective effect  from 25.09.1988 in the post of Deputy Director and thereafter in the post of Director from 25.09.1992 along with all back salaries and allowances in respect of those posts and other attending benefits.

2.             Facts leading to filing of this civil Petition for Leave to Appeal, in brief, are:

         The present petitioner filed A.T. Case No. 53 of 2010 under section 4(2) of the Administrative Tribunals Act, 1980 before the Administrative Tribunal-1, Dhaka, stating, inter alia, that he is a freedom fighter and successfully attended the Special Superior Service Examination under the Public Service Commission in 1972. As per merit list, pursuant to the said examination, he was supposed to be appointed as Class-I Gazetted Officer but inadvertently he was appointed in the post of Sub-Divisional Relief and Rehabilitation Officer on 25.3.1974 under the present respondents No. 1 to 4 vide Memo No. GENL-2/74/658(45) which was a Class-II Gazetted Officer post. On bringing the matter to the notice of the concerned authority, the said post of Sub-Divisional Relief and Rehabilitation Officer was upgraded as Class-I Gazetted Officer with effect from 09.01.1983. Thereafter the petitioner was promoted to the post of District Relief and Rehabilitation Officer on 05.09.1983 where he joined on 09.10.1983. Being  promoted to the post of Deputy Director on 28.06.1998 the petitioner joined the said post on 01.07.1998 and lastly being promoted to the post of Director on 08.03.2006 he joined the said post on the same day. After completion of his service the petitioner went on retirement on 25.01.2008.

3.             On the other hand, respondents No. 5 and 6 herein joined Jatiya Jubo Sangstha (National Youth Organization) in 1981 as Sub-Divisional Executive Officers. However, they lost their jobs after abolition of the said department on 21.4.1982 and subsequently with the approval of the Ministry of Establishment the said two respondents got fresh appointment and were absorbed in the Relief and Rehabilitation Department, as Assistant Directors where they joined on 30.01.1989 and 07.02.1989 respectively and thereafter both of them were promoted to the post of Deputy Director on 05.01.2004 and 11.12.2007 respectively. As per Recruitment Rules, 1995, although the said respondents No. 5 and 6 had requisite qualifications and became eligible for promotion to the post of Director but the authority did not take any step in that respect. Hence they filed AT Cases No. 48 of 2006 and 49 of 2006 before the Administrative Tribunal for getting promotion with retrospective effect. Having succeeded in the said cases the authority gave them promotion with retrospective effect in compliance with the  order passed in the aforesaid cases.

4.             On 27.10.2010, upon coming to know about the said matter, the petitioner filed a petition before the concerned authority for reconsideration of his case by review stating, inter alia, that since his service record was very clean, unblemished and no adverse remark being made there in his ACR and since, as per Service Rules, 1995, he is entitled to get promotion to the post of Deputy Director and Director with retrospective effect from 25.3. 1987 and 25.3.1992 respectively along with financial benefits and all attending benefits and since the respondents No. 5 and 6 have been given such promotion and benefits, he be given the same. But the authority did not do anything on the said review petition  even within two months from the date of filing of the same. Hence the petitioner filed the aforesaid AT Case No. 53 of 2010 before the Administrative Tribunal-1, Dhaka, which has been renumbered as AT Case No. 61 of 2012 on transfer to the Administrative Tribunal-2 Dhaka.

5.             The respondents contested the said case by filing  written statement denying the claims and material allegations made by the petitioner and contending, inter alia, that the petitioner was duly promoted to the higher posts  and ultimately on completion of his service he retired on 25.1.2008 upon receiving all pension benefits without raising any objection, whatsoever. It is further alleged that during his service period or at the time of promotion or even at the time of taking pension benefits the petitioner did not raise any such claim of promotion with retrospective effect nor did he lodge any complaint that he was not given promotion in due course. It is further contended that the petitioner claimed benefit of the order passed in AT Case No. 48 of 2006 and 49 of 2006 obtained by respondents No. 5 and 6 respectively, which he can not claim as he was neither  a party in the said cases nor any direction was given  in the said cases to deal with cases of similar nature or particularly in his favour. Hence the petitioner’s case is liable to be dismissed.

6.             The Tribunal upon hearing the parties and considering the materials on record allowed the petitioner’s A.T. case by the order dated 26.09.2013, against which the government preferred A.A.T. Appeal No. 256 of 2013 before the Administrative Appellate Tribunal, Dhaka, which has been allowed upon hearing the parties and on consideration of the materials on record by the order dated 04.04.2016 upon setting aside the order of the Tribunal dated 26.9.2013.

7.             Being aggrieved by the aforesaid order dated 04.04.2016, the petitioner herein, filed the present civil petition for leave to appeal before this Division.

8.             Mrs. Sufia Khatun, the learned Advocate-On-Record, appearing on behalf of the leave-petitioner submits that the Administrative Appellate Tribunal erred in law in not appreciating the fact that the petitioner was not only senior to respondents No. 5 and 6, but he also possessed other qualities like efficiency, good conduct, character, integrity, dynamic personality which are essential for consideration in case of promotion  and his service record was all along clean, unblemished with no adverse remarks in the ACR and even no explanation, whatsoever, was ever called from him by the authority while discharging his duties and functions in his whole service career. Upon considering all these qualifications the Administrative Tribunal gave relief to the petitioner which the Appellate Tribunal failed to consider. She submits that the Appellate Tribunal failed to appreciate that pursuant to the order of the Tribunal respondent No.1 gave ante dated seniority and promotion to respondents No. 5 and 6 without preferring any appeal against the said order of the Administrative Tribunal  but in the case of the petitioner, instead of treating him equally along with respondents No. 5 and 6, the respondents treated him with mala fide, arbitrary and discriminatory manner and thereby refused to grant him promotion which the Tribunal upon considering all the materials on record granted in his favour.  She also submits that by the order dated 4.10.2009 respondent No.1 having given ante-dated seniority and promotion to  respondents No. 5 and 6 but refused to do so in respect of the petitioner ignoring his review petition which is violative of the doctrine of equality, fairness and reasonableness as guaranteed under Articles 27, 29 and 31 of the Constitution of the People’s Republic of Bangladesh.  Hence the order complained of herein is liable to be set aside.

9.             No one represented the respondents.

10.         Considering the facts and circumstances and the submissions as stated above, it appears that on 04.04.2010 the petitioner filed Administrative Tribunal Case No. 61 of 2012 (originally AT Case No. 53 of 2010) alleging that respondents No. 5 and 6, who were appointed in 1989, much later than the petitioner as Assistant Directors in the same department and were promoted as Deputy Directors on 05.01.2004 and 11.12.2007 respectively were given ante dated seniority and promotion with retrospective effect from the date of attaining their requisite length of service pursuant to the Tribunal’s order. But though the petitioner attained the requisite length of service and seniority for being promoted to the post of Director much earlier than those of the said two respondents, his case was not considered by the authority. Hence the petitioner also obtained similar order from the Tribunal, upon coming to know about the favour in respect of the respondents No. 5 and 6, after more than two and half years of his retirement but his case has not been considered. Thus the petitioner claimed that the authority instead of dealing with him equally, challenged the Tribunal’s order obtained by him, in appeal before the Appellate Tribunal in violation of and upon infringing his fundamental rights, which they did not do in the case of respondents No. 5 and 6.

11.         Now the question is whether the petitioner, under such circumstances, is entitled to get promotion and benefits with retrospective effect after he has gone on retirement with entire benefit and whether the authority failed to equate him with respondents No. 5 and 6 in dealing with his case and thereby he has been discriminated.

12.         The Administrative Appellate Tribunal considering the materials on record and the law and also considering several decisions of the apex court, came to the conclusion that the petitioner is not eligible to get such promotion and benefit with retrospective effect nor can he be equated with respondents No. 5 and 6 and as such no question of discrimination arises in his case.

13.         By now it has already been settled that ‘promotion is not a matter of right’. It is to be earned by meritorious service along with efficiency and good conduct, character and integrity. Seniority, though not the sole criteria for promotion but it is one of the primary requirements for promotion. However, it appears from the submissions of the learned Advocate-on-Record for the petitioner that the petitioner claims retrospectivity of his promotion and benefits on the ground of seniority as well as equality.

14.         The term ‘equality before law’ should not be interpreted in its absolute sense to hold that all persons are equal in all respect disregarding different conditions and circumstances in which they are placed or with special quality and characteristics which some of them may possess, but which are lacking in others. In fact the term ‘equality before law’ means all persons are not equal in all respects but the persons similarly situated should be treated alike. Equal protection of law is a guarantee, that similar people should be dealt with in a similar way/manner and that people placed on different circumstances cannot be treated uniformly to all persons disregarding the basic differences among them. If these differences are identified, then the persons may be classified into different groups or categories according to those distinctions.

15.         The accepted principle is that seniority alone does not create right to promotion.  Along with it some other factors, like merit, sincerity, honesty, integrity, efficiency, sense of responsibilities and values etc. are also to be taken into consideration for promotion.  Keeping such factors in mind, it is for the authority to   make decision as regards the matter of promotion of an employee concerned. For the aforesaid reasons it would not be within the jurisdiction of the Court to direct an authority to promote a particular employee and thereby deprive the authority of considering the case of promotion of a  particular or any other employee in the background of the facts available to it. It is within the domain of the authority to make decision in the matter of promotion to its employee in the back ground of the facts available as well as upon due compliance with the rules/regulations, if any. If the decision so made is challenged then only it would be quite within the jurisdiction of the court to examine whether in arriving at such decision the authority has complied with the aforementioned requirements as well as the existing rules/regulations or that the authority proceeded on flawed procedure and material or left out materials which was very much integral to the matter of promotion process.

16.         In the instant case the distinction, which can be drawn between the petitioner and respondents No. 5 and 6 is that the petitioner has gone on retirement upon accepting all retirement benefits without any objection whatsoever, whereas respondents No. 5 and 6 have not i.e. they were very much in service. Thus after two and half years of his retirement and upon receiving all retirement benefits,  the petitioner cannot claim that he is equal with respondent No. 5 and 6, who are very much in service at the relevant time. From the materials on record, as well as from the facts and circumstances of the case as stated above, it is clear that the petitioner took a chance of comparing himself with respondents No. 5 and 6 and as they have been given promotion with retrospective effect he should also be given the same, treating him at per with them and as such he should not have been deprived, as he has been, according to him, being of equal status. But the case is quite different. The petitioner being not in service and having not raised any such question any time during his tenure in the service, before his retirement, he cannot, under any circumstances, equate himself with respondents No. 5 and 6 and as such he cannot claim promotion and benefit after two and half years of his retirement on receipt of the full pension benefits without any objection on the basis of equating him with the said two respondents. His case can never be considered to be at per or of equal status with respondents No. 5 and 6. 

17.         On the other hand if it is taken into consideration that the petitioner made a prayer for review or made a representation for re-consideration of his case on the face of promotion of respondents No. 5 and 6 the same also cannot improve the petitioner’s case. Had it been a case that he had been trying hard to get such benefit prior to his retirement then the case probably would have been otherwise.

18.         So the question whether the petitioner, who is not in service, as against those of the respondents No. 5 and 6 who are in service, can claim that he has not been treated equally. The answer is emphatically no. Because the petitioner is not at per or is not in a similarly placed position/category. Nor he can be termed as equal amongst the equals. Thus he cannot claim that he has been discriminated and thereby he has been deprived of getting the same benefit or treatment as has been given to respondents No. 5 and 6.

19.         Considering the facts and circumstances as stated hereinabove, and having gone through the impugned order of the Administrative Appellate Tribunal we are of the view that the Administrative Appellate Tribunal correctly appreciated the law and considered the facts of the case and came to a correct conclusion for which it is difficult to find any legal infirmity/flaw in the same and as such there is nothing to interfere with.

20.         Accordingly, this civil petition for leave to appeal is dismissed without any order as to cost.
