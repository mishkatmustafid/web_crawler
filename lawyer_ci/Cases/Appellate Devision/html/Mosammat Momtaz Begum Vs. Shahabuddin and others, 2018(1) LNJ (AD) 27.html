JUDGMENT

Muhammad Imman Ali, J: This civil petition for leave to appeal is directed against the judgement and order dated 20.07.2014 passed by a Single Bench of the High Court Division in Civil Revision No. 4514 of 2002 discharging the Rule.  

2.            The facts, relevant for disposal of the instant civil petition for leave to appeal, are that Senayet Ali the predecessor of the respondents as plaintiff filed Other Class Suit No. 27 of 1979, which was subsequently renumbered several times and finally numbered as Other Class Suit No. 71 of 1994, in the Court of Assistant Judge, Ramu, Cox’s Bazar, for declaration of title and recovery of khas possession in respect of the suit land described in the schedule of the plaint. The original plaintiff having died his heirs were substituted as plaintiffs in the suit. It was contended, inter alia, that one Md. Jalal Ahmed obtained the suit land on 19.02.1963 by a settlement under khatian No. 1247/418 of Jhilenza Mouza vide Settlement Case No. 156/1961-62 and, thereafter, on 10.03.1967 he sold out the said land in favour of Senayet Ali by two registered deeds. Senayet Ali possessed the land by paying rent through one Sultan Ahmed and Saidullah. He took half share of the land by kabala in the name of the defendant No. 10, who had no right, title, interest or possession of the land. Defendant No. 7 also obtained settlement by the same settlement case from Jalal Ahmed and got 50 decimals of land to the contiguous east of the suit land. Defendant No. 7 subsequently sold out the same in favour of one Moulvi Emdadul Hoq and others. The defendants in the absence of the plaintiffs in the first part of 1997 trespassed into the suit land and upon dispossessing the plaintiffs, constructed 4 huts therein. They also ignored the request of the plaintiffs’ caretaker as well as the plaintiffs’ request to vacate the premises. The defendants also in collusion with the settlement officials recorded their names in the B.S. khatian which constrained the plaintiffs’ predecessor to file the suit.

3.            Defendant Nos. 1 and 4-6 contested the suit by filing written statement contending, inter alia, that defendant No. 7 and 8 obtained settlement of 50 decimals of ‘nul’ land in settlement khatian No. 8105/2 which is adjacent west of the suit land. Jalal Ahmed obtained settlement of 40 decimals of hilly land and the remaining 30 decimals of land of suit plot remained as khas. The M.R.R. khatian No. 8105/9289 was recorded in the name of the defendant No. 7. Defendant No. 7 thereafter by two sets of deeds dated 26.04.1976 and 28.04.1976 sold 14 decimals of land to defendant No. 4 and by another kabala dated 26.08.1977 defendant No. 7 sold to defendant No. 1. After acquiring the land by purchase, these defendants have been possessing the suit land by constructing their dwelling house and filling earth. The case of contesting defendant No. 32 is that by Settlement Case No. 156/1961-62 this defendant obtained 50 decimals of land and accordingly the M.R.R. and B.S. khatian were recorded. This defendant by kabala dated 26.08.1977 purchased 10 decimals of land from the suit land and, thereafter, by filling earth converted the ‘nul’ land to ‘bhiti’ and by constructing dwelling house is possessing the same. The plaintiffs having no right, title, interest and possession in the suit land filed the suit on false allegation.

4.            After hearing the parties and considering the evidence and materials on record, the Assistant Judge, Ramu, Cox’s Bazar, by his judgement and decree dated 11.11.1995 decreed the suit. Then defendant No. 32 filed Other Class Appeal No. 10 of 1996. The other contesting defendants filed Other Class Appeal No. 107 of 1995. The appeals were heard and disposed of by the 2nd Court of Joint District Judge, Cox’s Bazar, who by his judgement and decree dated 06.03.2002 dismissed the appeal affirming the judgement and decree passed by the trial Court.   

5.            Against the aforesaid judgement and decree passed by the appellate Court, the added defendant No. 32 filed Civil Revision No. 4514 of 2002 before the High Court Division and obtained Rule. Other contesting defendants filed Civil Revision No. 4489 of 2002. Upon hearing the parties both the Rules were was discharged. Hence, the added defendant No. 32 is now before us having filed the instant civil petition for leave to appeal. 

6.            Mr. Abdus Sobhan, learned Advocate appearing on behalf of the petitioner submitted that the High Court Division failed to consider that the suit is for recovery of khas possession and admittedly the plaintiffs did not mention the boundary of the suit land in the schedule of the plaint and thus the Courts below as well as the High Court Division could not determine the actual quantum of dispossessed land in suit, and as such the impugned judgement and order is liable to be set aside. 

7.            He further submitted that admittedly the defendants have been possessing the suit land by constructing house after purchasing the land by several registered deeds and thus the plaintiffs cannot get relief without filing a suit for partition. He also submitted that the Courts below erred in determining the possession of the suit land on the basis of a report of the Advocate Commissioner but the High Court Division failed to consider that aspect.        

8.            No one has appeared for the respondents.

9.            We have considered the submissions of the learned Advocates for the petitioners, perused the impugned judgement and order of the High Court Division and other connected papers on record. 

10.        We find from the judgement of the trial Court that it was noted that D.W. 8 deposed in support of the case of defendant No. 32, but that witness did not state in his deposition that either he or any of them had any interest in the suit land. It was further observed that since the plaintiffs claimed all of the land of plot No., there is no need to mention the boundaries in the plaint. It was observed that in any event the plaintiffs’ witnesses correctly mentioned the boundaries and also D.W. 3 and D.W.4 admitted the said boundaries in their cross-examination.

11.        The appellate Court in affirming the judgement of the trial Court observed that the plaintiffs were able to prove by oral and documentary evidence that their predecessor Senayet Ali got the land described in the schedule to the plaint in 1967 by purchase from Jalal Ahmed and were dispossessed by the defendants in 1977. It was also observed that D.W. 7 in his cross-examination stated that he did not know whether the plaintiffs were in possession of the suit land until the first part of 1977 and whether Sultan Ahmed and Syed Ullah were managing the property on behalf of the plaintiffs. Hence, the appellate Court noted that there was no specific denial about the possession of the suit land by the plaintiffs.

12.        The High Court Division in discharging the Rules upheld the concurrent findings of facts of the Courts below. It was further observed that there was no cross-examination from the side of the contesting defendants of the plaintiffs’ witness No. 2 with regard to his statement in examination-in-chief about possession and dispossession. The High Court Division also noted that there were two reports of Advocate Commissioner dated 03.06.1987 and 06.09.1986. It was apparent from the reports that defendant No. 1 and 4-6 have erected huts on plot No. which belonged to the plaintiffs and they have been dispossessed from the land measuring an area of 18¾  decimals and defendant No. 1 dispossessed the plaintiffs from 15 decimals of land of the suit jote.  The High Court Division concluded that the relevant evidence both oral and documentary have been properly assessed.

13.        The revisional jurisdiction of the High Court Division is limited to addressing the issue as to whether there was misreading or non-reading of evidence or non-consideration of material facts by the Courts below, and the High Court Division did not find any such illegality or infirmity.   

14.        In view of the discussion above, we do not find any illegality or impropriety in the impugned judgement and order of the High Court Division.

15.        Accordingly, the civil petition for leave to appeal is dismissed.
