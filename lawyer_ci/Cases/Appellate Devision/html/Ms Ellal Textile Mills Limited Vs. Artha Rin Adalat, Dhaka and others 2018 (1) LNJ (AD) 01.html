JUDGMENT

Md. Abdul Wahhab Miah. J: This appeal, by leave, is from the order dated the 23rd day of March, 2008 passed by a Division Bench of the High Court Division in Writ Petition No.2206 of 2008 summarily rejecting an application filed under article 102 of the Constitution of the People's Republic of Bangladesh.

2.          The appellant filed the writ petition before the High Court Division challenging order No.8 dated 31.05.2006 and order No.23 dated 23.06.2008 passed by Artha Rin Adalat, 3rd Court, Dhaka in Artha Rin Execution Case No.255 of 2005.

3.          The back ground in filing the writ petition was that the writ petitioner obtained a loan from writ respondent No.2, i.e., Agrani Bank, Bangladesh Wapda Branch of 11, Ellat Chamber, Motijheel Commercial Area, Dhaka. In due course, writ respondent No.2 as the plaintiff instituted Money Suit No.209 of 1997 in Artha Rin Adalat, 1st Court, Dhaka for recovery of the loan money amounting to Tk. 12,77,53,891.37. Eventually, the suit having been transferred to the Court of Subordinate Judge and Additional Artha Rin Adalat Dhaka was renumbered as Money Suit No. 184 of 1998. The suit was decreed on 2.5.2005 and a preliminary decree under Order XXXIV, rule 4(1) of the Code of Civil Procedure was drawn up on 24.5.2005. In due course, writ respondent No.2, i.e Agrani Bank levied Artha Execution Case No.255 of 2005 on 18.07.2005. The executing Court by order No.4 dated 30.10.2005 fixed 12.01.2006 for receiving auction bid, but auction could not take place on that date i.e. 12.01.2006 as it was a public holiday. Then the record was placed before the executing Court on 15.01.2006 and the Court noted that nobody submitted any tender and fixed 09.03.2006 for taking steps. On 09.03.2006, the decree holder filed an application under section 33(7) of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain, in short, the Ain 2003 for a certificate of title to the mortgaged property in its favour and the executing Court by order No.8 dated 31.05.2006 allowed the prayer and passed order for issuing a certificate as prayed for and, in fact, the certificate was issued on 02.08.2006 and on this date further order was passed to send a copy of the certificate to the concerned Sub Registrar. Thereafter on 29.01.2008 the decree holder Bank filed an application under Order, XXI, rules 95, 96, 98 and 99 read with sections 26 and 57 of the Ain, 2003 to deliver the actual possession of the property acquired by it pursuant to the certificate issued under section 33(7) of the Ain, 2003 and the Court by its order No.23 dated 26.02.2008 allowed the prayer and issued writ of deliveryof possession fixing 27.04.2008 for the return of the writ of delivery of possession. Challenging order Nos.8 and 23 dated 31.05.2006 and 26.02.2008 respectively, the appellant filed an application before the High Court Division under article 102 of the Constitution and a Division Bench hearing the same as motion rejected the same summarily by the impugned order. Against the impugned order, the appellant filed Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No.1503 of 2008 before this Court and leave was granted to consider the submissions made by its learned Counsel as under;

"The learned Counsel for the petitioner submits that subsection 7 of Section 33 of the Ain, 2003 provides that if the decree-holder Bank is willing to acquire the title of the mortgage property they are required by law to prefer an application before the Adalat and upon receipt of such application Adalat will take step under Sections 33(1),(2) and (3) and will abstain from 33(4) and (5) in other wards one open auction must be held but in the instant case without holding one open auction certificate was issued under section 33(7) of the Ain, 2003. Hence, the impugned judgment of the High Court Division is liable to be set aside. He further submits that section 33(7) of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain contemplates actual holding of the auction once and not a show of holding auction and in the instant case no actual auction having been held, the High Court Division ought not to have allowed the application of the decree-holder under section 33(7) of the said Ain and as such the impugned judgment of the High Court Division is liable to be set aside."

4.          Mr. A.J. Mohammad AH, learned Counsel for the appellant has, in fact, made submissions in the light of leave granting order, so those are not reproduced.

5.          Mr. Mahbubey Alam, learned Counsel for the respondent Bank, on the other hand, has tried to support the impugned order.

6.          From the record, it appears that the execution case in question was levied on 21.07.2005. It further appears from order No.4 dated 30.10.2005 that 12.01.2006 was fixed for holding the auction of the mortgaged property. Accordingly, order was passed for issuance of auction sale notice. Order No.5 dated 15.01.2006 shows that 12.01.2006 was a public holiday and the record was put up before the Court on 15.01.2006 and the executing Court fixed the next date on 09.03.2006 for tadbir under section 33(4) stating clearly that no tender was dropped on that date. 7.          Then on the next date i.e 09.03.2006, an application was filed on behalf of the decree-holder to issue a certificate pursuant to the provisions of sub section (7) of section 33 of the Ain, 2003. The application was fixed for hearing on 23.04.2006 and ultimately the application was heard on 31.05.2006 and on this date a certificate was issued to the decree-holder Bank under section 33(7) of the Ain, 2003.

8.          A reading of sub-section (7) of section 33 of the Ain, 2003 clearly shows that if the decree-holder is willing to acquire title to the mortgaged property, it has to file an application before the Artha Rin Adalat, in short, the Adalat and upon receipt of such application the Adalat will take step under sub sections (1), (2) (2ka), (2kha), (2ga) and (3) thereof and will not take any step under sub sections (4) and (5) i.e one open auction must be held before issuing a certificate under Sub section (7) of section 33 of the Ain. But from order Nos.4, 5 and 8 it is prima facie clear that no auction as contemplated in sub section (7) was ever held including the one in question. 12.01.2006 was fixed for holding the auction of the property in question but that being a holiday auction could not be held on that day. Fixation of a date for holding auction on a holiday can in no way be regarded or accepted as an auction within the meaning of sections (1), (2), (2ka), (2kha), (2ga) and (3) and thus to comply with the mandate of sub section (7) of the section 33. Further though the executing Court fixed 09.03.2006 for taking step in the matter, the decree-holder without taking any step in that regard, filed application to issue certificate in its favour in respect of the property in question under Sub section (7) of section 33 and accordingly the same was issued. It also appears to us that one auction as contemplated in sub sections (1), (2), (2ka), (2kha), (2ga) and (3) must be an actual auction and not a show of auction as has been done in the instant case. So, the Adalat was totally wrong in issuing certificate under sub section (7) of section 33 of the Ain, 2003 and then to issue writ of delivery of possession of the property in question in favour of the decree holder and as such, order Nos.8 and 23 passed by the Adalat were passed illegally and those cannot be sustained and therefore, must be set aside and at the same time direction need be given to the decree-holder to take positive step to hold one auction in the real sense within the meaning of sub sections (1), (2), 2(ka), 2(kha) 2(ga) and 3 of the Ain, 2003. The High Court Division totally failed to consider the true purport and scope of sub sections (1), (2), (2ka), (2kha), 2(ga) and (3) in summarily rejecting the application under article 102 of the constitution.

9.          For the discussions made above, we find substance in the appeal.

10.      Accordingly, the appeal is allowed. The impugned order of the High Court Division and those of the executing Court dated 31.05.2006 and 26.02.2008 are set aside. The executing Court is directed to hold fresh auction of the property in question in accordance with law. The decree-holder is directed to take positive step in the matter in that regard.

11.      Mr. A.J Mohammd Ali, however, submits that direction may be given by this Court upon the decree-holder Bank to accept the decretal amount from the judgment-debtor, if it can arrange the money and pay the same. It does not require any direction/order by this Court, because the judgment-debtor can always settle the matter with the decree-holder Bank arranging the payment of the decretal amount.
