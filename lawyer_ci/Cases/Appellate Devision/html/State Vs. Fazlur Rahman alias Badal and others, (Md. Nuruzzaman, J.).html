Constitution of Bangladesh, 1972

Article 103

Since the High Court Division has acquitted all the convicts and thereby reversed the judgment of the trial Court holding that evidences adduced by the prosecution are dubious, we are, therefore, of the view that to find out the truth and as to whether the High Court Division’s opinion and decision is correct or not, we must scan the testimonies of the witnesses although generally this Division does not do so. In view of the discussion of the vivid testimony of the witnesses it clearly appears that the prosecution witnesses P.Ws.2, 4, 6 and 14 categorically proved the prosecution case and the police personnel who investigated the case appeared before the Court as witness who also in same voice supported the prosecution case. But the defence could not shake the credibility of the prosecution witnesses through cross-examination. . . . (13 and 33)

Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898)

Section 164

It is the settled principle of law that the statement of co-accused under section 164 of the Code of Criminal Procedure cannot be the basis for conviction of other co-accused, unless there is strong circumstantial and corroborative evidence to justify the statement made by the accused under section 164 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. On perusal of the 164 statement of the accused Tutul and testimony of the P.Ws.2 and 4, we are of the view that the testimony of P.Ws.2 and 4 not only supports the statement made by the accused Tutul under section 164 of the Code of Criminal Procedure but those corroborates the same. It is also proved that confessional statement was voluntary and true and properly recorded by Mr. Narayan Chandra Das, Metropolitan Magistrate, P.W.3, in accordance with law. So, there is no iota of doubt about the abduction and then killing of the deceased, Rony by the accused persons.                          . . . (35)

Constitution of Bangladesh, 1972

Article 103

We should act with utmost circumspection in the exercise of our powers under Article 103 of the Constitution for setting aside the judgment of the High Court Division. However, where ends of justice requires exercise of such powers, this Court will be failing in its duty if it does not do so. Where the acquittal is based on irrelevant grounds or where the High Court Division allows itself to be deflected by the red herrings drawn across the track or where the evidence accepted by the trial Court but the same is rejected  by the High Court Division upon a perfunctory consideration or where the High Court Division upon baneful approach has resulted in vital and crucial evidence being ignored, for any such adequate reason this Court may feel obliged to step in to secure the interests of justice, to appease the judicial conscience. This case in our view, is eminently one of such where the interests of justice is called for interference.    . . . (38, 40 and 41)

Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898)

Section 376

From the deposition of P.Ws.2, 3, 4 and 14 it is proved beyond all the reasonable doubt that the accused Tutul and Badal in a pre-planned manner abducted and killed Rony in front of P.W.2 Badsha. Therefore, the judgment and order so far it relates to acquitting the accused Badal and Tutul is liable to be set aside. However, we are of the opinion that justice would be best served to commute the sentence of death to life imprisonment considering the age of the accused Fazlur Rahman Badal and Monir Hossain Tutul. . . . JUDGMENT

Md. Nuruzzaman, J: These 4 (four) criminal appeals, by leave, at the instance of the State are directed against the judgment and order dated 12.05.2011 passed by the High Court Division in Death Reference No.157 of 2005 along with Criminal Appeal Nos. 4488 of 2005, 4946 of 2005, 78 of 2006, and 1017 of 2006 with Jail Appeal Nos.1250 and 1251 of 2005 rejecting the death reference and allowing the criminal appeals and jail appeals and thereby setting aside the judgment and order of conviction and sentence dated 12.10.2005 passed by the learned Additional Metropolitan Sessions Judge, First Court, Dhaka and thereby acquitting the convict respondents in Metro Sessions Case No.1545 of 2002 arising out of G.R. Case No.2890 of 1998 corresponding to Demra Police Station Case No.15(6)98.

2.             All these criminal appeals are heard together and they are dealt with by this single judgment. 

3.             Prosecution case, in brief, was that on 02.06.1998 at around 8.30 p.m. the informant while staying at the house of his elder sister Shaheda at Jigatola his nephew Mominul Islam Mamun informed him over telephone that Rony, his (informant’s) brother’s son had been abducted by some boys from Uttar Kazla. Upon receipt of such information, the informant rushed there and searched the victim but to no effect. At around 11.00 P.M. someone informed him that the victim had been shifted to Dhaka Medical College Hospital. He, therefore, rushed to that hospital, wherein Rustom Ali (P.W.4) and Badsha Mia (P.W.2) told him that on the same day i.e. 02.06.1998 at around 7.30 P.M. they, along with victim Rony were going on by rickshaw towards Jatrabari from Kazlarpar and when they reached near a garage some 5/6 unknown young men stopped their rickshaw and abducted them with intent to kill them. At one stage, Rustom Ali (P.W.4) managed to escape from the clutches of those accused persons and informed the fact to his locality. That the accused persons took Badsha and Rony crossing a Marsh to a place wherein the accused persons fastened a rope on the neck of Rony, they pulled the rope on the neck of Rony and also inflicted assaults on his person. Meanwhile, Badsha (P.W.2) managed to escape from the clutches of those accused persons and informed the fact to the Dholpur City  Polly area whereupon the local people along with the police rushed to that place and rescued the victim Rony in an unconscious condition where in a rope was found fastened around his   neck. Rony was then taken to the Dhaka Medical College Hospital wherein he died. It is mentioned in the F.I.R. that Rustom Ali (P.W.4) and Badsha (P.W.2) told the informant that out of enmity accused Tutul and Golzar along with 3/4 other persons killed Rony in the alleged manner. Accordingly, Demra Police Station Case No.15 dated 02.06.98 arising out of G.R. Case No.2890 of 1998 under sections 364/302/34 of the Penal Code was started against the accused persons.

4.             The trial Court, in due course of trial considering the evidence adduced by both the parties and other materials on record by its Judgment and Order dated 12/10/2005 convicted the accused respondent Monir Hossain alias Tutul and Fazlur Rahman alias Badal under sections 302/34 of the Penal Code and sentenced them to death and also to pay a fine of Tk.20,000/- each. It also convicted the other accused, namely Golzar Hossain, Kabir Ahmed alias Tufan and Ahmed Ali alias Tiger (absconding) under sections 302/34 of the Penal Code and sentenced each of them to imprisonment for life,further to pay a fine of Tk.20,000/-, in default, to suffer rigorous imprisonment for two years more. The trial Court also convicted the accused Golzar Hossain, Kabir Ahmed alias Tufan and Ahmed Ali alias Tiger (absconding) under section 364 of the Penal Code and sentenced them to rigorous imprisonment for 10 years and to pay a fine of Tk.5,000/- each, in default, to suffer further rigorous imprisonment for 6 months more mentioning that all the sentences shall run concurrently.

5.             In due course of Reference, under section 374 of the Criminal Procedure Code, the trial court sent the death reference with connected case record to the High Court Division for confirmation of sentences which was registered as Death Reference No.157 of 2005.

6.             Feeling aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the judgment and order of conviction and sentence dated 12.10.2005 passed by the trial Court the condemned prisoners and the other convicts (excepting absconding accused Ahmed Ali alias Tiger) preferred the above mentioned Criminal Appeals and jail appeals before the High Court Division. The High Court Division, upon hearing both the parties, rejected the Death Reference No.157 of 2005 and allowed the criminal appeals and jail appeals and thereby set aside the judgment and order of conviction and sentence of the trial Court by its judgment and order dated 12.05.2011 holding that the evidence adduced by the prosecution are dubious.

7.             Hence, the State feeling aggrieved by the judgment and order dated 12.05.2011 of the High Court Division preferred Criminal Petition for Leave to Appeal Nos.348-350 of 2011 and 290 of 2014 before this Division and obtained leave, which, gave rise to the instant appeals.

8.             Mr. Biswajit Debnath, the learned Deputy Attorney General appearing on behalf of the appellants in all the criminal appeals submits that it is a case of pre-planned murder of a young boy and in this case the prosecution adduced sufficient evidence to prove that these accused-respondents murdered this young boy, Rony by kidnapping him from the road; there are 2 eye witnesses of this occurrence of kidnapping and also of murder and they are P.W.2 and P.W.4, who have deposed before the Court narrating the whole occurrence; that these P.W.2 and P.W.4 are most vital witnesses and their evidence have been corroborated also by P.Ws.1, 6 and 14 and also by the confessional statement of accused Monir Hossain alias Tutul; this confessional statement of accused Monir Hossain alias Tutul is voluntary and true and this has corroborated the prosecution case fully; the trial Court, on consideration of these evidence adduced by the prosecution and considering the confessional statement of the accused rightly found that the prosecution case was proved and, therefore, rightly convicted and sentenced the accused persons; but relying on some minor discrepancies in the statements of the prosecution witnesses, the High Court Division took those to be major discrepancies,  disbelieved them, though they are eye witnesses, and most disinterested and reliable witnesses and thereby most unjustly and improperly misread and misconstrued the evidence of prosecution witnesses and arrived at a wrong finding and decision; the High Court Division also failed to assess and evaluate the evidence of the prosecution witnesses properly, and thus, failed to come to a correct decision and as such, the judgment and order of the High Court Division is liable to be set aside. He further submits that the accused Golzar Hossain and Monir Hossain alias Tutul have been named in the F.I.R. which was lodged immediately after the occurrence and another accused, namely, Kabir Ahmed alias Tufan was identified in T.I. parade by the eye witnesses P.W.1 and P.W.2 during investigation of the case and other 2 accused respondents have been named in the confessional statement of co-accused Monir Hossain alias Tutul but the High Court Division failed to assess and evaluate the evidence of the prosecution witnesses properly and thus, failed to come to a correct decision. He next submits that the High Court Division ignoring all these facts and circumstances and evidence on record and also ignoring the findings and decision of the trial Court most illegally acquitted all the accused persons thus the High Court Division committed wrong and illegality in acquitting the accused persons by setting aside the judgment and order of conviction and sentence passed by the trial Court. He finally submits that the prosecution has been able to prove its case beyond all shadow of doubt upon considering and examining the independent witnesses mentioning the time, place and manner of occurrence as a result of which the trial Court convicted and sentenced the accused respondents as they were found guilty of the offence of abduction and murder. But the High Court Division ignoring all the settled principles of law rejected the death reference and acquitted the accused respondents from the charge leveled against them and as such, the impugned judgment and order passed by the High Court Division is liable to be set aside. Hence, the instant appeals may be allowed.

32.         On bare reading and assessment of the testimony of all the above mentioned prosecution witnesses it appear that the prosecution was able to prove the case regarding the murder of the deceased Rony. However, for the purpose of drawing a conclusion and to find out the truth we have gone through the judgment of the High Court Division. It appears that the High Court Division did not assess the testimony of P.Ws.2, 4 and 14 correctly, rather, it relied upon the others P.Ws. who neither saw the occurrence nor was present in the place of occurrence. On perusal of the testimony of P.Ws.2, 4 and 14 as discussed above we are of the view that the High Court Division erred in law in not discussing the vital portion of the testimony of P.Ws.2 and 4 who are the eye witnesses of the occurrence and P.W.14 who recovered the body of the deceased Rony from the place of occurrence with a rope around his neck which has been marked as material exhibit-I and P.W.1 correctly produced and recognized the material exhibit-I, that is, the rope by which the throat and neck of Roni was fastened. P.W.2 in his deposition very vividly disclosed the occurrence of killing Rony by the convict Tutul, son of Mannan Fakir and Fazlur Rahman alias Badal. No.2-Badsha Miah, who saw the occurrence in his own eyes. P.W.14-Nayak Rafiqul Islam who along with others rescued the body of the deceased Rony from the place of occurrence, which is a dry land surrounded by the water. However, the High Court Division has failed to assess the above testimony of the P.W.2 Badsha, rather opined that there is discrepancy in the FIR story and the testimonies of the prosecution witnesses.

33.         In view of the discussion of the vivid testimony of the witnesses it clearly appears that the prosecution witnesses P.Ws.2, 4, 6 and 14 categorically proved the prosecution case and the police personnel who investigated the case appeared before the Court as witness who also in same voice supported the prosecution case. But the defence could not shake the credibility of the prosecution witnesses through cross-examination.

34.         Moreover, P.Ws.2 and 4 are the eye witnesses who identified the accused persons in  T.I. parade as well as on the dock of the Court. Apart from the above, accused Tutul made a confessional statement on 28.06.1998 wherein he clearly admitted that he along with all other accused persons have abducted three persons from the main road of Kazlarpar and they caught the deceased Rony and brought him to the Bibir Bagicha through Kazlarpar wherein accused Badal had been waiting for them and on reaching there, they handed over Rony and Badsha to Badal in presence of other persons. However, it appears from the confessional statement of the accused Tutul that he disclosed the material facts but he did not include himself in the murder. However, according to his confessional statement it appears that he abducted the deceased Rony and P.Ws.2 and 4.

35.         It is the settled principle of law that the statement of co-accused under section 164 of the Code of Criminal Procedure cannot be the basis for conviction of other co-accused, unless there is strong circumstantial and corroborative evidence to justify the statement made by the accused under section 164 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. On perusal of the 164 statement of the accused Tutul and testimony of the P.Ws.2 and 4, we are of the view that the testimony of P.Ws.2 and 4 not only supports the statement made by the accused Tutul under section 164 of the Code of Criminal Procedure but those corroborates the same. It is also proved that confessional statement was voluntary and true and properly recorded by Mr. Narayan Chandra Das, Metropolitan Magistrate, P.W.3, in accordance with law. So, there is no iota of doubt about the abduction and then killing of the deceased, Rony by the accused persons.

36.         The evidence of the witnesses relied on by the learned Sessions Judge has been established beyond the shadow of doubt that the convicts Fazlur Rahman Badal and Tutul are the perpetrators of the crime. In spite of such evidence being on record the High Court Division has entertained imaginary doubts and concluded that ‘evidences are dubious and as such, the guilt of the accused are not conclusively proved’. The High Court Division overlooked that the law does not require conclusive proof; but only proof beyond reasonable doubt. Besides, the High Court Division has failed to take into consideration that the body of the deceased Rony was recovered by police from the dry land which was surrounded by water at the information of P.W. No.4 which is clearly corroborative by the testimony of the P.W.2 Badsha Mia.

37.         More so, P.W.14 Nayek Md. Rafiqul Islam has clearly deposed in Court regarding the recovery of the body of the deceased Rony, from the place of occurrence at the direction of Badsha P.W.2, and brought the same to the Dhaka Medical College Hospital wherein the Doctor declared that Rony is dead. He informed the occurrence to the Demra Police Station and handed over the corpus to the Demra Police at the Dhaka Medical. It is, therefore, proved that the prosecution story is true and proved beyond the shadow of reasonable doubts that accused persons abducted Rony, Badsha and Rustom. Thereafter, the accused Fazlur Rahman Badal and Tutul killed Rony in front of P.W.2 Badsha, who was also abducted and kept tied at place of occurrence.

38.         Since we are dealing with an appeal against acquittal. We should act with utmost circumspection in the exercise of our powers under Article 103 of the Constitution for setting aside the judgment of the High Court Division. However, where ends of justice requires exercise of such powers, this Court will be failing in its duty if it does not do so.

39.         In dealing with an appeal against acquittal the Court will, naturally, keep in mind the presumption of innocence in favour of the accused, it reinforced, by the judgment of acquittal. But, in the appropriate case, this Division will not abjure its duty to prevent violent miscarriage of justice by hesitating to interfere where interference is imperative.

40.         Where the acquittal is based on irrelevant grounds or where the High Court Division allows itself to be deflected by the red herrings drawn across the track or where the evidence accepted by the trial Court but the same is rejected  by the High Court Division upon a perfunctory consideration or where the High Court Division upon baneful approach has resulted in vital and crucial evidence being ignored, for any such adequate reason this Court may feel obliged to step in to secure the interests of justice, to appease the judicial conscience.

41.         This case in our view, is eminently one of such where the interests of justice is called for interference.*               

42.         The learned Judges of the High Court Division has failed to consider the above mentioned testimonies of witnesses as discussed hereinabove. Therefore, we are of the view that the judgment and order of conviction and sentence passed by the trial Court are not tainted or perversed, rather the impugned judgment of the High Court Division in acquitting all the convicts is upon misreading of the testimony which cannot be sustained in the eye of law. From the deposition of P.Ws.2, 3, 4 and 14 it is proved beyond all the reasonable doubt that the accused Tutul and Badal in a pre-planned manner abducted and killed Rony in front of P.W.2 Badsha. Therefore, the judgment and order so far it relates to acquitting the accused Badal and Tutul is liable to be set aside. However, we are of the opinion that justice would be best served to commute the sentence of death to life imprisonment considering the age of the accused Fazlur Rahman Badal and Monir Hossain Tutul.

43.         Accordingly, Criminal Appeal Nos.98 and 101 of 2014 against the accused Fazlur Rahman Badal and Monir Hossain Tutul are allowed. The judgment and order of acquittal passed by the High Court Division is set aside and those of the trial Court is affirmed. However, the sentence of death is commuted to sentence for life in respect of convict respondents Fazlur Rahman Badal and Monir Hossain Tutul. The respondents Fazlur Rahman Badal and Monir Hossain Tutul respondents in Criminal Appeal Nos.98 and 101 of 2014 are directed to surrender before the Metropolitan Additional Sessions Judge, 1st Court, Dhaka within 2(two) weeks from the date of receipt of this judgment to serve out the rest of the sentence, failing which, the said Court shall take steps to bring them in jail custody in accordance with law.

44.         The Criminal Appeal Nos.99 and 100 of 2014 are dismissed.
