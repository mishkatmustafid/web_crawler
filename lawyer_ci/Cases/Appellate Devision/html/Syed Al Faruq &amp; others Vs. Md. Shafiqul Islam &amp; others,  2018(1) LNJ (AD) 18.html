10.        The High Court Division observed that it is true “that a plaint of a suit should not be rejected under order 7 rule 11 of the Code of Civil Procedure only on point of res-judicata. But the facts of the present case are quite different. Apparently it shows that the predecessor of the plaintiffs-opposite parties lost their title up to Appellate Division and after settling dispute finally by the Appellate Division the present plaintiff-opposite parties purchased some lands in 2009 from the predecessor of the defendants of the former suit and they instituted present suit for declaration of title, confirmation of possession and for further declaration that the decree passed in previous Title Suit No. 235 of 1972 against the predecessor of the present plaintiffs are void and not binding upon them. The issue of present title suit was decided by the Court finally in previously instituted suit and that issue cannot be agitated in the subsequently suit.”

11.        The High Court Division concluded that the present plaintiffs-opposite parties having knowledge about the previous suit and having lost their title up to this Division, instituted the present suit which is nothing but a futile exercise.

12.        Having considered the facts and circumstances discussed above, we are of the view that the High Court Division has properly considered the relevant law and facts involved in the case. The decision arrived at does not call for interference by this Division and, accordingly, the civil petition for leave to appeal is dismissed.

Muhammad Imman Ali, J: This civil petition for leave to appeal is directed against the judgment and order dated 12.07.2012 passed by a Division Bench of the High Court Division in Civil Revision No. 1611 of 2012 discharging the Rule.

2.            The facts, relevant for disposal of the instant civil petition for leave to appeal, are that respondent No. 1 herein as plaintiff filed Title Suit No. 32 of 2009 in the Court of Joint District Judge, 3rd Court, Dhaka against the present petitioners and respondent Nos. 2-6 for setting aside the ex parte judgment and order dated 06.01.1987 passed by the then Subordinate Judge, 1st Court, Dhaka in Title Suit No. 436 of 1985 and which is not binding upon the plaintiff; and for cancelling the deed No. 5327 dated 08.04.1987 which was registered on the basis of aforesaid judgment and order dated 06.01.1987; and for cancellation of the deed of irrevocable General Power of Attorney dated 08.12.1996 which was executed and registered on the basis of deed No. 5327 dated 08.04.1987 and declaring that the plaintiff is the sole owner of the suit property and defendant Nos. 4-7 are bound to mutate the suit property in the name of the plaintiff and approve the plan on receiving the service charge from the plaintiff. 

3.            The present petitioners as defendant Nos. 1-2 contested the suit by filing written statement denying the material averments made in the plaint. They stated, inter alia, that the suit property originally belonged to the then Dhaka Improvement Trust (DIT) now RAJUK. Thereafter, DIT allotted the suit property to Md. Isa declared his intention to sell the suit property. Defendant No. 3 had agreed to purchase the same and accordingly on 02.03.1971 an agreement for sale was drawn up between them. Consideration was fixed at Tk. 80,000/-. On 02.03.1971 said Md. Isa received Tk. 70,000/- as baina money from defendant Nos. 3 in presence of witnesses. Thereafter, Md. Isa received further Tk. 5,000/- and on 28.12.1974 he received further Tk. 3000/- and on 30.12.1982 Md. Isa received Tk. 1000/- from defendant No. 3 and gave undertaking in the back page of the bainapatra. Md. Isa received Tk. 79,000/- out Tk. 80,000/- from defendant No. 3, but he did not execute and register the sale deed in favour of defendant No. 3. Thereafter, defendant No. 3 as plaintiff filed Title Suit No. 436/1985 against Md. Thereafter, the Court also delivered possession to defendant No. 3 on 22.03.1999 through Process Server.
