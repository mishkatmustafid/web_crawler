JUDGMENT

Muhammad Imman Ali, J: This criminal petition for leave to appeal is directed against the judgement and order dated 11.08.2016 passed by the High Court Division in Criminal Appeal No.5185 of 2011 allowing the appeal in part acquitting accused No.2 Hannan @ Hanu of the charge and commuting the sentence of accused Nayan from imprisonment for life to imprisonment of 10(ten) years.

2.            The facts relevant for disposal of the case are that   one Md. Azimuddin lodged a First Information Report (FIR) with Palash Police Station on 09.01.2004 against the accused persons alleging, inter alia, that the informant had longstanding land dispute with the accused party and in order to mitigate the same, some respectable persons of the village came forward and settled the same by giving 5 gondas of land to the informant and on 09.1.2004 at 8.00 a.m. the FIR named accused persons made an attempt to construct a road through the middle of the land as given to the informant on salish by cutting earth from both the sides and while the son of the informant went to the place of occurrence and protested, he was assaulted causing serious injuries and in the course of assault, the  FIR named accused No.l dealt a senda blow on the left knee of the deceased and another accused dealt lathi blows on the victim and when the informant and other witnesses went to the place of occurrence, they were also assaulted. After the alleged occurrence the son of the informant was taken to Kaligonj Thana Health Complex, but on the way he succumbed to the injuries and hence, the present case.

3.            The police investigated the case and submitted charge sheet against the accused persons under sections 143/447/323/302/34 of the Penal Code. The case record was transmitted to the Court of the Sessions Judge, Narsingdi, and subsequently it was transferred to the Court of the Additional Sessions Judge, Narsingdi, for holding trial, who framed charge against the accused persons under sections 302/34 of the Penal Code which was read over to the accused persons who pleaded not guilty and prayed to be tried.

4.            Prosecution examined as many as 9 witnesses and the defence examined none.

5.            On conclusion of the trial, the Court convicted the appellants (petitioners herein) under section 302/34 of the Penal Code and sentenced them to suffer rigorous imprisonment for life and also to pay a fine of Tk.50,000/- each, in default to suffer rigorous imprisonment for one year more.

6.            Being aggrieved thereby, the accused petitioners preferred Criminal Appeal No.5185 of 2011 before the High Court Division, which upon hearing the parties was allowed in part acquitting the accused appellant No.2 Hannan @ Hanu of the charge and altering the conviction of accused-appellant No.1 Nayan from one under section 302/34 to one under the second part of section 304 of the Penal Code and modifying the sentence from imprisonment for life to rigorous imprisonment for 10(ten) years.

7.            Against the judgement and order of High Court Division, the State filed the instant criminal petition for leave to appeal before this Division.  

8.            Mr. Khondker Diliruzzaman, learned Deputy Attorney General appearing for the State submitted that the High Court Division upon misreading and misinterpreting the material points on record and relevant law of the case in question most erroneously passed the impugned judgement and order occasioning failure of justice. He further submitted that the High Court Division having not properly perused the evidence on record and on misdirecting itself on the facts and circumstances of the case, most erroneously passed the impugned judgement and  order, which is non-judicial and arbitrary and not sustainable in the light of the facts and circumstances of the case. The learned DAG also submitted that the accused-respondent is connected with the alleged occurrence and the prosecution will be able to prove the same, and the High Court Division miserably failed to consider the same in passing the impugned judgement and order. He submitted that there is specific allegation against the accused-respondent, and the offence was disclosed under that section of law in the light of the facts and circumstances of the case against the accused which the High Court Division miserably failed to take into consideration. He further submitted that the informant, who deposed as P.W.1, told the Daroga about the injury caused by accused Hannan to the victim’s head, but the Daroga did not write it and the High Court Division miserably failed to consider the same in passing the impugned judgement and order.  The learned D.A.G. lastly submitted that the allegation made and offence charged was proved against the accused respondent, which is clearly evident from the totality of the case, which the High Court Division failed to consider, and hence the impugned order is liable to be set aside.

9.            Mr. Md. Bahar Uddin Al Razi, learned Advocate-on-Record appearing for the respondents made submissions in support of the impugned judgement and order of the High Court Division.

10.        We have considered the submissions of the learned Advocates for the parties concerned and perused the impugned judgement and other connected papers on record.

The two respondents before us, namely accused Nayan and Hannan @ Hanu were convicted by the trial Court under sections 302/34 of the Penal Code and sentenced to suffer imprisonment for life and also to pay a fine of Tk.50,000/- each, in default to suffer rigorous imprisonment for one year more. By the impugned judgement the High Court Division acquitted accused respondent Hannan @ Hanu and altered the conviction of respondent Nayan to one under the second part of section 304 of the Penal Code and reduced his sentence to rigorous imprisonment for 10(ten) years, while maintaining the sentence of fine.

11.      The case against accused respondent Hannan @ Hanu as made out before the trial Court was that he dealt a Dagger blow to the back of the head of the victim. According to the trial Court, the witnesses supported such allegation. However, the High Court Division upon assessment of the evidence and materials on record acquitted Hannan @ Hanu because in the FIR, which was lodged within about 7 hours of the occurrence, the informant did not mention that Abdul Hannan caused any injury to the victim. The High Court Division found that the allegation against Abdul Hannan was an embellishment. 

12.        We find from the FIR that there was no specific allegation of any injury caused by accused Abdul Hannan. Moreover, we find from cross-examination of the informant, who deposed as P.W.1, that he told the Daroga about the injury caused by the accused Hannan to the victim’s head, but the Daroga did not write it. However, there is no evidence on record that the informant took any step against such omission by the Daroga. Also we note from the cross-examination of P.W.7, the Investigating Officer that the FIR was written by the informant in his own hand. In view of such facts and circumstances, we did not find any illegality in the finding of the High Court Division that the case against accused Hannan was not proved beyond reasonable doubt.

13.        With regard to accused Nayan, the allegation is that he dealt seni blow to the knee of the victim. The High Court Division observed that the occurrence took place as a result of land dispute between the parties. It is admitted by the informant in his deposition that there was land dispute. The High Court Division noted that accused Nayan gave only one blow to the left knee which is not a vital part of the body showing that he did not intend to kill the victim for which the killing cannot be termed as murder. The High Court Division concluded that it is not a case of culpable homicide amounting to murder, but one of culpable homicide not amounting to murder and, accordingly, altered the conviction to one under the second part of section 304 of the Penal Code.

         In the facts and circumstances discussed above, we do not find any illegality or infirmity in the impugned judgement. Accordingly, the criminal petition for leave to appeal is dismissed.
