JUDGMENT

Soumendra Sarker, J. The Rule was issued calling upon the opposite parties to show cause as to why the delay of 3343 days in filing the revisional application against the judgment and decree dated 24.10.1992 passed by the learned the then Subordinate Judge, Pabna in Other class Suit No. 73 of 1980 affirming those dated 29.02.1980 passed by the learned the then Munsif, 3rd Court, Pabna in Other Class Suit No. 23 of 1970 should not be condoned and/or pass such other or further order or orders as to this Court may seem fit and proper.

2.             The facts giving rise to the issuance of the Rule in a nutshell can be stated thus: The present opposite parties as plaintiffs instituted the original suit for declaration of title in the court of the then Munsif, 3rd Court, Pabna being Other Class Suit No. 23 of 1997 against the defendant-petitioner-applicants. After trial the suit was decreed on contest on 29.02.1980.

3.             Being aggrieved the defendants preferred an appeal being Other Class Appeal No. 73 of 1980 in the court of the learned District Judge, Pabna which was transmitted to the court of learned subordinate Judge, Pabna for hearing and disposal and the learned appellate court on hearing the appeal disallowed the same and affirmed the judgment and decree passed by the learned trial court on 24.10.1992.

4.             Being aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the impugned judgment and decree, the defendant-petitioner-applicants preferred a revisional application under Section 115(1) of the Code of Civil Procedure, before this court, but in filing of the revisional application there has been a delay of 3343 days.

5.             For condonation of the said delay the defendant-petitioners have filed an application under section 05 read with Section 14 of the Limitation Act contending inter alia that the defendant-applicants being dissatisfied with the judgment and decree passed by the learned Appellate Court in Other Class Appeal No. 73 of 1980 filed a review application under Order XLVII Rule 1 of the Code of Civil Procedure and the application for review was heard and disposed of on 12.11.2001. The application for review since was dismissed the defendants felt aggrieved and have preferred the revisional application before this court. The further case of the defendant-applicants is such that in wrong forum the review application was preferred against the judgment and decree passed by the appellate court and for that reason the delay occurred which is unintentional and bona fide.

6.             On the said application, a Rule was issued and during hearing of the Rule Mr. S. N. Goswami the learned Advocate appeared on behalf of the opposite parties but none appeared on behalf of the petitioners, despite; the matter was posted in the delay cause list of this Bench with the name of the learned Advocate for the petitioners for days together.

7.             The grounds as alleged within the contents of the application for condonation of delay is such that the proceedings of the case was in wrong forum and due to wrong forum since the delay occurred, the petitioners have prayed for condonation of delay which is 3343 days.

8.             As against the grounds of the application for condonation of delay, the learned Advocate appearing on behalf of the opposite parties after referring two decisions of this court  reported in 24 BLT 240 and 25 BLT 29   controverting the grounds of the application for condonation of delay submits that the provisions laid down in section 05 read with section 14 of the Limitation Act cannot be invoked in the instant case inasmuch as  from the facts and circumstances of the case it appears from the face of the record that the original Other Class Suit No. 23 of 1970 and the subsequent appeal which was filed by the present petitioners were disposed of on contest. On 24.10.1992 the judgment and decree was passed in Other Appeal No. 73 of 1980 which was well within the knowledge of the petitioners and the petitioners showing gross negligence and not in clean hands instead of filing of the revisional application deliberately after an inordinate delay of 3343 days, have filed the revisional application and there is no scope to hold such a view that for bona fide  reason the delay occurred. The learned Advocate  further submits that there is no satisfactory explanation from the side of the petitioners as to why the unusual delay of 3343 days occurred and it is a decided matter in our judicature that inasmuch as from the conduct of the petitioners it is apparent that they have preferred a review application before the appellate court after disposal of the appeal which is admittedly the wrong forum and that was chosen by the defendant-petitioner-applicants themselves, it cannot be treated sufficient cause within the meaning of section 05 of the Limitation Act. This Bench in the case of Ms. Ok-Kyung Oh vs. Tea Hung Packaging  (Bd.) Ltd. and others, 24 BLT 240 held :

            “ the conduct of the petitioner herself in choosing wrong forum cannot be treated ‘sufficient cause’ within the meaning  of  section 5 of the Limitation Act. Besides this; the ground of the application for condonation of delay as it appears is not proper to hold such a view that the mistake done by the plaintiff-appellant-petitioner was bonafide and she was diligent in filing the civil revision within time. Cause of delay as cited within the contents of the application under section 5 of the Limitation Act (IX of 1908) appears to be evasive and it is no doubt that only due to self-impression of the petitioner this delay of 2091 days occurred; which is not at all satisfactory. Moreover; it is settled that in the application filed under section 5 of the Limitation Act the petitioner is required to explain each day’s delay to the satisfaction of the Court.”

9.             In order to appreciate the submission advanced from the side of the learned counsel for the opposite parties, on perusal of the grounds of the application for condonation of delay filed under section 05 read with section 14 of the Limitation Act, 1908 it is obvious to note that the explanation given  from the side of the petitioner-applicants is not at all satisfactory and it cannot be treated as bona fide for condonation of the said inordinate delay of 3343 days. Apart from this, admittedly the review application was dismissed on 12.11.2001 but the revisional application has been filed on 30.03.2002 and there is no explanation as to why this delay was caused, when 12.11.2001 was within the knowledge of the petitioners. Furthermore, it is apparent from the face of the records that in between the dates as cited within the contents of the application, there exist no reasonable link. There is nothing on record to show that in approaching the wrong forum there was any bona fide of the learned lawyer or the petitioners themselves. There is also noting to hold such a view that there was due diligence and caution from the side of the petitioners before moving the wrong forum  under the ambit of  relevant law. In case of such inordinate delay it is a decided matter in our arena that the delay of each and every day must be explained satisfactorily from the expiry of the date  of limitation up to the date of institution [Ref. 5 DLR 20; 25 DLR 254 ].

10.         Furthermore, within the ambit of section 05 read with section 14 of the Limitation Act, 1908 where the party concern can prove that they were due diligent and bona fide  and were prevented by sufficient cause from bringing the case within time, the delay can be condoned. But in the instant case  as I have come across that the petitioner-applicants are not at all diligent or bona fide in filing the revisional application within time, rather; they were very much negligent, dormant and callous in filing the case within time and law does not permit the court to help any  such dormant.

11.         Having regard to the facts, circumstances and the discussions referred to above, I am constrained to hold such a view that the argument advanced from the side of the learned counsel Mr. S. N. Goshawmi is well founded and hence, the Rule has got no substance to succeed.

12.         In the result; the Rule is discharged without any order as to costs.
