Penal Code (XLV of 1860)

Section 300

The condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique with the previous intention to murder the victim Atiqur Rahman Atique which was pre-meditative, under the ambit of section 300 of the Penal Code committed the murder.                                               ...(48)

 

Evidence Act (I of 1872)

Section 8

The absconding condemned-convict and his accomplices the appellant Sumon @ Mama Sumon and Khokon are directly involved in the occurrence of killing the victim Atique and under section 8 of the Evidence Act, 1872 the abscondence of the condemned-convict and Sumon after misusing the bail privilege being on guilty conscience, is a strong corroboration to the prosecution case that they are the culprits. The extra-judicial confession which is transpires from the testimony of the witnesses, are also a corroboration of the prosecution case as well as the confession made by the confessing condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique.      ...(49)

 

Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898)

Section 164 and 364

On meticulous consideration of the evidences on record along with the case record and scanning the material exhibits including the medical examination report, FIR, inquest report and the confessional statement of the condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique, it transpires that the series of evidences as adduced from the side of the prosecution, has corroborated one another as to the link of this case and the learned Magistrate who recorded the confessional statement of the principal accused the condemned-convict Shafique, during recording of the statement strictly complied with the provisions laid down in section 164 and 364 of the Code of Criminal Procedure and it was clearly explained to the accused before recording of his confessional statement that, (i) the learned Magistrate is not an officer of police but a Magistrate; (ii) that he is not bound to make a confession; (iii) that if does make a confession it may be used in evidence against him; (iv) that he should not say anything because others have told him to say it but is at liberty to say whatever he really desires to say; (v) that he should say nothing which is untrue.                      ...(43)

JUDGMENT

 

Soumendra Sarker, J. This reference under section 374 of the Code of Criminal Procedure has been made by the learned Metropolitan Sessions Judge, Sylhet for confirmation of the sentence of death passed on the condemned-convict namely Md. Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique (absconding) in Sessions Case No.1808 of 2013 (previous Sessions Case No.736 of 2012) arising out of Kotwali Model Police Station Case No.88, dated 30.11.2010 corresponding to G.R. Case No.1164 of 2010 under section 302/34 of the Penal Code.

 

2.            The co-accused of this case Sumon @ Mama Sumon against whom the learned Sessions Judge awarded lifelong imprisonment with a fine of Tk.10,000/- (Ten thousand) in default to pay the fine, additional imprisonment for a term of two months under the above mentioned sections of the Penal Code, has filed a criminal appeal being No.3378 of 2015.

 

3.            The Death Reference and the aforesaid Appeal have been heard together and are being disposed of by this single judgment.

 

4.            The prosecution case as made out in the ejahar in a nutshell can be stated thus: One Haji Md. Khorshed Ali, son of late Md. Abdul Motaleb of village 12/A, Kumarpara, Ward No.18, Kotwali police station under Sylhet District lodged the First Information Report (FIR) with Kotwali Model Police Station before the Officer-in-Charge on 30.11.2010contending inter alia that the informant is the proprietor of Hotel Anurag situated at Dhupa dighir Uttarpar of Sylhet town and he was an immigrant of United Kingdom. Due to his old age with different diseases he returned back to this country and as a proprietor of the above mentioned Hotel started the same. The further case of the informant is such that his son the victim of this case, Atiqur Rahman @ Atique used to reside in London and prior to three years of the occurrence, the informant asked him to come back and the deceased Atiqur Rahman @ Atique after his return to this country was entrusted to supervise and manage the Hotel ‘Anurag’. The deceased Atiqur Rahman @ Atique got him introduced with the condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman Shafiq through one Mosharaff, a businessman of Latif Centre, Sylhet. The condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman was a businessman of VCD and DVD and he used to sale the same to the deceased Atiqur Rahman and that the condemned-convict Shafique used to come to the deceased very often and stayed in his hotel as a boarder. On 23.11.2010 the convict Shafiqur Rahman hired the room No.322 of that hotel, identifying him as a son of one Hajee Rahmat Ullah and Shahebjadi Begum and he stayed there untill the date of occurrence i.e. up to 29.11.2010. On the date of occurrence at about 3.00 p.m. at the request of the convict Shafiqur Rahman the deceased Atiqur Rahman @ Atique returned back to the hotel Anurag and went to the room No.322. After some time of his arrival in the said place of occurrence room, the convict Shafiqur Rahman and his two accomplices while where leaving the hotel, the Managers of that Hotel namely Salauddin and Shahabuddin asked the convict Shafiqur Rahman and his two accomplices as to where they are going. In reply to that, they told to the Managers that, for collecting money from S.A. Paribahan they are going outside. Subsequently, the General Manager of the hotel namely Mahmuduzzaman Jewel finding the mobile phone of the deceased Atiqur Rahman stopped, along with 4/5 employees of that hotel started to look after Atiqur Rahman, who was their junior boss and at a stage of searching, they went in front of room No.322 and hearing sound of water falling inside the said room No.322 which was under lock and key, with the help of duplicate key of the room opened the room and found the deceased Atiqur Rahman @ Atique fastened by a rope in his legs, hands, neck and waist and the mouth of the deceased Atique at that time was closed by a scotch tape. Finding that condition of the deceased Atique, the General Manager told the driver of the deceased namely Alamgir to bring the informant Hajee Md. Khorshed Ali from his residence. The General Manager informed the Rapid Action Battalion (RAB) and police requesting them to come to the place of occurrence. Subsequently, the informant along with his wife and the deceased’s wife coming to the hotel found the victim Atiqur Rahman @ Atique in the aforesaid condition and became senseless. After some time, the members of RAB and police force coming to the place of occurrence found the deceased and the police seized a Tiger Energy drink bottle from the place of occurrence room having some poisonous substance therein. They also seized some CD and VCD which were scattered inside the room. The mobile phone set and money bag of the deceased were missing, but the wristwatch of the deceased was on the floor of that room. The further case of the informant is such that the body of his son Atique was sent to Sylhet Osmani Medical College Hospital, from where he was declared ‘dead’. Subsequently, the condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique made a mobile phone call from his mobile phone No. ‘0175206307’ to other mobile phone of the deceased Atique at 01.20 a.m. being No. ‘01746065732’ and that phone call was received by the paternal father-in-law of the deceased namely Badrul Islam. The convict Shafiqur Rahman by that phone call wanted to know the condition of Atique, disclosing his identity as a friend of Atique. The phone number which was used by the condemned-convict Sofique was known to the hotel boy Mizan, inasmuch as in that phone number prior to the occurrence Sofique requested the hotel boy Mizan on different occasions to give flexi load from outside of the hotel.

 

5.            Subsequently, after getting First Information Report, investigation was started by the concerned Kotwali Police Station, Sylhet and one Sub-Inspector Md. Khasrul Alam Badal and Sub-inspector Md. Abdul Alim were entrusted to investigate the case. During investigation the condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman Shafique was arrested and he confessed his guilt in killing the deceased Atikur Rahman @ Atik before a Magistrate, 1st Class and his confessional statement was recorded by the learned Magistrate under section 164 of the Code of Criminal Procedure.  After closer of the investigation, the investigating officer finding prima-facie ingredients of the commission of offence against the convicted accused, namely Shafiqur Rahman Shafique and his accomplices Sumon @ Mama Sumon and Khokon, submitted charge sheet No.445 dated 18.06.2011 under section 302/34 of the Penal Code.

 

6.            While the case was ready for trial, it was sent to the learned Metropolitan Sessions Judge, Sylhet. The learned Metropolitan Sessions Judge after framing charge on 11.09.2012 under section 302/34 of the Penal Code read over and explained the same to the presentee accused persons Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique and Sumon @ Mama Sumon, who pleaded not guilty and claimed to be tried. The other convicted person Khokon having been absconded, the charge was not possible to read over and explain to him.

 

7.            During trial one State Defence lawyer was appointed to defend the absconding accused persons.

 

8.            The prosecution at the time of trial has examined as many as 17 witnesses including the informant Hajee Md. Khorshed and the learned Magistrate, who recorded the confessional statement of the convict Shafiqur Rahmain @ Shafique.

 

9.            After the close of evidence from the side of the prosecution since only the condemned-convict Shafique was present in the accused-dock and the other two accused were absconding, he was examined under section 342 of the Code of Criminal Procedure and at that time the condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique pleading him not guilty declined to adduce evidence and claimed to be tried.

 

10.        From the trend of cross-examination of the witnesses and examination of the condemned-convict Shafique under section 342 of the Code of Criminal Procedure the case of the defence is such that the accused are innocent and in no way connected with the killing of the deceased Atiqur Rahman @ Atique.

 

11.        After trial of the sessions case the learned Metropolitan Sessions Judge, Sylhet considering the evidence and materials on record as well as facts and circumstances of the case, finding the convict persons guilty, sentenced them under section 302/34 of the Penal Code. The condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique was sentenced to death and his other co-accused Sumon @ Mama Sumon and Khokon (all along absconding) was sentenced to suffer imprisonment for life with fine of Tk.10,000/- (Ten thousand) each, in default to payment of fine, to suffer additional imprisonment of two months each.

 

12.        Out of the said judgment and order of conviction and sentence this Death Reference No.78 of 2014 has been sent for the condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman and for the other convicted person Sumon @ Mama Sumon Criminal Appeal No.3378 of 2015 was preferred against the judgment and order of conviction and sentence against him.

 

13.        With regard to the Death Reference as well as Criminal Appeal No. 3378 of 2015 Mr. Md. Aminul Islam, the learned Deputy Attorney General with Ms. Shamsun-nahar (Laizu) and Ms. Farhana Afroze, the learned Assistant Attorney Generals appeared on behalf of the State. While Mrs. Hasna Begum was appointed state defence lawyer for the absconding accused.

 

14.        Mr. Md. Aminul Islam, the learned Deputy Attorney General (hereinafter DAG) submits that the FIR of the case was lodged on 30.11.2010 at 22.10 hours andthe date of occurrence was 29.11.2010 and the time of occurrence was between 3.04 p.m. to 3.30 p.m. The place of occurrence was Hotel Anurag, 2nd Floor, Room No.322. The learned DAG further submits that after proper investigation of the case police submitted charge sheet under section 302/34 of the Penal Code on 18.06.2011. The charge was framed by the learned Metropolitan Sessions Judge, on 11.09.2012 and out of 31 charge-sheeted witnesses, 17 witnesses were examined by the prosecution, those who have successfully proved the case of the prosecution beyond all reasonable doubt. There is an extra-judicial confession, in this case and about this confession, the prosecution witness No.2, 11, 12 and 13 testified before the Court. The learned DAG also submits that the principal accused of this case is the condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique, who has confessed his guilt under section 164 of the Code of Criminal Procedure and clearly implicated the other co-accused of this case as of his two accomplices in the occurrence of killing the victim Atiqur Rahman @ Atique. The learned DAG argued that the Managers of the hotel Anurag, the prosecution witness No.3 and 7 categorically in their testimony testified that they have seen the convict persons and the prosecution case was totally corroborated by the witnesses including the managers. The learned DAG submits that the investigating officers of the case had no necessity to produce any witness to identify the assailants through T.I. parade, since there is a clear(distinct) video footage of the accused persons which was in the close circuit camera of the Hotel Anurag and that video footage was seized by the investigating officer and preserved, from where it is apparent that the two accomplice of the principal accused Shafique were accompanied by Shafique at the time of occurrence. The learned DAG submits that, without any mental or physical torture, fear or threat after the apprehension the condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique, when he(Shafique) wanted to confess his guilt, he was produced before the learned Magistrate, 1st Class and the learned Magistrate complying the provisions of law properly, recorded the confessional statement of the condemned-convict Shafique under section 164 of the Code of Criminal Procedure and it is from the confessional statement itself that for reflection, three hours time was given to the convict Shafique before his 164 statement and as such there is nothing to discard the confessional statement of the condemned-convict Shafique. The learned DAG lastly submits that the prosecution witness No.2 Alamgir, who is the driver of the deceased as well as the informant, is a very important witness of this case, who is competent enough to corroborate the prosecution case. The Assistant Manager of Hotel Anurag the witness No.3 is also an important witness and the witness No.2, 3 7 and 10 are the direct ocular witnesses, who has witnessed the convicted persons to leave the place of occurrence immediately after the occurrence. The witness No.11 of the prosecution is also an important witness, who has talked with the condemned-convict over telephone after the occurrence. The witness No.12 and 13 are the witnesses of extra-judicial confession and the inquest report of the deceased which was immediately after the occurrence at 16.45 hours of the date of occurrence and has clearly corroborated the injuries which were found in the dead body of the deceased Atique. From the contents of this report it is crystal clear that there is no subsequent embellishment and the FIR is very much consistent with the inquest report and postmortem report of the deceased, wherefrom it is clear that there was a distinct mark of strangulation and that the link of the occurrence has been well proved from the evidences on record and it is also well proved that it was a preplanned cool-blooded murder, since the rope which was used in tiding in the neck of the victim was brought by the assailant Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique prior to the occurrence from outside. The learned DAG in support of his contention has referred a decision of this Court reported in 55 DLR 116 and relying upon the said decision in his concluding submission submits that the prosecution has successfully discharged their onus of proof  by sufficient competent tangible and credible evidence and it is also proved that prior to the confession of the principal accused Shafique before the learned Magistrate, (P.W.16, Mr. Shahabuddin Masum) there was no torture, threat or provocation to him by anybody including the police and therefore, the judgment and order of conviction and sentence passed by the learned Metropolitan Sessions Judge, Sylhet should be upheld.

 

15.        As against the aforesaid argument, Mrs. Hasna Begum, the learned Advocate appearing as State Defence lawyer in support of the defence submits that, against the condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique in the instant case there is no eye witness in support of the prosecution case and there is no T.I. parade by which the assailants of the occurrence were identified. The learned State Defence Advocate further submits that there is no pre-meditation of the condemned-convict Shafique prior to the occurrence and there are several contradictions among the witnesses, who were examined in support of the prosecution case. The learned State Defence counsel lastly submits that the confessional statement of the condemned-convict Shafique is not true and voluntary and it was not properly recorded by the learned Magistrate and the driver of the informant namely Alamgir, who was examined as prosecution witness No.2 admittedly has no appointment letter and as such the evidence of the driver cannot be relied on and there is no paper relating to business relationship of the deceased with the condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique and as such the Death Reference is liable to be rejected.

 

16.        In respect of the Criminal Appeal filed on behalf of the convict-appellant Sumon @ Mama Sumon, none appeared to make any submission at the time of hearing despite; the matter has been posted in the daily cause list of this bench for days together with name of the learned Advocate for the appellant.

 

17.        In view of the argument advanced from both the sides, let us now scan the evidence adduced from the side of the prosecution and cross-examination of the prosecution witnesses including other evidence of this case. In the instant case, prosecution have examined as many as 17 witnesses including the Doctor, who did the postmortem of the dead body and the learned Magistrate, who recorded the confessional statement of the condemned-convict Shafique under section 164 of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

 

18.        P.W.1 of the prosecution is the informant of this case Hajee Md. Khorshed Ali. This witness in his testimony testifies that he is the informant of this case and proprietor of the place of occurrence Hotel Anurag. This witness also testifies that the victim Atiqur Rahman @ Atique happens to be his son and he was killed brutally. This witness in support of his ejahar corroborated the same in his deposition and at a stage categorically testified that after the occurrence the accused Shafique and his two accomplices while were leaving the hotel, the hotel Manager Salahuddin and Shahabuddin finding the accused asked them, as to where they are going and in reply, the accused Shafique told that they are going to S.A. Paribahan for bringing money. The informant of this case in his testimony identified his signature in the ejahar and clearly testifies that the ejahar was written and typed according to his statement and going through the ejahar he put his signature therein. He has identified the ejahar and his signature therein which has been marked as Exhibit-‘1’ and ‘1/1’ respectively. The informant in his deposition also testified that the accused Shafique was arrested by the members of Rapid Action Battalion (RAB) on 15.02.2011 from Dhaka, who confessed his guilt after that, before a Judicial Magistrate under section 164 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. The informant has identified the condemned-convict Shafique who was present then in the accused-dock. He also identified the co-accused Sumon who was also present at that time. The informant in his testimony testified at a stage that the video picture which was within the Close Circuit Camera of his hotel go to show that the accused Shafique, Khokon and Sumon came to his hotel and left his(P.W.1) hotel after the occurrence and the video footage of the Close Circuit Camera was seized by the investigating authority in his presence and he put his signature in the seizure list. The informant identified the seizure list and the signature therein which have been marked as Exhdibit-2 and 2/1 respectively.

 

19.        During cross-examination the informant in a reply to a question from the side of the defence admitted that his son Atiqur Rahman used to purchase CD and VCD from the condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique and he (P.W.1) has heard from his driver Alamgir that Atikur Rahman at the time of returning to his residence, was called on by the accused Shafique through mobile phone and getting that mobile call Atique returned back to the hotel and went to the room No.322, which was allotted in the name of the condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique and its number is 322. The informant specifically denied that the accused Shafique because of torture after his arrest gave his confessional statement.

 

20.        P.W.2 of the prosecution, Md. Alamgir is a driver by profession and he testified before the Court during his testimony that he is the driver of the informant’s private car and the date of occurrence is on 29.11.2010. On that date he brought the victim Atiqur Rahman at about 9.00 a.m. to his hotel Anurag and at about 2.45 p.m. Atiqur Rahman started to return back to his  residence from hotel and when they reached to a place namely ‘Nayarpar’ nearer to ‘Fortune Garden Hotel’, Atiqur Rahman received a phone call and with regard to this P.W.2 testifies as follows:

21.           This prosecution witness No.2 Alamgir in the last portion of his examination-in-chief testifies that after the arrest of Shafique, he (P.W.2) has seen him (Shafique) in the RAB camp, where Shafique made a confession that he along with Sumon and Khokon has killed Atiqur Rahman.

 

22.        During cross-examination the witness No.2 testified at a stage that at the time of their returning back to the hotel, he asked the victim Atique, who has called him over phone. This witness in his testimony corroborated the prosecution case and denied the suggestion of the defence that he did not enquire into the caller of the phone, who over mobile phone called on Atique. This witness also denied that the accused Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique did not give any phone call to the victim, telling him to come back to the hotel. He also denied that the accused Shafique did not confess his guilt before the RAB.

 

23.        P.W.3 Shahabuddin is the Assistant Manager of Hotel Anurag. This witness in his testimony testified that, prior to one year of the occurrence he joined in his post and the date of occurrence is 29.11.2010. On that date he was on duty from 3.00 p.m. and 3/5 minutes prior to that, he found  Atiqur Rahman to start for his residence, but after 4/5 minutes Atique again come back to the hotel and went to the 2nd floor, and about 15/20 minutes to that, this witness observed that one boarder of that hotel ‘Shafique’ along with two others coming out of his room and going outside of the hotel, at which this witness asked him(Shafique) as to whether he will leave or come back to the hotel and in reply  to that question, Shafique told him that for bringing money from S.A. Paribahan he is going outside and he wanted to know the location of S.A. Paribahan office from this P.W.3. This witness also in his testimony testified that their General Manager Mahmuduzzaman @ Jewel after 10/15 minutes, coming before him informed that the mobile phone of their junior boss Atiqur Rahman is stopped, and stating that, their  G.M. Mahmuduzzaman Jewel started to find out Atique and going in front of room No.322 hearing the sound of shower inside that room on bringing duplicate key, opened that room and same times after that, they informed this witness that their junior boss has been killed. Thereafter, P.W.3 went to the place of occurrence room and found Atique fastened with the ‘sofa’ of that room, tiding neck, waist, hands, legs by rope and the mouth was closed by scotch tape. Finding that occurrence, this witness informed their senior boss over telephone and the G.M. of the hotel informed the police station and RAB through phone. At a stage of deposition, this P.W.3 testifies that, hearing about the arrest of accused Shafique by RAB, they went to the office of RAB and in front of all, Shafique confessed that he along with Sumon and Khokon has killed their junior boss.

 

24.        During cross-examination P.W.3 testified at a stage that he has deposed before the police officer sitting in the hotel, but he cannot recollect that date. P.W.3 denied the suggestion that he did not tell anything before the Investigating Officer about his statement in the court.

 

25.        P.W.4 Md. Abdul Monim is a seizure list witness. This witness testified before the Court that the date of occurrence is 29.11.2010 and at about 4.09 p.m. he has heard that Atiqur Rahman has been killed in hotel Anurag. In presence of this witness on 01.12.2010 the Investigating Officer from the ground floor of hotel Anurag within office room of the hotel seized the name and address of the assailant, which was written in the hotel register. This witness identified the seizure list and his signature therein which has been marked as Exhibit-3 and 3/1 respectively.

 

26.        During cross-examination P.W.4 testified at a stage that Atiqur Rahman was his friend.

 

27.        P.W.5 Abul Hasan Babul in his testimony testified that the informant Khorshed Ali happens to be his maternal uncle and the victim Atiqur Rahman is his brother. On 29.11.2010 the occurrence was held and in presence of this witness the police officer prepared the inquest report of the victim Atique. This witness as witness of the inquest report put his signature. He identified the inquest report and his signature therein which has been marked as Exhdibit-4 and 4/1 respectively. This witness also testified that he has heard about the occurrence that in the boarder’s room No.322 of the hotel, accused Shafique along with his accomplices committed the killing and after killing the deceased Atique, they have left the hotel at about 3.00/3.30 p.m. and the two other accomplices of the killer Shafique were Sumon and Khokon.

 

28.        P.W.6 Mahmuduzzaman @ Jewel is the General Manager of Hotel Anurag and in his testimony he has testified about the case of the prosecution and corroborated the ejahar with other prosecution witnesses No.2, 3, 4 and 5. During his deposition P.W.6 the General Manager of the Hotel categorically testified that when he noticed that the mobile phone of their junior boss Atiqur Rahman is switched off, he started searching and at a stage of that, he went in front of room No.322 and heard water falling sound from the closed room No.322 which is under lock and key from outside. Thereafter, bringing duplicate key of that room he opened the room and found their junior boss fastened by a rope with a ‘sofa set’ tiding his hands, legs, waist and neck by that rope and the mouth of the deceased Atique was closed by a scotch tape. Finding that scenario this witness told the driver to inform the owner of the hotel instantly and he informed the RAB and police about the occurrence. After getting his information police and RAB came to the place of occurrence. During his deposition this witness No.6 Jewel testified at a stage that on 01.12.2010 from the office room of the hotel, investigating officer seized the bio-data of the boarder of the place of occurrence room No.322, the condemned-convict Shafique and in his(P.W.6) presence the seizure list which was prepared for that seized alamat(papers), he put his signature. This witness identified the seizure list, his signature and the bio-data of the condemned-convict Shafique which were in two forms and marked as article Exhibit No. I and I/1 respectively. This witness also testified in his testimony that in the video of the CC Camera of that hotel it is found that the co-accused Khokon, and Sumon along with principal accused Shafiqur Rahman is entering into the hotel and going outside of the hotel and the police seized these videos through seizure list which were identified by this witness.

 

29.        During cross-examination this witness categorically testified at a stage that he has appointment letter about his appointment as General Manager of the hotel and his duty was in the hotel from morning to afternoon till 4.00 p.m. and thereafter, from 06.00-11.00 p.m. This witness in a reply to a question from the side of the defence, specifically testified that at the time of occurrence the Managers were Salauddin and Sahabuddin, and they were on duty also.

 

30.        P.W.7 Md. Salauddin corroborated the witness No.06 in toto and clearly testified before the trial court that he was on duty from 3.00 p.m., and 20-25 minutes after his joining in the duty, he found the friend of their junior boss Atique namely Shafique and two others are going outside from the hotel. Finding them, this witness and his co-manager asked Shafique as to where they are going and in reply, Shafique told that they are going outside for bringing money from S.A. Paribahan. This witness in his testimony corroborated the prosecution case and also identified the accused Shafique in the accused-dock.

 

31.        P.W.8 Moloy Dutta Mithu is a hear-say witness of this case. This witness has heard about the occurrence and in his presence the inquest report was prepared. As witness of the inquest report he gave his signature therein and besides this; in his presence the material exhibits i.e. the rope, the tiger drink bottle, the bad-sheet, scotch tape, CD cover, one undervest and two bags were seized by police. The sofa set was also seized and a seizure list was prepared and as witness this P.W.8 gave his signature therein.

P.W.10 also testified that, 5/6 days prior to the occurrence, Shafique coming to their hotel became the boarder of room No.322, and on 29.11.2010 two guest of Shafique came to his room and stayed there till afternoon and at about 3.30 p.m. all the three accused jointly came out of the room and went outside, and subsequent to that, finding their junior boss no where they started searching and went to the 2nd floor of the hotel and in front of room No.322 they have heard sound of water falling and the room is under lock and key from outside. Thereafter, by duplicate key opening the door of the room they found their junior boss Atique, but his waist, hands, legs and neck were tide by a rope and the body was fastened with the sofa-set and his mouth was also closed by scotch tape and Atique was senseless and sent to Osmani Medical College Hospital, wherefrom the doctor told that he is dead.

Khorshed Ali and also deposed that in their presence the accused Shafique disclosed before RAB that he along with Sumon and Khokon killed the victim Atique for want of money.

This witness in his testimony testified that during his investigation he went to the place of occurrence and prepared the sketch map, index etc. and also prepared the inquest report of the dead body and seized the alamats including 46 cubits long rope, one bloodstained handkerchief, one bed-sheet, one undervest , two pillow covers and a scotch tape through a seizure list. He also seized some VCD, an old bag, and one royal tiger drinks plastic bottle. 43.        On meticulous consideration of the evidences on record along with the case record and scanning the material exhibits including the medical examination report, FIR, inquest report and the confessional statement of the condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique, it transpires that the series of evidences as adduced from the side of the prosecution, has corroborated one another as to the link of this case and the learned Magistrate who recorded the confessional statement of the principal accused the condemned-convict Shafique, during recording of the statement strictly complied with the provisions laid down in section 164 and 364 of the Code of Criminal Procedure and it was clearly explained to the accused before recording of his confessional statement that, (i) the learned Magistrate is not an officer of police but a Magistrate; (ii) that he is not bound to make a confession; (iii) that if does make a confession it may be used in evidence against him; (iv) that he should not say anything because others have told him to say it but is at liberty to say whatever he really desires to say; (v) that he should say nothing which is untrue.

 

44.        Apart from this, there is a clear declaration of the learned Magistrate to the effect that the confession was voluntarily made and it is true. In this context; in the case of ABM Nazmus Sakib Ashik –vs.- State, 12 BLC (AD) 203 our Apex Court held, the Magistrate appears to have recorded his satisfaction as to the voluntariness and spontaneous nature of the confession of the accused. It appears that the confession has not been vitiated by any illegality. Besides, this Court in the case of Abul Hossain –vs.- State, 46 DLR 77 opined that the provisions under sections 164 and 364 of the Code of Criminal Procedure are mandatory and required to be strictly followed to make the confession voluntary and true and fit for reliance for convicting the accused on his confession. In the case of Nausher Ali Sarder –Vs.- The State, 39 DLR (AD) 194, their lordships held that the surrounding circumstances are ordinarily the only material from which the inference of a confession may be drawn. Confession when proved against the confessing accused, it can be taken into consideration against co-accused in the same offence.

45.        Having regard to the facts, circumstances and the discussions referred to above, we are constrained to hold such a view that here in this case it is proved beyond all shadow of doubt by series of cogent evidence that the condemned-convict Shafique is directly involved with the offence of killing the victim Atiqur Rahman Atique. We also find that the learned trial court in fact during examination of the convicted-appellant  Suman @ mama Suman under section 342 of the Code of Criminal Procedure complied with the provisions by giving attention of the accused to the incriminating evidences which were produced against him and as such no illegality or irregularity is noticed by which it can be held that the convicted persons have been prejudiced or has been deprived of getting justice from the learned Metropolitan Sessions Judge, Sylhet.

 

46.        Furthermore, obviously we have every reason to draw such inference that there was no ambiguity or confusion  in the mind of the accused before giving reply to the question put before him by the learned trial court and in view of the facts and circumstances of the case we are of the considered view that the learned Metropolitan Sessions Judge, Sylhet during deciding the fate of the case in favour of the prosecution committed no such infirmity by dint of which the impugned judgment and order of conviction and sentence of death can be interfered with. Moreover, it is observed that the learned trial court rightly considered the case in its true perspective.

 

47.        Having gone through the evidences on record we find on our anxious consideration over the testimonies of the ocular witnesses, who personally witnessed the convicted persons immediately after the occurrence at the time of their leaving the place of occurrence hotel and being satisfied on the assessment of the evidences by the trial court in its total approach, we are inclined to hold such a view that the trial court committed no wrong. Moreover, the ingredients of the offence as we have spelt out were also duly appreciated by the learned trial court and as such in the discussions made above particularly the nature of the offence and the confession of the principal accused as to the implication and participation of the convicts in the heinous crime of killing the victim Atiqur Rahman Atique, we are of the view that the prosecution has been able to prove the case beyond all shadow of doubt and as such the impugned judgment and order of conviction and sentence should be sustained.

 

48.        Considering the facts and circumstances of the case it is crystal clear from the factual aspects, that the condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique with the previous intention to murder the victim Atiqur Rahman Atique which was pre-meditative, under the ambit of section 300 of the Penal Code committed the murder. As such we find nothing in the circumstances of the case and the conduct of the condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman to take lenient view in the matter of sentence despite our best concern to temper justice with mercy. Considering all aspect of the matter we are obliged to the law to sustain the order of conviction and sentence.

 

49.        Before parting, however, we must observe that the circumstantial evidences which are evident from the face of the records are cogent and despite in the instant case there is no direct eye witness of the occurrence, the circumstantial evidence as it appears is trustworthy, tangible and credible, and hence, it cannot be ignored and definitely the circumstances makes it clear that the absconding condemned-convict and his accomplices the appellant Sumon @ Mama Sumon and Khokon are directly involved in the occurrence of killing the victim Atique and under section 8 of the Evidence Act, 1872 the abscondence of the condemned-convict and Sumon after misusing the bail privilege being on guilty conscience, is a strong corroboration to the prosecution case that they are the culprits. The extra-judicial confession which is transpires from the testimony of the witnesses, are also a corroboration of the prosecution case as well as the confession made by the confessing condemned-convict Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique.

 

50.        Be that as it may, we are inclined to hold such a conclusion that the argument advanced from the side of the learned Deputy Attorney General Mr. Md. Aminul Islam is consistent with the case record and as such these are well founded.

 

51.        In the result, the Death Reference No.78 of 2014 is accepted and consequently, the Criminal Appeal No.3378 of 2015 be dismissed. The impugned judgment and order of conviction and sentence dated 12.11.2014 awarded by the learned Metropolitan Sessions Judge, Sylhet in Sessions Case No.1808 of 2013 (previous Sessions Case No.736 of 2012) is hereby upheld and confirmed.

 

52.        Let the Death Sentence of the condemned-convict namely Md. Shafiqur Rahman @ Shafique (absconding), son of Hazi Rahmat Ullah, 28 Ganaktuli Lane, Police Station-Hazaribag, Dhaka, be executed after his arrest/surrender in accordance with law and the judgment and order of conviction and sentence passed by the learned Metropolitan Sessions Judge, Sylhet in Sessions Case No.1808 of 2013 (previous Sessions Case No.736 of 2012) in respect of the remaining convict-appellant Sumon @ mama Sumon is affirmed.
