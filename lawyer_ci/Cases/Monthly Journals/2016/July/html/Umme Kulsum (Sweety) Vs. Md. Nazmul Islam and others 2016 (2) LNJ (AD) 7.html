Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898)
Section 561A
Nari-o-Shishu Nirjatan Daman Ain (VIII of 2000)
Section 27
The enquiry report as mentioned in the judgment does not contain any proof that the complainant requested the police officer or the empowered person to receive the complaint and failed as such, it is clear that there is no proof in support of this part of the requirement of law and as such, the Tribunal ought to have dismissed the complaint, but it without doing so took cognizance on the plea that there were prima-facie materials in support of the allegation against the accused, but that was not sufficient. The Tribunal has to be satisfied that proof of both the requirements were available in the report. Thus taking cognizance of the offence in the instant case was illegal and although this point was not considered by the High Court Division, we are of the view that on this count also the proceedings was also liable to be quashed. Sub-section 1(Ga), of the Ain has given power to the Tribunal to take cognizance of the offence on some other grounds in case no recommendation for taking cognizance is made in the enquiry report, but it has not given any authority to the Tribunal to take cognizance of an offence if the fact of going to the police officer to lodge the complaint is not stated in the report. We find no illegality in the impugned judgment and order passed by the High Court Division.      ...(11 and 12)

JUDGMENT

Md. Nizamul Huq, J: This criminal petition for leave to appeal is directed against the judgment and order dated 20.07.2011 passed by a Division Bench of the High Court Division in Criminal Miscellaneous Case No.2502 of 2011 arising out of Nari-O-Shishu Nirjatan Daman Case No.94 of 2010 under section 11(Ga)/30 of the Nari-O-Shishu Nirjatan Daman Ain-2000(as amended in 2003), hereinafter referred to as the Ain, now pending in the Court of Nari-O-Shishu Nirjatan Daman Tribunal, Patuakhali making the Rule absolute and thereby quashing the proceedings of the above mentioned case.
  1. The facts, leading to the filing of the petition for leave to appeal, are that the petitioner as complainant filed a petition of complainant before the Nari-O-Shishu Nirjatan Daman Tribunal, Patuakhali on the allegation that she had been married to respondent No.1 Md. Nazmul Islam on 30.10.2009, that soon after marriage at the instance of other respondents he demanded dowry of Tk. 1,20,000/- to purchase a motorbike, and asked her to bring the dowry and sent her to her father’s house, later on a meeting of reconciliation was held at the residence of the complainant but it failed, and as the accused persons were leaving the house of the complainant, he tried to resist them along with the other inmate of the house, respondent No.1 with the intention to kill her inflicted a stick blow on her head for dowry.
  2. Upon examination of the complainant the learned Judge of the Nari-O-Shishu Nirjatan Tribunal sent the matter to the Head Master, Hajikhali Secondary School, for enquiry, who submitted report on 07.04.2010 stating that he found substance in the allegations and on the basis of the report the learned Judge of the Tribunal took cognizance against respondent Nos.1-4 hereinafter referred to as the respondents on 25.04.2010 under section 11(Ga) and 30 of the Ain.
  3. After issuance of the process the respondents appeared before the Tribunal on 22.06.2010 and they were enlarged on bail.
  4. Thereafter, the respondents as petitioners filed a petition under section 561A of the Code of Criminal Procedure before the High Court Division for quashment of the proceeding of the Nari-O-Shishu Case NO.94 of 2010 under section 11(Ga)/30 of the Ain and obtained the Rule. The petition was numbered as Miscellaneous Case No.2502 of 2010. The complainant petitioner as opposite party contested the miscellaneous case by filing a counter affidavit denying all the statements made by the respondents.
  5. After hearing the miscellaneous case, the High Court Division by its judgment and order dated 20.07.2011, made the Rule absolute and quashed the proceedings on the finding that the allegations made in the petition of complaint were absurd, improbable and the case had been instituted only to harass the respondents and the impugned proceedings was nothing but an abuse of the process of the Court. Against the said judgment and order of the High Court Division the complainant has filed the instant Criminal Petition for Leave to Appeal. 
  6. We have heard Mr. Pankaj Kumar Kundu, the learned Advocate for the complainant and Mr. Zainul Abedin, learned Advocate-on-record who entered caveat on behalf of the respondents.
  7. Mr. Pankaj Kumar Kundu submitted that upon plain reading of the petition of complaint it was clear that there was specific allegation against the respondents in respect of an offence under section 11(Ga) of the Ain and an inquiry made by a Head Master of the school the allegations were found true and upon receipt of the inquiry report the learned Judge of the Tribunal took cognizance of the case against the respondents. In such context without taking evidence it was not proper to say that the case was false and not believable and the impugned proceedings could not be said to be an abuse of the process of the Court and as such, the impugned judgment and order passed by the High Court Division is liable to be interfered by giving leave.
  8. It appears that in the petition of complaint the complainant alleged that her husband and others (the respondents) went to the residence of her father and thereafter tortured her there demanding dowry which the High Court found not believable at all. Upon perusal of the allegations we find that the High Court Division has rightly observed that allegations were absurd and improbable and the case should not be allowed to continue on the basis of such allegations. It also appears that the High Court Division considered that respondent No.1 instituted civil suit for cancellation of the Kabinanma on 15.01.2010 before the filing of the petition of complaint and after that the instant complaint was filed, which also created a doubt about the truth of the complaint case. Considering all these things the High Court Division came to the finding that if they allow to continue the further proceeding of the instant case, it would be an illegal harassment of the respondents and invoking its inherent jurisdiction under section 561A of the Code of Criminal Procedure quashed the proceedings to secure ends of justice.
  9. It further appears that beside, the above, there is another point which has not been considered by the High Court Division. As stated earlier the Nari-O-Shishu Nirjatan Daman Tribunal, Patuakhali upon receipt of the petition of complaint sent the same for inquiry to the Head Master Hajikhali High School and the Head Master in his inquiry report dated 07.04.2010 stated only that-

  1. From the above, it is clear that if a petition of complaint is filed, the Tribunal is to see whether any affidavit has been filed stating the facts that the complainant requested one police officer as mentioned in sub-section(1) or empowered person to receive the complaint and failed and if it is found that the affidavit has been filed stating that fact, then the Tribunal will examine the complainant and if satisfied will send the petition of complaint for enquiry to any Magistrate or any other person and the concerned person shall within 7(seven) days submit a report to the Tribunal. If the Tribunal is not satisfied then he will dismiss the complaint. In this case the requirement of law so far this part is concerned has been complied with. But Clause second (Kha) of sub-section 1 (Kha) states that upon receipt of the report under sub-section 1(Ka), if the Tribunal finds that there was no proof that the complainant failed upon requesting one police officer under sub-section (1) or the empowered person to receive the complaint and that there was no prima-facie evidence in support of the allegation made in the petition of complaint in that case, the Tribunal shall dismiss the complaint. In the instant case we find that the enquiry report as mentioned above does not contain any proof that the complainant requested the police officer or the empowered person to receive the complaint and failed as such, it is clear that there is no proof in support of this part of the requirement of law and as such, the Tribunal ought to have dismissed the complaint, but it without doing so took cognizance on the plea that there were prima-facie materials in support of the allegation against the accused, but that was not sufficient. The Tribunal has to be satisfied that proof of both the requirements were available in the report. Thus taking cognizance of the offence in the instant case was illegal and although this point was not considered by the High Court Division, we are of the view that on this count also the proceedings was also liable to be quashed. Sub-section 1(Ga), of the Ain has given power to the Tribunal to take cognizance of the offence on some other grounds in case no recommendation for taking cognizance is made in the enquiry report, but it has not given any authority to the Tribunal to take cognizance of an offence if the fact of going to the police officer to lodge the complaint is not stated in the report.
  2. In view of the above, we find no illegality in the impugned judgment and order passed by the High Court Division. As such, the petition has got no merit.           
Accordingly, this Criminal Petition for Leave to Appeal is dismissed.
