Limitation Act (IX of 1908)

Section 142

In a suit for declaration of title and recovery of khash possession, the plaintiffs in addition to proving their title to the suit land must prove that the suit has been filed within 12 years from the alleged date of dispossession. In the case in hand, the plaintiffs miserably failed to prove that the suit has been filed within 12 years from the alleged date of dispossession.                        … (23)

Ahmed Miaji and others Vs. Eakub Ali Munshi and others (1960) 12 DLR 708; Syed Moin Ahmed Vs. Khondkar Mohiuddin (1967) 19 DLR and Amanatullah and others Vs. Ali Mohammad Bhuiyan and another, (1997) 2 BLC (AD) 134 ref.

For the Appellants: Mr. Mahbubey Alam, Attorney General, instructed by Mr. Haridas Paul, Advocate-on-Record.

For the Respondents: Mr. A. M. Aminuddin, Senior Advocate, instructed by Mr. Syed Mahbubur Rahman, Advocate-on-Record.

JUDGMENT

Syed Mahmud Hossain, J: This appeal by leave is directed against the judgment and order dated 08.06.2011 passed by a Single Bench of the High Court Division in Civil Revision No.3070 of 2010 discharging the Rule and affirming the judgment and decree dated 05.04.2010 passed by the learned Additional District Judge, Sixth Court, Dhaka in Title Appeal No.07 of 2009 allowing the appeal and  reversing the judgment and decree dated 28.06.2007 passed by the learned Joint District Judge, Additional Court, Dhaka in Title Suit No.234 of 2003 dismissing the same.

2.            The facts, leading to the filing of this appeal, in brief, are :

         Al-haj Md. Serajuddin as the plaintiff filed Title Suit No.(number not stated) of 1998 renumbered as Title Suit No.234 of 2003 in the Additional Court of Joint District Judge, Dhaka praying for declaration of title and recovery of khash possession on removing the construction standing on the suit property. His case, in short, is that he purchased the land of ‘Ka’ schedule to the plaint with two storied building, garage, different plants situated on S.A. Dag No.10 of Mouza-Kakrail under previous Lalbagh Police Station now Ramna within Dhaka Collectorate from Syed Farooq Mirja, son of Syed Fakrul Hasan Mirja in 1971. After purchase, the plaintiff got his name mutated and had been paying taxes regularly. While the plaintiff was abroad, the ‘Ka’ schedule was wrongly declared abandoned property. Having come to know of the aforesaid fact, the plaintiff filed an application to the Government of Bangladesh and the Government having been satisfied released the suit property in favour of the plaintiff and issued memo dated 19.06.1983 from the office of defendant No.3 but defendant No.1 did not return the land shown in schedule ‘Kha’ to the plaint. Consequently, the plaintiff filed Title Suit No.53 of 1988 in the Court of Assistant Judge, Fourth Court, Dhaka, which was decreed on 17.12.1988. After that, the plaintiff requested defendant Nos.1 and 2 to deliver ‘Kha’ schedule land to him on a number of dates from 26.03.1989 up to 03.05.1997 and lastly, on 08.07.1997 but defendant No.1 did not vacate or deliver ‘Kha’ schedule land to the plaintiff. As such the plaintiff issued legal notice upon defendant No.1 on 01.09.1997. But defendant No.1 did not pay any heed to the said request made by the plaintiff and consequently, the suit was filed. Along with plaint, the plaintiff filed sketch map showing position of the garage. During pendency of the suit, the sole-plaintiff died and his heirs were substituted as the plaintiffs.       

3.            Defendant Nos.1 and 3 by filing written statements denying all the material statements made in the plaint. It appears that at first defendant No.3 filed written statement on 30.03.2000 and subsequently on 02.05.2000. In the first written statement, it has been written by defendant No.3 that the suit land was allotted to defendant No.1, vide memo No.2FV-123/72/2103 dated 09.12.1974 at a rental of Tk.1,000/- per month and later on, vide memo No.Sef-V/1M/D7/70 (PT)/402 dated 03.08.1984 to the Ministry of Home Affairs for using the same for the police department. Since then police department had been enjoying the suit schedule-B land. It is further stated that Title Suit No.53 of 1988 was decreed against defendant No.3 on contest and ex parte against defendant Nos.1 and 2. The aforesaid suit was filed by a person who had no right, title, interest and possession in the suit land. Summons of the aforesaid suit was not served upon defendant Nos.1 and 2. The garage mentioned in the instant suit is unspecified and as such, Civil Court Commissioner is required to be appointed to identify the location of the garage. It is further stated that defendant No.1 has been possessing  the property for more than 20 years and as such acquired title by adverse possession. In the second written statement, the Government stated that the suit land had been vested in the Government as per provision of P.O. (President’s Order) No.16 of 1972 as abandoned property by gazette notification No.9762(8) at serial No.10 according to the provision of Ordinance LIV of 1985 on 23.09.1986 and it was allotted to defendant Nos.1 and 2 and they have been possessing the suit schedule-B property.

4.            The trial Court upon hearing the parties by its judgment and order dated 28.06.2007 dismissed the suit.   

5.            Against the judgment and decree passed by the trial Court, the plaintiffs preferred Title Appeal No.07 of 2009 before the learned District Judge, Dhaka. On transfer, the appeal was heard and disposed of by the then learned Additional District Judge, Sixth Court, Dhaka, who by his judgment and order dated 05.04.2010 allowed the appeal reversing the judgment and decree of the trial Court. 

6.            Being aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the judgment and decree passed by the appellate Court, the defendants filed a revisional application before the High Court Division and obtained Rule in Civil Revision No.3070 of 2010. The learned Single Judge of the High Court Division by the judgment and order dated 08.06.2011 discharged the Rule with direction affirming the judgment and decree of the appellate Court.

7.            Feeling aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the judgment and order passed by the High Court Division, the defendant-leave-petitioners have filed Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No.1887 2011 before this Division, in which, leave was granted on 15.09.2014 resulting in Civil Appeal No.230 of 2014.

8.            Mr. Mahbubey Alam, learned Attorney General, appearing on behalf of the leave appellants, submits that the plaintiffs in the plaint alleged that they had been dispossessed in 1974 and Title Suit No.(number not stated) of 1998 renumbered as Title Suit No.234 of 2003 having been filed on 19.02.1998 beyond the statutory period of limitation for recovery of possession, the plaintiffs are not entitled to a decree for recovery of possession in the instant suit. He also submits that the plaintiffs filed Title Suit No. 52 of 1988 for permanent injunction against the appellants and that decree was passed restraining the appellants from changing the nature and character of the suit land and that the plaintiff of that suit had not asserted his possession in the suit land (‘Kha’ schedule land to the plaint) and as such, the decree passed in Title Suit No.52 of 1988 is of no avail to the plaintiffs and even that suit for permanent injunction was also filed beyond the statutory period of limitation of 12 years from his dispossession in 1974.

9.            Mr. A. M. Aminuddin, learned Senior Advocate, appearing on behalf of the respondents, on the other hand, submits that the suit has been filed within the statutory period of limitation and as such, the High Court Division was justified in discharging the Rule.

10.        We have considered the submission of the learned Attorney General for the appellants and the learned Senior Advocate for the respondents, perused the impugned judgment and the materials on record.

11.        Before entering into the merit of the appeal, it would be pertinent to quote the grounds, for which, leave was granted as under:

“The land purchased by the plaintiffs from S.A. Plot No.10 having been recorded in R.S. Plot No.932, the Court of appeal below failed to take into consideration that the garage claimed by the plaintiffs is not within their R.S. Plot No.932; rather the same is within R.S. Plot No.933 which is an abandoned property and under the occupation of Police Department and the High Court Division committed an error in upholding the decision of the Court of appeal below.

The subject matter of the instant suit being a garage and whether the same is within the purchased land of the plaintiffs or beyond their purchased land being the subject matter of local investigation with reference to their title deeds and in the instant case the plaintiff having not taken any initiative for local investigation, the Court of appeal below illegally decreed the suit and the High Court Division committed error of law in upholding the said decree.

The plaintiff alleged that they have been dispossessed in the year 1974 and they having filed an earlier suit being Title Suit No.52 of 1988 and in the said suit they having not prayed for recovery of possession from the defendant, the plaintiff is not entitled to a decree for recovery of possession in the instant suit in respect of which they have allegedly been dispossessed in the year 1974.”

12.        It transpired from the plaint that the original plaintiff, Al-haj Md. Serajuddin purchased the suit land under Holding No.10, New Baily Road in 1971. After the death of the original plaintiff his heirs were substituted in his place as the plaintiffs. They claimed that the suit land is part and parcel of Holding No.10, New Baily Road and not Holding No.10/A, New Baily Road. The plaintiffs alleged that the defendants dispossessed them from the suit land treating it as an abandoned property.

13.        Admittedly, the plaintiffs filed Title Suit No.53 of 1988 for permanent injunction against (1). Superintendent of Police, Special Branch, 10/A, New Baily Road, Police Station-Ramna, Dhaka, (2) Deputy Inspector General, Special Branch, 10/A, New Baily Road, Police Station-Ramna, Dhaka and (3). Government of Bangladesh, represented by the Secretary, Ministry of Public Works. The plaint of Title Suit No.53 of 1988 was not exhibited. From judgment delivered in Title Suit No.53 of 1988, we find that the learned Assistant Judge, Fourth Court, Dhaka, while describing the case of the plaintiff stated, amongst others, as under:

14.        From the aforesaid averment of the plaint, it appears that even in 1978 the sole plaintiff Al-haj Serajuddin was out of possession of the suit land prior to filing of Title Suit No.53 of 1988 for permanent injunction.

15.        From the judgment of Title Suit No.53 of 1988, we find that the Government of Bangladesh, represented by the Secretary, Ministry of Public Works by filing written statement asserted that the disputed ’Kha’ schedule land is part and parcel of Holding No.10/A, of New Baily Road. Defendant No.3 further asserted that defendant Nos.1 and 2 had been in possession of the suit land (‘Kha’ schedule land). Having gone through the judgment delivered in Title Suit No.53 of 1988, we find that the learned Assistant Judge, Fourth Court, Dhaka, restrained the defendants of Title Suit No.53 of 1988 from changing the nature and character of ‘Kha’ schedule land to the plaint by a decree of permanent injunction. From the judgment, it appears that the plaintiff himself admitted that he was out of possession of ‘Kha’ schedule land when the suit for permanent injunction was filed.

16.        Subsequently, the plaintiff filed present Title Suit No.234 of 2003. The High Court Division having considered the documentary evidence came to a finding that the plaintiff acquired title from the original owner of the suit property who was a non-Bengali. The High Court Division also found that the defendants had no title to the suit land and that they were restrained by an order of permanent injunction for making construction or renovation in the suit land. The High Court Division also found that defendant Nos.1 and 2 had been possessing the suit land on the strength of their possession in the garage defying the decree of the Court passed in Title Suit No.53 of 1988.

17.        We have already noticed that even in the plaint of Title Suit No.53 of 1988 for permanent injunction, the  plaintiff Al-haj Md. Serajuddin, the predecessor-in-interest of the plaintiff-respondents of Title Suit No.234 of 2003, stated that he was out of possession of the ‘Kha’ schedule land. Subsequently, the present suit was filed for declaration of title and recovery of possession.

18.        P.W.1, Md. Sharifuddin, in his evidence stated that within their boundary the Special Branch had been in possession. He further stated that they (defendants) took possession in 1974 and that subsequently his father filed the suit.

19.        Having gone through the record, the judgment delivered in Title Suit No.53 of 1988 and the evidence of P.W.1 given in Title Suit No.234 of 2003, we find that the plaintiff had been out of possession of the suit land (‘Kha’ schedule land) according to their admission from 1974. The present suit for declaration of title and recovery of khash possession was filed in 1998. Therefore, the suit is hopelessly barred by limitation. The plaintiff was required to file the suit for declaration of title and recovery of possession within 12 years from the alleged date of dispossession but they have been failed to do so and as such, they are not entitled to get a decree for recovery of possession.

20.        In the case of Ahmed Miaji and others vs. Eakub Ali Munshi and others (1960)12 DLR 708 it has been held that where the plaintiffs have set up a case of possession followed by dispossession, they must prove possession within 12 years of the suit as contemplated under Article 142 of the Limitation Act.

21.        In the case of Syed Moin Ahmed vs. Khondkar Mohiuddin (1967) 19 DLR 912, it has been held that under Article 142  of the limitation Act, it is the duty of the plaintiff first to prove to the satisfaction of the Court that he was in possession of the suit premises within 12 years prior to the filing of the suit and if he fails to do so he cannot get decree for declaration of title and recovery of possession. 

22.        Reliance may be placed on the case of Amanatullah and others vs. Ali Mohammad Bhuiyan and another (1997)2 BLC (AD)134 where it has been held as under:

“The last but not the least point that has been noticed by this Court at the time of hearing of the appeal is that there is no averment in the plaint of plaintiffs’ dispossession from the suit land within twelve years of bringing the suit which is for recovery of khas possession. Notwithstanding this, both the trial Court and the lower appellate Court concurrently found that the plaintiffs had never been in possession of the suit land. In such circumstances, the suit was not maintainable. On this ground alone the plaintiffs’ suit was liable for dismissal.”

23.        On consideration of the cases referred to above, it appears that in a suit for declaration of title and recovery of khash possession, the plaintiffs in addition to proving their title to the suit land must prove that the suit has been filed within 12 years from the alleged date of dispossession. In the case in hand, the plaintiffs miserably failed to prove that the suit has been filed within 12 years from the alleged date of dispossession.

24.        In the light of the findings, we are of the view that the High Court Division erroneously decreed the suit for declaration of title and recovery of khash possession.

         Accordingly, this appeal is allowed and the impugned judgment delivered by the High Court Division is set aside.
