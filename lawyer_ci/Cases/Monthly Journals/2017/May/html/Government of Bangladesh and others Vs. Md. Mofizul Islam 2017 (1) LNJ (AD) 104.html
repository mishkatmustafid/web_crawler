Police Officers (Special Provisions) Ordinance (LXXXIV of 1976)

 

Section 6

 

There is no requirement of allowing the delinquent police officer to cross examine the witnesses in an inquiry. Only if he desires then he should get a chance of hearing in person and in this case admittedly he was present before the inquiry authority and chance of hearing was given. We have no hesitation to find that the enquiry proceeding was a legal one and as such, the punishment imposed upon the respondent was also legal one and there was nothing to interfere with the order of punishment. JUDGMENT

 

Md. Nizamul huq, j. This appeal, by leave, is from the decision dated 24.02.2008 passed by the Administrative Appellate Tribunal, Khulna in Administrative Appellate Tribunal Appeal No.6 of 2008 dismissing the appeal and thereby affirming the order dated 23.10.2007 passed by the Administrative Tribunal, Khulna in Administrative Tribunal Case No.23 of 2007 allowing the case of the respondent with all arrear salaries and other allowances with the further direction to reinstate him in his service.

 

2.            The facts, leading to the filing of this appeal, in a nutshell, are:

 

         The respondent, while had been serving as Sub-Inspector of Police at Satkhira District, the Additional Superintendent of Police held a preliminary enquiry against him on the basis of petition filed by one Ashad Ali through the Assistant Superintendent of Police, Satkhira. On the basis of that enquiry the respondent was suspended and the departmental proceeding was started against him and after completion of the departmental proceedings, he was retired from service compulsorily by the order dated 08.04.2007. Against the order the respondent preferred departmental appeal before the appellate authority and it was rejected on 14.05.2007. Then the respondent filed Administrative Tribunal Case No.23 of 2007 before the Administrative Tribunal, Khulna for setting aside the order of his compulsory retirement.

 

         The appellants contested the case by filing written statement contending, inter-alia, that the departmental enquiry against the respondent was held according to law and since he was proved to be a corrupt officer, he was rightly given compulsory retirement from service according to the Police Officers(special provision) Ordinance-1976, shortly the Ordinance, 1976.

 

         The Administrative Tribunal, Khulna, upon hearing both the sides by its decision dated 23.10.2008 allowed the case declaring that the order dated 08.04.2007, compulsory retiring the respondent from service was illegal, inoperative and not binding upon the respondent. It was further declared that the respondent was still in service and the appellants were directed to pay him the due salaries and other benefits and also to reinstate him in service. 

 

         Being aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the decision dated 23.10.2007 passed by the  Administrative Tribunal, Khulna, the appellants herein preferred Administrative Appellate Tribunal Appeal No.06 of 2008 before the Administrative Appellate Tribunal, Khulna. The Administrative Appellate Tribunal, upon hearing the parties, by its decision dated 23.10.2007 dismissed the appeal and affirmed the decision passed by the Administrative Tribunal, Khulna.  

 

         Feeling aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the decision passed by the Administrative Appellate Tribunal Khulna, the appellants filed civil petition for leave to appeal No.1398 of 2008 before this Division and leave was granted to consider the submission that both the Tribunals failed to consider that there was no legal bar in proceeding against the respondent “on allegation and charges”.

5.            On the other hand, Mr. Abdul Wadud Bhuiyan, learned Advocate for the respondent has supported the decision of the Tribunals and has prayed for dismissal of the appeal. 

 

6.            We have considered the submissions of the learned Deputy Attorney General and the learned Advocate for the respondent and perused the impugned decision and the other materials on record.

 

7.            It appears that the Administrative Tribunal declared the order of compulsory retirement of the respondent relying on the decision given in the case of Government of Bangladesh represented by the Secretary, Ministry of Home Affairs and others versus Syed Ali Imam decided by the Administrative Appellate Tribunal in Administrative Appellate Tribunal Appeal No.84 of 1998 wherein it was held as under:

 

   “The proceeding case record shows that the said preliminary enquiry was held by Reserve Inspector Mr. Abdul Aziz who examined the aforesaid witnesses and submitted report accordingly. Use of such preliminary report and ex-parte statements of witnesses behind the back of the petitioner is against the cardinal principle of natural justice. Holding him guilty of the charge on the basis of such preliminary evidence in an informal inquiry and punishing him accordingly in a formal proceeding has been quite illegal.”

 

8.            From the judgment of the Appellate Tribunal it appear that it simply endorsed the view of the Administrative Tribunal without applying its our independent mind and considering the provision of the Ordinance, 1976.

 

9.            Upon consideration of the petition filed before the Administrative Tribunal by the respondent and upon consideration of section-6 of the Ordinance, we are satisfied that the authority followed the inquiry procedure as laid down in the said section. There is no requirement of allowing the delinquent police officer to cross examine the witnesses in an inquiry. Only if he desires then he should get a chance of hearing in person and in this case admittedly he was present before the inquiry authority and chance of hearing was given. We have no hesitation to find that the enquiry proceeding was a legal one and as such, the punishment imposed upon the respondent was also legal one and there was nothing to interfere with the order of punishment. So the decisions made by the Tribunals below are not sustainable and are liable to be set aside.

 

10.        Admittedly the respondent was a Sub-inspector of Police to the Provisions of Ordinance, 1976 were applicable to him. And in section 6 of the Ordinance, it has been laid down as to how a police officer shall be proceeded against for the commission of any of the offices mentioned in section 4 thereof.

 

11.        In the result, the appeal is allowed. The order of compulsory retirement imposed upon the respondent is hereby affirmed.

 

    There will be no order as to cost.
