Code of Civil Procedure (V of 1908)

Order XXIII, Rule 3

The High Court Division observed that the petitioner and opposite party No. 24 (Md. Abdul Jalil Baki) through entering solenama divided some portion of the suit land amongst themselves in an ex parte decree wherein 23 other co-sharers of the property were left out. The High Court Division observed that the appellate Court rightly pointed out that the 23 appellants had been deprived of their legal right to get the property partitioned in accordance with law. When any number of parties to a suit reach a compromise in the absence of other parties, clearly those, who are excluded from the compromise, are deprived of their opportunity to put forward any claim that they may have in the suit land. The impugned judgement does not suffer from any illegality or impropriety and does not call for any interference by this Division.                    . . . (12 to 14)

JUDGMENT

Muhammad Imman Ali, J: This civil petition for leave to appeal is directed against the judgement and order dated 13.05.2012 passed by a Division Bench of the High Court Division in Civil Revision No.542 of 2010 discharging the Rule.

2.      The facts, relevant for disposal of the instant civil petition for leave to appeal, are that Abdul Jalil Baki respondent No.24 herein as plaintiff instituted Other Suit No.138 of 2008 in the Court of Joint District Judge, First Court, Bogra, for setting aside the preliminary decree dated 24.08.2006 and final decree dated 24.11.2007 passed in Partition Suit No. 2 of 2004. He stated, inter alia, that Mst. Rezia and Motiar Rahman defendant Nos.48 and 49 respectively mortgaged the suit property to Rupali Bank, and since they failed to repay the loan, Rupali Bank filed Artha Rin Case No.129 of 2000 and the suit was decreed and, thereafter, Money Execution Case No.233 of 2007 was filed and in that execution case the property was auction sold and the plaintiff purchased 1.27 acres of land out of 1.53 acres of Plot No.28 and got possession of the case land through Court. The further case of the plaintiff is that Amir Hossain defendant No. 1 by manufacturing some false papers claimed that he purchased land of Plot No.28 from Azizur Rahman (defendant No. 47) his predecessor and, thereafter, claiming as a co-sharer he filed Partition Suit No.2 of 2004, and by showing false service got an ex parte decree on 24.8.2006 and on the basis of such false decree obtained final decree on 28.10.2007. When the defendant went to take possession, the plaintiff learnt about the partition decree and then filed the instant suit. 

3.      The petitioner herein as defendant contested the suit by filing written statement and denied the claim of the plaintiff. He contended, inter alia, that the suit property belonged to Mrs. Hameda Mohammad Ali, wife of late Mohammad Ali, Ex-Prime Minister of Pakistan and after her death her son Hatem Ali Chowdhury got the property through a family arrangement and sold the same to the petitioner. The petitioner after purchasing the suit property took possession of the land through Court and established a Madrasha, which is known as Abdul Jabbar Madrasha with a Hostel built therein for the students. He also planted 250 saplings. When the petitioner learnt about the Partition Suit No.55 of 2000 in respect of the suit property, he got himself added as a defendant and filed written statement. Thereafter, the plaintiff did not proceed with the suit and the same was dismissed for default. Then the petitioner filed Partition Suit No.2 of 2004 to demarcate his purchased land, but as the defendant did not contest the suit, the said suit was decreed ex parte and the final decree was passed on 28.11.2007. He filed Partition Execution Case No.6 of 2008 and by an order of the Court dated 30.08.2008 an Advocate Commissioner was appointed. When they went to the suit land the plaintiff did not allow them to enter into the land, so the Advocate Commissioner with the help of police gave possession of the suit land to the petitioner. Thereafter, the petitioner and Abdul Jalil Baki in order to resolve the dispute about their interest in the suit land filed a compromise petition. The Joint District Judge allowed the compromise petition and the suit was disposed of as per terms of the solenama.

4.      The petitioner claimed that opposite party Nos. 1-23 in the civil revision before the High Court Division (respondent Nos. 1-23 herein) were defendant Nos. 23-45 in the said suit, but did not contest the suit nor filed any written statement.  The compromise petition between the petitioner and opposite party No.24 Abdul Jalil Baki related to Plot No.28, which comprised 1.53 acres of land and the petitioner claimed 78 decimals out of it and the respondents claimed rest of the land in Plot No.28. He stated that respondent Nos.1-23 had no interest in that plot. They have not been affected by the compromise and, if they are aggrieved, they could file a partition suit to state their claim, if there is any, in the suit land.

5.      Respondent Nos.1-23 by filing counter affidavit before the High Court Division stated that the compromise petition filed by Mohammad Amir Hussain, the petitioner with Md. Abdul Jalil Baki, opposite party No. 24 in the civil revision, (respondent No. 24 herein) was illegal and could not be entertained. The joint District Judge, 1st Court, Bogra committed an error of law in accepting the compromise petition overlooking the nature of the suit and declaring the final decree dated 24.08.2006 in Partition Suit No.02 of 2004, which was collusive, null and void and not acted upon and, therefore, confers no title and interest upon the suit land and prayed for setting aside the said decree dated 24.08.2006 passed in Partition Suit No.02 of 2004.   

6.      Respondent Nos. 1-23 (defendant Nos.23-45 of the Partition Suit No.02 of 2004), claimed that they did not receive any notice of the Partition Suit No.02 of 2004, in the Court of Joint District Judge, 1st Court, Bogra and the decree was obtained by the plaintiff by practicing fraud upon the Court, and as such, Other Suit No.138 of 2008 is required to be proceeded up to its finality to look into the matter whether the ex-parte decree passed in Partition Suit No.02 of 2004 was correct. They claimed to have right, title, interest and possession in the suit land and they are seriously prejudiced by the ex-parte decree passed on 24.08.2006 and had they been aware of the filing of Partition Suit No.02 of 2004 they would have contested the suit claiming their saham. They further claimed that the judgement dated 20.01.2010 passed in Other Class Appeal No. 147 of 2009 allowing the appeal was correct in setting aside the order dated 24.05.2009 in Other Class Suit No. 138 of 2008, however, the appellate Court in Other Class Appeal No. 147 of 2009 failed to notice the nature of the suit and the prayer of Other Class Suit No. 138 of 2008, wherein the Court decreed the suit in terms of the final decree dated 24.08.2006 in Partition Suit No. 02 of 2004, which was illegal, collusive and null and void and bore no right, title and interest for Abdul Jalil Baki in schedule Nos. 1 and 2 of the plaint.

7.      After hearing the parties and considering the evidence and materials on record, the Joint District Judge, First Court, Bogra, allowed the compromise petition and the suit was thus disposed of as per terms of the solenama by the judgement and decree dated 24.05.2009.

8.      Being aggrieved, the defendant Nos.1-23 preferred Title Appeal No.147 of 2009 before the District Judge, Bogra and the same was heard by the 1st Additional District Judge, Bogra, who by his judgement and decree dated 20.01.2010 allowed the appeal. Then the plaintiff preferred Civil Revision No.542 of 2010 before the High Court Division and obtained Rule, which upon hearing the parties was discharged. Hence, the plaintiff is now before us having filed the civil petition for leave to appeal.    

9.      Mr. Md. Abdur Rezzaque Miah, learned Advocate appearing on behalf of the petitioner submitted that the High Court Division did not apply its mind in evaluating the facts and materials on record since there is proof available in the record of the case that the defendants of Other Class Suit No. 138 of 2008 received notice of the suit in time, but they did not contest and claim their saham in the suit land. He also submitted that the High Court Division failed to appreciate that the decree passed in Other Class Suit No. 138 of 2008 has been validly executed by the petitioner and respondent No. 24 to settle the dispute in respect of land of plot no. 28 where present respondent Nos. 1 to 23 had no interest and the Joint District Judge, First Court, Bogra, rightly determined the suit based on the solenama and declaring the terms of the solenama as part of the decree and hence, the impugned judgement is liable to be set aside. He further submitted that the High Court Division failed to appreciate that admittedly defendant Nos. 48 and 49 of the original suit mortgaged the suit property with Rupali Bank and when they failed to repay the loan, the bank filed Artha Rin Case No. 129 of 2000 and the suit was decreed. Thereafter, the bank filed Money Execution Case No. 233 of 2007 and in due course the property was sold in auction and respondent No. 24 purchased 75 decimals of land of plot No. 28 and got possession thereof through Court, it has been clearly described in the solenama, and hence, the High Court Division erred in discharging the Rule on the ground that 23 co-sharers of the property were left out of the solenama.  

10.  Mr. Munsur Habib, learned Advocate appearing for respondent Nos.1-23 made submissions in support of the impugned judgement and order of the High Court Division. 

11.  We find from the judgement of the appellate Court, being the last court of fact that out of the 7.24 acres of land detailed in the schedule, there was no apportionment of the property in respect of 51 defendants and that the plaint and written statement did not resolve the controversy between the parties entirely the solenama was signed by the plaintiff Md. Abdul Jalil Baki and Md. Amir Hossain on behalf of the defendants. These two persons are not the sole owners of the property in the jote. It was observed by the appellate Court that the 23 appellants, who are defendants in the suit, were not party to the solenama.

12.  The High Court Division observed that the petitioner and opposite party No. 24 (Md. Abdul Jalil Baki) through entering solenama divided some portion of the suit land amongst be clouded. Whatever, title the decree holder bank got in respect of the mortgaged property is subject to the right, title and interest of the judgment debtors had in the themselves in an ex parte decree wherein 23 other co-sharers of the property were left out. The High Court Division observed that the appellate Court rightly pointed out that the 23 appellants had been deprived of their legal right to get the property partitioned in accordance with law.

13.  When any number of parties to a suit reach a compromise in the absence of other parties, clearly those, who are excluded from the compromise, are deprived of their opportunity to put forward any claim that they may have in the suit land.

14.  In the facts and circumstances discussed above, we are of the view that the impugned judgement does not suffer from any illegality or impropriety and does not call for any interference by this Division.

         Accordingly, the civil petition for leave to appeal is dismissed.
