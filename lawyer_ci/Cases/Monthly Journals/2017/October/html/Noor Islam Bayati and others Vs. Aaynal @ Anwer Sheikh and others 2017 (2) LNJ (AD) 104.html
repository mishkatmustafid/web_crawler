JUDGMENT

Md. Abdul Wahhab Miah. J: Delay of 233 days in filing the petition is condoned.

2.          This petition for leave to appeal has been filed by the plaintiffs against the judgment and order dated 11.08.2015 passed by a Single Bench of the High Court Division in Civil Revision No. 2170 of 2013 making the Rule absolute.

3.          Short facts necessary to dispose this petition are that the predecessor-in-interest of petitioner Nos.1(a)-1(j) and the other petitioners i.e. petitioner Nos.2-12 as the plaintiffs filed partition Suit No.45 of 2005 in the Court of Assistant Judge, Munshigonj for declaration of title and for partition claiming saham to the extent of “10.0 decimals land.”

4.          The suit was contested by the predecessor-in-interest of defendant Nos.1(a)-1(d) and 2-5 by filing written statement.

5.          At the trial, both the parties produced evidence, oral and documentary.

6.          The trial Court by its judgment and decree dated 30.10.2008 dismissed the suit. Being aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the judgment and decree of the trial Court, the plaintiffs filed Title Appeal No. 124 of 2008 before the District Judge, Munshigonj. The learned Joint District Judge, Second Court, Munshigonj by the judgment and decree dated 27.05.2013 allowed the appeal and decreed the suit giving saham to the plaintiff as prayed for. Against the judgment and decree of the Appellate Court, the defendants preferred the above mentioned civil revision before the High Court Division. It appears that before the High Court Division an application was filed on behalf of the defendants for holding local investigation to ascertain the possession of the parties. The High Court Division made the Rule absolute and sent the suit back to the trial Court to ascertain the possession of the respective party in the suit land; hence this petition for leave to appeal.

7.          Heard Mr. Zakir Hossain, learned Advocate for the petitioners and Mr. Farid Ahmed, learned Advocate who entered caveat on behalf of defendant-respondent Nos.l(a)-l(d), 2, 4-7.

8.          It is a common knowledge that, in a suit for partition the Court decides the share of the parties to the suit. It is also a settled legal position that in a suit for partition, the defendants are also in the category of plaintiff and they can also pray for share. It is also a settled legal position that the suit for partition is decreed in preliminary form and the Court after giving saham to the plaintiff or the other party(s) direct them to partition the suit land amicably and in case the parties fail to partition the suit land amicably as per the saham granted by the Court, a survey knowing Advocate is appointed as advocate commissioner to partition the suit land as per the preliminary decree and the Commissioner while giving saham also takes in to consideration the possession of the respective party. Therefore, we do not see any scope and necessity to appoint any Advocate Commissioner to ascertain the possession of either of the party. Further possession is a question of fact and that has to be proved by producing evidence and that cannot be decided by holding local investigation. Be that as it may since the High Court Division did not dispose of the revision on merit discussing the evidence on record we find no other alternative hut to send the revision back to the High Court Division for hearing afresh and for disposal on merit in accordance with law on the evidence on record. Accordingly, this petition is disposed of in the following terms;

The impugned judgment and order of the High Court Division is set aside. The revision is sent back to the High Court Division for hearing afresh and for disposal on merit in accordance with law discussing the evidence on record. In no case, the High Court Division shall send the revision back to either of the Court below. The revision shall be heard by a Single Bench other than the author Judge of the impugned judgment and order.

9.          If so advised, the parties may mention the revision before the competent Bench for early hearing, and if mentioned, the concerned Bench shall hear the same on priority basis.
