JUDGMENT

Syed Muhammad Dastagir Hussain, J: This appeal is directed against the judgment and order of conviction and sentence dated 3.4.2008 passed by the Special Judge, Special Court No. 5, Dhaka in Special Case No. 17 of 2007 arising out of Dhanmondi Police Station Case No. 37 dated 11.7.2007 corresponding to Metro Special Case No. 156 of 2007 and ACC G.R. No. 63 of 2007 convicting the accused appellant under section 26(2) of the Anti Corruption Commission Act, 2004 sentencing him to suffer 2 (two) years rigorous imprisonment and under section 27(1) of the Anti Corruption Commission Act, 2004 and sentencing him to suffer 10 (ten) years rigorous imprisonment and both the sentences to run concurrently and to pay a fine of Tk. 1,00,00,000/- in default to suffer rigorous imprisonment for 1 (one) year more and confiscating the wealth acquired by improper means to the State.

2.            The short fact relevant for disposal of this appeal is that the accused appellant was served with a notice dated 18.2.2007 under the signature of the their secretary of the said Commission directing to submit his wealth statement while he had been in custody as per provisions laid down under Rule 15Gha(1), 15(Gha)(2) of the Emergency Power Rules, 2007 together with section 18 and 26(1) of the Anti Corruption Commission Act, 2004 requiring the accused appellant to submit a statement of all immovable and movable properties owned and possessed in his name and in the name of his wife, son, daughter and other members of his family or any other name in his favour and the source of acquiring earning such property.

3.            The accused appellant in 15.2.2007 submitted wealth statement to Anti Corruption Commission through his wife advocate Sigma Huda wherein on enquiry, Anti Corruption Commission found that he had concealed wealth of Tk. 2,19,15,77.04 only.

4.            Anti Corruption Commission on enquiry has further found that as per Income Tax Returns upto 2006-2007 Tax year total net wealth of accused appellant Nazmul Huda stood at Tk. 87,92,212/- only. As per Income Tax Returns upto 2006-2007 Tax year total net wealth of appellants wife accused No. 2 Advocate Sigma Huda stood at Tk. 12,21,376/- only.

5.            As per Income Tax Return upto 2006-2007 Tax year their investment in Chancery Chamber stood at Tk. 1,15,000/- only. Anti Corruption Commission thus found that as per Income Tax Returns upto 2006-2007 Tax year total net wealth of appellant Barrister Nazmul Huda and his wife Advocate Sigma Huda comes at Tk. 1,01,28,588/- only but actually real value of their total properties stood at Tk. 9,04,17,077.90 only.

6.            Accused appellant Barrister Nazmul Huda opened income tax file of 1985-1986 Tax year while his wife Advocate Sigma Huda opened 1988-1989 Tax year. In that way their total savings, as shown in the income tax returns, stood at Tk. 1,01,28,588/- only and thereby Barrister Nazmul Huda himself and in the name of his wife and daughters had amassed  wealth of Tk. 8,02,88,489.90 only which is disproportionate to his legal and known source of income.

7.            On such allegations on 11.7.2007 Mr. Md. Talebur Rahman, Assistant Director, Anti Corruption Commission being informant lodged First Information Report with Dhanmondi Police Station which was registered and numbered as Dhanmondi Police Station Case No. 37 dated 11.7.2007 under sections 26(2) and 27(1) of the Anti Corruption Commission Act, 2004 and Rule 15Gha(5) of the Emergency Power Rules, 2007 and Section 109 of the Penal Code.

8.            The investigation officer received the case for investigation on 24.07.2007 vide Memo No. C-73-2007(Tadanta-2)/5238 dated 17.7.2007. Subsequently the investigating officer investigated the case and submitted charge sheet on 17.11.2007 after expiry of the specified time under sections 26(2) and 27(1) of the Anti Corruption Commission Act, 2004, 15(Gha)(5) of the Emergency Power Rules, 2007 and section 109 of the Penal Code. It was approved on 14.11.2007 by Mr. Habibur Rhaman, Commissioner, Anti Corruption Commission vide Memo No. 10807 against the accused appellant and his wife Advocate Sigma Huda. Then the case was transferred to the court of Metropolitan Senior Special Judge, Dhaka where it was numbered as Metro Special Case No. 156 of 2007. The Senior Special Judge transferred the case to the court of Special Judge No. 5 for trial where the case has been renumbered as Special Case No. 17 of 2007. The Court after hearing the parities on 9.1.2008 framed charge against the appellant under Section 26(2), 27(1) of the Anti Corruption Commission Act, 2004 and against his wife together with section 109 of the Penal Code.

9.            The Trial Court took evidence of 88 prosecution witnesses and accused appellant adduced 3 witnesses including himself and his wife the accused No. 2. The appellant has submitted a written statement under section 342 of the Code of Criminal Procedure and submitted cogent documentary evidence, placed certain rulings including those of the Supreme Court of India and a written argument in support of his case. The Special Judge, Court No. 5, Dhaka after conclusion of the trial by his judgment and order dated 3.4.2008 found the present and thereafter appellant guilty convicted under the said sections. Being aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the said judgment and order the appellant preferred this appeal under section 10 of the Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1958.

10.        Mr. Ahsanul Karim, the learned advocate appearing on behalf of the accused appellant submits that the Secretary of the Anti-Corruption Commission issued the order on 18.2.2007 under purported authority of section 26(1) and 18 of the Anti Corruption Commission Act, 2004 read with Rules, 15Gha(1) and 15Gha(2) of the Emergency Power Rules, 2007 directing him to submit his statement of assets. The secretary of the Anti Corruption Commission had no authority to issue any such order. Order under section 26(1) can only be exercised by the Commission. Commission means a Commission constituted under section 3 read with section 5 of the Anti Corruption Act. From 07.02.2007 to 25.02.2007 there was no Commission since all the commissioners tendered their resignation simultaneously. He submits that the president of the Republic by way of promulgating an Ordinance on 18.04.2007 inserted a clause being 18(2) after the existing clause 18 providing that any act done by any of the officer of the Commission during 7th February, 2007 and 24th February, 2007 may be given post facto approval provided however, such act exercised by the officers of the Commission is in consonance with the objective of the Act of 2004 and the functions of the Commission. The evidence of record speaks that the said commission never gave any post facto approval of the notice dated 18.02.2007. Therefore, there was never any post facto approval of the said notice. He further submitted that under section 18(2) of the said Act, only such of the acts of the Anti Corruption Commission could be given post facto approval which acts are consistent with the provision of this Act and are in consonance with the functions of the commission. The power under section 26(1) not being a power subject to delegation, therefore the notice issued by the secretary under section 26(1) of the said Act is not curable. Hence the said notice is void ab initio Mr. Karim submitted that since the order under section 26 is void ab inito, all the proceedings and the conviction are also illegal and void. Mr. Karim in support of his submission referred to the decision of the Appellate Division pronounced in the case of ACC versus Dr. Mohiuddin Khan Alamgir in Criminal Petition for Leave to Appeal No. 393 of 2009 Judgment dated 04.07.2010, wherein their lordships of the Appellate Division held that the order dated 18.02.2007 having been issued by the secretary to the Commission, the same is without any lawful authority and as such is void and any proceeding based on the said void order is a nullity in the eye of law. He then submits that the valuation made by the PWD is inconsistent with the then marked rate and they are not the expert and without any supportive evidences the conviction and sentence on the basis of those papers it is a nullity. Therefore the judgment and order of conviction and sentence passed by the Court below is required to be set aside for the ends of justice and equity Mr. Haque further submits that to take the statement of wealth mentioned in the Income Tax Return corresponding to the appellants income in 2005-2006 financial years was outrageously erroneous in assessing the appellants known sources of income without any thorough investigation into the sources of income since a lawyer could still earn legitimately and legally without such income being disclosed to the Income Tax authorities, such non disclosure being culpable under the Income Tax Ordinance but certainly not actionable as and act of corruption. Therefore the judgment and order of conviction and sentence passed by the court below is required to be set aside and the appeal should be allowed.

11.        On the other hand the learned advocate appearing on behalf of the Respondent No. 2 Anti-Corruption Commission submits that the notice is illegal as per the decision of the Appellate Division. But the notice as has been issued by the Secretary has got approval by a resolution dated 25.02.2007. He further submits that the Ordinance has given ex-post facto approval on 18.4.2007 under the strength of the section 18(2) of the said Act of 2004. Therefore the notice as has been severed is in accordance with law. Further he submits that only one sanction is sufficient as per decision laid down by the Appellate Division. He then referred a case reported in 2000 A.I.R. (SC) 948 and A.I.R. 1962(SC)495 that repeal of statute or stoppage of operation of stated by efflux of time or expiry of temporary stature of fixed  period do not automatically revive the repeated statute in the absence of express intention to revive. But in the present case it is altogether different Section 18(2) has not been approved by the Parliament. Therefore the cited  decisions are not applicable in the present case. He then prays that the appeal should be dismissed.

104.    Therefore, the Secretary of the Commission is to act as per direction of the Chairman. Further it appears that there is no commission in existence in between 7.2.2007 to 24.2.2007 as such notice served on 18.2.2007 is not a notice under law.

105.    From the trend of the examination of the witnesses, it appears that the Secretary of the Anti Corruption Commission issued a notice on 18.2.2007 under purported authority of section 26(1) and 18 of the Anti Corruption Commission Act, 2004 read with Rules 15 Gha(1) and 15(Gha(2) of the Emergency Power Rules, 2007 upon the appellant directing him to submit his statement of assets. The secretary of the Anti Corruption Commission had no authority to issue any such notice. Notice under section 26(1) can only be exercised by the Commission. Commission means Commission constituted under section 3 read with Section 5 of the Anti Corruption Act. From 7.2.2007 to 25.2.2007 there was no Commission since all the Commissioner tendered their resignation simultaneously. The president of the Republic by way of promulgating an Ordinance on 18.4.2007 inserted a clause being 18(2) after the existing clause 18 providing that any Act done by any of the officer of the commission during 7th February, 2007 and 24th February, 2007 may be given post facto approval provided however, such act exercised by the officers of the commission is in consonance with the objective of the Act and the functions of the Commission. The evidence on record speaks that the said Commission never gave any post facto approval of the order dated 18.2.2007. Therefore, there was never any post facto approval of the said order. Further under section 18(2) of the said Act, only such acts of Anti-Corruption Commission could be given post facto approval which acts are consistent with the provision of this Act and are in consonance with the functions of the Commission. The power under section 26(1) not being a power subject to delegation, therefore the notice issued by the secretary under section 26(1) of the said act is not curable. Hence the said order is void ab initio and the proceeding initiated and the conviction based on such void and illegal order are all illegal and void. In this regard it is profitable to refer the decision of the Appellate Division  in Criminal Petition for Leave to Appeal No. 398 of 2009 wherein their Lordships of the Appellate Division held as follows:-

“In the present case, the notice dated 18-2-2007, under section 26 of the Act, was issued by the Secretary of the Commission but he does not represented the Commission, he is only one of its employees, to carry out the decision of the Commission. But at the relevant time there were no Commissioner, as such apparently, the Secretary issued the notice on 18-2-2007, on his own, without any satisfaction and decision of the Commission, in violation of section 26 of the Act. The concerned authority on realizing this error, tried to cover it up by inserting sub section (2) in section 18 on 18-4-2004, by Ordinance No. VII of 2007. Sub-section (2) provides for ex post facto ratification of the acts done by the officers of the Commission during the period from, 7.2.2007 to 24.2.2007, without any authorization from the Commission, but the question of jurisdiction goes to the root of the matter. If any person acts beyond his authority, to the prejudice of any person, such acts cannot be ratified or validated by post facto legislation, his action remains void.

Besides, section 26 envisages that before issuance of the notice, the Commissioner(s) must be satisfied about the allegation. It is their satisfaction and of nobody elses. But by sub-section (2) of section 18, the Commissioners can only ratify the ‘satisfaction’ of the Secretary which is certainly not stipulated in section 26.

As such, the notice dated 18.2.2007, issued by the Secretary in favour of the respondent No. 1, was not issued on behalf of the Commission as envisaged under section 26, and is without jurisdiction and void abinitio.

Besides, the notice dated 18.2.2007 was not a notice required by law, the notice directed the respondent No. 1, a detenu, to submit return of his assets within a period of 72 hours, is itself a worst example of arbitrary action on the part of the concerned authority. A notice must allow a reasonable time to check-up the details of the assets of a person, if necessary, on examination of his records and after consultation with his lawyers and other concerned persons. Section 26 certainly does not envisage a notice upon a person who is in detention and he is not expected to give any details of his assets within the time specified. The person concerned must be afforded a fair and reasonable opportunity to respond to the notice, otherwise, it is no notice in the eye of law. A notice issued under section 26 of the Act to a detenu, away from his hearth and home cannot be said to be a fair and bonafide exercise of power.

Under the circumstances, we are of the opinion that the notice dated 18.2.2007, issued by Secretary to the Commission, was without any lawful authority, as such, void and any proceeding based on the said void notice is a nullity in the eye of law.

There is, however, no legal impediment for the Commission to issue fresh notice under section 26 of the Act, if so advised, but not in those cases where the accused has already been acquitted on merit of the case as is in this case.

With these observations, this petition is dismissed”
