Section 60

The legal proposition as has been enacted in the sub-section 1 of section 60 clearly indicated that a personal Deposit Pension Scheme which is the private arrangement between the bank and a private person would not come within the purview of the proviso of sub-section 1 of section 60, rather, it would be applicable and operate as impediment to attached the same if it had the outcome of service benefit of an employee of the service of the Republic. JUDGMENT

Md. Nuruzzaman, J. On an application under section 115(1) of the Code of Civil Procedure the instant Rule was issued calling upon the opposite party No.1 to show cause as to why order No.32 dated 10.03.2016 passed by the Joint District Judge, Brahmanbaria rejecting an application for attachment before judgment of the amount of Tk. 3,98,760.00/- in DPS account No. 01574 at present 005015715 under the account head deposit pension scheme with the plaintiff Bank in suit No. 01 of 2012 should not be set aside.

2.             The short facts, necessary, for disposal of the instant Rule, are that, the petitioner as plaintiff instituted a Money suit being No. 01 of 2012 in the Court of learned Joint District Judge, Brahmanbaria against the opposite party No. 1 and 2 and others as defendant for recovery of  loan money of TK. 16,28,63,824.21/-.

3.             It has been contended, inter alia, that the defendant-opposite party No.1 had been discharging the function of Manager of the plaintiff Bank since 06.02.2008. The defendant opposite party No. 2 and 3 were in-charge of pension bill section of general banking of the plaintiff bank. The defendant opposite parties in collusion with each other misappropriated Tk. 16,28,63,424.21/- of pension bill and it is stated that the defendant admitted the facts of misappropriate of the said amount. Hence, the plaintiff Bank has been compelled to file the instant Money suit for realization of the misappropriate money amounting of Tk. 16,28,63,824.21/-.

4.             During the pendency of the suit the plaintiff bank filed an application under Order 38, Rule 5 of the Code of Civil Procedure, hereinafter to be referred as the Code, in the Court of Joint Distirct Judge, Brahmanbaria for attachment before judgment of an amount of Tk. 3,98,760/- deposited in the personal deposit pension scheme in account No.01571 which renumbered as 005015715/- of the defendant No.1 lying with the plaintiff Bank which has already been matured and payable to him.

5.             The learned Joint District Judge, rejected the application for attachment before judgment by his judgment and order dated 10.03.2016.

6.             The plaintiff-bank being aggrieved by the impugned judgment and order preferred the instant revision application before this Division of the Supreme Court of Bangladesh and obtained the instant Rule with an order of stay.

7.             Though the opposite party appeared in the Rule through his learned Advocate however, at the time of hearing and pronouncement of judgment none was present to depend the opposite party.

8.             Mr. M. A. Sobhan, the learned Advocate has submitted that the suit has been brought against all the defendants for recovery of Tk. 16,28,63,824.21/-.

9.             The defendants admitting the misappropriation by way of forgery deposited an amount of Tk. 1,15,00,000/- out of total misappropriated money with the petitioner bank. In such, facts and circumstances, the plaintiff bank filed an application for attachment before judgment of the above mentioned personal deposit pension scheme of defendant No.1 to prevent the defendant No.1 to withdraw money of the above mentioned deposit pension scheme before disposal of the suit.

10.         He has further submitted that the learned Joint District Judge has committed serious error of law in opining that the application for attachment before judgment is hit by proviso of section 60 of the Code which is clearly a misconception and misinterpretation of the law as provided in section 60 of the Code because the deposit pension scheme (DPS) opened by defendant No.1 in the category of a private person as general public nor in the category of an officer of the government. The defendant No.1 is neither an employee or officer of the government or any statutory body in respect of opening the abovementioned deposit pension scheme (DPS) which does not come within the purview of section 60 of the Code therefore, the impugned judgment and order is liable to be set aside. 

11.         We have anxiously considered the submissions advanced by the learned Advocate for the petitioner. To answer the above legal proposition we have carefully gone through the section 60 of the Code. We are, therefore, of the view that it is necessary to reproduce the provision of section 60 of the Code for better understanding and ready reference which runs as follows:-

“60.(1) The following property is liable to attachment and sale in execution of a decree, namely, lands, houses or other buildings, goods, money, bank-notes, cheques, bills of exchange, hundis, promissory notes, Government securities, bonds or other securities for money, debts, shares in a corporation and, save as hereinafter mentioned, all other saleable property, movable or immovable, belonging to the judgment-debtor or over which, or the profits of which, he has a disposing power which he may exercise for his own benefit, whether the same be held in the name of the judgment debtor or by another person in trust for him or on his behalf:

Provided that the following particulars shall not be liable to such attachment or sale, namely:-

(a)    the necessary wearing-apparel, cooking vessels, beds and bedding of the judgment-debtor, his wife and children, and such personal ornaments as, in accordance with religious usage, cannot be parted with by any woman;

(b)    tools of artisans, and, where the judgment-debtor is an agriculturist, his implements of husbandry and such cattle and seed-grain as may, in the opinion of the Court, be necessary to enable him to earn his livelihood as such, and such portion of agricultural produce or of any class of agricultural produce as may have been declared to be free from liability under the provision of the next following section;

(c)    house and other buildings (with the materials and the sites thereof and the land immediately appurtenant thereto and necessary for their enjoyment) belonging to an agriculturist and occupied by him;

(d)    books of account;

(e)    a mere right to sue for damages;

(f)     any right of personal service;

(g)    stipends and gratuities allowed to pensioners of the Government, or payable out of any service family pension fund notified in the official Gazette by the Government of the Government in this behalf, and political pensions;

(h)   the wages of labourers and domestic servants whether payable in money or in kind;

(i)     Salary to the extent of the first hundred Taka and one-half the remainder:

Provided that where such salary is the salary of a servant of the 1[Republic] or a servant of 2[the Railway] or local authority, and the whole or any part of the portion of such salary liable to attachment has been under attachment, whether continuously or intermittently for a total period of twenty-four months, such portion shall be exempt from attachment until the expiry of a further period of twelve months and, where such attachment has been made in execution of one and the same decree, shall be finally exempt from attachment in execution of that decree;

(j)     the pay and allowances of persons to whom the 3[Army Act, 1952, Navy Ordinance,1961, or the Air Force Act, 1953,] applies;

(k)    all compulsory deposits and other sums in or derived from any fund to which the provident Funds Act,1925, for the time being applies in so far as they are declared by the said Act not to be liable to attachment;

(l)     any allowance forming part of the emoluments of any servant of the 1[Republic]or of any servant of 2[the Railway]or local authority which the Government may by notification in the official Gazette declare to be exempt from attachment, and any subsistence grant or allowance made to any such servant while under suspensions;

(m)  an expectancy of succession by survivorship or other merely contingent or possible right or interest;

(n)   a right to future maintenance;

(o)    any allowance declared by any Bangladesh law to be exempt from liability to attachment or sale in execution of a decree; and,

(p)   where the judgment-debtor is a person liable for the payment of land-revenue, any movable property which, under any law for the time being applicable to him, is exempt from sale for the recovery of an arrear of such revenue.

Explanation 1. – The particulars mentioned in clauses (g), (h, (i), (j),(l) and (o) are exempt from attachment or sale whether before or after they are actually payable, and in the case of salary other than salary of a servant of the 1[Republic]  or a servant of 2[the Railway] or local authority the attachable portion thereof is exempt from attachment until it is actually payable.

Explanation 2. – In clauses (h) and (i), ‘salary’ means the total monthly emoluments, excluding any allowance declared exempt from attachment under the provisions of clause (1), derived by a person from his employment whether on duty or on leave.

(2) Nothing in this section shall be deemed to exempt houses and other buildings (with the materials and the sites thereof and the lands immediately appurtenant thereto and necessary for their enjoyment) from attachment or sale in execution of decrees for rent of any such house, building, site or land. ”

12.         On a meticulous examination of the provisions of section 60 of the Code it appears that the legislature intend to provide some legal consequence in respect of attachment or stayed for particulars subject which needs to consider and exempted at the time of passing the attachment before judgment. The provisions as has been derived from the proviso of section 60 of the Code it is divulged that there is specific reason has been mentioned not to attach those facilities or belongings to an agriculturing or an government officer which surely outcome of his pension, gratuity, family, service family and other personal right as service holder which is apparent on a bare reading of proviso.

13.         Now the moot question before us whether a deposit pension scheme opened by an employee of the bank not in the capacity of his service but surely as a private person of general public would come within the purview of the proviso of section 60 of the Code.

14.         Considering the provision of the proviso of sub-section 1 of section 60 we find substance in the submissions of the learned Advocate for the petitioner because the legal proposition as had been enacted in the sub-section 1 of section 60 clearly indicated that a personal Deposit Pension Scheme which is the private arrangement between the bank and a private person would not come within the purview of the proviso of sub-section 1 of section 60, rather, it would be applicable and operate as impediment to attached the same if it had the outcome of service benefit of an employee of the service of the Republic. 

15.         From the above discussions, facts and circumstances and considering the legal proposition as laid down in proviso of sub-section 1 of section 60 of the Code by the legislature we find that there is no scope to interpret that a private deposit pension scheme by a government employee or officer or any private person in a personal capacity and an arrangement between the Bank and its employee or officer in personal capacity would be effective as a condition precedence in other ward would come to amenable as bar as has been narrated in proviso of sub section 1 of the  section 60 of the Code.

16.         Therefore, we find that the Rule bears substance.

17.         Accordingly, the Rule is made absolute.

18.         The judgment and order dated 10.03.2016 is hereby set aside.

19.         The application dated 13.08.2015 filed by the plaintiff bank under order 38 Rule 5 of the Code of Civil Procedure for attachment before judgment is allowed.

20.         The amount of Taka 3,98,760/- in deposit pension scheme account No.01574 and at present account No. 005015715 of the defendant No.1 lying with the plaintiff-bank which has already been matured and payable is hereby attached until disposal of the suit.

21.         However, the trial Court is directed to dispose of the suit expeditiously preferably within 06 (six) months from the date of receipt of this judgment.

22.         The office is directed to communicate the judgment at once.
