JUDGMENT

Muhammad Imman Ali, J: This civil petition for leave to appeal is directed against the judgement and order dated 07.04.2014 passed by a Division Bench of the High Court Division in Civil Revision No.1005 of 2012 making the Rule absolute.

2.            Petitioner Nos. 1 and 2 herein as plaintiffs on 21.1.2010 instituted Other Class Suit No.4 of 2010 in the First Court of Joint District Judge, Cox’s Bazar against the respondents for declaration of title and confirmation of possession in respect of the land described in the schedule to the plaint and for further declaration that the decree dated 31.7.1986 passed by the then Munsif, Cox’s Bazar in Other Class Suit No. 235 of 1972, is void and inoperative.

3.            In that suit defendant filed an application under Order VII Rule 11(d) read with section 151 of the Code of Civil procedure alleging that the predecessors of defendant Nos. 1-3 had instituted Other Class Suit No.235 of 1972 before the then Munsif, Cox’s Bazar impleading the predecessor of defendant No.4 and others for declaration of their title on the self-same land and for khas possession. The said suit was decreed on contest on 31.7.1986 and that the predecessor of the defendant No.4 and others had filed Other Appeal No.144 of 1986 in the Court of District Judge, Cox’s Bazar. ultimately the Subordinate Judge, Cox’s Bazar after hearing, by judgement and decree dated 17.03.1987 allowed the appeal and thereby set aside the judgement and decree passed by the trial Court. Thereafter, the defendants and their predecessor filed Civil Revision No.562 of 1987 before the High Court Division and obtained Rule, which upon hearing was made absolute and the title Suit No.235 of 1972 was decreed. The predecessor of the present plaintiffs after 6 years filed Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No.870 of 2009 before this Division, and after hearing, by judgement and order dated 12.11.2009 the petition was dismissed. Hence, the defendants of the instant suit filed the application for rejection of the plaint as being barred by law.

4.            The plaintiffs filed written objection against the application for rejection of plaint.

5.            The learned Judge by order No.23 dated 26.2.2012 rejected the said application for rejection of plaint on the finding that without examining the witnesses at the time of hearing of the suit the point raised by the petitioners cannot be decided.

6.            Being aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the said order of the joint District Judge, the defendants filed Civil Revision No.1005 of 2012 before the High Court Division and obtained Rule, which after hearing was made absolute. Hence, the plaintiffs are now before us having filed the instant civil petition for leave to appeal. 

7.            Mr. Tobarak Hossain, learned Advocate appearing on behalf of the petitioner submitted that the High Court Division failed to appreciate that the petitioners owned and are in possession of the land in question for 23 years without any claim from any quarter and that the application under Order VII Rule 11(d) of the Code of Civil Procedure was filed only to delay the matter. He submitted that the trial Court rightly rejected the said application, but the High Court Division without properly considering the facts made the Rule absolute. He further submitted that the High Court Division failed to appreciate that the schedule of the land of the defendants-opposite parties is not the same and without considering this aspect the High Court Division made the Rule absolute thereby allowing the application for rejection of plaint. Lastly, the learned Advocate submitted that the trial Court correctly found that the matters raised in the application under Order VII Rule 11 would have to be decided after taking evidence, but the High Court Division erroneously reversed the decision of the trial Court.

8.            Mr. Shashanka Sekhor Sarker, learned Advocate appearing for respondent No. 1 made submissions in support of the impugned judgement and order of the High Court Division. 

9.            From the evidence and materials on record we find that the predecessor of defendant Nos. 1, 2 and 3 instituted Other Class Suit No. 235 of 1972 impleading the predecessor of defendant No. 4 and others and that suit was decreed on contest. The said decree was upheld up to this Division. The plaintiffs of the instant suit purchased the suit land from the heirs of the defendants of the earlier Title Suit No. 235 of 1972. In that suit the plaintiffs’ vendors had lost the claim of title up to this Division. We note that the plaintiffs of the instant suit purchased the suit land after the earlier litigation had concluded. Thereafter, they purchased from persons who did not have title to the land. Moreover, in the instant suit there is no claim of any fraud.

10.        The High Court Division observed that it is true “that a plaint of a suit should not be rejected under order 7 rule 11 of the Code of Civil Procedure only on point of res-judicata. But the facts of the present case are quite different. Apparently it shows that the predecessor of the plaintiffs-opposite parties lost their title up to Appellate Division and after settling dispute finally by the Appellate Division the present plaintiff-opposite parties purchased some lands in 2009 from the predecessor of the defendants of the former suit and they instituted present suit for declaration of title, confirmation of possession and for further declaration that the decree passed in previous Title Suit No. 235 of 1972 against the predecessor of the present plaintiffs are void and not binding upon them. The issue of present title suit was decided by the Court finally in previously instituted suit and that issue cannot be agitated in the subsequently suit.”

11.        The High Court Division concluded that the present plaintiffs-opposite parties having knowledge about the previous suit and having lost their title up to this Division, instituted the present suit which is nothing but a futile exercise.

12.           Having considered the facts and circumstances discussed above, we are of the view that the High Court Division has properly considered the relevant law and facts involved in the case. The decision arrived at does not call for interference by this Division and, accordingly, the civil petition for leave to appeal is dismissed.
