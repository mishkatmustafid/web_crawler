Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898)

Section 205D

When there is a complaint case and a police case over the selfsame occurrence against some persons as accused, the proceeding of the complaint case would be stayed giving way to the police to conclude the investigation of the case and if the police report does not relate to all accused persons in the complaint, the Magistrate shall hold an inquiry into the complaint and upon such inquiry, he shall proceed with the trial of both the cases analogously as if the complaint was made with police or send the case to the Court of Session if the offences are triable by the said Court. In such a case, both the cases are deemed to be instituted on a police-report.         . . .(13)

Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898)

Sections 205D and 417(2)

Since a police case and a complaint case over the selfsame occurrence were filed against the same accused persons and police report has been submitted in the police case, both the cases would be deemed to have been instituted on a police-report. In such a situation, complainant-respondent No.2 cannot prefer an appeal under section 417(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure in view of the provisions of sub-sections (1) and (2) of section 205D of the Code of Criminal Procedure. Therefore, the appeal filed by complainant-respondent No.1 was not competent one. But the complainant-respondent was not without any remedy. When there is a wrong there must be a remedy. The complainant-respondent can file a criminal revision under section 439A of the Code of Criminal Procedure against the judgment and order dated 28.09.2002 passed by the learned Metropolitan Magistrate, Dhaka in G.R. No.495 of 2001 but inadvertently he filed an appeal. On the facts and in the circumstances of the case, we are of the view that the memo of appeal may be treated as a revision and the learned Sessions Judge or any other Court shall dispose of the revision in accordance with law.         . . . (15)

Moklesur Rahman Vs. Rabeya Porvin Chowdhury and others, 4 MlR (AD) 260 ref.

JUDGMENT

Syed Mahmud Hossain, J: This appeal by leave is directed against the judgment and order dated 07.05.2006 passed by the High Court Division in Criminal Miscellaneous Case No.9002 of 2004 discharging the Rule arising out of Criminal Appeal No.517 of 2002 subsequently renumbered as 21 of 2003 relating to  Khilgaon P.S. Case No.50(12)2001 corresponding to G.R.4957 of 2001 under sections 306/34 of the Penal Code.  

2.            The facts, leading to the filing of this criminal appeal, are précised below:

Deceased Shimi Banu, an adult daughter of complainant-respondent No.2 committed suicide. On 29.12.2001, police filed a case under sections 306/34 of the Penal Code registered as Khilgaon P.S. Case No.50 dated 29.02.2001 and on 24.12.2001`complainant-respondent No.2, the father of the deceased filed another case registered as U.D. Case No.31 of 2001. The case initiated by the police was investigated and ultimately was sent for trial and after conclusion of the trial, the learned Magistrate passed the conviction and sentence. Since the sentence is inadequate, complainant-respondent No.2 filed Criminal Appeal No.21 of 2003 before the learned Judge, Jananirapatta Bighnakari Aparadh Daman Tribunal No.1, Dhaka. The accused-appellant raised objection as to admission and acceptance of the appeal for hearing by the learned Judge. The leaned Judge by an order dated 08.01.2004 rejected the application for dismissing the appeal. 

3.            Being aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the order dated 08.01.2006, the accused-appellants filed Criminal Miscellaneous Case No.9002 of 2004 before the High Court Division. The learned Judges of the High Court Division by the judgment and order dated 07.05.2006 discharged the Rule.

4.            Feeling aggrieved by and dissatisfied with judgment and order passed by the High Court Division, the accused as the leave-petitioners filed Criminal Petition for Leave to Appeal No.417 of 2007 before this Division, on which, leave was granted on 15.10.2008, resulting in Criminal Appeal No.56 of 2008.

5.            Mr. Khandoker Mahbub Hossain, learned Senior Advocate, appearing on behalf of the appellants, submits that the High Court Division failed to consider that section 417(1) provides that only Government may in any case direct the Public Prosecutor to present an appeal in the appellate Court and where the case is instituted upon complaint, the complainant may present an appeal to the High Court Division against such acquittal under section 417(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure on any ground which involves matter of law only and as such, the impugned judgment should be set aside.

6.            Mr. Mahbubey Alam, learned Attorney General, appearing on behalf of respondent No.1, on the other hand, submits that the complainant is not without any remedy and as such, the appeal filed by him under section 417(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure was maintainable.

7.            Mrs. Sufia Khatun, learned Advocate-on-Record, appearing on behalf of respondent No.2 adopts the submissions made by the learned Attorney General.

8.            We have considered the submissions of the learned Senior Advocate for the appellants and the learned Attorney General for respondent No.1 and the learned Advocate-on-Record for respondent No.2, perused the impugned judgment and the materials on record.

9.            Leave was granted to consider whether the appeal filed by the complainant under section 417(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure was competent where over the selfsame incident FIR was filed and police also submitted charge sheet.

10.        Admittedly Shimi Banu, an adult daughter of respondent No.2 committed suicide. Over this incident, one case under sections 306/34 of the Penal Code was registered on 29.12.2001 as Kilgoan Police Station Case No.50 dated 29.02.2001 and another case was lodged by complainant-respondent No.2, that is, the father of the deceased on 24.12.2001 which was registered as U.D. Case No.31 of 2001. The case initiated by the police was investigated in which charge-sheet was submitted and was sent for trial. After conclusion of trial, the learned Magistrate convicted the accused-appellants under section 237 of Dhaka Metropolitan Police Ordinance, 1976 and sentenced each of them to suffer simple imprisonment for 1 (one) year and to pay a fine of Tk.1000/- each, in default, to suffer simple imprisonment for 1 (one) year more.

11.        Complainant-respondent No.2, the father of the deceased was dissatisfied with the conviction and sentence and as such, he preferred Criminal Appeal No.21 of 2003 before the learned Sessions Judge, Dhaka. The case was transferred to Jananirapatta Bighnakari Aparadh Daman Tribunal No.1, Dhaka for disposal. By the impugned order dated 18.03.2004, the learned Judge rejected the application dated 08.01.2004 field by the appellant for dismissing the appeal as being not maintainable.

12.        Now it is to be resolved whether the appeal filed by complainant-respondent No.2 was competent. In this connection, it is pertinent to quote section 205D of the Code of Criminal Procedure, which is as under:

“205D. Procedure to be followed when there is a complaint case and police investigation in respect of the same offence.-

(1)       When in a case instituted otherwise than on a police-report (hereinafter referred to as a complaint case), it is made to appear to the Magistrate during the course of the inquiry or trial held by him, that an investigation by the police is in progress in relation to the offence which is the subject matter of the inquiry or trial held by him, the Magistrate shall stay the proceedings of such inquiry or trial and call for a report on the matter from the police-officer conducting the investigation.

(2)       If a report is made by the investigating police-officer under section 173 and on such report cognizance of any offence is taken by the Magistrate against any person who is an accused in the complaint case, the Magistrate shall inquire into or try together the complaint case and the case arising out of the police-report as if both the cases were instituted on a police-report.

(3)       If the police-report does not relate to any accused in the complaint case or if the Magistrate does not take cognizance of any offence on the police-report, he shall proceed with the inquiry or trial, which was stayed by him, in accordance with the provisions of this Code.”

13.        From a plain reading of sub-sections (1) and (2) of the above section, we find that when there is a complaint case and a police case over the selfsame occurrence against some persons as accused, the proceeding of the complaint case would be stayed giving way to the police to conclude the investigation of the case and if the police report does not relate to all accused persons in the complaint, the Magistrate shall hold an inquiry into the complaint and upon such inquiry, he shall proceed with the trial of both the cases analogously as if the complaint was made with police or send the case to the Court of Sessions if the offences are triable by the said Courts. In such a case, both the cases are deemed to be instituted on a police-report. But according to sub-section (3) if the police report does not relate to any accused in a complaint case, the Magistrate shall proceed with the inquiry and trial which was stayed by him in accordance with the provisions of the Code.

14.        In this connection reliance may be placed on the case of Moklesur Rahman Vs. Rabeya Parvin Chowdhury and others 4 MLR (AD)260 where it has been held that as provided under section 205D Cr.P.C. both the cases, one instituted on police report and the other on complaint on the self-same occurrence, shall be tried by the Magistrate in the same trial treating both the cases as if instituted on a police report.  

15.        In the case in hand, since a police case and a complaint case over the selfsame proceeding were filed against the same accused persons and police report has been submitted in the police case, both the cases would be deemed to have been instituted on a police-report. In such a situation, complainant-respondent No.2 cannot prefer an appeal under section 417(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure in view of the provisions of sub-sections (1) and (2) of section 205D of the Code of Criminal Procedure. Therefore, the appeal filed by complainant-respondent No.1 was not competent one. But the complainant-respondent was not without any remedy. When there is a wrong there must be a remedy. The complainant-respondent can file a criminal revision under section 439A of the Code of Criminal Procedure against the judgment and order dated 28.09.2002 passed by the learned Metropolitan Magistrate, Dhaka in G.R. No.495 of 2001 but inadvertently he filed an appeal. On the facts and in the circumstances of the case, we are of the view that the memo of appeal may be treated as a revision and the learned Sessions Judge or any other Court shall dispose of the revision in accordance with law.

16.        Accordingly, this criminal appeal is disposed of and the memo of appeal filed in Criminal Appeal No.21 of 2003 be treated as revision and the learned Sessions Judge or any other competent Court is directed to hear the revision against the order of conviction and inadequate sentence passed by the learned Metropolitan Magistrate in G.R. Case No.4957 of 2001 corresponding to T.R. Case No.239 of 2002 arising out of Khilgoan Police Station Case No.50 dated 29.02.2001.
