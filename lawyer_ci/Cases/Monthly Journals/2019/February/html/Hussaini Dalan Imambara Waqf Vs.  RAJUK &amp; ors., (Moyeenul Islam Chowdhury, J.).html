4.             The respondent nos. 1, 6 and 13 have contested the Rule by filing separate Affidavits-in-Opposition. Their case, in brief, runs as follows:

5.             The respondent no. 13, that is to say, Image Properties Limited is not constructing a 12-storied building on the case property; rather it is constructing a 9-storied building along with one basement thereon in compliance with the terms and conditions of the Special Project Certificate dated 01.11.2016 and the approved construction plan dated 17.01.2017. The respondent nos. 2, 3, 4, 5, 9, 10 and 11 died long ago and the respondent nos. 6, 7 and 8 are the owners-in-possession of the case property. Since the case property is situated adjacent to Hussaini Dalan, the respondent no. 1 sought opinion from the Department of Archaeology with regard to the approval of the Land Use Certificate. The Secretary of the Special Committee of RAJUK wrote a letter to the Regional Director, Department of Archaeology on 20.07.2016 contending that since the proposed building is not a high-rise one under section 2(63) of the Building Construction Act, 1952, the construction of the building requires permission. 8.             At the outset, Mr. Junayed Ahmed Chowdhury, learned Advocate appearing on behalf of the petitioner, submits that indisputably the impugned construction of the 9-storied building is being raised on the case property which is adjacent to the boundary wall of Hussaini Dalan and the distance between Hussaini Dalan and the case property is 220 feet and the opinion of the Department of Archaeology given pursuant to Rule 61(kha) of the Rules of 2008 clearly shows that the building should not be more than 3-storied keeping in mind the aesthetic importance of Hussaini Dalan and despite such recommendation, RAJUK issued the Land Use Certificate by its Memo No. 25.39.0000.030.99. 028.17-1028 dated 20.11.2017 for the 12-storied building without any explanation whatsoever.

9.             Mr. Junayed Ahmed Chowdhury also submits that under Rule 9(2) of the Rules of 2008, RAJUK is required to disclose the drawings of the impugned construction for public observation and comments and to take public opinion including that of the petitioner for a period of 15(fifteen) days before issuance of the Land Use Certificate; but unfortunately the petitioner was not consulted before RAJUK allowed the impugned construction to proceed.

10.         Mr. Junayed Ahmed Chowdhury further submits that assuming for the sake of argument that RAJUK does not have any obligation to consult the petitioner under the Rules of 2008, in that event, RAJUK has a common-law duty to consult the petitioner to ensure fairness, especially when Hussaini Dalan is the next door neighbour of the respondent no. 6 and the impugned construction would clearly have an impact on its aesthetic appearance as evidenced by the opinion rendered by the Department of Archaeology.

12.         Mr. Junayed Ahmed Chowdhury also submits that the citizens have a right to protection from the threats of man-made hazards and the State is bound to protect the health and longevity of its people and the right to life includes the citizens’ freedom from violence, but RAJUK allowed the impugned construction on the case property ignoring a valid security issue in view of the fact that on 26.10.2015 due to terrorist bomb blasts in Hussaini Dalan, fatalities and injuries occurred.

29.         There is no gainsaying the fact that the respondent no. 1 (RAJUK) has acknowledged Hussaini Dalan as a historical establishment. It is also admitted that the 9-storied building on the case property is being raised at a distance of 220 feet from Hussaini Dalan. Undeniably there are many multi-storied and high-rise buildings within a radius of 250 metres from Hussaini Dalan. It is also undisputed that the petitioner does not lay any claim to the case property. The petitioner is only interested in ensuring the aesthetic beauty of Hussaini Dalan.

30.         Anyway, the petitioner has made a categorical statement on oath that the respondent nos. 6 and 13 are constructing a 12-storied building on the case property, that is to say, on C. S. Plot No. 107 under Holding No. 29, Hussaini Dalan Road, Lalbagh, Dhaka. It transpires that there is a signboard hung on the case property in front of the impugned construction. The petitioner is represented by its Superintendent who is also the Member-Secretary of its Managing Committee. In the natural course of things, a man of ordinary prudence is set to take notice of the signboard and read the writings thereon. We are unable to understand as to why the Superintendent of Hussaini Dalan failed to take notice of the signboard and the fact that a 9-storied building is being constructed on the case property. The Superintendent of Hussaini Dalan is an educated person. He is not Tom, Dick and Harry. He is not a man in the street.

31.         Before making any statement on oath before this Court, the Superintendent of Hussaini Dalan should have been very careful and circumspect. Had he been so, he would have instructed his lawyer that a 9-storied (instead of 12-storied) building is being erected on the case property. In this context, it is to be remembered that a statement made on oath before any Court of law has its sanctity and inviolability. Since the Superintendent has made a misleading statement in the Writ Petition with regard to the number of stories in the impugned construction, we strongly disapprove and deprecate his conduct in this respect.

32.         In order to be on the safe side, the petitioner could have filed an application to RAJUK under the relevant provisions of abÉ A¢dL¡l BCe, 2009 to know the exact nature of the impugned construction (inclusive of the number of stories) that is being raised on the case property. But the petitioner did not bother thereabout. It seems that the petitioner did not exercise due diligence in this regard.

33.         At this juncture, let us address the question of non-maintainability of the Writ Petition before this Court by the Superintendent of Hussaini Dalan as raised by Mr. Md. Nurul Amin. According to Mr. Md. Nurul Amin, the Superintendent was not authorized by any resolution of the Managing Committee of Hussaini Dalan to file the Writ Petition and on this score, he should be shown the door.

34.         Section 34(1) of the Waqfs Ordinance, 1962 provides that notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Ordinance, or any other law for the time being in force, or in any decree or order of any court, or in any deed or instrument, the Administrator may, by notification in the official Gazette, take over and assume the administration, control, management and maintenance of any Waqf property including any shrine, dargah, imambara or other religious institution appertaining to such Waqf property. Again section 34(3) of the Waqfs Ordinance, 1962 postulates that  the Administrator may manage the Waqf property taken over by him under sub-section (1) through any officer subordinate to him or by an agent or official Mutawalli or, if he considers necessary, by appointing a Managing Committee in the case of a shrine, dargah, imambara or other religious institutions.

35.         In the present case before us, a Managing Committee of Hussaini Dalan is in place. As already observed, the Superintendent of Hussaini Dalan is the Member-Secretary of its Managing Committee. A reference to Annexure- ‘K’ series to the Supplementary Affidavit dated 11.07.2018 unerringly shows that there has been a Managing Committee of Hussaini Dalan Imambara Waqf Estate since 1959 and this Waqf Estate is being represented all through by its Superintendent. The incumbent Superintendent of Hussaini Dalan is Mr. M. M. Firoz Hossain. A conjoint reading of the aforesaid Annexure-‘K’ series lead us to hold that the incumbent Superintendent Mr. M. M. Firoz Hossain is amply authorized by the Managing Committee to file/institute or defend any case or other legal proceeding in any Court of law for and on behalf of Hussaini Dalan. In this perspective, Mr. M. M. Firoz Hossain has preferred the instant Writ Petition in the High Court Division under article 102 of the Constitution. So no objection can be raised to the maintainability of the Writ Petition before this Court. In the result, the contention of Mr. Md. Nurul Amin that the Writ Petition is not maintainable stands discarded.

It is admitted that in the prescribed Land Use Certificate Form, the respondents concerned ticked “e¡” meaning thereby that the proposed construction of the 9-storied building along with one basement is not within a radius of 250 meters from the historical establishment, that is to say, Hussaini Dalan.

40.         Now a pertinent question arises: what is the consequence of ticking “e¡” in clause 6(R) of the prescribed Land Use Certificate Form by the concerned respondents? This ticking of “e¡” was enough to mislead the respondent no. 1 (RAJUK) into believing that the impugned construction is outside the radius of 250 meters of Hussaini Dalan, though the respondent Nos. 6 and 13 knew very well that the same is well therewithin from Hussaini Dalan. In the facts and circumstances of the case, we are of the opinion that this ticking of “e¡” was done deliberately in order to obtain the Land Use Certificate from RAJUK without any hitch. The consequence of furnishing any erroneous or misleading information in the prescribed Land Use Certificate Form is that it will automatically lead to the cancellation of the Land Use Certificate itself in accordance with its clause 5. What we are driving at boils down to this: the Land Use Certificate granted by the respondent no. 1 (RAJUK) in favour of the respondent nos. 6 and 13 has stood cancelled automatically because of the deliberately supplied misleading information in the Land Use Certificate Form. No further order is required from RAJUK for cancellation of this Land Use Certificate.

41.         Furthermore, clause 4 of the Special Project Certificate issued by RAJUK in favour of the respondent nos. 6 and 13 under Rule 8(1)(Q) of the Rules of 2008 spells out that “R¡sf­œl ®k ®L¡e e£¢aj¡m¡ m´Oe, B­hcefœ, eLÚn¡ ¢Lwh¡ B­hceL¡l£ La«ÑL ®fnL«a AeÉ¡eÉ c¢mm CaÉ¡¢c­a E­ŸnÉj§mL i¡­h fËLªa a­bÉl i¥m EfØq¡fe h¡ ®N¡fe L¢l­m ¢h­no R¡sfœ h¡¢am h¢mu¡ NZÉ qC­hz” (Underlined by us). In this regard as well, no further order of cancellation of the Special Project Certificate is required from RAJUK.

42.         It is worthy of notice that the Rules of 2008 were framed under the Building Construction Act, 1952 and accordingly RAJUK issued sanctions for the impugned construction on the case property and slapped some stipulations both in the Land Use Certificate and the Special Project Certificate as per section 3 of the aforesaid Act of 1952. For intentionally furnishing the wrong information by the respondent nos. 6 and 13 as above, the sanctions granted by RAJUK in respect of the impugned construction on the case property can not be maintained at all pursuant to section 9 of the Act of 1952.

43.         As this is a classic case of perpetration of fraud by the respondent nos. 6 and 13, both the Land Use Certificate and the Special Project Certificate have become non-existent in the eye of law. By the way, we are reminded of an off-quoted legal dictum- “Fraud vitiates everything”. It is well-settled that no party can be allowed to enjoy the fruit of its fraud.

44.         Be that as it may, the respondent nos. 6 and 13 will have to apply to RAJUK afresh for a Land Use Certificate and a Special Project Certificate for the impugned construction on the case property. If those respondents file applications to RAJUK afresh to the above effect, then RAJUK will review the entire gamut of the issues involved and consider their pros and cons and arrive at a correct decision as to granting or non-granting of any Land Use Certificate and Special Project Certificate in the light of the relevant laws.

45.         Since we have confined ourselves to the questions of maintainability of the Writ Petition and perpetration of fraud upon RAJUK in issuing the Land Use Certificate and the Special Project Certificate for the impugned construction, we refrain from making any observations and recording any findings on the submissions of Mr. Junayed Ahmed Chowdhury and the counter-submissions of Mr. Md. Nurul Amin relating to the merit of the Rule.

46.         In view of the foregoing discussions and in the facts and circumstances of the case, the Rule is disposed of without any order as to costs. However, the impugned construction on the case property shall remain stayed pending any decision by RAJUK in the light of the observations made in the body of this judgment.
