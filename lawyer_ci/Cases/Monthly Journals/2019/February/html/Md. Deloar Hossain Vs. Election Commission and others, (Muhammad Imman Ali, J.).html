JUDGMENT

Muhammad Imman Ali, J: This civil petition for leave to appeal is directed against the judgement and order dated 05.04.2017 passed by the High Court Division in Writ Petition No.6986 of 2016 discharging the Rule Nisi.

2.             The facts of the case, in brief, are that the writ petitioner was elected Chairman of Shialkathi Union Parishad of Kawkhali Upazilla, District-Pirojpur in 2011, and performing his function and discharging his duties as Chairman in accordance with law. In the Union Parishad Election-2016, the writ petitioner was candidate for the post of Chairman of Shiaklathi Union of Kawkhali Upazilla, Pirojpur as nominated by the Jatio Party (J.P.). His nomination paper was declared valid and he was allocated a Symbol of "Bi-cycle".

3.             On 22.03.2016 the election of No.5 Shialkathi Union Parished of Kawkhali Upazilla, District-Pirojpur was held, but in the midst of election, some unruly people started rigging of vote in  No.47 Moddho Shialkathi Government Primary School Centre and then the concerned Presiding Officer   stopped the election and informed the higher authority concerned.

4.             While the voting was taking place peacefully in other centres, some miscreants made attempt to cast false votes and then the Presiding Officer Mr. Md. Fayjul Kabir took help of law enforcing agency and for ousting the unruly people from the centre, stopped the vote for the time being and after controlling the situation, started taking votes again within a few moment. After completion of polling and after counting the votes, the result was declared by the Presiding Officer.

5.             The Returning Officer, (Upazilla Jubo Unnayon  Officer)  after  counting  the  votes in presence of respective Polling Agents of the candidates formally declared the result of 8 centres and forwarded the same to the Election Commission with copy to all concerned.

6.             The individual result of 8 centres were declared separately, where the total number of votes in favour of each candidate, rejected votes and other relevant information was duly given.

7.             The Presiding Officer, Centre No.47 of Moddho Shialkathi Government Primary School (Molla Bari) lodged Kawkhali Police Station Case No.14/26 dated 28.03.2016 under   sections 147/148/149/448/332/353/386/506 of the Penal Code and section 15 of the Special Powers Act, 1974. The information of lodging cases in different centres was communicated to the Election Commission by the District Election Officer of Pirojpur on 04.04.2016, vide Memo No.17.06.7900.000.41.001.016.127/1(7).

8.             The Presiding Officer of Centre No.46, South Shialkathi Government Primary School submitted a report to the District Election Officer on 17.04.2016 stating the facts about the situation that prevailed in the said centre on the day of election, about stopping the vote for a few minutes and taking the votes again. It has also been mentioned in the report that he did not send any letter or issue any certificate to anyone in this regard, except submitting the report to the Election Commission and expressed his apprehension that by using his name, any one may create a forged document or certificate.

9.             The Writ petitioner being an elected public representative has requested all concerned for taking necessary steps for holding election in No.47 of Moddho Shialkathi Government Primary School as early as possible, where the election was stopped by the Presiding Officer due to unusual situation and all of them verbally assured him that necessary steps are being taken for holding election in the said centre as early as possible, and accordingly the writ petitioner was waiting for the response of the writ-respondents for by-election in Centre No.47. Then all of a sudden a letter was issued on 17.5.2016 under the signature of writ-respondent No.4, stating that the election of Centre No. 46 at Shialkathi Government Primary School has been cancelled under Rule 90(Gha) of the Local Government (Union Parishad) Rules, 2010.

10.         Added respondent No.5, Siddiqur Rahman, contested the writ petition by filing supplementary affidavit contending, inter alia, that on 23.03.2016, he filed an application to the District Election, Pirojpur for holding re-election of Centre No.46, South Shialkathi Union Parishad. He made an allegation that after stopping the polling, the authority did not restart the polling again, but at the time of preparing result sheet, the result was shown for the polling Centre No.3 that 800 votes were cast out of total 1260 votes. It was also shown that 236 votes were cancelled in which the bi-cycle obtained 467 votes which were forcibly used for the symbol bi-cycle. 

11.         In due course after hearing both the parties, by the impugned judgement and order the said Rule Nisi was discharged. Hence, the writ petitioner is now before us having filed the instant civil petition for leave to appeal.

12.         Mr. S.M. Rezaul Karim, learned Advocate appearing on behalf of the petitioner submitted that since it is a matter of 'Election' of the Chairman of Shialkathi Union Parishad of Kawkhali Upazilla, District-Pirojpur, which involves the question of public importance with public interest, despite that, without appreciating this point, the High Court Division discharged the Rule ignoring the public demand, as such the impugned judgement and order passed by the High Court Division calls for interference by this Division. He further submitted that the Election Commission most illegally issued the impugned letter dated 17.05.2016, being influenced by the opponent of the petitioner, since the election in the centre namely, 46 North Shialkathi Government Primary School was held in accordance with law and the result of election of the said centre was declared by the Presiding Officer and the result of total 8 centres (except No.47 Moddho Shialkathi Government Primary School) was prepared by the Returning Officer and was sent to all concerned on 22.03.2016, while nobody has raised any objection about the result of the 8 centres, but long after declaration of the result of 8 centres, unexpectedly the Election Commission has issued the impugned letter dated 17.05.2016, i.e. after about 2 months, without assigning any reason whatsoever and by misinterpreting the provision of law, such act of the respondents is absolutely illegal and mala fide and also without jurisdiction. He submitted that in issuing the impugned letter dated 17.05.2016, the Election Commission has misinterpreted rule 90(Gha) of  the Local Government (Union Parishad) Rules, 2010, because the above provision has not authorized the Election Commission to cancel the result of election, where the result has already been declared by the competent authority, i.e. the Presiding Officer and subsequently Returning Officer. The High Court Division manifestly erred in law resulting in an error occasioning failure of justice in misreading the affidavit evidence on record and failed to consider the legal and admitted facts and in basing decision on mere surmise and conjecture, as such the impugned judgement and order passed by the High Court Division calls for interference by this Division. He submitted that in the usual process of election, by-election is required for Centre No.47 Moddho Shialkathi Government Primary School (Molla Bari), where the Presiding Officer stopped the election owing to reasonable cause which had arisen, but the election of Centre No.46 of South Shialkathi Government Primary School cannot be held further at all, since it has already been held and nowhere it has been stated as to why such decision has been taken by the Election Commission and for the interest of whom? In view of the above facts and circumstances it is clear that the Election Commission has issued the impugned letter beyond its jurisdiction, the High Court Division has misread, misconstrued and misunderstood the materials on record and consequently failed to comprehend the facts of the case and misapplied the law, thus the impugned judgement and order passed by the High Court Division calls interference by this this Division. He submitted that section 22 of the Local Government (Union Parishad) Ain, 2009 provides that relating to election or its procedure, if any, objection is raised, it can be adjudicate only by the Election Tribunal to be constituted under section 23 of the Ain and the objection or complain of any aggrieved party to be raised within 30 days of the constitution of Election Tribunal, but the Election Commission by using the impugned letter dated 17.5.2016 has sit over the jurisdiction of the Election Tribunal, which are absolutely without jurisdiction of the Election Commission, the High Court Division failed to appreciate the facts and circumstances of this case in its true perspective, as a result of which there has been serious miscarriage of justice and as such the impugned judgement and order as passed by the High Court Division is liable to be set aside by this Division. He submitted that it is the settled principle of law by different pronouncement of this Division that the election process starts from the Notification issued by the competent authority declaring election schedule and culminate in the declaration of result of election by a gazette notification, it has been further decided that election disputes can only by raised by way of an election petition. In the instant case the result of centre No.46 of South Shialkathi Government Primary School has been declared after holding election on 22.03.2016, whereas the election and its result has been cancelled through the impugned letter dated 17.05.2016 by the Election Commission without assigning any reason, if any dispute raised from any corner, it must be treated as "Election Dispute", such dispute only can be adjudicated by the Tribunal constituted under section 23 of the Local Government (Union Parishad) Ain. 2009 through the process of section 22 of the said Ain. He submitted that the petitioner being an honest social worker has contributed lot for the local people and as such he is very much popular to the local people and due to that, his opponents being failed to defeat him in the fair election process, has attempted to forcibly occupy the vote centre and by rigging vote wanted to defeat the petitioner, but due to resistance by the local people, their attempt has been failed on 22.03.2016, where the Presiding Officer stopped election of centre No.47 of Moddho Shaialkathi Government Primary School, but the election in centre No.46 was held and the result was declared in presence of the agent of contesting candidates, at that time, no objection was raised from anyone, but long after by using the Election Commission, the impugned letter was issued at the behest of opponent of the  petitioner, which are absolutely illegal, mala fide, unjust, unfair and beyond the scope of law. He submitted that the respondents are duty bound to act in accordance with law, but by exercising power beyond its jurisdiction has issued the impugned letter dated 17.05.2016 in order to prejudice the petitioner and has been done for the satisfaction and interest of the opponent of the petitioner, backed by the influential ruling party. He submitted that in canceling the election and result of 46 of South  Shialkathi Government Primary School, no inquiry was held, vis-a-vis, petitioner was not given any opportunity to defend himself, he was not even asked to explain his position, where the petitioner got the highest vote in the said centre, so he has acquired a valuable right, in such a situation, issuance of the impugned letter on 17.05.2016 is absolutely violation of settled principle of natural justice, because the law provides, no action can be taken against the interest or right of one without allowing him to defend in accordance with law and only in accordance with law.

13.         Mr. Ruhul Quddus, learned Advocate appearing for the respondents made submission in support of the impugned judgement and order of the High Court Division.

14.         We have considered the submissions of the learned Advocates appearing for the parties concerned, perused the impugned judgement and order of the High Court Division and other connected papers on record. 

15.         Admittedly, the election was suspended albeit for a while. In such a situation, the Presiding Officer was palpably wrong in declaring the result of the election. We find that the Election Commission rightly cancelled the election for that polling centre.

16.         In view of the discussion above, we do not find any illegality or impropriety in the impugned judgement and order of the High Court Division.

17.         Accordingly, the civil petition for leave to appeal is dismissed.
