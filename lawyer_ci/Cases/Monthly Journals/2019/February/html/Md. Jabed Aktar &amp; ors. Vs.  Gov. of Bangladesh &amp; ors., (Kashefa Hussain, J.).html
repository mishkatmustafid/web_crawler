                        JUDGMENT

Kashefa Hussain, J. Rule was issued in the instant Civil Revisional Application calling upon the opposite parties to show cause as to why the impugned judgment and order dated 05.03.2014 passed by the learned Joint District Judge, 1st Court, Dinajpur in Miscellaneous Case No. 110 of 2010 under Order 9 Rule 13 of the Code of Civil Procedure allowing the miscellaneous case on setting aside the expartee decree dated 29.07.2010 passed by the Joint District Judge, 1st Court, Dinajpur in Title Suit No. 4 of 1010 should not be set aside and/or pass such other or further order or orders as to this court may seem fit and proper.

2.             Facts relevant for disposal of the Rule is that the instant petitioner as plaintiff filed Title Suit No. 04 of 2004 in the 1st court of Joint District Judge, Dinajpur against the defendants in the suit being opposite parties for declaration of title etc.

3.             During the pendency of the Suit the plaintiff also filed an application for injunction under Order 39 Rule 1 and 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure which was allowed and against the defendant-opposite parties filed a written objection against the petitioner and granting of temporary injunction. However, on 29.07.2010 the defendant-opposite parties failed to appear before the trial court and the court below upon taking evidence of PW-1 decreed the suit expartee on the very same date that is 29.07.2010. Therefore the opposite parties as petitioners filed Miscellaneous Case No. 110 of 2010 under Order 9 Rule 13 along with section 151 of the Code of Civil Procedure on several allegations including that the suit was declared expartee not because of any fault of the defendant, but one of the several reasons being that the clerk of the learned AGP (Assistant Government Pleader) mistakenly noted the next date of the Suit as 27.07.2010 instead of 29.07.2010 and as such he could not any steps on 27.07.2010. The plaintiff in Title Suit No. 04 of 2010 being the instant petitioners as opposite parties contested the said Miscellaneous Case under Order 9 Rule 13 of the Code of Civil Procedure upon filing a written objection inter-alia on the allegation that the case is not maintainable being barred by limitation etc. The trial Court however after hearing the parties and upon examination of documents on record allowed the miscellaneous case by order dated 05.02.2014 and set-aside the expartee decree dated 29.07.2010 passed by the Joint District Judge, 1st Court, Dinajpur.

4.             Being aggrieved by the impugned Judgment and order dated 05.03.2014 in Miscellaneous Case No. 110 of 2010 under order 9 Rule 13 of the Code of Civil Procedure passed by the learned Joint District Judge, 1st Court, Dinajpur allowing the Miscellaneous Case on setting aside the expartee decree dated 29.07.2010 passed in Title Suit No. 4 of 2010 by the Learned Joint District Judge, 1st Court, Dinajpur. The plaintiff-opposite parties-petitioners preferred the instant civil Revisional application which is before us for disposal.

5.             Learned Senior Advocate Mr. Wahiduzzaman Sohel along with Learned Advocat Mr. Tariqul Islam appeared on behalf of the petitioners while the Learned Assistant Attorney General Mrs. Syeda Rabia Begum represented the opposite parties.

6.             Learned Senior Advocate Wahiduzzaman Sohel on behalf of the plaintiff-opposite parties petitioners submits that the Trial Court unlawfully without proper consideration of the records allowed the Miscellaneous Case leading to serious error of law. He elaborates his submissions upon assertion that the suit was decreed on 29.07.2010 while the Miscellaneous Case No. 110 of 2010 under Order 9 Rule 13 was filed on 23.09.2010 and which is therefore barred by limitation. Upon assertion the Learned Advocate tried to draw up and infer upon several factual aspects.

7.             Relying upon such factual aspects inter-alia he asserts that the defendants were aware of the date fixed for judgment on 29.07.2010 but in spite of having knowledge of the fixed date of judgment they did not appear on that date. He further asserts that the conduct of the defendants-petitioners-opposite parties clearly indicate that they intentionally did not appear on that date. In the light of his submissions he concludes that the judgment and order of the Trial Court in the Miscellaneous Case No. 110 of 2010 under order 9 Rule 13 of the Code of Civil of Procedure passed by the learned Joint district, 1st Court, Dinajpur allowing the Miscellaneous Case defendant-opposite parties ought to be set-aside and the Rule be made Absolute for ends of Justice.

8.             On the other hand the Learned Assistant Attorney General on behalf of defendant-petitioners-opposite parties upon filing a counter affidavit opposes the Rule and takes us to the records including the judgment and order of the Trial Court. She inter-alia argues that it is evident from the records and from the judgment and order of the Court below that the defendant-petitioners-opposite parties were not barred by limitation in filing the Miscellaneous case against the expartee decree on 29.07.2010. Relying upon the documents and the impugned judgment and order in the Miscellaneous case No. 110 of 2010 she argues that the defendants failed to appear on the day of judgment that is 29.07.2010 not for any fault of their own but for reason and circumstances beyond their control. In this context she submits that the non appearance of the defendants-petitioners-opposite parties on the date of judgment that is 29.07.2010 was due to the fault or mistake of the clerk of the AGP who somehow mistakenly on 28.06.2010 entered the wrong date in the dairy, that is, instead of 29.07.2010 the clerk mistakenly entered 27.09.2010 as the next date which was also the due date for judgment. She asserts that hence the defendants-petitioners-opposite parties could not appear on the due date that is on 29.07.2010 because of lack of knowledge as a result of the mistake of the clerk of the AGP and not for lack of diligence or any other laches whatsoever. She concludes her submissions upon assailing that a party can not suffer because of the fault of the pleader or his clerk and thus the trial Court correctly allowed the application under Order 9 Rule 13 after proper scrutiny into the documents on record and therefore the judgment and order of the Trial Court in Miscellaneous Case No. 110 of 2010 needs no interference and the Rule bears no merits and ought to be discharged.

9.             Heard the Learned advocates from both sides, perused the documents and materials on records including the impugned judgment and order. Upon perusal it appears that the defendants-petitioners-opposite parties were not actually callous or does not seem to suffer from any deliberate apathy in pursuing the case. It is apparent from the records and the impugned judgment and order that they had taken steps to file an objection under order 39 Rule 4 of the Code of Civil Procedure against the order of temporary injunction earlier passed on 08.02.2010. While pursuing the same, on 13.09.2010 they discovered for the first time that the judgment and Order in the Title Suit No. 4 of 2010 was already passed on 29.07.2010 by way of an expartee decree in favour of the plaintiff. The Trial Court in its judgment referred to the ‘mistake’ of the clerk of the learned AGP in entering the wrong date in the dairy, that is instead of 29.07.2010 the clerk of the AGP wrongly entered 29.07.2010 as the due date of judgment. The petitioners however neither in the Revisional Application nor in their submissions made any statements or submissions controverting the findings of the Trial Court regarding the wrong date of next hearing/judgment ‘mistakenly’ entered by the AGP’s clerk and due to which the opposite parties till 13.09.2010 did not have knowledge of the expartee decree dated 29.07.2010. It is a settled principle that in considering an application under Order 9 Rule 13 of the Code of Civil Procedure, the factor of date of knowledge is important. In this instant the plaintiff-opposite parties-petitioners could not disprove or otherwise controvert the claim of the mistaken date in the dairy during the case. Hence it is our considered finding that the entry of the wrong date in the dairy of the clerk of the AGP resulted in the defendant-petitioners-opposite parties’ lack of knowledge of the actual date of the judgment. We are of the considered opinion that the Trial Court correctly observed that for the mistake or whatsoever of the clerk or of the concerned lawyer the parties cannot and should not suffer. In this case we are inclined to primarily consider that the actual date fixed for the judgment, that is 29.07.2010 was beyond the knowledge of the defendant-opposite parties and which they discovered only on 13.09.2010 while pursuing filing of an objection under order 39 Rule 4 against granting of temporary injunction. Hence it is our considered finding that the defendants-petitioner-opposite parties having gained knowledge of the expartee decree on 13.09.2010, therefore the Miscellaneous Case under Order 9 Rule 13 of the Code of Civil Procedure being filed on 22.09.2010 is not barred by limitation and is hence maintainable in law.

10.         Under the facts and circumstance and from the fore going discussions made above and upon hearing the learned Advocates including perusal of the records we are of the considered finding that the impugned judgment and order passed in Miscellaneous Case No. 110 of 2010 under order 9 Rule 13 of the Code of Civil Procedure, by the learned Joint District Judge, 1st Court, Dinajpur allowing the Miscellaneous case setting aside the expartee decree dated 29.07.2010 passed in Title Suit No. 4 of 2010, suffers no infirmity and was correctly passed.

11.         Hence we find no merit in the Rule.

12.         In the result, the Rule is discharged without any order as to costs.

13.         The trial court is hereby directed to positively dispose of the Suit within 06(six) months from the date of receipt of this order.

14.         The order of stay granted earlier by this court is hereby vacated.

15.         Send down the Lower Court’s Records.

16.         Communicate this Judgment and order at once.
