JUDGMENT

Soumendra Sarker, J. The Rule issued calling upon the opposite parties to show cause as to why the order No.58 dated 15.07.2018 passed by the learned Senior Assistant Judge, Sadar, Dinajpur in Small Causes Case No.01 of 2010 allowing the application for addition of party should not be set aside and/or such other order or further order or orders passed as to this court may seem fit and proper.

2.             The facts giving rise to the issuance of the Rule in a nutshell can be stated thus: The present petitioner as plaintiff instituted the original Small Cause Courts Suit No.01 of 2010 in the Court of learned Senior Assistant Judge, Sadar, Dinajpur against the defendant-opposite parties for ejectment of tenants from the suit shop, contending inter alia that, the suit property originally belonged to one Md. Shahid Ali. The defendants are the President and Secretary of a Club namely ‘Friends Club’ and the defendants were the tenants under the landlord Shahid Ali. After the death of Shahid Ali his wife Firoza Begum and three sons namely, Md. Mirajul Islam, Md. Sirajul Islam, Md. Ferojul Islam and daughter Mst. Ifthesham Ali become the legal heirs of Md. Shahid Ali and the defendants were continuing their tenancy as monthly ejectable tenants under the aforesaid heirs of Shahid Ali. The monthly rent of the suit shop was fixed at Tk.1,000/- (one thousand). Subsequently, the plaintiff to the original suit purchased the land from the heirs of Shahid Ali by registered sub-kabala dated 20.11.2006 at a consideration of Tk.15,00,000/- (Fifteen lac). After purchasing the suit property while the defendants stopped in paying the rent in favour of the plaintiff, the plaintiff served a legal notice to the defendants to pay the rents in favour of her, on 02.03.2007, but the defendants did not pay the rent. Thereafter, the plaintiff served a notice under section 106 of the Transfer of Property Act, on 03.11.2009 and by this notice the plaintiff has terminated the tenancy with the defendants and asked the defendants to deliver vacant possession of the suit premises in favour of the plaintiff. The defendants received the notice by putting their signatures in the acknowledgment card on 08.11.2009. The further case of the plaintiff is such that the defendants out of ill-motive without payment of the rents to the plaintiff after filing a House Rent Control Case No.17 of 2007 depositing the rents to the court. The suit shop is badly required for the bonafide purpose of the plaintiff to construct a multistoried building thereon. During pendency of the original suit the defendants after making their appearance contested the suit by filing a written statement and at this stage the present opposite parties No.3-7 filed an application for addition of party under Order I rule 10 of the Code of Civil Procedure, alleging that the suit shop is the ancestral property of the applicant’s predecessor Wahida Begum, who was the sister of Md. Shahid Ali. Shahid Ali was possessing the property in ejmali with the predecessor of the applicants and thereafter died leaving behind three sons, one wife and one daughter. For getting a separate saham in the ejmali property, Wahida Begum filed a Partition Suit being No.192 of 2003 in the Court of learned Senior Assistant Judge, Sadar, Dinajpur, which is pending for disposal. The further case of the applicants is such that the sub-kabala deed of the plaintiff dated 20.11.2006 is illegal, inoperative and not binding upon the predecessor of the applicants Wahida Begum and challenging the said deed the successors of Wahida Begum i.e. the applicants filed an Other Class Suit No.105 of 2008 in the Court of learned Joint District Judge, 1st Court, Dinajpur, which is also pending for disposal.

3.             The learned trial court by its order dated 15.07.2018 allowed the application for addition of party and being aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the impugned order the plaintiff as petitioner has preferred this civil revision under section 25 of the Small Cause Courts Act, 1887 and obtained the Rule with an interim order of stay.

4.             During hearing of the Rule, Mr. Probir Neogi, the learned Advocate appeared on behalf of the petitioner while Mr. A.H.M Mushfiqur Rahman, the learned Advocate appeared on behalf of the added defendant-opposite parties No.3-7.

5.             The learned Advocate appearing on behalf of the petitioner submits that the learned trial court in allowing the application for addition of party committed illegality. The learned Advocate further submits that the learned trial court failed to appreciate that the suit was filed by the owner of the suit premises for ejectment of tenants and the matter of adjudication in the instant suit is as to whether the relationship between the plaintiff and the defendants are landlord and tenants and as such the question of title to the suit premises is not relevant and not a matter of adjudication and as such the added defendants are not necessary parties or proper parties to the suit. The learned Advocate also submits that the decision cited by the learned trial court is not applicable in the instant suit due to distinguishable facts and feature of the case and the defendants after filing a rent control case have already recognized the plaintiff as owner to the suit premises, but the learned trial court in allowing the application for addition of party committed an error in law resulting in an error in the decision occasioning failure of justice. The learned Advocate in support of his submission referred some decisions of our Apex Court reported in 16 BLD(AD) 237, 1981 BLD (AD)128 and a decision of this Court reported in 56 DLR 53.

6.             As against the aforesaid submission of the learned Advocate for the petitioner, the learned Advocate appearing on behalf of the added opposite parties opposing the Rule controverted the argument advanced from the side of the petitioner and submits that, the learned trial Court during passing the impugned order dated 15.07.2018 in Small Causes Case No.01 of 2010 committed no illegality or irregularity. The learned Advocate further submits that the predecessor of the applicants is a co-sharer in the suit property with the vendor’s predecessor of the plaintiff Md. Shahid Ali. The learned court below rightly apprised the decision reported in 2 MLR(AD)357 and thereafter, allowed the application for addition of parties. The learned Advocate also submits that the opposite party’s predecessor-in-interest Wahida Begum was the full sister of Md. Shahid Ali and the suit property is the ejmali property of the co-sharers. In order to get a separate saham in the suit property the predecessor of the applicants, Wahida Begum filed a Partition Suit being No.192 of 2003 in the Court of learned Senior Assistant Judge, Dinajpur Sadar, which is awaiting for disposal. Furthermore, challenging the sub-kabala deed of the plaintiff from the heirs of Md. Shahid Ali, dated 14.02.1993 the predecessor of the applicant filed an Other Suit No.105 of 2008 in the 1st Court of learned Joint District Judge, Dinajpur, which is also pending for disposal and considering all these facts and circumstances the learned trial court committed no wrong in passing the impugned order dated 15.07.2018. The learned Advocate in support of his argument referred 32 DLR (AD) 170.

7.             In order to appreciate the submission advanced from the sides of the learned counsels for the respective parties, having gone through the impugned order No.58 dated 15.07.2018 in Small Causes Case (hereinafter SCC) Suit No.01 of 2010, the application filed by the applicant-opposite parties under Order I rule 10 of the Code of Civil Procedure, the plaint of the original SCC Suit No.01 of 2010 (Annexure-A), the written statement filed by the opposite party No.1 and 2 (Annexure-B) and the additional written statement of the defendant-opposite party No.1 and 2, it transpires that the original SCC Suit No.01 of 2010 was instituted by one Anjuman Ara Begum for ejectment of tenants stating that she has purchased the suit premises from the heirs of Md. Shahid Ali , who was the owner of the suit premises by a registered sub-kabala dated 20.11.2006 at a consideration Tk.15,00,000/- (Fifteen lac). It was further contended by the plaintiff to the suit that the suit shops are very old and dilapidated, which can be broken at any time and since the defendants are defaulters in paying the rents, they are liable to be evicted from the suit premises and meanwhile the plaintiff served a notice under section 106 of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882 terminating the ‘landlord and varatia’ relationship directing the defendant No.1 and 2 to handover the vacant possession of the suit premises.

8.             On perusal of the written statement, which is the pleadings of the defendant No.1 and 2, I find that the defendants are the tenants under Md. Shahid Ali, the ex-landlord of the suit premises and after the death of Md. Shahid Ali his wife, sons and daughter inherited the property. The contention of the defendant No.1 and 2 as it appears from their written statement is such that the defendants are not habitual defaulter under the plaintiff and while the plaintiff denied to accept rent for the month of November and December, 2006 and January of 2007, the defendants under the ambit of Rent Control Ordinance depositing the rents regularly after filing of a Rent Control Case No.17 of 2007 and as such the defendants are not habitual defaulter and they cannot be evicted from the suit shops. It was further alleged by the contesting defendant No.1 and 2 that late Md. Shahid Ali was the owner of the suit premises and on the said premises there are some shops. On the roof of those shops after taking rent by an agreement dated 05.05.1992, the defendants have erected a Club namely Friends Club. It was further contended by the contesting defendants in their pleadings that they have paid rent at the rate of Tk.1,000/- (one thousand) per month in favour of Md. Shahid Ali according to ‘varatia agreement’ and after the death of Md. Shahid Ali, his successive owners also realized rent for the schedule premises at the rate of Tk.1,000/- (one thousand) per month.

9.             In view of the papers, facts and circumstances of the case it is noticed that the plaintiff is a subsequent purchaser from the successive landlords, who are the legal heirs of the original landlord Md. Shahid Ali and it is the case of the plaintiff that she has purchased the suit premises by a registered sub-kabala deed dated 20.11.2006. Under the ambit of Small Cause Courts Act, 1887 the landlord is empower to evict the tenant, when it is established that the tenant is a habitual defaulter in payment of rents and the landlord has basic need of the suit premises.

10.         Consulting the case laws under reference, it is evident to note that in a suit for ejectment of tenant, as the instant case be; their lordships in the case of Additional Deputy Commissioner (Revenue) and Assistant Custodian, Vested Property, Barisal –vs.- Md. Mozammel Haque and another 16 BLD(AD)237 have clearly held that,

“In the application for addition of party the petitioner asserted that the property being vested property the plaintiff has no right, title and interest in the suit premises to maintain the suit for ejectment against the defendant who is a lessee from the custodian of vested property. The trial court rightly rejected the petitioner’s prayer holding, inter alia, that in a suit for an ejectment of monthly tenant the question of determination of title cannot be gone into and as such there is no necessity of the petitioner’s presence in the suit.”

11.         In another decision reported in 1981 BLD(AD) 128 our Apex Court held that in SCC suit, the court has no jurisdiction to go into the question of title, except incidentally, or to set aside the decree of a civil court. Besides this, this court relying upon the aforesaid decisions in the case of Noor Mohammad –vs.- Md. Noor Hossain & another 56 DLR 53 held, in a suit for ejectment of a tenant third party, who is not claiming tenancy under the plaintiff is neither a necessary party nor a proper party.

12.         Small Cause Courts Act (IX of 1887),

         Section 15 of the aforesaid Act, does not confer any jurisdiction to the court in a S.C.C. suit to enter into the question of title in respect of the suit premises, except incidentally or to set aside the decree passed in a civil court.

13.         In the instant case, I have come across that the applicants were intended to become a party to the original SCC Suit No.01 of 2010 on the basis of co-sharership in the suit holding, and in this connection it is obvious to note that the predecessor of the applicants on the basis of her co-sharership by way of inheritance meanwhile instituted a partition suit and a separate declaratory suit challenging the sub-kabala deed of the plaintiff and both the two suits are pending for disposal.

14.               It is a fact that the above mentioned suits which are pending for disposal are separate proceedings and it would be decided on merit, but in the instant suit, I find that the learned trial court failed to appraise the actual proposition of law and facts and circumstances of the case. it is to be noted here that the decisions of our Apex Court as cited in the impugned order of the learned trial court viz. 2 MLR (AD)357 is not identical with the instant case and there is apparent distinguishable facts and feature of the case on which their lordships opined that addition of co-sharers in a proceedings of SCC suit is not illegal and here in that case it is observed by their lordships that, where the co-sharers are in possession of the suit premises, their application can be entertained under the ambit of Order I rule 10 of the Code of Civil Procedure, but in the instant case it is not the case of the applicants that they are in possession of the suit premises along with the defendant No. 1 and 2.

15.               Be that as it may, I am constrained to hold such a view that, in passing the impugned order the trial court was not justified and lawful.

16.               In the result, the Rule is made absolute without any order as to costs. The impugned order No.58 dated 15.07.2018 passed by the learned Senior Assistant Judge, Sadar, Dinajpur in SCC Suit No.01 of 2010 allowing the application for addition of party is hereby set aside.

17.               The order of stay granted earlier by this Court at the time of issuance of the Rule stands vacated.

18.               Communicate the judgment and order at once.
