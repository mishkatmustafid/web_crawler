JUDGMENT

 

Md. Moinul Islam Chowdhury, J: At the instance of the present plaintiff-respondent-petitioners, Tapsiruddin being dead his heirs Md. Golam Mostafa and others, this Rule has been issued calling upon the contesting opposite parties No. 1 to 5 to show cause as to why the impugned judgment dated 16.02.1987 and decree dated 26.2.1987 passed by the learned Sub-ordinate Judge, Jessore in Title Appeal No. 27 of 1979 reversing the judgment dated 25.09.1978 and decree dated 08.11.1978 passed by the Trial Court in Title Suit No. 145 of 1975 should not be set aside.

 

2.             The relevant facts for disposal of this Rule, inter-alia, are that the predecessor of the present petitioners as the plaintiff filed the Title Suit No. 145 of 1975 in the Court of the than Munsif, 3rd Court, Jessore for a specific performance of contract of an agreement entered by and between the parties regarding the suit land. The plaint contains that the suit land originally belonged to the defendant opposite party Nos. 3 and 5 Kali Pada Biswas and Aswini Kumar Biswas and also opposite party No. 4 Jitendra Nath Biswas. The opposite party Nos. 1 and 2 Abdul Khaleque (died on 02.03.1987), Abdus Samad and one Nogendra Nath who were in possession of the suit land measuring 72 decimals. The said defendant entered into a contract with the plaintiff to sale the suit land at taka 350/- (Taka three hundred fifty) and out of which taka 325/- (Taka three hundred twenty five) was paid as baina money and a written bainapatra was executed on 27.07.1966. Since the vendor defendants were Hindu believers, they had to obtain a clearance certificate for registering a sale deed under the provisions of law which could not be obtained. However, the present defendant opposite parties delivered the possession of the suit land to the present plaintiff petitioners. Subsequently the said vendor defendants sold the suit land measuring 36 decimals evenafter knowing fully well about the aforementioned baina. Later on, while the plaintiff petitioners requested the vendors to execute a register kabala deed they denied to do so in the year of 1381 B.S. Thus the suit was filed.

 

3.             The present opposite parties as the defendants contested the suit by filing two separate written statements. The present opposite party Nos. 3-5 as the defendant Nos. 1-3 contended that the suit was not maintainable as the suit was barred by limitation. It is further contended that  the suit land out of total 72 decimals 36 decimals of land was originally belonged to the present opposite party Nos. 3-5 and they were in possession of the suit land through Bargader being the present defendant opposite party No. 1 Abdul Khalque and they sold the suit land to the present opposite party No. 1 and 2 (defendant Nos. 4 and 5) and the possession was handed over to them. It is further contended that the present defendant opposite party Nos. 3-5 never entered into any contract for sale of the suit land by way of baina deed in favour of the present plaintiff petitioners and the baina was a forged and created document in order to grab the suit land.

 

4.             The present opposite party Nos. 1 and 2 as the defendant Nos. 4 and 5 filed a written statement contending, inter-alia, that they have purchased the suit land measuring 36 decimals at a consideration of taka 4000/- (four thousand) from the present opposite party Nos. 3-5 (defendant Nos. 1-3) by a registered sale deed dated 06.01.1975 as a bonafide purchaser for valuable consideration as they had no knowledge regarding any bainapatra as the defendant Nos. 1-3, opposite party Nos. 3-5, clearly denied as to the execution of any bainapatra and delivery of possession.

 

5.             After considering the evidence adduced and produced by the parties the suit was decreed by the learned trial court by his judgment and decree dated 25.09.1978. Being aggrieved the present opposite party Nos. 1 and 2 as the appellants preferred the Title Appeal  No. 27 of 1979 in the court of learned District Judge, Jessore which  was heard by the learned the than Sub-ordinate Judge, Jessore on transfer who allowed the appeal by the judgment and decree dated 26.02.1987. This revisional application has been filed challenging the said impugned judgment and this Rule was issued on 15.06.1987 which is more than 30(thirty) years back.

     

6.             Mr. Md. Abdur Rouf, the learned Advocate appearing for the present petitioners submits that the court of appeal below acted illegally and with material irregularity in reversing the findings of the trial court without cogent reasons and the court of appeal below acted illegally and with material irregularity in not following the provisions of order XL Rule 31 of the Code of Civil Procedure and thus committed an error.

 

7.             He further submits that the suit was not barred by limitation according to law of limitation as the time runs from the date of the requisite permission which enables the executant of the bainapatra to register the sale deed and in the present case the permission is a must and no  permission has been shown to have been received by the executant therefore limitation did not run upto the date of filing of the suit thus holding the suit to be barred by limitation by the court of appeal below is an error.

 

8.             The Rule has been opposed by the present opposite party Nos. 1 and 2 by filing a counter affidavit contending, inter-alia, that the learned trial court failed to consider the relevant evidence and document in particular exhinbit-1 being the baina dated 27.07.1966, therefore, came to a wrongful conclusion to decree the suit filed by the present plaintiff petitioners however, the learned appellate court below clearly expressed doubt about the execution of the said baina dated 27.07.1966 as well as handing over of the possession, as such, came to a lawful conclusion to allow the appeal by setting aside the judgment and decree passed by the learned trial court.

 

9.             Mr. Sk. Atiar Rahman, the learned Advocate, appearing with the learned Advocate Mr. Md. Amjad Hossain for the opposite party, submits that under Section 21 (g) of the Specific Relief Act a bainapatra or a contract for sale has to be performed by both the parties within 3(three) years from the date of its execution but the present bainaptra which is a collusion and forged document therefore it was not executed and registered a sale deed within 3(three) years and the present petitioners as the plaintiff filed the suit after 11(eleven) years which is  clearly beyond limitation period thus the Rule should be discharged. The learned Advocate further submits that the present opposite party Nos. 1 and 2 purchased the suit land from the present opposite party Nos. 3-5 by a deed dated 06.01.1975 (discussed in the judgment but not exhibited). However, pursuant to the said deed land was mutated in their names and the record of right published in their names in S. A. and R. S. They are paying Khajna to the Government, as such, the present Rule was obtained by the present petitioner by misleading the court which is liable to be discharged.

 

10.         Considering the above submissions made by the learned Advocates appearing for the respective parties and also considering the revisional application filed under Section 115(1) of the Code of Civil Procedure along with the annexures therein, in particular, the impugned judgment and also considering the materials in the lower court records, it appears to me that the present petitioners as the plaintiff filed the title suit for specific performance of contract being a bainanama dated 12 Sravan, 1373 B.S. corresponding to date 27.07.1966 (exhibit-1). The said baina contains a condition for registration of a sale deed after obtaining a clearance certificate from the relevant authority as the vendors were Hindu believers. However the present opposite party Nos. 3-5 as the defendant Nos. 1-3absolutly denied any execution of such bainapatra in order to sale a suit land measuring 36 decimals.

 

11.         In view of the above conflicting factual aspects between the parties, I have examined exhibit-1which was claimed to have executed on 27.07.1966. After perusing the said documents, I found some inconsistencies in the document itself and also considering the deposition of the PWs and DWs, I found that the attesting witnesses of the said baina were illiterate villagers who use to execute documents by way of their left thump impression (L.T.I.), however, the said document contains their signatures which are inconsist and doubtful for execution of the document, therefore, any claim of execution of a sale deed pursuant to the said bainapatra the defendant opposite party Nos. 3-5 were not obliged to perform as per the said purported document.

 

12.         Another most important aspect in his suit is that limitation period. The said bainapatra was claim to have executed in the year of 1966 but this suit was filed in the year of 1975 which is beyond the limitation period as it was filed after 11(eleven) years. In this regard Section 21of the Limitation Act contains the following provision:

 

“21. Contracts not specifically enforceable-The following contracts cannot be specifically enforced:-

(a)-------------------

(b)-----------------

(c)------------------

(d)------------------

(e)-------------------

(f)---------------------

 

(g) a contract the performance of which involves the performance of a continuous duty extending over a longer period than three years from its date;”

 

13.         From the above provision of law any baina cannot be a valid baina after the stipulated period mentioned in this statute therefore eventhough there was a baina it lost its legal characters after 3(three) years as such no specific performance of contract is entertainable  by any trial court, but the learned trial court came to a wrongful conclusion to decree the suit by ignoring the above vital provision of law.

 

14.         Regarding the subsequent transfer of the suit land in favour of the present opposite party Nos. 1 and 2 by the deed dated 06.01.1975. I have considered the discussion made by the courts below but the deed itself was not exhibited in the record. Nevertheless, the present opposite party Nos. 1 and 2 submitted a counter affidavit annexing the said document and after perusing the document, I am satisfied the transfer was completed by and between the present opposite party Nos. 3-5 and the opposite party Nos. 1 and 2. Therefore, after conclusion of the transfer from the vendors to the purchasers the present petitioners cannot claim any specific performance of contract on the basis of an invalid document.

 

15.         Now I am inclined to examine the judgment and decree passed by the learned courts below. The learned trial court came to a wrongful conclusion to decree the suit on the basis of the following findings:

 

“Again, the Bainapatra ext. I was executed on 27.07.2006 and the  deed Ext. 3 was executed and registered on 07.08.1963. So the possibility of creating Ext. 3 for the sake of Ext. 1 is ruled out since Ext. 3 is a prior document and alternatively the question of creating Ext. 1 for the sake of Ext. 3 does not arise. So at the demand of our prudence and conscience we are going to believe both the documents Ext. 1 and Ext. 3.”

 

16.         However, the learned appellate court below by reversing the judgment passed by the learned trial court came to a lawful conclusion to allow the appeal by setting aside the judgment and decree passed by the learned trial court on the basis of the following findings:

 

“In the absence of such specific period the limitation shall be counted from the alleged date of execution of bainapatra i.e. 27.7.1966. But the plaintiff instituted the suit on 06.02.1975 i.e. after long 11 years. On perusal of the evidence of  PWs. it appear that the asked the plaintiff to execute an register a sale deed in his favour from time to time but he has miserably failed to prove it that he asked them within every three years from the date of execution of the alleged bainapatra till the filing of the suit. So the suit is hopelessly barred by the law of limitation.”

 

17.               In view of the above discussions and also after perusal of the judgment and decree passed by the learned courts below I consider that the learned appellate court below committed no error of law by allowing the appeal preferred by the present petitioners and also by setting aside the  judgment and decree passed by the learned trial court which was passed on the wrong  findings of the law and fact.

 

18.               I am, therefore, not inclined to interfere into the judgment and decree passed by the learned appellate court below.

 

19.               Accordingly, I do not find merit in the Rule.

 

20.               In the result, the Rule is discharged.

 

21.               The interim order of stay granted at the time of issuance of the Rule upon the impugned judgment and decree is hereby recalled and vacated.

 

22.               The office is directed to communicate the judgment and order to the concern court immediately and the Section is also directed to send down the lower court record at once.
