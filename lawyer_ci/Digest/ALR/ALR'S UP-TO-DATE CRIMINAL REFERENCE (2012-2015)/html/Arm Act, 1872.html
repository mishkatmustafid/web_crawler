Sections 19A and 19(f) Evidence Act [i of 1872]

Section 114(g)

As for the present case, it appears that neither the seizure list witnesses nor the local witnesses were examined by the prosecution. In the context of the fact of cited case, relating to corroboration appears to be distinguishable from the present case. Under the circumstances there is no hesitation in holding that the prosecution has totally failed to prove the recovery of the arms and bullet from the exclusive control and possession of the petitioner. It appears that non-examination of important witnesses creates presumption under section 114(g) of the Evidence Act and raises doubt about the prosecution case and benefit of doubt would always go in favour of the accused petitioner. The prosecution has miserably failed to prove recovery of arms and bullet from the exclusive control and possession of the petitioner by adducing reliable and independent evidence before the Tribunal. The impugned judgment and order is quashed.@ Galcuta Hanif andHanif.-Vs-The State 1 ALR (HCDJ109.

Sections 19A and 19(f)

It transpires that the evidence of all the prosecution witnesses in respect of recovery of the arms from the convict-appellants are consistent, uniform and corroborative with each other in respect of all materials particulars. There is absolutely no reason to disbelieve the consistent and corroborative evidence of those competent witnesses having no reason whatsoever to depose falsely against the appellants.Abdul Azid alias Azid Dacoit -Vs.-The State. 4 ALR 2014(2) 351.
