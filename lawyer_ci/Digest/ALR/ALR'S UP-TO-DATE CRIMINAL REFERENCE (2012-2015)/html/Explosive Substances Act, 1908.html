                                      Explosive Substances Act (VI of 1908)

Section 3 - Explosion of a nature likely to endanger life or to cause serious injury.

It is crystal clear that accused Masum unlawfully or maliciously caused by an explosive substance an explosion of a nature likely to endanger life or to cause serious injury to person or property and because of the bomb explosion, many people were injured and one Mozibul Haque sustained a grievous splinter injury on his person resulting in his death. This being the position, it leaves no room for doubt that accused Masum committed the offence punishable under Section 3 of the Explosive Substances Act. Masumur Rahman - Vs. - The State 3 ALR(2014)(1) 312

Extra- judicial confession to be the basis of conviction needs corroboration by independent and disinterested witness.

Admittedly the disputed sarees were not recovered from the possession of the accused petitioner but these were recovered from the roof-top the bus.The assertion of the prosecution that the accused petitioner claimed the sarees as his own after these were seized by the police, invariably warranting criminal liability, is hardly a relievable story .In the absence of any corroboration to the evidence of the members of the recovery party, the impugned order of conviction cannot be sustained. Motahar Hossain - VS- The State 2 ALR(2013)(HCD) 49.

Further investigation or re-investigation

The police has filed charge sheet finding the allegation prima facie proved. It may be mentioned here that there is no bar in any law for further investigation or re­investigation of any case and for submission of charge sheet where, earlier, the police filed final report finding no evidence. In the present case since police, on investigation, found the allegation prima facie proved there is no bar in submission of charge sheet and also in accepting the same by the Court.Ms. Ok Kyung Oh-Vs.-Mr. Bo-Sun Park and another 6 ALR (AD) 2015 (2)143.

Facts and circumstances incompatible with the innocence of the accused invariably lead to the inference of his guilt.

When the facts and circumstances of the case are found totally incompatible with the innocence of the accused and there can be no reasonable hypothesis other than his guilt, conviction can be safely awarded to the accused.The State - Vs.- Monir Hossain alias Monir 2 ALR (2013)(HCD) 373.

Extenuating circumstances

Since a considerable period has been spent in the death cell and the sword of death has been hanging on the neck of the petitioner, who suffered from mental agony of death for the long period which may be considered as extenuating circumstances for commuting the sentence of death penalty to that of imprisonment for life. Monirul Islam @ Manir-Vs.- The State. 4 ALR (AD) 2014 (2) 156

Judicial decrees may not change the heart, but they can restrain the heartless.

Morality cannot be legislated, but behavior can be regulated. Judicial decrees may not change the heart, but they can restrain the heartless. Sukur Mahmood and others. -Vs.-The State. 4 ALR (AD) 2014 (2) 81

Failure to enhiit the 'falah' as material exhibit cannot make prosecution case doubtful.

Soon after the occurrence, accused petitioner who was carrying the 'falah' fled away from the place of occurrence with the 'falah' and as such, the failure of the prosecution in making the 'falah' as material exhibit cannot make its case doubtful. Shahjahan Khalifa - Vs.- The State 5 ALR (AD)2015(1) 55.

Fugitive from justice cannot seek justice without surrounding before a Court of law.

It is a settled principle of law that a fugitive from justice cannot get the protection of law. The accused did not surrender to the trial court after his bail was cancelled and warrant of arrest issued. He moved the High Court Division without surrendering there, and, therefore, the revisional petition ought not to have been entertained nor the Rule issued. The swearing of the affidavit was illegal. The revisional petition filed before the High Court Division shows that the respondent did not appear before the Commissioner of Affidavits and the cause title of the petition does not state that the petitioner was in custody, or was on bail, or appeared in person before the High Court Division. The issuance of the Rule was illegal as was the grant of bail, which was cancelled by the Appellate Division and warrant of arrest was directed to be issued. The State- - Vs.- Dr. Fazlur Rahman 5 ALR (AD)2015(1) 136.
