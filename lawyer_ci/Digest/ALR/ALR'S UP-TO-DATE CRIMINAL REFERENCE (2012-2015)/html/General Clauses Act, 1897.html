Section 26 - The offender cannot be punished twice for the same offence.

Where an act or omission constitutes an offence under two or more enactments, then the offender shall be liable to be prosecuted and punished under either or any of those enactments, but shall not be liable to be punished twice for the same offence. But the offence punishable under Section 302 of the Penal Code and the offence punishable under Section 3 of the Explosive Substances Act are not one and the same; rather they are distinct and separate offences. Askk they are distinct and separate offences. In the facts of the case of Section 26 of the General Clauses Act cannot be invoked. Masumur Rahman - Vs.- The State 3 ALR(2014)(1) 312.

Grounds of detention must not be vague, indefinite, unspecific and omnibus.

In the grounds of detention the detenu was described as a narcotics trader, illegal arms-holder, collector of illegal toll , extortionist and a terrorist but the grounds did not disclose any fact and material showing that he was an illegal arms-holder, toll collector and an extortionist. These grounds of detention were evidently quite vague and indefinite and did not give sufficient materials to the detenu enabling him to make an effective representation to the detaining authority against his order of detention. In such view of the matter , the initial order of detention passed against the detenu must be held illegal. Consequently, the subsequent order of the Government extending the detention for a further period of 90 days is equally illegal and unauthorized. Md. Shafiqul Islam -Vs.- The state 2 ALR(2013)(HCD) 196.
