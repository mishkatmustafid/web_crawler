Section 3 (2) - The Appellate Division does not find any illegality in their Appellate judgment. Hence the Review petition is dismissed The Appellate Division held that petitioner's ruthless Badr Bahini being instigated, suggested, provoked and incited by the petitioner had kidnapped and killed the intellectuals which was cold blooded savagery. Such barbaric gruesome brutal crimes which are comparable with the Hitler's Gas Chamber Genocide or Jalilianbag massacre. Does Islam permit killing of those unarmed people? While awarding the sentence, the Court must take into consideration the unbearable pains, tears rolling down the cheeks and sufferings of the widows and children of the victims who cried for getting justice for about 43 years. The barbaric gruesome and heinous crimes which under the petitioner's leadership his Badr Bahini committed is a revolt against the humanity. As leader of Badr Bahini the petitioner can not escape from the liability. It is the duty of the court to impose proper punishment depending upon the criminality and proportionate to the gravity of the offence. The Appellate Division does not find any wrong in the ultimate conclusion of the Tribunal which has been affirmed by the Appellate judgment. The Appellate Division' does not find any illegality in their  judgment. Hence the Review petition is dismissed.Ali Ahsan Muhammad Mujahid-Vs.-The Chief, Prosecutor, International Crimes Tribunal Dhaka,Bangladesh 6 ALR (AD) 2015 (2)154.

Inquest Report is the best evidence

Since the name of the accused appellant was not disclosed neither by P.W.I nor by P.W.I5 who were the eye witnesses of the occurrence at the time of inquest of the dead body by the P.W.I6 the investigating officer. Therefore the name of the accused appellant was omitted in the body of the inquest report. The delay of 8 hours in lodging the F.I.R. with Police station also remained unexplained. The prosecution witnesses are the local chairman and his men who after arrived at the place of occurrence on the same date after sun set then the informant lodged the F.I.R. by mentioning the name of the accused appellant for the first time suddenly. While the inimical to the accused appellant with the local chairman P.W.7 is admitted by the men of the local Chairman P.Ws. 4, 6, 8 and 9 including the conduct of the Chairman P.W.7 himself. The High Court Division find that the inquest report exhibit No. 7 of the dead body of the victim and its statement of the investigating officer P.W.I6 is the direct or primary evidence in the eye of law. Thus the same is the best evidence in the case and it is not proved as to be suspected and unreliable perfunctory or dishonest otherwise.The State. - Vs.- Abdul Khalek 6 ALR - 2015(2)54

Judicial confessions

The High Court Division does do not find any explanation from the side of the defence as to the safe departure of the victim Azim Uddin from the company of the accused Uzzal and Shamim after he was called away at 7:00 P.M. on 30.09.2004. It is admitted that the dead body of the victim was recovered from the sugarcane field on the following day (01.10.2004). The non-furnishing of any explanation, let alone the question of any reasonable explanation, as to the safe departure of the victim Azim from the company of the accused Uzzal and Shamim is indicative of their guilt corroborating their judicial confessions made to the Magistrate (P.W. 13). Md. Uzzal Hossain and others -Vs-The State 6 ALR - 2015(2)269.

Leniency in awarding punishment when not justified.

When the offence is not only heinous in nature but also shockingly cruel and pre­meditated, the offender is apt to be regarded as enemy of the society. In such a case, no leniency in the matter of sentence is called for. Shahid Ullah -Vs.- The State 3 ALR(2014)(1)(AD) 192.
