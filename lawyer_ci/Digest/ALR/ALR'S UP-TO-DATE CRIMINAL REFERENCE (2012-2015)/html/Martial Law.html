                                                                 Martial Law

There is no such law in Bangladesh as Martial Law and there is no such Authority as Martial Law Authority as such and if any person declares martial law, he will be liable for high treason against the Republic. Obedience to superior orders is no defence. M. Anwar Hossain -Vs.- Government of Bangladesh 2 ALR (2013)(HCD) 253.

The taking over of powers of the Government of the People's Republic of Bangladesh with effect form the morning of 15th August, 1975 by Khandakar Mustak Ahmed an usurper, placing Bangladesh under martial law and his assumption of office of the President of Bangladesh, were in clear violation of the Constitution, as such, illegal, without lawful authority. M. Anwar Hossain -Vs.- Government of Bangladesh 2 ALR (2013)(HCD) 253

In view of the sequence of events and the evidence on record it is evidently clear that the requirements of a "fair trial" were conspicuously missing in the case. The so-called regulation that engendered the so-called tribunal did not lay down the above- cited requirements. The trial was held in camera and as such it lacked public character. An oath of secrecy thrust upon all who took part in the trial process further devastated its acceptability and rendered the purported trial a clandestine exercise. M. Anwar Hossain -Vs.- Government of Bangladesh 2 ALR (2013)(HCD) 253.

Death sentence passed on Col. Taher was by far the most dreadful factor that totally and, indeed, conspicuously, infested the whole scenario with squalid stink because of the legislative schemes, and even the so-called Martial Law Regulations did not empowers the so-called Tribunal to pass death sentence on the date the impugned trial commenced. The purported death sentence was "thus" imposed refluxing sheer gun power in whimsical and ludicrous defiance of those laws that were promulgated by the military usurpers.M. Anwar Hossain -Vs.- Government of Bangladesh 2 ALR (2013)(HCD) 253

The sentence of death imposed on Col. Taher and the consequent execution is nothing but a cold-blooded murder on three counts: first, the execution being corried out without any legal sanction is murder, secondly it was a clear murder because even on the date of commencement of the so-called trial, none of the charges brought against the accused prescribed death sentence and finally, the death sentence was pre­determined by General Ziaur Rahaman well before the Special Martial Law Tribunal was constituted, at a meeting of the formation commanders. Irrespective of the question of the legal status of the so-called Tribunal, the events that prevailed within the canopy of the Dhaka Central Jail, can by no means, be termed as a judicially recognized criminal trial. It cannot even be termed as a drama of a criminal trial- It was a hoax in the protext of a judicial trial. It is thus clear that the excution of Col. Taher Bir-Uttom, was a cold-blooded marder engineered by General Ziaur Rahman. M. Anwar Hossain -Vs.- Government of Bangladesh 2 ALR (2013)(HCD) 253

The dastardly killing of Col. Taher, Bir-Uttam deprived Bangladesh of the service of a valiant freedom fighter whose contribution to our liberation War can hardly be exaggerated. This loathsome event will represent a blotted era in our history.M. Anwar Hossain -Vs.- Government of Bangladesh 2 ALR (2013)(HCD) 253

In view of the findings of the Hon'ble Court an obligation is cast upon the authorities to wipe out the names of all the accused and the spouses of petitioner Nos. 2 and 3 of Writ Petition No.7236/10 from the criminal records that came into being in consequence of the illegal conviction and to project them with unblemished image, while the execution of Col. Taher Bir-Uttam must be recorded as a clear cut extra-judicial murder. Order dismissing Major Ziauddin, Corporal Shamsul Hauque, Habilder Abdul Hai Mazumder (Writ Petition No. 1048/11) and Abdul Majid(Writ Petition No. 1059/11) must be reversed and they shall be deemed to be in service till their normal retirement age. M. Anwar Hossain -Vs.- Government of Bangladesh 2 ALR (2013)(HCD) 253.

Regard being had to the fact that Major Ziauddin, is a Freedom Fighter of national repute, with his commendable achievement during his cadetship, it is reasonable to believe that he would have risen at least to the rank of a Brigadier General had he not been illegally dismissed.M. Anwar Hossain -Vs.- Government of Bangladesh 2 ALR (2013)(HCD) 253.

Since promotion of the officer of the armed forces is not automatic, the authority is directed to pay Major Ziauddin the money he would have received as a Major along with all service benefits had he not been illegally dismissed from service till his normal retirement. The same shall apply to Corporal Shamsul Hauque, Habilder Abdul Hai Mazumder, and Md. Abdul Majid. M. Anwar Hossain -Vs.- Government of Bangladesh 2 ALR (2013) (HCD) 253.

It will be open to the authority to decorate Major Ziauddin with the superior rank and honour in recognition of his most brilliant contribution to our War of Liberation.M. Anwar Hossain -Vs.- Government of Bangladesh 2 ALR (2013) (HCD) 253.
