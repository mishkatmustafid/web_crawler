                                       Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961

                                                     [VIII of 1961]

Section  6(1) (5)  

Any man who contracts another marriage meaning that it is the man who contracts the 2nd marriage without permission of the Arbitration Council shall be liable for punishment for such offence but under this section the abettor cannot be proceeded with. Since the said Ordinance has not made any provision providing punishment for abetment of the said offence, the abettor, who-ever he may be, can not be proceeded against for alleged abetment of offence punishable under section 6 (5) of the said Ordinance. Abdulla Mamoon (Rabin). - Vs. - Md. Mahtab Uddin. 3 ALR(2014)(1) 366.

 

Sections 6(5) and 7 - Remanding the case to enable the complainant /respondent to adduce and examine more witnesses.

The High Court Division is swayed to the finding that the learned Appellate Court below was absolutely right to hold that there was no divorce between the accused and the complaint's as provisions in section 7 of the Muslim Family Law Ordinance 1961, was not complied with. The High Court Division also note with approval the learned Appellate Court below's finding that the complainant was deprived of the opportunity to adduce enough witnesses to prove her claim, particularly by adducing witness and papers to settle the issue as to whether or not Arbitration Council's permission was obtained. The High Court Division is of view, in the attendant circumstances, the Appeal Court below entertained no error by remanding the case to enable the complainant /respondent to adduce and examine more witnesses.  

Md. Del war Hossain - Vs.- The State 6 ALR- 2015(2)148
