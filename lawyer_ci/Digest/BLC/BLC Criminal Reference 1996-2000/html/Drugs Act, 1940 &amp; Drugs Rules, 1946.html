Drugs Act, 1940 & Drugs Rules, 1946

Citation: 3 BLC 22

Case Year: 1940

Subject: Drugs

Delivery Date: 1970-01-01

 

 

 

Drugs Act [XXIII of 1940]


Sections 22, 23 and 25—

The Inspector while exercising his power under section 22 of the Drugs Act inspected the store and took samples of the drug namely, the “Ergotan Tablet’, and sent the sample to the Government Analyst for analysis but the owner of the shop and store having failed to produce any warranty neither samples nor the report of the analyst could be supplied to the petitioners and as such there had not been any contravention of any provision of sections 23 and 25 of the said Act.

Square Pharmaceuticals Limited and another vs Government of Bangladesh, and another 3 BLC 22.


Drugs Rules, 1946


Rule 44—

In exercise of the power under Rule 44 of the Drugs Rules, 1946 the show cause notice was issued to the petitioners who gave reply but the respondents found it unsatisfactory and the licence of the petitioners was suspended temporarily and in taking such action no provision of the Drugs Act, 1940 has been violated.

Square Pharmaceuticals Limited and another vs Government of Bangladesh, and another 3 BLC 22

 

Rule 44 (b)—

The remedy by way of appeal provided in rule 44(b) of the Drugs Rules, 1946 having not been exhausted the petitioners are not entitled to invoke writ jurisdiction.

Square Pharmaceuticals Limited and another vs Government of Bangladesh, and another 3 BLC 22.
