Sections 2 & 5—Per Md Joynal Abedin ] (disagreeing in part): On due and careful con­sideration of the relevant provisions of the Army Act, the Army Act as a whole or in part cannot be made applicable to hold the trial for such a huge and staggering number of accused persons spreading over 40 other locations in the country by giving retrospec­tive operation in the absence of such empow­erment in the Army Act itself. Hence any trial under the Army Act of the accused BDR per­sonnel by issuing notification under section 5(1) of the Army Act will be ultra vires of the Army Act itself. Special Reference No. 1 of 2009

 

Sections 2, 5 and 8—Per MA Matin, J (delivering the main Judgment) : Since opinion given under Article 106 is not a judgment or law declared it has no binding effect on future litigation and therefore, the question of prejudice does not arise. The provision of sec­tion 2(1) d(i) of the Army Act also cannot bring BDR personnel within the mischief of the Army Act and therefore BDR men cannot be made subject to Army Act, in other words, the provisions of Army Act cannot be made applicable to BDR personnel by taking resort to either 2(l)(c) or (d) of the Army Act. This follows that our answer to question A of para 17 of the Reference is in the negative.

Sub-section (2) of section 5 speaks of the consequence and manner of application of the notification contemplated by sub-sec­tion^) of section 5 of the Act. The expression in sub-section (2) "shall have effect" and in I sub-section (3) "these provision shall be exer-Jcised" are sufficient to explain that the notifi­cation contemplated by section 5(1) is a prospective one i.e. to be effective from the date of the notification. The section does not give any retrospective operation of such noti­fication either by express language or by implication. Since section 5(1) does not authorise giving retrospective effect to the lotification contemplated by section 5(1) of ! Army Act, Government by notification not give retrospective effect to it so as to bply such notification against BDR involved | the incidents of 25th and 26th February, 09. This follows that our answer to ques-ion B of para. 17 of the reference is in the negative. Special Reference No. 1 of2009 15 BLC (AD) 1.

 

 

Sections 2 and 8—Per ABM Khairul Haqite J (agreeing): It is clear that on the 25th and 26th February, the BDR personnel were not subject to Army Act, rather, they were under the ambit of the BDR Order as envis­aged under Article 3 of the Order. It is true that they are members of a disciplined force but simply being members of a disciplined force would not bring them within the ambit of the Army Act. It is possible to apply all or any provisions of the Army Act to any force raised and maintained in Bangladesh, such as the BDR, by a notification, but only prospec­tively and certainly not retrospectively. Special Reference No. 1 of 2009 15 BLC (AD) 1.
