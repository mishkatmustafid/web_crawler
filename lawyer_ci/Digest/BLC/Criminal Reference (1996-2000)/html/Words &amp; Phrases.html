Words & Phrases

 

Affray and Public Place—

 

The word 'affray' is derived from the French word i.e. to terrify. An 'affray' is an offence against the public peace because it is committed in a public place and is likely to cause general alarm and disturbance. 'Public Place' is a place where the public go, no matter whether they have a right to go or not.

 

State vs Md Zillur Rahman and others 4 BLC 241.

 

Applicability of Quranic Law in a sex-offence case—

 

This High Court Division has already held in another case that the provisions of our Constitution indicate that Quranic injunctions shall have to be followed strictly and without any deviation. As the Quranic Law declares that the testimony of a woman in a case questioning her chastity must be accepted finally as against the testimony of the accuser. The underlying principle of this Law is applicable in a sex-offence case.

 

Jahangir Hossain vs State 1 BLC 292.

 

Bail and Anticipatory Bail—

 

Per Latifur Rahman J : The word bail is generally meant a temporary release of the arrested or imprisoned person upon providing surety with the undertaking that his/her presence on a particular day or place is secured. Anticipatory bail means bail in anticipation of arrest. The distinction is that bail is granted after arrest and anticipatory bail is granted in anticipation of arrest, and therefore, effective at the very moment of arrest. In other words, unlike a post- arrest order of bail, it is a pre-arrest order of bail.

 

State vs Abdul Wahab Shah Chowdhury 4 BLC (AD) 195.

 

"Cognizance"   and   "Prosecution"— Meaning of—

 

Cognizance means taking notice of an offence by a Criminal Court initiating a judicial proceeding against the offender in respect of that offence or taking steps to see whether there is any basis for initiating any such judicial proceeding. On the other hand, prosecution means bringing specific accusations against the accused before the court which, if satisfied, frames a formal charge and calls upon the accused to answer the same and proceeds with his trial.

 

Abdul Gafur vs State 1 BLC 48.

 

"Control"    and    "possession"—

 

The word "Control" means effective "control" may be essential for "possession" and unless one has control over an article he may not be said to be in "possession" of it but one can have "Control" over an article without being in "possession".

 

Sukkur AH Kha vs State 3 BLC 206.

 

Falsus  Uno  Falsus  Omnibus—

 

It  is well-settled that the maxim "Falsus Uno Falsus Omnibus" does not apply in our country.

 

State vs Ranjit Kumar Mallik 2 BLC 211.

 

General Power of Attorney—

 

Where there is a general power-of-attorney authorising the power-of-attorney holder to sign complaint and appear and act on behalf of the grantor in all courts the attorney can do everything on behalf of the grantor including signing and presenting the petition of complaint. Therefore signing of the petition of complaint by and examination under section 200 CrPC by attorney were valid and proper and there was nothing wrong or illegal in taking cognizance in such circumstances.

 

Tamizul Hague vs Anisul Hague 1 BLC (AD) 169.

 

"Inherent"—

 

What is 'inherent' is an inseparable incident of a thing or an institution in which it inheres. This rule has its source in the maxim quado lex aliquid alicui concedit, concoders videtur id sine quo ipsa esse non potest which means that "when the law gives anything to anyone it gives also all those things without which the thing itself could not exist."

 

Nazrul Islam alias Amirul Islam vs State 3 BLC 246.

 

Legislature should incorporate a section in the Code of Criminal Procedure for granting anticipatory bail alike India—Per Latifur Rahman J : Since there is a Law Commission in Bangladesh the matter may be referred by the Government before the Law Commission for incoiporating a section in the Code of Criminal Procedure for granting anticipatory bail as has been done by the Law Commission of India by inserting section 438 in the Indian Criminal Procedure Code. By such legislation there will be a specific statutory sanction of granting anticipatory bail with positive conditions and directions.

 

State vs Abdul Wahab Shah Chowdhury 4 BLC (AD) 195.

The con-temner working in the Judicial Munshikhana dared to suppress notices sent by this Court for service upon the concerned parties in collusion with the accused persons and when orders granting bails are issued from this Court concerned parties are harassed and if the office of the Deputy Commissioner does not act in right earnest then administration of criminal justice by this Court cannot run smoothly.

 

Almas Ali vs Sohrab Miah & others 2 BLC 66.

 

Martial Law—

 

It is well settled that Martial Law is not a part of the constitutional scheme of this country. It is an extra constitutional dispensation. It is a temporary measure, a short term arrangement. It meets only an interim need. When it leaves, it usually legalises all past actions for purposes of immunity, with the tacit acknowledgment that its interference with the constitutional process is an aberration and needs to be condoned, But while leaving, the Martial Law does not leave a train and it is good as long as it lasts, but with its departure it no longer casts a shadow upon the ordinary laws of the land.

 

Saidur Rahman vs State 1 BLC 135.

 

Natural Justice—

 

This Court has power to see whether forum for review followed the proper procedures while deciding the applications for review of the petitioners that the principles of natural justice were observed by the said forum.

 

Shah (Md) vs Secretary to the President, People's Republic of Bangladesh and ors 1 BLC 8.

 

Our Code of Criminal Procedure and the Penal Code require to be redrafted as has been done by the Indian Parliament—

 

The Code of Criminal Procedure does not have any section regarding sentencing reflection and on the contrary by fixing minimum sentence which must be awarded to a guilty some penal sections prohibit to some extent such reflection. If a bullet is recovered from the possession of a boy of sixteen years his minimum punishment as prescribed in the Arms Act is ten years' rigorous imprisonment but the young age and the nature of the offence is in favour of rehabilitative treatment. The discretion of awarding sentences in such cases may not be taken away from the courts by the legislature. Section 75 of the Penal Code prescribes enhanced punishment for certain offences after previous conviction but the High Courts always held that section 75 should not be applied mechanically when the Indian Parliament has already redrafted the Code of Criminal Procedure by incorporating the provisions of giving opportunity to a convict to make his representation on the proposed sentence. They have enacted law giving power to the Courts to release certain offenders after admonition or on probation of good conduct. To harmonise with the modern trend our laws need be redrafted.

 

Nidan AH (Md) vs State 4 BLC 264.

 

Per incuriam—

 

The expression "per incuriam" is a Latin word which means through inadvertence or through want of care. According to PS Osborn's, a concise law dictionary, "A decision of the court is not a binding precedent if given per incuriam, that is, without the court's attention having been drawn to the relevant authorities". A decision can be said generally to be given per incuriam when the Court has decided a case in ignorance of a law applicable in the case or when the Court has decided a case through oversight of law applicable in the case, which were not considered and had it been considered the result would have been otherwise.

 

Abul Kalarn Khan vs Reaz Morshed and another 5 BLC 528.

 

Ramdao and dagger—

 

These are generally dangerous weapons of offence and are usually kept and maintained by criminally inclined people for criminal purposes and ramdao and daggers are not ordinary village household implements.

 

Ismail and another vs State 2 BLC (AD) 37.

 

Ratio decidendi—

 

The ratio decidendi of the case of Mahtabuddin Ahmed vs Principal Secretary, President's Secretariat and others reported in 42 DLR 1 is that if any application for review filed by any affected persons was pending on the date of withdrawal of Martial Law the affected person's right to have his application for review decided subsisted and accordingly, direction was given to create a forum for disposing of the application for review filed by such persons which still remained undecided.

 

Shah (Md) vs Secretary to the President, People's Republic of Bangladesh and ors 1 BLC 8.

 

Rule disposed of—

 

It appears that the petitioner is an old man and professor of a university suffering from ailment and is not able to go to Gopalganj. Considering the nature of the case, the case may be withdrawn from the Magistrate Court, Gopalganj to the Court of Chief Metropolitan Magistrate, Dhaka, where the petitioner must surrender and obtain bail and accordingly the Rule is disposed of with a direction.

 

Dr Ahmed Sharif vs State and another 1 BLC 563.

 

Suspicion—

 

Suspicion or doubt, however strong it might be, it cannot take the place of evidence. If there be any want of legal evidence or there be slightest doubt as to the involvement of the accused in the crime he cannot be convicted.

 

Fazlu alias Md Fazlur Rahman and others vs State 1 BLC 558.

 

Suspicion—

 

It is well-settled that suspicion however strong is not substitute for proof. The mind is apt to take a pleasure in adapting circumstances to one another and even straining them a little to force them to form parts of one connected whole. The trial Court failed to distinguish between evidence and suspicion. Hence, the impugned conviction cannot be sustained.

 

Swapan vs State 1 BLC 537.
