CRUELTY TO WOMEN (DETERRENT PUNISHMENT) ORDINANCE, 1983

(LX OF 1983)

Section—5

The possession of a woman obtained through import or export or sale or hire or disposal in any manner with the intent that such woman might be used or employed for the purpose of prostitution or for any immoral purposes, constitutes the offence of trafficking in woman. The two explanations appended to section 5 of the Ordinance make it abundantly clear that the possession of such a woman until the contrary is proved shall be presumed that such possession was for the purpose of prostitution.

In the absence of any evidence to show that the possession of the victim was obtained by the accused persons for the purpose of prostitution or for any immoral purpose, section 5 of the Ordinance is not attracted.

Motaleb and others Vs. The State, 14 BLD (HCD) 286.

 

Section—-6

To attract of the offence under section 6 of the Ordinance, there must be causing of death or grievous hurt or attempt to cause death or grievous hurt to a woman for dowry. Causing of simple hurt or attempt to cause simple hurt does not bring the offence within the mischief of this section.

Dr. Atikur Rahman Vs. The State, 14BLD(HCD)303

 

Section—6

‘Dowry’ under section 6 of the Ordinance not only includes money etc. agreed to be paid before or at the time of the marriage but also money etc. demanded afresh after the marriage for continuing the marriage already solemnized. So, any valuable property, which includes money, demanded by the husband or his relations from the wife or her relations afresh after the marriage is dowry within the meaning of section 6 of the Ordinance.

Ajit Kumar Pramanik and others Vs. Bokul Rani Pramanik and others, 14BLD (HCD)335

Section—9

From the FIR it is clear that a prima facie case of abetment punishable under section 9 of the Ordinance has been made out. The Special Tribunal on consideration of the available materials on record and on hearing the parties framed charge of abetment against the appellant. The Special Tribunal has not committed any illegality in framing the charge.

Anowara Begum Vs Most. Sultana Jeshmin Khan (Shoapa) and State, 17BLD (HCD)291.
