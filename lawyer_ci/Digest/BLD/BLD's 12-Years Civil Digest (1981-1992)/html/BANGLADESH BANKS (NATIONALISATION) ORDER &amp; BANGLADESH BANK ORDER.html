BANGLADESH BANKS (NATIONALISATION) ORDER (P.O. 26 OF 1972)

 

Article—23

Service matter—Bye-laws containing service rules framed by bank prior to promulgation of P.O. 26 of 1972 have no statutory force—Employees of bank cannot invoke writ jurisdiction in respect of violation such byelaws—But employees cannot be deprived of the principles of natural justice—Constitution of Bangladesh. 1972, Article. 102.

A.T.M. Zahirul Huque Vs. Secretaiy, Ministry of Finance and others; 1 BLD (AD) 236.

Ref: 29DLR (SC) 41.

 

BANGLADESH BANK ORDER, 1972 (P.O. 127 OF 1972)

 

Article—82

Whether any right is ve.ted in an employee under Accommodation Rules—Accommodation Rules of Bangladesh Bank, in absence of any regulation, cannot be treated as binding statutory rules and they shall be considered as rules for guidance giving no vested right to any employee—Accommodation Rules having no force of law, the plaintiff has no prima facie case for an order of temporary injunction.

Md. Nurul Haque Khan and others Vs. Bangladesh Bank; 6BLD (HD) 3

Ref. I BLD 129; 1 BLD (AD) 103; 28 DLR SC) 415; 29DLR (SC) 41; PLD 1964 (SC) 21.
