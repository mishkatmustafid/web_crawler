BANGLADESH LEGAL PRACTITIONERS AND BAR COUNCIL ORDER (P.O. 46 OF 1972) AND BANGLADESH LEGAL PRACTITIONERS AND BAR COUNCIL RULES, 1972

Articles—33, 34 and 35

Tribunal set up under Bangladesh Legal Practitioners and Bar Council Order is not a civil Court, but shall be so considered for some specific purposes mentioned in the Order—Although the Tribunal may have the trappings of a court—It is not really a Court of law which a civil court is.

Fariduddin Ahmed Vs. Secretary Bangladesh Bar Council and another 1 BLD (HCD) 428.

 

Articles—34 and 35

Stay proceedings before the Tribunal—. An application for stay of a proceeding before a Tribunal pending disposal of a suit between the same parties over the same matter is mis- conceived and not maintainable—Code of Civil Procedure (V of 1908),

Fariduddin Ahmed Vs. Secretary, Bangladesh Bar Council and another, 1 BLD (HCD) 428.

 

Articles—34 and 36

Legal Practitioners and Bar Council Order—Disciplinary proceeding against an Advocate—Suspensioñ of sanad under Rule 51 pending enquiry is not a punishment under Article 32 of the Order—Notice to show cause why sanad shall not be suspended pending enquiry is not an order under Article 34 and therefore not appealable under Article 36 of the Order.

Fariduddin Ahmed Vs. Secretary, Bangladesh Bar Council and another; 1 BLD (HCD) 428.

 

Article—32(1)

Punishment for professional misconduct—Question of severity of punishment— considering that the appellant was of fairly advanced age and was in bruised health the Tribunal decided to award him the intermediate punishment of suspension for 8 years—We do not find any reason why the intermediate punishment should now be relegated to the softest one—The appellant has no regrets, no remorse—He has chalked out a path of self justification—We find no extenuating circumstances to reduce the sentence.

Harun-Ar-Rashid Talukdar Vs. District Manager, Pubali Bank, Jainalpur Branch and another, 8 BLD (HCD) 216.

 

Clause (1) Chapter II

Professional misconduct — Advocate’s conduct with regard to his client—The appellant (Advocate) sought to create a title in him by purchasing the suit property under attachment at a time when he was the engaged Advocate of the Bank conducting the execution case—To acquire an interest adverse to the interest of the client amounts to professional misconduct.   Ibid.
