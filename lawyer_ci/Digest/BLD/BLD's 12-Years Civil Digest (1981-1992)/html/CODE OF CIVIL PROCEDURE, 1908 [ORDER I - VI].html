Order 1 Rules 3 and 4

Joinder of parties

Plaintiff can join all persons as defendants against whom .any right to relief in respect of the same cause of action, arising against them involves, common questions of law and fact.

Bangladesh Railway Vs. MIs. Sinafracht Chartering and Shipbreaking Corporation through their local agents United Liner Agencies and others, 5 BLD (AD) 115

 

Order 1 Rule 3 and 4

The terms proforma means for the sake of form only but this expression does not appear in the Code of Civil Procedure. A defendant might be a necessary party whose presence is a must or he may be a proper party w-ho though not a necessary party, yet a party whose presence before the Court may be required for proper adjudication of the matters in issue. If the proforma defendant is for the sake of form only, the decree would not bind him—A party

though described as a proforma in a suit would be bound by a decree passed in his presence after he has contested the suit by filing a written statement in which an adverse claim is made against the property in suit.

 

Power Development Board Vs. sonali Bank, 9 BLD29

Ref. A.I.R. 1947 (Bom) 217; A.I.R. 1960 (SC) 388—Cited.

 

Order 1 Rule 3 and 10

Necessary Party

In a suit for declaration that a decree is null and void, none excepting the persons who are parties to the decree and who are parties in the suit in which the decree was passed and their legal representatives are necessary parties—.In such a suit abandoned property authority is not a necessary party.

 

Md. Hanif and others Vs. Akhtar Rahim and others, 2 BLD (HCD) 160.
