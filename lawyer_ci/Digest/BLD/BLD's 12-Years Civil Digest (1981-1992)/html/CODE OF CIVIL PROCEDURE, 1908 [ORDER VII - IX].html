Order VII Rule 11

Rejection of plaint—The power should be invoked at the earliest opportunity.

It is true that the Court is competent to reject the plaint at any stage of the suit, but it should be done at the earliest opportunity to save the parties from unnecessary expenses and to avoid prolongation of the dispute— Although there is no hard and fast rule as to when an application for rejection of plaint is to be filed, but having regard to the scheme of things and the order in which the law makers have placed this particular provision in the statute book, the provision should be invoked by the defendant at the earliest and not after the suit had reached the stage of peremptory hearing.

Alhaj Md. Yasuf Sardar Vs. Md. Hossam Khan and another, 1BLD (HCD) 361
