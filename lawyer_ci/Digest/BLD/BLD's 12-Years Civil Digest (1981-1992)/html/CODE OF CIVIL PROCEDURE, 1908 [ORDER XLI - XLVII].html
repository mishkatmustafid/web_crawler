Order XLVII Rule 1

Power of review — Extent of the jurisdiction — Jurisdiction of a Court for review is not that of a Court of appeal Although the right is a substantive right like a of appeal, unless the applicant can bring his case within the ambit of the Order he is not entitled to invoke review jurisdiction -—- There is no scope to give a party an opportunity of rehearing of the case on merit in the garb of a review petition — “Mistake or error apparent on the face of the record” is an error which of not require any elaborate argument.

Khairuzzaman and others. Vs. Nur Hosain. 9BLD (HCD) 453

Order XLVII

Exparte decree — Relief against such decree — Apart from the remedy of getting

a decree set aside by filing an application under order 9 rule I 3 C.P.C. there are other remedies open by way of appeal review and also independent suit on grounds of fraud.

Order XLVII Rule 1

Decision—An order upon an application under Order XLVII Rule I C.P.C. is not a decision within the meaning of sub-section (4) of section 29 of the Local Government (Union Parishads) Ordinance, 1983 and does not relate to certain dispute on merits, more so in view the expression final’ used in sub-section (3) and sub-section (4) of section 29 of the ordinance.

Abdus Salam vs. Sirajul Islam, 1IBLD (HCD) 492

Ref. 7BLD 114—Cited.

 

Order XLVII Rule 1

Remedies against ex parte decree

A person aggrieved by an exparte decree zapursue three remedies in the Courts

(I) by filing an application under Order IX Rule 13 for setting aside the ex parte decree, (2) by filing an application for review under Order XLVII Rule I. or (3) by filing an appeal under section 96 of the C.P.C. Of the three remedies. the remedy by way ol an appeal is the widest when an appeal is dismissed he will have no other remedy available to him.

Md. Abdul Mannan Vs. Jobeda Khatun and others, 12BLD (AD) I

Order XLVII Rule I

Review—Order XLVII Rule I covers both cases of review on the grounds of mistake or error of fact as well as law. Provided it is apparent on the face of the record— An error apparent either in the order itself or any other document which is incorporated in that order — It must be an evident error which does not require extraneous matter to its incorrectness.

Ali and other Vs. Mohainniad A/i and others, 12 BLD(HCD)387

 

Order XLVII Rule 7(2)

Limitation in review

Provision for restoration of the review application is subject to Article I 60 of the Limitation Act — The alplicant is to satisfy within time that he was prevented by sufficient cause from appearing on the fixed date — The Court has got no power to extend the period prescribed for filing an application for restoration — Review is an extraordinary remedy — He who seeks review of a Court’s order must be vigilant.

M.S. Zaman and Co. Vs. Bangladesh Sugar and Food Industries Corporation and others, 7BLD (AD) 209
