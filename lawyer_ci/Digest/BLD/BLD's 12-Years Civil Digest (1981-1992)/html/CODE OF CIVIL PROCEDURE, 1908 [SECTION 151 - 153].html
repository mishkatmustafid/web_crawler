Sections—151, 152 and 153

Partition Suit—Formal defect in preliminary decree does not nullify the work of the Pleader Commissioner

Defect in preliminary decree can be corrected ex-debito justifies.

It will be plainly an act of injustice if for errors of the Court the entire work of the Pleader Commissioner is nullified; more so when in allocating shams he has not gone beyond the order portion of the judgment. The Pleader Commissioner’s report cannot be set aside on the ground that he lacked any jurisdictional basis. His jurisdiction under the circumstances is unaffected by the formal defects in the preliminary decree which can be corrected ex-debito justifies—Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (V of 1908), Order 26 Rule 14.

Md. Wasiq Khan Vs. Md. Sabiq Khan and others, 1 BLD (HCD) 46.

Ref. AIR. 1922(PC)269; L.R. 3 PC.(475); A.I.R. 1957 (M.B.) 17; 36 C.W.N. 665; 30 DLR(SC)30—Cited.

Compromise in revision

Parties in a civil entitled to effect compromise.

The Court for ends of justice may also record compromise under section 15 1 of the Code where the revision application having not arises out of an original miscellaneous proceeding. The provision of Or. 23. R.3 of the Code does not apply—Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (V of 1908), Section. 115, 141 and Or. 23 R3.

Section—151

Banking Companies Ordinance

Property attached by the Company Judge—district Judge held auction on the direction of the Company Judge—District Judge not competent to question the attachment and to set aside the auction sale.

The attachment was made under orders of the Company Court and the sale was also held in pursuance of the direction of that Court. Under section 61 of the Banking Companies Ordinance the Company Court has exclusive jurisdiction to decide any claim by or against Banking Company. Section 63 of the Ordinance empowers the Court to entertain an application even after the statutory period of limitation.

The executing Court was required to proceed with the matter taking the decree as a valid one and capable of execution. It was not open to the executing Court to decide whether the decree was a valid one or not. If there was a claim on the attached property by any one it was for him to move the High Court Division and it is for this Court to investigate and decide the issue. The District Judge illegally questioned the attachment of the property by the High Court Division and acted beyond his jurisdiction in setting aside the auction sale.

In re Kurnud Ranjan Sarker and others, 1 BLD (HCD) 251.

Ref. AIR. l962(Cal)86; 20 DLR 982: 27 DLR(SC) 133; I BLD96.

 

Section—151

Stay of proceeding before the Tribunal

An application for stay of proceeding before a Tribunal pending disposal of a suit between the same parties over the same matter is misconceived and not maintainable— Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (V of: 1908), S. 10; Bangladesh Legal Practitioners and Bar Council Order. 1972, (P.O. 46 of 1972), Article 34 and 35.

Fariduddin Ahmed Vs. Secretary, Bangladesh Bar Council and another, 1 BLD (HCD) 428.

 

Section—15 1

Restitution—Restitution in the case of an order in a pre-emption case—Order in such a case may have the force of a decree but it is not included in section 144 C.P.C. Restitution allowed in such a case is therefore treated as one having been passed under section 151 C.P.C.

If any law specifically provides for restitution such as section 144 of the Code of Civil Procedure restitution must be granted by the Court under this section, its terms being mandatory. But this section is not exhaustive: it only defines the procedure for restitution in the case of a reversal of a decree. In the case of reversal of an order the same remedy should be given by the Court in the exercise of its inherent power under section 151 C.P.C.—Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (V of 1908).

Abdul Hamid Vs. Abdul Jabbar, 2 BLD (AD) 172.

Ref. A.LR. 1952 (Nag) 190; 53 C.L.J. 49; 26 C.W.N. 408; 34 C.W.N. 746;. 54 C.W.N. 896; A.I.R. 1922(PS) 269—Cited.

Court’s inherent power

Inherent power of the Court

Section—151

The Court, whether can set aside its own order, whether has power to maintain its jurisdiction.

The Court cannot set aside its own order passed on merit unless the Court has jurisdiction to do so. The Court has inherent power to maintain its jurisdiction and render it effective on behalf of the litigants. Generally speaking the Court has the power to control its own proceedings, at least during the term of the Court, or so long as those are pending.

Right of review, whether is a statutory right and a creature of law

A right of review is a statutory right and a creature of law. Sections—152 and 34(2)

Amending the decree

Court became functus officio in respe of further amending the judgment and de cree—Grounds stated in the petition and the ground for allowing it for amending the decree are not covered by the legal provision, rather it violates the provision—Since the decree is for a definite sum of money and the judgment and decree are silent regarding the payment of further interest, the Court must be deemed to have refused it—If the petitioner was aggrieved by the judgment and decree, the remedy certainly did not lie in filing an application under section 152 C.P.C. The case reported in A.I.R. 1961 Orissa 18 is distinguishable as in that case the judgment made a provision for further interest.

 

Liakat Rice and Oil Mills and another Vs. Janata Bank, 4 BLD (HCD) 120

Sections—152, 151 and 97

Amendment of decree—when it can be done?

The Court can always amend the decree if there is a mistake that had crept in for bringing the decree in conformity with the judgment.

Section—152

Amendment of order

The order of sentence contains some apparent mistake—The contemner can be tenced to S.I. but. in default he cannot be asked to pay any fine—He can be fined but there must be some order of S.I. in default of payment of fine—The order of sentence gainst contemner No. 1 is therefore corrected.

 

M: A. Monir Vs. The State, 8 BLD (HCD) 434

Section—153 read with Order 6 Rule 17

Amendment of the plaint—changing. averments do not necessarily mean changing nature and character of suit.

Whether the amendment of the plaint that a party in a suit for partition is not an heir of the original owner can be allowed?— Amendment of the plaint changing the averment regarding a party will not change the nature and character of the suit—A change in one of the links in the geneology has not the effect of changing the fundamental character of the plaint— Amendment of the plaint for getting out of the admission—Such amendment can be allowed if it is necessary for the determination of the real question of controversy between the parties.

 

Shahiman Bewa Vs. Shafiruddin Ahmed and others, 6 BLD (HCD)58
