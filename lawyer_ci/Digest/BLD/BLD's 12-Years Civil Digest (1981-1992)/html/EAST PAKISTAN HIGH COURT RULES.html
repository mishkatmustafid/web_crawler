EAST PAKISTAN HIGH COURT RULES

 

Rules 7 and 9

Addition of party — Addition of party in a writ petition — Whether a tenant allotted in possession of a property should be impleaded as respondent in a writ petition challenging taking over of the property as abandonment property and praying for restoration of possession — The Court may while hearing a writ petition or even before such hearing order a person to be added as a party to the writ petition — The discretion to add a person as a party is that of the Court — The Court alone is to decide whether it is necessary to add some person as a party to the proceeding — When a tenant has seen in occupation of the property since before the property was included in the use of abandoned properties it is to be decided whether he would he ordered to be evicted from the premises after the property is declared to be not abandoned property and as such discretion exercised by the Court in adding him as a party cannot be disturbed — But an allottee inducted by the Government cannot continue in the premises as allottee of the Government after the property is declared not to be an abandoned property and as such the allottee cannot be made a party in the proc ceding.

Md. Ayub Vs. Bangladesh and others; 7BLD(AD)243
