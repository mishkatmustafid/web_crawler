ENGINEERING AND TECHNOLOGICAL UNIVERSITY EMPLOYEES ORDINANCE

(XXX VI OF 1.61) SECOND STATUTES (EFFICIENCY AND DISCIPLINE)

 

 Statutes — 6, 7 and 15

Suspension — Whether an order of suspension can be passed against an employee of Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology until and unless a formal charge- sheet under statute 7 is served upon the delinquent employee — From the language of clause (2) of statute 15 it cannot be said that the authority is precluded from passing an order of suspension unless and until a formal charge sheet under statute 7 is served upon a delinquent employee — Clause(2) of statute 15 applies when the “conduct of a delinquent employee is under investigation of a charge’ — — In the statutes care has been taken to describe a formal charge as a “charge sheet” — The stage to suspend a delinquent employee arises when the authority has taken a decision that the allegations, if established, would entail a penalty of dismissal or removal from service — The authority may arrive at that decision with or without making a preliminary investigation under clause(2) of statute (6) — But once the authority takes this decision, its power to suspend is attracted under clause(2) of statute 15 — This power is not dependent upon the framing and service of charge-sheet under statute 7.

Md. Mahtab Hossain Vs. Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology, Dhaka and others; 7BLD (HCD) 315.
