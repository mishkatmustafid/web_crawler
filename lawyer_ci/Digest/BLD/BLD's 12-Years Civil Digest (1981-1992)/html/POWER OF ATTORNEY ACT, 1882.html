POWER OF ATTORNEY ACT, 1882

(VII OF 1882)

 

Section—2

Power of Attorney—Authentication of- Whether a power of attorney is to be authenticated for authorizing the attorney by a Notary Public—Whether a power of attorney duly executed and registered by the Sub-Registrar can be used as a valid document—Whether written statement filed by the attorney of the defendant on the strength of the power of attorney duly executed by the defendant and registered by the Sub-Registrar can be accepted or liable to be expunged—Section 85 Evidence Act raises a presumption as to the execution and authentication of a power of attorney executed and authenticated by the executants and the authority described in the section itself—Although the power of attorney was not authenticated by the Notary Public a required under section 85 of the Evidence Act the same being duly executed and registered under the provisions of Registration Act is a valid document—Section 85 does not say that a duly executed and a registered power of attorney in Bangladesh is unacceptable and invalid document for the reason of non authentication—Written statement filed by the attorney of the defendant on the strength of such a power of attorney was duly presented and cannot be expunged—Evidence.. Act (I of 1872) S. 85—Registration Act (XVI of 1908)S.33.
