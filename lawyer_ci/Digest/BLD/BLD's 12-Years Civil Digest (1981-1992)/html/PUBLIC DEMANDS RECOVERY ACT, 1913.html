PUBLIC DEMANDS RECOVERY ACT, 1913

 

Section—3(6), Article 7 of Schedule 1

Public demand—Whether compensation for use and occupation of Government land is a public demand—Whether such compensation can be realised by certificate proceeding— Certificate proceedings are inappropriate for realisation of compensation for. illegal occupation of ‘Government property in as much as, demand which can be realized, must be in pursuance of contract enforceable in law and as such the Government’s right to realise compensation for use and occupation against a trespasser does not come under Article 7 of schedule I of Public Demands Recovery Act nor is it a public demand as defined in section 3(6) of the said. Act.

Nakul Das Mridha Vs. Bangladesh and others, 6 BLD (HCD) 331.

Ref: 14 DLR (1962) 213;—Cited.

Section—23

Whether Court can suo motu set aside an auction sale—The Court can set aside an auction slae suo motu without any prayer for setting aside the sale either by the certificate debtors or some one on their behalf, if there is material irregularity in publishing and conducting the sale—The Court can set aside an auction sale upon an application by some one holding a power of attorney even if the said power of attorney is not genuine, if the sale is found to have been vitiated by fraud or material irregularity such as non-service of notice upon the certificate debtors—A sale can be set aside in part.

Shaikh Mosharraf Hossain Shasanka Debnath, 5BLD (HCD) 234

Ref: 12 DLR 215—Cited.

 

Section—36

Limitation for filing suit for setting aside sale under certificate—Limitation is one year from the date of delivery of possession—When sale is brought about by means of fraud plaintiff is competent to take advantage of section 18 of the Limitation Act.

Mokbul Hossain and others Vs. Akhil Kumar Shaha and others, 5BLD (HCD) 76

Ref: 7 DLR 50; 13 DLR 70; 30. DLR I 16;—Cited. V

 

Section—37

Whether jurisdiction of civil Court barred—There is general bar to the jurisdiction of the civil Court to entertain a suit relating to execution, discharge and satisfaction of a certificate or relating to confirmation and setting aside of sale between the parties—But jurisdiction of the civil Court to entertain a suit On the ground of fraud is not barred.

Mokbul Hossain and others Vs. Akhil Kuinar Shaha and others, 5BLD (HCD) 76

Ref: 7 DLR 50; 13 DLR 70; 30 DLRI 16— Cited.

 

Section—37

Bar to the jurisdiction of Civil Court— Section 37 of the Public Demands Recovery Act, which is a special law, gives a general bar to the jurisdiction of the Civil Courts.

Except that a civil Court may entertain a suit on the question arises between the certificate-holder and the certificate debtor relating to the making, execution, discharge or satisfaction of the certificate on the ground of fraud.

Once the parties had availed of the special procedure laid down in the special Act fix] their remedy they were precluded from coming to the civil Court even though remedy in the civil Court as such was available to them.

Therefore after having exhausted all the procedures under the Public Demands Recovery Act which is a special law the plaintiffs are precluded from coming to the civil Court.

Marida Begum and ors. Vs. Moulavi Md. Hasan and others, 10BLD(HCD)334

Ref: 37 U. 284; A.I.R. 1940(PC) 105; A.I.R. 1931 (Patna) 182; (1922)35 C.L.J. 304:

 

Sections—51 and 53

Revision—Whether a person can file a revision without filing an appeal against the order of the certificate officer—Without availing the remedy by way of appeal within the time limit a party has no right to seek revision under the section 53 of the P.D.R. Act—But the Collector or Additional Deputy Commissioner can suo motu revise the order passed by the Certificate officer—The Additional Deputy Commissioner (Rev) correctly exercised his revisional jurisdiction.

Abdul Gafur Chowdhury Vs. Govt. of Bangladesh and others, 4 BLD (HCD) 163

 

 

 

Rule—67 schedule II

 

Auction purchase—Auction purchase of co-sharer’s property by certificate debtor—The certificate debtors having purchased the suit property in the benami of their sons, the purchase is in effect payment of arrear rent ensuring to the benefit of all the co-sharers and they are entitled to get reconveyance of their property.

Matiur Rahman and another Vs. Abdul Gani and others, 8 BLD(HCD)191
