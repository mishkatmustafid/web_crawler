TRADE MARKS ACT, 1940

(V of 1940)

 

Section — 10(2)

 

Continuous User—Continuous honest and concurrent user for a long time of a trade mark is a special circumstances which wan-ant consideration of a case specially under subsection (2). When the Trade Marks Act, was enacted in 1940, the legislators in their best wisdom might foresee that a situation like common or concurrent use of the same trade mark by more than one might arise in the future in certain special circumstances, and in order to overcome such an unforeseen situation and predicament they enacted the provisions as contained in section 10 of the Trade Mark Act.

After emergence of Bangladesh certain special circumstances arose which led the legislature to enact a law like P.O. No. 19 of 1973 for summary registration of trade marks given before creation of Bangladesh. The provisions of section 10 of the Act, shall be read along with Article 5 of P.O. No. 19 of 1937 for summary registration in the present case.

M/s. Sattar Match Works and others Vs. Bangladesh Chemical Industries Corporation and others, 11BLD (HCD) 571

 

 

Sections—37 and 46

Trade Mark—The Registrar was legally bound to consider both the applications together since both the parties applied for the self-same trade-mark ‘capri’ for their toilet soaps. So, according to registration to respondent No. I leaving aside the application of the petitioner company, who had reasonable arid legal grounds to apply, cannot be sustained in law.
