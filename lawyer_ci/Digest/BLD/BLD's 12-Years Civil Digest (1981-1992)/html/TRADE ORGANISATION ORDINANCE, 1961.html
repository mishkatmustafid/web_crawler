TRADE ORGANISATION ORDINANCE, 1961

(XLV OF 1961)

 

Section — 8(1)(b)

Trade Organisation Ordinance— Whether Government can prohibit a registered trade organisation from carrying on business of weighment, measurement etc. by directing it to delete the relevant clause of its memorandum of association — Government has the power to direct a registered trade organisation to alter or modify its memorandum and articles of association.

Bangladesh Steamer Agents Association Vs. Bangladesh, 1BLD (AD) 467

 

Section —22

Ouster of jurisdiction of Civil Court — Whether the civil Court can entertain a suit when a statute ousts its jurisdiction in any matter?—Ouster clause of a statute cannot completely oust the jurisdiction of the civil Court — If an action is taken illegally wjthout or in excess of jurisdiction, without following the procedure provided in the statute or malafide then the civil Court acquires junsdiction to entertain the suit — Trade Organisation Ordinance, 1961 provides that an order made under this Ordinance shall not be questioned in any Court — Civil Court jurisdiction not ousted.

Deputy Secretary, Ministry of Commerce Vs. Nizamuddin Haider, 5 BLD (HCD) 57
