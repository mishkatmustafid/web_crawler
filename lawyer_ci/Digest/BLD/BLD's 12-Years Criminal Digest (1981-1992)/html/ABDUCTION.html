ABDUCTION

Abduction and rape — On the basis of the facts of each case the Court has to come to a finding as to whether it is safe to act upon the testimony of the victim without being supported by independent corroborative evidence — Statement of the victim girl after the commission of the offence is legally admissible as corrobo­ration but the value and weight it will carry is, however, a different matter — Evidence Act (I of 1872) S 157 Abdul Quddus & another Vs. The State 3 BLD (HCD) 18.

Abduction and rape — Uncorrobo­rated testimony of the prosecutrix is not sufficient for conviction.

It is understandable that an occurrence of abduction and rape may not be actually witnessed by any one other than the vic­tim but even in such a situation it is re­quired that such an occurrence shall have to be proved by strong circumstantial evi­dence supporting the case of the victim. Zainul Abedin & others Vs. The State 3 BLD (HCD) 108. Ref. 19 DLR (SC) 259; 12 DLR (SC) 165

Abduction — Section 364 of the Penal Code — The Section is attracted even though the person abducted was not, in fact, murdered in consequence of the ab­duction Al-Haj Abdul Gani Vs. The State 12 BLD (HCD) 490
