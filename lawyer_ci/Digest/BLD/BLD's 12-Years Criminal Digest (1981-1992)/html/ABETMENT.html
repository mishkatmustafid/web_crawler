ABETMENT

Abetment— Abetment is an offence by itself and unless it is specifically made punishable in a Special Act, a person can not be called upon to answer a charge of abetment merely by reference to the Penal Code — The purpose of Section 6(5) of the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961 seems to be that the person who contra­venes the provision is alone liable and none else Abdul Halim Pattadar and others Vs. M. Rahmat All and another 1 BLD (HCD) 377

Abetment — After having discharged the principal accused no useful purpose can be served by continuing the proceed­ing against the abettors — After having discharged the principal accused there is no valid reason to prosecute the abettors only. Moyna Miah Vs. The State and another 5 BLD (AD) 99 (101).

Abetment — Law does not provide that in the absence of any formal charge order of conviction cannot be passed for abetment on the facts proved while charged for the substantive offence — It is only as a rule of prudence that an alternative charge for abetment is framed Abdur Rouf and others Vs. The State 6 BLD (HCD) 402

Abetment — After the principal of­fender was acquitted in the previous trial, no useful purpose can be served by trying the abettors in a subsequent proceeding Twahid Khan alias Twahid Kha and others Vs. The State & others 7 BLD (HCD) 11(13)

Abetment — Mere taking away of the victim from his house without any overt act in the form of any hostile attitude or initial intention to kill will not justify conviction for abetment — The theory of 'last seen' must carry along with it a high degree of probability, excluding all other theories 'except the hypothesis of the guilt of the accused. Soleman others Vs. The State 10 BLD (HCD) 179.

Abetment — The charge of abetment fails when the substantive offence is not established against the principal offender__ Abetment under the Penal Code in­volves actual complicity on the part of the abettor prior to the actual commission of the offence — The essence of the offence of abetment is that the abettor should substantially assist the principal offender towards commission of the offence. Dabir Talukder Vs.The State 12 BLD (HCD) 429 (435)

Abetment — Gist of the offence — In order to make an accused liable for commission of any offence, it must be shown that he has committed the crime or aided or instigated the same in the same form or manner as the other accused did _ It is a settled principle of law that the mere presence of the accused near about the place of occurrence does not consti­tute the offence under Section 109 of the Penal Code — Intentional aiding and active complicity is the gist of the offence of abetment committed by the accused — Penal Code (XLV of 1860) S 109 Mostain Mollah and others Vs. The State 11 BLD (HCD) 552
