APPEAL

Criminal Appeal — Appeals are crea­tures of statutes — There is no appeal against conviction and sentence passed by the Labour Court — Such an appeal is in­competent — Employment of Labour (Standing Orders) Act (VIII of 1965) S 26.

Labour Court 'trying a case under the provisions of Standing Orders Act is not invested with powers of a Magistrate 1st Class — Labour Court is not an inferior court — High Court Division has no re-visional jurisdiction to interfere with the conviction and sentence passed under the Standing Orders Act — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 439 — Em­ployment of Labour (Standing Order) Act, (VII VIII of 1965 of 1965) S 26 Anisuzzaman & ors. Vs. Chairman, 1st Labour Court & anr. 2 BLD (HCD) 93

 

Criminal Appeal — Disposal only on the question of sentence without deciding the appeal on merit — In a Criminal ap­peal a duty is cast upon the appellate court to dispose of the appeal on merit — Without giving its finding as to the con­viction it is not permissible to dispose of the appeal on the ground of sentence only, notwithstanding any concession made by the counsel — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 423 Mil JashimuMn Vs. The State 6 BLD (AD) 198 Ref. AIR 1935 (PC) 89; AIR 1960 (SC) 748; PLD 1967 (SC) 545.

Judgment in Criminal Appeal — Ex-parte disposal in the absence of the Advo­cate of the appellant — It is a well set­tled principle of law that for disposal of a Criminal appeal the presence of the Ad­vocate of the appellant is not essential but still then the Court is to write down the judgment in accordance with the provis­ion of Section 367(1) of the Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 367).

Judgment by the Appellate Court is to show the application of the mind inde­pendently — The Appellate Court is to formulate the points for determination, consider the evidence with reference to the points in issue and give decision thereon with reasons — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) Ss. 367 & 424 Ahul Bashar & ors. Vs. The State 7 BLD (HCD) 345  Ref. 3 BCR (HCD) 239; 22 DLR (SC) 263; 25 DLR (Dhaka) 330

Withdrawal of Criminal Appeal —

The right of appeal is a creature of statute and is a privilege — If a prisoner having filed an appeal from the judgment of con­viction elects not to pursue the appeal and wants to withdraw it, the court should not insist that the appeal should be heard on merit — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 423 Nasiruddin Mia Vs. The State 8 BLD (HCD) 503 Ref. PLD 1959 (SC) 119; 11 DLR (SC) 200

 

POWER OF APPELLATE COURT

Power of Appellate Court — Resto­ration of possession — An appellate court is competent to pass an order for restora­tion of possession in an appeal against the order of conviction — No illegality com­mitted by the Sessions Judge in allowing the petition for restoration of possession — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 522(3) Sorab Ali ors. Vs. The State 2 BLD (HCD) 116. Ref. PLD 1960 (Dhaka) 23; AIR 1957 (Cal) 390; AIR 1960 (Mysore) 24

 

Criminal Appeal — Power of the Ap­pellate Court — Meaning of the word "finding" — It is the result of a judicial examination into matters of fact — The expression "may alter the finding" clearly empowers the appellate court to consider the entire evidence, both as regards fact and law, to substitute its own finding — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 423(1 )(b) Nabir Md alias Nabiruddin Vs. The State 2 BLD (HCD) 153 Ref. 6 DLR (FC) 65; PLD 1952 (Lahore) 11.

 

Criminal Appeal — Disposal without discussion of evidence — Its dismissal without writing a full fledged judgment is not proper — There should have been discussion of the evidence before affirm­ing the conviction of the appellants — There being no discussion about the nature of the offence or the part played by the individual accused persons the impugned judgment cannot be sustained — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 423 Sirajaddoula alias Md Sirajaddoula ors. Vs. The State 4 BLD (AD) 213
