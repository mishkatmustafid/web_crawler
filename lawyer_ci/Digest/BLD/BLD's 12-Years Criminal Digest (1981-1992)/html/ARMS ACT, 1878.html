Right to possess Fire Arms — The granting of license for firm arms is dis­cretionary — No one has a right to pos­sess or carry a fire arm — The legal right to possess or carry a fire arm is created only after the license is granted — The grant of license amounts to conferment of a privilege.

 

The licensee acquires a right to possess, carry, defend and maintain his fire arms once a license is issued to him — So long as he does not violate the terms and con­ditions of his license and so long as the appropriate authority does not deem it necessary to cancel or suspend his license for the security of public  peace, he acquires a right to defend his right and privilege to possess and carry fire arms — A licensee of a fire arm can challenge the cancellation of his license by filing writ petition under Article 102 of the Constitution of Bangladesh.

The appropriate authority should record the reason of cancellation himself— Only in rare cases he may pass an order ditto­ing the reasons and suggestion of his subordinate officer — If he does not record the reason himself the Court will certainly scrutinize his order and will strike it down if it has been passed with­out application of mind — Arms Act (XI of 1878) S 18 „VA. The Arms Act, 1878 (XI of 1878) S 18 — Cancellation of license for fire arm — Since there is no provision of ap­peal against an order of cancellation, the order will not be struck down for non-communicating the reasons — A licensee has, however, the right to know the rea­sons if the order is challenged in a Court of law and the appropriate authority must lay before the Court the reasons for its decision and the materials behind it.

 

Cancellation of license for fire arm without notice and principle of natural justice — The absence of a prior no­tice/hearing may not always invalidate the order cancelling license for fire arm, the subject matter being directly related to the security of the public peace — It may not be possible nor advisable to adhere to the principle of natural justice of a prior show-cause in every case because of exi­gencies of the situation — Each case has to be examined on its own merit to see whether a prior notice has to be given.

 

Cancellation of license for fire arm — When the violation of the principles of natural justice is deprecated — It is no case where the police was making a report to the Magistrate in course of his normal public duty, nor a case where the police received information in course of their ordinary investigation — The en­quiry was undertaken on a sprawling pri­vate complaint — There appeared to be no emergency or any compelling situation preventing giving of notice of enquiry' to the appellant — There is nothing on re­cord to show that any specific case was started against him involving misuse of his revolver — In the facts of the case it was absolutely necessary to hold an en­quiry with notice to and in presence of the appellant — The authority did not act properly in the matter and we have no hesitation in holding that the impugned order is of no legal effect as the same has been passed in violation of the principle of natural justice — The Arms Act, 1878 (XI of 1878) S 18. Arms and Ammunitions — Cancella­tion of Licence of fire arms — Under Arms Rules, 1924, Licence for the possession of firm arms and ammunitions may be granted by the District Magistrate or the Sub-Divisional Magistrate specially empowered by the Government in that behalf — Under Section 16 of the Arms Act such licence may be cancelled by the officer by whom the same was granted or by any authority to whom he may be subordinate or by any Magistrate of a District for reasons to be recorded in writing for the security of the public peace or by any Judge or Magistrate convicting a licensee of fire arms for an offence under the Arms Act — When the District Magistrate has granted the licence the same can only be cancelled or suspended by the District Magistrate himself or by any authority to whom he is subordinate for reasons to be recorded by him in writing for the security of the public peace — There is nothing to show that the Government especially empo­wered any Sub-Divisional Magistrate to grant any licence for possessing the fire arms — The Sub-Divisional Magistrate, Shariatpur had no authority to cancel the licence granted by the District Magistrate, Faridpur-Arms Act (XI of 1878) Ss. 17 & 18 — Arms Rules 1924 R 33 Md Anwar Hossain Majhee & ors.
