BAIL

 

Bail — Court cannot demand deposit of a sum of money instead of furnishing bail bond for release of the accused on bail — Granting of bail with a condition which cannot possibly be complied with tantamounts to refusal of the bail — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) Ss. 499 and 513 R. K. M. Reza Vs. The State 1 BLD (HCD) 66

 

Bail — refusal on mere apprehension of absconding — In the absence of any facts or materials mere apprehension that the accused may abscond does not justify the refusal of the bail to curtail the liberty of a citizen — Bail was granted by the Appellate Division till the conclusion of the trial but the trial court was given full liberty to deal with the question of bail, if misused Md, Laik alias Kayum Vs. The State 2 BLD (AD) 182.

 

Bail — When can be granted — Facts alleged constitute offence under Sections 302/149 of the Penal Code and there is continuing dispute between the parties and the trial is likely to commense within a short time — In such circumstances bail cannot be granted. Ful Mia & ors. Vs. The State 2 BLD (AD) 183

 

Bail — When there is dispute between parties over properties — When there exists a dispute between the parties over properties and there is pendng ltitigation in the civil court — Bail should not be refused to the accused — Code of Crimi­nal Procedure ( V of 1898) S 498 Syed Mahhuhur Rahman & anr. Vs. Md Abdul Matin & anr. 5 BLD(AD) 217

 

Bail — Exercise of judicial discretion—Other accused alleged to have committed the same offence being on bail, the refusal of bail to the appellants by the learned judges without considering this fact and further failing to notice the cir­cumstances that there was specific allega­tion against the appellants cannot be said to be a proper exercise of discretion in rejecting the prayer for bail of the appel­lants — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 498 Firoj Ali & Vs. The State 7 BLD (AD) 91

 

Bail — Cancellation when not proper—The Special Judge did not exercise his discretion properly in cancelling the bail of the appellant merely upon the appre­hension expressed by the prosecution as to his possible abscondence, particularly when he did not do so during the last six years when he had been on bail — In the circumstances of the case the High Court Division was not justified in rejecting the application for bail summarily — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 497(5) Sajalendu Das Vs. The State 7BLD (AD) 154.

 

Bail — When it is reasonable to grant it — There is apprehension that the trial may be further delayed and the appellants will suffer prolonged custody — In the facts of the case it will not be unreason­able to allow the appellants to remain on bail till the tirlal commences — It wil be for the trial court to see whether the ap­pellants should continue on bail during the trial — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 498 Ashraf AH Mondal & ors. Vs. The State and anr. 8BLD(AD)21.

 

Bail — Prayer for bail in a pending appeal — Whether the appellant's bail was properly refused upon a correct as­sessment of facts — The seems that the High Court Division ignored facts stated in the petition — Having regard to the good academic background and future career of the appellant, there was enough justifiable reason to let him go on bail — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 426(2) Nurul Islam alias Bablu Vs. The State 8 BLD (AD) 208

 

Bail — Whether refusal of bail on the ground that prima facie there was no ille­gality in the trial is proper — The appel­lant was tried in absentia and convicted under Section 420 of the Penal Code and sentenced to R.I. for 7 years and a fine of Taka 35,000.00 — He surrendered to the court and was granted bail — The court erred is not allowing withdrawal of the case against him — In the peculiar cir­cumstances of the case the appellant is entitled to bail, particularly when there is hardly any chance of his abscondence — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 498 Shahjahan All Tara Vs. The State 9 BLD (AD)2

 

Bail — Bail to appellant on condition of payment of fine whether sustainable— Payment of fine involving a huge amount of money may not be possible and the purpose of granting bail may be defeated if payment of fine is made a condition for the bail — This is why the court has been given the discretion to stay realisation of fine pending disposal of the appeal — The impugned order for payment of fine as a condition for the bail is not supportable either in law or on the principle of reasonableness — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 426 M(L Iqbal alias Salim Vs. The State 9 BLD (AD) 3.

 

Bail — When no material against the accused gathered during investigation — The appellant ought to have been en­larged on bail when he was not named in the FIR. and the police could not gather any material against him although the in­vestigation has been going on for over a year — The appellant shall remain on bail till the commencement of the trial, if any—   Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 498 Sree Kalyan Kumar Chowdhury Vs. The State 9 BLD (AD) 12.

 

Bail — Right of bail — Whether a per­son convicted of a bailable offence is en­titled to bail as a matter of right — In a case of bailable offence the under-trial prisoners are entitled to bail as of right — But in the case of a convict he cannot claim bail as of right — It is the absolute discretion of the court to refuge or grant bail to the convicts — Of course this discretion of the appellate court must be exercised judicially — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) Ss. 426(1) and 496 Shamsul Hoque Pramanik & ors. Vs. The State 9 BLD (HCD) 520. Ref. 1969 (Pak) Cr.L.J 422; PLD 1967 (Lahore) 1302; 20 DLR (WP) (Lahore) 7.

 

Rejection of bail petition — After rejection whether remedy lies only in an appeal — The learned Judges of the High Court Division were not right in taking the view that once a petition for bail is rejected no further application for bail can be made and the remedy lies only in an appeal — It is also not right to say that in the facts, as in the present case, (enter­tainment of bail petition and granting of bail after the same was rejected by a Division Bench) an application for bail could not be filed before the Vacation Judge and that he had no jurisdiction to grant interim bail — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 498 A. Wahab, Advocate Vs. The State 10 BLD (AD) 50

 

Bail — Interim bail pending appeal — Interim bail can not be allowed to con­tinue for an indefinite period — There is no reason for further continuation of in­terim bail simply because the appeal against conviction is pending — The ad-interim will continue for a period of only six months and appellant shall surrender to the bail bond unless within this period his appeal is disposed of — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 498 Abdul Hakim Howlader Vs. The State 10 BLD (AD) 126

 

Bail — Ad-interim — Appellant's duty-steps to be taken for early disposal of the appeal — The Appellate Division granted ad-interim bail for a limited period indi­cating expeditious disposal of the appeal — The appellants taking advantage of the bail sat over the matter unconcerned — Procrastination proved and deprecated — In such circumstances, the court declines to continue ad interim bail for indefinite period — Parties directed to take steps for early disposal of the appeal within specified time during which the appel­lants would be on bail Azjmul Huq alias AzizmI Haque & ors. Vs. The State 10BLD(AD)205.

Bail and cancellation of bail — Case and counter-case over the self-same incident — Co-accused Chunnu Chowdhury against whom cognizance has been taken under Section 302 of the Penal Code is already on bail — High Court Division while issuing Rule also issued a suo motu Rule to show cause as to why the bail granted to co-accused Bakul Howlader should not be cancelled — While disposing of the main Miscella­neous case, the High Court Division cancelled the bail of accused Bakul Howlader making the suo motu Rule absolute — In the absence of any new material the High Court Division did not exercise its judicial discretion properly in cancelling the bail of the appellant — The High Court Division failed to consider the questions of the case and counter case over the self-same incident one co-accused was already on bail, absence of any allegation of tampering with evidence and absence of any new material produced by the state justifying cancellation of the bail_Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 498 Bakul Howlader alias Shamsul Alam Vs. The State 10 BLD (AD) 290.

 

Bail — Special Powers Act (XIV of 1974) - Sections 366, 376 and 4(C) of the Cruelty to Women (Deterrent Punish­ment) Ordinance, 1983 — Cancellation of bail suo motu by the learned Special Tribunal in the absence of any allegation of the abuse of the privilege of bail is arbitrary and violative of the principle of natural justice Ahmed AU & ors. Vs. The State 10 BLD (HCD) 451

 

Bail in a murder case — Appellant en­larged on ad-interim bail by the High Court Division but subsequently cancelled on a mere apprehension of the chance of tampering with evidence — Cancellation of the bail for the sake of fair and proper investigation of the case and apprehension of tampering with evidence not based on any tangible evidence — No allegation of misuse of the privilege of ad-interim bail__The High Court Division failed to ex­ercise its discretion properly in not con­firming the adinterim bail granted to the appellant — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 498 Penal Code (XLV of 1860) S 302 Gazi Abdul Hadi Vs. The State 11 BLD (AD) 64

 

Bail — Special Powers Act (XIV of 1974) Section 25B( 1) — The trial court convicted and sentenced the appellants for two years — The High Court Division admitted the appeal for hearing but re­jected the prayer for bail —.The Appel­late Division granted bail on the consid­erations that the sentence is for only two years and the disposal of such an appeal normally takes time — The High Court Division failed to properly exercise its judicial discretion in rejecting the prayer for bail Md Dhanu Mia & am. Vs. The State 11 BLD (AD) 96.

 

Ad-interim bail — Granted by the lower appellate court to move the High Court in revision — The High Court Di­vision refuged the prayer for bail and di­rected the appellants to surrender and thereafter to file a separate application for bail — The Appellate Division held that when the appellants were already on bail granted by the lower appellate court, the order of the High Court Division rejecting the prayer for bail is not proper and is not in keeping with the normal practice and procedure that is traditionally followed. Baneaz,uddin Ahmed < ors. Vs. The State 11 BLD (AD 239.

 

Bail — Special Powers Act (XIV of 1974) Section 32 — Penal Code (XLV of 1860) Section 376 — Prosecution case is an unusual one — Pending hearing of the appeal the appellant was allowed bail for a limited period. Md Shamsul Haque Vs. The State 11 BLD (HCD) 64.

 

Bail — Drug Control Act, 1990 (XX of 1990) — Section 19(1) Table 1 (Ka) — Special Powers Act, 1974 — Accused petitioner convicted and sentenced to suffer R.I. for three years — The material prosecution witnesses did not support the recovery of heroin from the possession of the accused petitioner — The petitioner was enlarged on bail by the High Court Division pending hearing of the appeal. Md Atiar Rahman Vs. The State 11 BLD (HCD) 291

 

Bail — Special Powers Act (XIV of 1974) Sections 32 and 25B — Petitioner convicted under Section 25B for two years — Petitioner was all along on bail during the trial and never misused the privilege of bail — Little chance of early disposal of the appeal — Petitioner was enlarged on bail. Nahab Ali Vs. The State 11 BLD (HCD) 494.Ref. 11 BLD (AD) 96; 43 DLR (AD) 119.

 

Bail — Special Powers Act, 1974 (XIV of 1974) — Section 32 of the Special Powers Act is not an absolute bar against granting of bail — Even when the prose­cution opposes the prayer for bail the court can release an accused on bail when it is satisfied that there are no rea­sonable grounds for believing that the ac­cused is guilty of the offence alleged — The High Court Division summarily re­jected the appellant's prayer for bail on an erroneous view of Section 32 of the Special Powers Act. Madar Chandra Basu Vs. The State 12 BLD (AD) 129

 

Bail — Section 497 Cr.P.C. — Section 497 of the Code of Criminal Procedure enjoins upon the court to exercise its ju­dicial discretion in the matter of granting bail for ascertaining whether the materi­alsplaced before the court by the prose­cution are of such a tangible nature that if left unrebutted, they may lead to the inference of guilt of the accused. A. K. M. Mosharaf Hossain Vs. The State 12 BLD (AD) 175

 

Bail — In a pending appeal — In a pending Criminal appeal when the appel­lant files an application for bail, the court should not ordinarily issue any rule — The court may grant or refuse the bail or ask the petitioner to came up with a separate petition and may hear the state, if necessary, before disposing of an appli­cation for bail Sree Manju Kumar Saha & ors. Vs. The State 12 BLD (AD) 213

 

Bail — Special Powers Act — Section 32 — Ground for granting bail — Bail can be granted only when the Special Tri­bunal or the court is satisfied on hearing both the parties that there are reasonable grounds for believing that the accused or convict is not guilty of the offence — Special Powers Act (XIV of 1974) S 32 Md Aminul Islam Vs. The State 11 BLD (HCD) 106

 

Bail — Special Powers Act (XIV of 1974) — Sections 30 and 32 — Customs Act (IV of 1969) — Section 156(8)(59) read with Section 25B of the Special Powers Act — Accused Joynal Abedin's name appeared neither in the FIR. nor in the charge sheet as an accused — In the charge sheet it was mentioned that he was the owners of seized foreign clothes with a valid licence — No summons was yet served upon the prosecution witnesses_Possibility of delay in the commence­ment of the trial — Prayer for bail al­lowed. Joy mil Abedin & another Vs. The State 12 BLD (HCD) 1. Ref. 27 DLR (HCD) 427; 32 DLR (AD) 35

Bail — Sections 498/339C(3) The Petitioner charged along with others under Sections 396/412 of the Penal Code — Trial not concluded within the specified time — Other accused persons moved revisional application before the High Court Division against the order rejecting the prayer for stopping the pro­ceeding — The High Corut Division stayed further proceeding till the disposal of the Criminal revision before it — The petitioner in hajat for a long period — Uncertain when the trial will commence__Other accused persons enjoying the privilege of bail — Petitioner stands on the same footing — Petitioner enlarged on bail till the commencement of the trial — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of1898) S 498 Arun Kumar Saha alias Narayan Vs. The State 12 BLD (HCD) 238.

 

Bail — Directly by High Court Division — Bail by the High Court direct is not granted as a matter of course but only in an exceptional case such as the physical inability to appear before the trial court, fear and lack of personal safety because of local rivalry, sheer lack of confidence and the pecuiliar circumstances of the case, the High Court may exercise its power in granting bail. Jahanara Imam and others Vs. The State 12 BLD (HCD) 314. Ref. 18 DLR(SC) 393
