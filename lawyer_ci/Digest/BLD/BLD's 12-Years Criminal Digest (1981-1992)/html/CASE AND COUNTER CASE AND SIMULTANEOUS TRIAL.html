CASE AND COUNTER CASE AND SIMULTANEOUS TRIAL

Case and counter-case — Final report by the Investigation Officer — It is none of his business to decide the case for himself when two sides came up with contradictory versions of the same occur­rence — The quality of evidence is the same in both the cases and there were in­juries on both sides — In a case like this both the version should be placed before a court of law so that truth can be found out upon receiving evidence from both sides and justice done to the parties — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 173 Syed Abdul Hannan Vs. The State 3 BLD (HCD) 156.

Criminal Trial—Trial of case and counter-case — When there is a case andcounter-case, both the cases should be tried one after another by the same court — The trial court in such a case should be on its guard so that the evidence of one case is not imported in the other and thereby divert the course of justice to a wrong channel Abdul Aziz Vs. The State 6 BLD (AD)3 Ref. 7 DLR 395, 13 DLR 414, 18 DLR (SC) 474,21 DLR 958 

Criminal Trial — Practice and Proce­dure — The triai of cross-cases — Trail of cross-cases arising from the same inci­dent by the same court enables it to better appreciate the evidence — By such trial the court gets both sides of the picture and arrives at correct decision avoiding conflicting findings — Though no express provision of law requires trial of cross-cases by the same court, practice and experience of over a century dictate such a trial. Shamsul Huq alias Shamsul and others Vs. The State 6 BLD (AD) 216

Simultaneous Trial of Two Cases — Requirements for holding such trial — The requirement of a simultaneous trial is that the trials must be held in quick suc­cession so that there occurs an over-all picture to the mind of the judge — If there is a long gap between the two trials, the purpose for which a simultaneous trial is held by the same court is almost lost Alauddin Molla and others Vs. The State and others 8 BLD (AD) 221 

Usual practice of trial of cross-cases

— The usual practice of trial of cross cases is that both should be tried by the same judge simultaneously or one after another and judgment therein should be delivered after concluding the hearing of both the cases — When there is a fight between the two rival factions giving rise to the complaint and the counter-com-plant, the most desirable course is that both the cases should be tried by the same judge and judgments in these cases should be delivered after concluding the hearing of both the cases — In such trials, records of each case should be kept distinct and separate — The evidence of one case cannot be used in the other case and each case must be decided on its own evidence on record Siddique Munshi Vs. The State 12 BLD (AD) 59. Ref. 7 DLR 395; 13 DLR 414; 1971 PLD (SC) 713
