CODE OF CRIMINAL PROCEDURE (SECOND AMENDMENT) ORDINANCE (XXIV OF 1982)

 

Section 35(C) — Time-limit for trial in a pending case — Ordinance No. XXIV of 1982 and the amending Ordinance No. XL of 1982 did not provide the legal consequences that would flow if the Court would not complete the trial of a pending case within a period of 180 days or 360 days — The provisions of Section 35(C) of Ordinance No. XXIV of 1982 have been held to be directory and not mandatory in nature — But a proceeding instituted on or after the commencement of Ordinance No XXIV of 1982 on 28.1.82, the trial of the case has to be concluded within the specified time or the extended period failing which further proceedings in respect of the trial 'shall stand stopped" and the accused persons "shall be released" Channu Mia alias Yusufuzzaman and others Vs. The State 3 BLD (HCD) 166.

                            

Section 35(D) — Time-limit for in­vestigation in a pending case — It is not mandatory but merely directory — The inability of the Investigating Officer to comply with its provision is not attended with any legal consequence and as such the accused is not entitled to be released. Mozainmel Haque Vs. The State and another 3 BLD (HCD) 168.

 

Section 35 — Whether time-limit imposed by Section 339C Cr.PC is applicable in a pending case — The time limit under Section 339C Cr P C as amended has no application in the pending case — The transitory provision as laid down under Section 35 of Ordinance No. XXIV of 1982 are applicable to a pending case — The time limit for disposal of a pending criminal case is merely directory in as much as the legislature has not provided for any penal provision for non­compliance of the same Bardir Molla and other Vs. The State 4 BLD (HCD) 571.Ref. 32 DLR (AD) 100; 33 DLR (AD) 154.
