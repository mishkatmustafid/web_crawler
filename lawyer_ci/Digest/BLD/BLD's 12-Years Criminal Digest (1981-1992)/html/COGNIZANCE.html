Cognizance by a private complaint

 

A private individual cannot prosecute a person for offences committed in relation to a proceeding in a court Such a proceeding is barred under the provision of clause (c) of sub-section (I) of Section 195 of the Code of Criminal Procedure when the offence is committed by the parties "as such" in a proceeding before any court — The expression "party to a proceeding" needs be interpreted strictly and in its ordinary meaning — Sections 195 and 476 Cr.P C. are to be read together in as much as the former section lays down the bar while the latter section lays down the method for removing the bar — Section 476 enjoins the civil court to make an enquiry when it appears that the offence has been committed in or in relation to a proceeding "in that court" — Section 195(C) Cr.P.C. applies only to a case where an offence has been committed in respect of a document which has been produced or given in evidence in such a proceeding — When the offence had been committed before the accused became a party to the proceeding, provision of Section. 195(C) is not applicable — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) Ss 195 and 476 Mosammat Saleha Khatun Vs. The State 5 (BLD) (HCD) 193 Ref. AIR 1931 (All) 443

Cognizance — Whether the special judge can take cognizance on police report and not upon a complaint from the court concerned — Whether sub-section 1(C) of Section 195 of Cr PC has been superceded by the Criminal Law Amend­ment Act. 1958.

 

The provision for complaint from the court has not been specifically done away with in the Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1958 and as such the general provision of the Code remains unaffected When an offence within the meaning of the Code is committed in a proceeding before a court, then the complaint shall have to be filed by the court concerned In view of Section 6 of the Act. the provision as to taking of cognizance of a case upon a complaint from the court has remained unaffected and as such if an offence falls within the ambit of Section 195(1 )(C) of the Code then cognizance cannot be taken except upon a complaint from the Court — Criminal Law Amend­ment Act (XL of 1958) S 6 Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 195 (l)(C) Mil. Cognizance Without the complaint of the court — Section 195 Cr.P.C pro­vides that if a document is "produced" or "given in evidence" in a proceeding before a court, the only person competent to make a complaint with regard to such a document is the Presiding Office of the Court itself and none else The word "or" between the words "produced" and "given in evidence" in the section, the two things are disjunctive — This means that the law gives the authority to the court concerned to make a complaint not only if the document is given in evidence but also if it is produced in the proceeding.

Filing of a forged document in a pro­ceeding before a Court amounts to production of the document within the meaning of Section 195(1 )(C) Cr.P.C Mohammad Herman Ali and others Vs. The State 11 BLD (HCD) 120 Ref. 8 BLD (AD) 195.
