COMMUTATION OF SENTENCE — EXTENUATING CIRCUMSTANCES

 

Extenuating circumstances — Consider­ing the facts and circumstances of the case and the further fact that the accused is a young man and his death sentence has remained pending for over two years, the sentence of death is altered to a sentence of transportation for life. Mujibar Rahman Vs. The State 3 BLD (HCD) 145.

 

Extenuating circumstances Warran­ting modification of death sentence —Fact revealed that the accused sought to revenge assault and injury on his father only because of his youthful wrath and rashness — In consideration of his young age and the fact that he was rotten in the death cell for over two years the accused is sentenced to transportation for life in­stead of death — Penal Code (XLV of 1860) S 302 Abdul Majid Vs. The State 3 BLD (HCD) 304.

 

Commutation of sentence — An irate and exasperated husband caused some in­juries to his wife without any weapon on the heat of the moment — He has a minor child by the deceased wife who has got to be looked after — It will be pretty inhuman to keep the father in confinement for long — His conviction is altered to Part II of Section 304 from Section 302 of the Penal Code and he is sentenced to suffer R.I. for 4 years only — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 376 Shafiullah alias Kala Mia Vs. The State 5 BLD (HCD) 129.

 

Commutation of Death Sentence —The condemned prisoner was used by some veteran criminals whom the court is unable to catch hold of and other culprits who engineered the whole game were not brought before the court either on the failure of the state machinery or the Public Prosecutor — The condemned prisoner participated in the war of liberation and he has been in jail hajat for 6 years before his conviction and he has been in the con­demned cell for 1 year and 5 months since his conviction — Delay in disposal of the Death reference may also be taken into consideration as an extenuating circum­stance to commute the death sentence to transportation for life — Code of Crimi­nal Procedure (V of 1898 ) S 376. Abdur Rouf and others Vs. The State 6 BLD (HCD) 402. Ref. 13 DLR 646/203; 21 DLR (WP) 49; 32 DLR 227; 4 BLD 228.

 

Commutation of death sentence —Inordinate delay in execution of the sen­tence may be a good ground for commutation of the sentence in some cases but delay which is not due to any laches of the prosecution should not be considered as a ground for commutation — Section 302 of the Penal Code does not specify in which case death sentence should be given and in which case transportation for life to be awarded but leaves the matter to the discretion of the court — Bitter matrimonial relationship played a part in the nefarious situation which cannot be overlooked — Ends of justice will be met if the appellants are sentenced to transportaton for life instead of death — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 376. Nowsher Ali Sardar and others Vs. The State 7 BLD (AD) 324

 

Sentence for murder — The High Court Division was right in taking the view that a delay of one year and seven months in disposal of the Death reference could not be taken as an extenuating factor for commutation of the death sentence.

 

Circumstances attending the crime are to be taken notice of — The occurrence followed a tenure of a rancorous married life, it was not a blissful union from the beginning and the parties thereto must have been under occasional strains — Death sentence is commuted to imprison­ment for life. Dipak Kumar Sarkar Vs. The State 8 BLD (AD) 109. Ref. 39DLR (AD) 194.

 

Commutation of death sentence —Facts showing mental agony of the ac­cused to be considered — The con­demned prisoner has been in custody since 15.6.76 — He was once before sentenced to death on 31.5.78, retried and again sentenced to death on 11.5.85 — He is in mental agony for over 10 years — His sentence is commuted to impris­onment for life — Code of Criminal Pro­cedure (V of 1898) S 376. The State Vs. Ful Mia 8 BLD (HCD) 79.

 

Death sentence — What constitutes extenuating circumstances — It is not ca­pable of any precise formulations but it will have to be judicially determined in the facts of each particular case — Among other things, grave and sudden provoca­tion is considered as an extenuating circumstance — Delay in execution of death sentence for want of confirmation cannot by itself constitute a mitigating cir­cumstance but an abnormal and excessive delay may be considered for commutation of death sentence — There was no circumstance to take lenient view in the matter of sentence to temper justice with mercy — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) Ss.367 and 374 — Penal Code (XLV of 1860) 302. Abed Ali Vs. The State 10 BLD (AD) 89.

 

Commutation of sentence — In view of the delay in the trial of the accused and disposal of the reference and appeal along with others extenuating - circumstances, justice will be sufficiently met if the sen­tence of death is commuted to imprison­ment of life — Code of Criminal Proce­dure (V of 1898) S 374 Hazrat Ali and another Vs. The State 10 BLD (HCD) 38.

 

Commutation of sentence and extenu­ating circumstances — There was no pre meditation for committing the murder — Condemned prisoner having two minor children (the younger one being only 20 days old on the date of occurrence) — Regard being had to the facts and circum­stances of the case, the sentence of death is altered to imprison-ment for life — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 374 — Penal Code (XLV of 1860) S 302. The State Vs. Kalu Bepari 10 (BLD (HCD) 373 .

 

Extenuating circumstances — Young age of the condemned prisoner and the delay in disposl of the reference are ex­tenuating circumstances for commuting the sentence of death to transportation for life — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 376 State Vs. Masudur Rahman alias Masud 4 BLD (HCD) 228 Ref. 13 DLR 646; 27 DLR 77
