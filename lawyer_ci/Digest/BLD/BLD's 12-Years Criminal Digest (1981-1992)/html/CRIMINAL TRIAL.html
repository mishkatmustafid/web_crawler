CRIMINAL TRIAL

 

Criminal Trial — Common object — It is incumbent on the prosecution to lead evidence as to which deadly weapons each of the accused was carrying for proving charges of unlawful assembly and rioting — The case of each individual accused ought to have been examined so that mere spectators or friends and relations of the accused party, who had not joined the assembly and who were unaware of its motive, had not been branded as members of the unlawful assembly — The Penal Code (XLV of 1860) S 149 Ali Akhar Khan and others Vs. The State 2 BLD (HCD) 170. Ref. AIR 1956 (SC) 181.

 

Abduction and Rape — Position of victim — In cases where the victim was abduced by deceitful means or by force or if she was ravished against her will, she can not be said to be an accomplice — In such circumstances, she is a victim of an outrage.

 

Acceptance of uncorroborated testi­mony — On the basis of facts of each case the Court has to come to a finding as to whether it is safe to act upon the testimony of the victim without being supported by independent corroborative evidence in material particulars — In case the judge decides to accept her un- corroborated testimony, he should state reasons — Evidence Act (1 of 1872) Ss. 114, 111 (b) and 133.

 

Statement of a victim girl after the commission of the offence — Such a statement is legally admissible as cor­roboration — Its value and weight is, however, a different matter — Evidence Act (1 of 1872) S 157 Abdul Quddus and another Vs. The State 3 BLD (HCD) 18. Ref. 1952 SCR (Ind) 377; 10 DLR 237; 2 KB (1916) 658

 

Criminal liability of the officers of the company — Officers of a company cannot be prosecuted for non-payment of outstanding dues from the company — Company incorporated under the Com­panies Act is a juristic person — Officers and agents are not personally liable for the inability, refusal or denial of the company to pay any outstanding dues and they cannot be prosecuted — Such criminal proceeding must be quashed — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 561A A. F. M. Abul Hasnat alias Raza Miah and others Vs. Idris AH and others 3 BLD (HCD) 182

 

Attempt and preparation to commit an offence of dealing in the black market — Mere possession of rationed commodities or fair price commodities will not per se attract the mischief of such an offence unless there is some evidence to show overt act or attempt or prepara­tion — The Special Powers Act (XIV of 1974) S 25 and S 6.The prosecution must prove that apart from possession of the articles the accused took some steps in devising or arranging means necessary for committing the offence Basiruddin Ahmed Vs. The State 3 BLD (HCD) 132Ref. 23 Cr.L J. 108; ILR 21 (1942) (Pat) 66.

 

Criminal breach of trust in respect of partnership property — A partner cannot be charged under Section 406 of the Penal Code in respect of partnership property jointly belonging to him and the complainant partner — Until dissolution of the partnership it cannot be said that he holds the property in a fiduciary capacity — In the absence of a special agreement between the partners that a particular property would be the separate property of a partner, there cannot be an entrustment of it to the other partner or partners — Penal Code (XLV of 1860) s 406. Mostafa Chowdhury and another Vs. The State and another 3 BLD (HCD) 190. ref. 55 CWN 541; 3 DLR 449; 17 DLR 382

 

Trial without the aid of assessors —Case records sent to the court of Sessions on 15.6.77 — Law reforms Ordinance came into force on 1.6.79 — Trial started with the framing of formal charge on 16.3 81 — Trial without the assessors is not vitiated — Law reforms Ordinance, 1978 (XLIX of 1978) S 4 Mahitullah Pk. and others Vs. The State 3 BLD (HCD) 277 Ref.l9DLR(SC)241.

 

Relationship is no ground to disbe­lieve a witness — Relationship is not a ground for disbelieving the evidence of witness when the witness's evidence appears to be truthful — Prosecution is not bound to examine each and every witness — Enemity cuts both ways and it cannot itself be a ground for disbelieving an eye witness — Evidence Act (1 of 1872) S 118 Mahitullah Pk. and others Vs. The State 3 BLD (HCD) 277.

 

A criminal proceeding when a civil suit is pending over the same matter —It is well-settled that when a matter has given rise to a civil liability and the dispute of a civil nature, the criminal proceeding should not be allowed to continue — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 561 A. Quamrun Nessa Begum and others Vs. The State and another 4 BLD(AD)215.

 

Discharging the Rule in a Criminal Revision in the absence of the Petitioner — While discharging the Rule in a criminal revision in the absence of the petitioner, the High Court Division should not comment on the merit of the case. Md Torab Ali Biswas Vs. The State and another 4 BLD (AD) 34.

 

A Magistrate has no jurisdiction to oust a person in possisson — A Magis­trate has no jurisdiction to oust a person in possession when the subject matter of the case before him is in seisin of the civil court. Professor Md Shahul Huda Vs. Md Shaft 4 BLD (AD 165.

 

Review of judgment or order — It is well-settled that a judgment or order passed on merit cannot be reviewed by the criminal Court — Section 369 Cr.P C operates in full force and applies to all judgments of all criminal courts — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 369 JohedAli Vs. The State 4 BLD (AD) 168.

 

Judgment in  Criminal  Appeal —Judgment in criminal appeal should discuss evidence, nature of the offence and the part played by individual accused persons — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 423 Serajuddowla and others Vs. The State 4 BLD (AD) 213.

 

Criminal Liability of a surety — Asurety incurs civil as well as criminal liability for his failure to produce the tractor before the court as promised — Both civil and criminal liabilities may co­exist and are not mutually exclusive — Penal Code (XLV of I860) S 405. Shahidullah Patwary Vs. The State 4 BLD (AD)25 Ref. AIR 1967 (Cal) 460.

 

Contents of judgment by the Appel­late Court — A judgment in criminal appeal should contain discussion of facts the evidence on record and conclusions with reasons, otherwise the appeal must be reheard Ibrahim Bepari and others Vs. Lutfur Rahman Bhuiyan and another 4 BLD (HCD) 297.

 

No revision lies when appeal is open— Court has no power to entertain any proceeding by way of revision at the in­stance of the party when an appeal was open to him. Alt Hossain and others Vs. The State and another 4 BLD (HCD) 221.

 

Objection to jurisdiction of a crimi­nal court — It can be taken at any stage of the proceeding even before the High Court Division in revision. If the Court had no jurisdiction to try the case, the question of submitting to jurisdiction cannot arise as parties by mutual consent can neither confer nor take away juris­diction Rajah AH and others Vs. The State 4 BLD (HCD) 264.

 

Restoration of a criminal case — Dropping of a criminal proceeding and release of the accused persons on erro­neous view of law do not operate either as discharge or as acquittal — In such a case the Court can restore the case Moklesur Rahman and others Vs. The State 4 BLD (HCD) 65.

 

Findings of the criminal court —These are not binding on the civil Court — An order passed under section 145 Cr.P.C. cannot be treated as substantive evidence of possession — A judgment of acquittal in a criminal case only decides that the accused has not been found guilty of the offence charged with but the opinion and conclusion expressed in the judgment are not admissible in evidence in civil courts — Evidence Act (1 of 1872) S 43. Akhter Hossain Shariff and others Vs. Munshi Akkas Hossain and others 3 BLD (AD) 334Restoration of a criminal revision dismissed for default — The order of dismissal of a criminal revision for default without touching the merit of the case is not a judgment of conviction or acquittal and as such an order of such dismissal can be vacated and the case heard again — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 369 Muzahar Sikder Vs. Fariduddin Ahmed and others 3 BLD (HCD) 96. Ref. AIR 1949 (FC) 1; 22 DLR (SC) 258.

 

Summary  dismissal  of appeal —Summary dismissal of an appeal without disposal on merit is not in accordance with law and the spirit of dispensation of justice — It is not contemptated by law — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 407 Jamsed and others Vs. Abdus Samad and other 3 BLD (HCD) 68.

 

Trademark dispute — When a bonafide dispute exists between the par­ties as to the right to use a trade mark action should be taken before a civil court and not before a criminal court Md. Salahuddin and another Vs. Abdul Hakim and another 3 BLD (HCD) 315Ref. ILR 22 (Mad) 488; 1 1 CWN 887 AIR 1936 (Cal) 488.

 

Duty of the prosecution in framing charge — The accused must be specifi­cally told in the accusation under Section 242 Cr.P.C with what specific offence and acts he is being charged with — The prosecution must also prove that apart from possession of articles   the accuser took some steps in devising or arranging means necessary for committing the offence Basiruddin Ahmed Vs. The State 3 BLD (HCD) 132Ref. 23 O.L.J (1922) 108, ILR 21 (1942) (Pat) 667.

 

Inmates of the house when not ma­terial witnesses — The deceased was not killed in his house and as such the inmates of the house and the relations of the deceased were not material witnesses. Sudhir and others Vs. The State 3 BLD (HCD) 293.

 

Order of conviction and sentence passed by Assistant Sessions Judge —Appeal against any judgment passed by the Assistant Sessions Judge deemed to be an Additional Sessions Judge involving sentence not exceeding five (5) years imprisonment lies to the Court of Sessions and not to the High Court Division — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) Ss. 9(3), 29C, 31(4), 408 and 410. Shawkat Ali Kayal Vs. The State 3 BLD (HCD) 250.

 

Adjournment cost in criminal pro­ceeding — Adjournment cost cannot be awarded against the absentee accused though in exceptional circumstances the criminal court can award adjournment cost — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 344 Md Habihur Rahman Vs. The State and another 1 BLD (HCD) 443.Ref. 19 Cr.L.J 326; 23 Cr L.J 243; 4 Cr L J 78; 48 CWN 684; 49 Cr.L.J. 304; AIR 1962 (Cal) 98.

 

Criminal Trial — Omnibus statement and assessment of evidence — Omnibus evidence in general terms to the effect that all the other accused persons armed with lathis have also assaulted the deceased need, of necessity, be scrutinized very strictly in order to eliminate all chances of false and mistaken implication. Anil Krishna Somaddar and other Vs. The State 1 BLD (HCD) 401.

 

 

Inordinate delay in holding trial —Shockingly inordinate delay in holding the trial is a ground for quashing the proceeding_Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 561A Md Shafiqullah Vs. The State 1 BLD (HCD) 119. Ref. PLD 1963 (Lahore) 32; PLD 1965 (HCD) 287

 

Imprisonment in default of payment of fine — Section 33(1) Cr PC authorizes the Magistrate to award terms of imprisonment in default of payment of fine__Section 33(1) Cr PC governs cases punishable with sentence and fine as well as cases punishable with fine only — Where the offence is punishable with imprisonment as well with fine and where the offence is punishable with fine only, the imprisonment in default of payment of fine shall be simple and the maximum term is for six months — All Courts possess power to direct recovery of fine when the offence is punishable with fine only by any of the three methods, such as by issuing distress warrants or by referring the matter to the Collector or by committing the offender to the prison — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) Ss. 33(1), 386(1) The State Vs. Abul Kashem 5 BLD (AD) 166

 

Conviction without framing charge _A conviction of an accused who was not charged with the offence cannot be maintained by taking recourse to Section 533 Cr.P.C. — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 535/(2) Joynal Abedin and others Vs. The State 5 BLD (AD) 257. Ref. 9 BLD (HCD)419.

Suo motu acquittal to non-appealing accused — Excersing suo motu power the High Court Division can set aside conviction and sentence of non-appealing accused while deciding an appeal filed by another accused, if such non-appealing accused is found not guilty of the offence Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 439 Mahmud AH and others Vs. Whether defence suggestion at the trial can be taken as the basis for conviction — In the existing scheme of criminal trials an accused can be convicted either on his pleading guilty to the charge or on his confession under Section 164 Cr.P.C. or extra-judicial confession when strongly corroborated — Suggestion by the defence lawyer can not be construed as an admission of guilt — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) Ss 164, 243 and 265E. Md Khaliluddin alias Kaliluddin Vs. The State 6 BLD (AD) 1.

 

Acquittal of non-appealing accused_It is now well-settled that the convic­tion of a non-appealing accused can be set aside while disposing of the appeal by the co-accused — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 439 Md All Haider and ors. Vs. The State 8 BLD (HCD) 305.

 

Managing Director, Chairman and Directors of a Company — They are the trustees of the company which comes to their hands or under their control and as such they can be prosecuted for mis­appropriation of the fund of the company Md Yameen and another Vs. Forum of appeal from sentence passed by the Assistant Sessions Judge__The law makers although amended Section 407 Cr.P.C. which deals with the forum of appeal from the sentence passed by the Magistrates of Second Class and Third Class did not amend Section 408 Cr.P.C. relating to forum of appeal from the sentence passed by the Assistant Sessions Judge and Magistrates of the First class — Such appeal shall lie to the Sessions Judge — The consensus of judicial opinion of different Benches, except the opinion of the Chittagong Bench expressed in a different context, is that notwithstanding the proviso to sub-Section (3) of Section 9 Cr.P.C. an appeal against an order of conviction and sentence not exceeding five years passed by an Assistant Sessions Judge deemed to be an Additional Sessions Judge shall lie to the court of Sessions under section 408 Cr.P.C. subject to the prov'ison to c)ause (b) and (c) thereunder when appeal shall lie to the High Court Division — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) Ss 9(3) and 408. Trial of Sessions Case — When it becomes pending — It will be too wide a proposition to say that with the taking of cognizance the trial becomes pending — In the instant case all the accused ap­peared and the case was taken up for hearing on 16.3 81 — This date makes the beginning of the pendencey of the trial — If the case was not taken out of the trial list on 15.8.1978 there would have been no difficulty in holding that the trial was to be held under the old law — Since the case went out of the trial list and all the accused appeared and the date was fixed for trial on 16.3.1981, the trial actually started on that date — Law reform' Ordinance (XLIX of 1978) S 4. Mohitulla Sk. alias Mohitulla and others Vs. The State 6 BLD (AD) 337. Ref. 19 DLR (SC) 243; AIR 1958 (All) 439; AIR 1933 (Cal) 551, AIR 1944 (FC) 1; AIR 1920(Mad) 337 (FB). AJR J 950 (All) 369.

 

Charge sheet and Final Report —Meaning of— A police report in which the accused is recommended to be prosecuted is called the charge-sheet and the police report in which no accused is recommended to be prosecuted is ordi­narily called a final report. Abdul Awal Vs. Abdul Mannan and another 6 BLD (AD) 328.

 

Tendering  prosecution  witness —When should be made — A witness may be tendered by the prosecution if his evidence is not of much importance or his evidence will make unnecessary addition to the evidence already adduced but an important witness should not be tendered — Tendering witness from motive is deprecated. Shamsul Huq alias Shamsul and others Vs. The State 6 BLD (AD) 216.

 

Up-Zilla Nirbahi Officer has no power to hear and dispose of pendingcases — Up-Zilla Nirbahi Officer though a Magistrate of the First Class has been given limited judicial power, namely to take cognizance of the offence under Sec­tion 190 of the Code of Criminal Proce­dure, to grant bail and to adjourn the hearing of pending cases when the Up-zilla Magistrate remains absent but he has been given no power to hear and dispose of pending cases — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 12. Shahab-uddin Vs. Tasiruddin and another 6 BLD (HCD) 416 Ref. 37 DLR 204.

 

Enhancement of sentence — Altera­tion of simple imprisonment into rigorous imprisonment amounts to enhancement of sentence and as such in the absence of any opportunity given to the accused peti­tioners to show cause, the order of the Session Judges altering the sentence of simple imprisonment to one of rigorous imprisonment cannot be sustained — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 423 Kalu Mirza and others Vs. The State and others 6 BLD (HCD) 161.

 

Sessions Judge has no power to quash a criminal proceeding — A Session Judge has no power to quash a criminal proceeding pending before the Magistrate as the High Court Division exercises exclusive power for quashing a criminal proceeding — If the Sessions Judge is of the opinion that no prima facie case has been made out he can refer the case to the High Court Division — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) Ss. 435, 438 439A and 561A. Mahmudul Huq Vs. Golam Mowla 6 BLD (HCD) 1 Ref. AIR 1924 (Cal) 1018; AIR 1925 (Mad) 39; AIR 1933 (Bomb) 409.

 

Exparte disposal of Criminal Motion without notice to the opposite party —A criminal motion can not be disposed of exparte by the Sessions Judge without serving any notice on the opposite party and without giving him an opportunity of being heard — The powers exercised by the Sessions Judge are the powers which may be exercised by the High Court Division and therefore the Sessions Judge is not only bound by the terms of Section 439 but is also bound by the procedure contained therein — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) Ss. 439 and 439A. Md. Abut Bashar and others Vs. The State and another 6 BLD (HCD) 249.

 

Strict scrutiny of oral evidence — In a case where witnesses are related and partisan and have strong motive to depose falsely, their evidence must be put to the strict scrutiny having regard to the attending circumstances Ibrahim Mollah and others Vs. The State 7 BLD (AD) 248.

 

Simple hurt and medical evidence —The evidence of a medical expert is merely an opinion which only lends corroboration to the direct evidence — When there are other reliable evidences to prove the offence, medical evidence is not indispensable — Penal Code (XLV of 1860) S 324. Conviction on pleading guilty — The admission of guilt is to be recorded in the language of the accused — The reply of the accused while pleading guilty to the charged should be set down as nearly as possible in his own words and that having not been done the Court is not in a position to know what he actually admitted — The accused may not understand the implication of the charge unless it is clearly explained to him that the consequences of admission of guilt may be fraught with danger — It is not safe to base the conviction of an accused on guilty pleading alone in a case of murder — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 243 Abdul Latif Vs. The State 7 BLD (HCD) 432 Ref. 11 DLR 5; AIR 1928 (Cal ) 775.

 

 

Ex-parte disposal of a criminal appeal — It is well settled that for the disposal of a criminal appeal the presence of the appellant's Advocate is not essential but the appellate court is to write down the judgment in accordance with the provision of Section 367(1) of the Code of Criminal Procedure — The appellate Court should not base its conclusion on the discussion in the judgment of trial Court — It is required to formulate the points for determination, consider the evidence with reference to the points in issue and give decision thereon with reasons — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) Ss. 367 and 424. Ahul Eashar and others Vs. The State 7 ELD (HCD) 345. Ref. 22 DLR (SC) 263, 25 DLR (Dhaka) 330; 3 BCR (HCD) 239.

 

Trial of Public Servants — Govern­ment servants cannot be tried by the ordinary criminal courts for offences of forgery and cheating with private persons — Such offences against the public servants are exclusively triable by the Special Judge appointed under the Criminal Law Amendment Act — The Additional District Magistrate has no jurisdiction to try the case involving offences under Sections 417 and 471 of the Penal Code — Criminal Law Amendment Act (XL of 1958) S 5 Md. Rahmat AH and others Vs. The State 7BLD (HCD) 137.

 

Groundless or preposterous allega­tion — Ground for quashing — A pro­ceeding is liable to be quashed when the allegation upon which it is based is, on the face of it, groundless or so prepos­terous that no man of ordinary prudence will take any notice of it — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 561 A. Md Samsuddin alias Lamhu and others Vs. The State and others 8 BLD (AD) 93.

 

 

Trial of an accused against whom no process has been issued — The power of the Sessions Judge to order for trial of an accused against whom no process has been issued is not included in the revi-sional power either of the Sessions Judge or of the High Court Division — When the complaint was dismissed on an erro­neous view of law the only course open to the Sessions Judge was to direct further enquiry — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 898) Ss.204, 436, 439 and 439A. Simultaneous trial of two cases —Requirements for holding such trial — The requirement of a simultaneous trial is that the trial must be held in quick succession so that there occurs an overall picture to the mind of the Judge — If there is a long gap between the two trials the purpose for which a simultaneous trial is held by the same court is almost lost. Alauddin Molla and others Vs. The State and others 8 BLD (AD) 221.

Concurrent finding of fact in a criminal case — By practice the Ap­pellate Division does not ordinarily in­terfere with a finding of fact, more so with a concurrent finding of fact of the High Court Division and the trial Court — interference by the Appellate Division is exceptional.

 

According to the practice of the Ap­pellate Division it interferes even with a concurrent finding of fact where it is based on no evidence or it has been ar­rived at in disregard of a judicial processor well-known principles of appreciation of evidence or it is the result of misreading of evidence or it is so unrea­sonable or grossly unjust that no rea­sonable person would judicially arrive at that conclusion — In criminal matters the Appellate Division also interferes where the prosecution case seems incredible and fiction-like and where the lone-witness belatedly discloses his knowledge of the commission of the offence. Abu Taker Chowdhury and others Vs. The State 11 BLD (AD) 2.

Syed Ali Mir and another Vs. Syed Omar Ali and another 10 BLD (AD) 168.

 

Time given by illiterate village woman__The idea of time of the occurrence with such a woman is often very vague and it becomes difficult to pinpoint the time of occurrence — This very material fact should not be overlooked while acquitting the accused in the face of positive ocular evidence of eye-witnesses on record — The order of acquittal of the principal accused-respondent is set aside and that of the trial court restored The State Vs. Mohammad Kha and others 10BLD (AD) 201.

Conviction by Martial Law Court —Whether conviction for a Martial Law offence brings one under disqualification for election — Martial Law is not a part of the constitutional scheme of the country and as such it may be good as long as Martial Law lasts but with its departure it no longer casts a shadow upon the ordinary laws of the land — The Local Government Ordinance makes no reference to a conviction by a Martial Law Court or to an offence under the Martial Law Regulations and as such the respondent did not incur any disqualifica­tion for election — The Local Govern­ment (Union Parishad) Ordinance (LI of 1983) S 7(2)(d) — Constitution of Bangladesh, 1972, Art. 102. Conviction on evidence — A Judge should dispose of a case on the basis of the evidence on record and the law ap­plicable thereto — Philosophical and moralizing comments are hardly relevant in determining the nature of sentence which an accused deserves in a particular case. Hazrat All and others Vs. The State 11 ELD (AD) 270.

 

Investigation Officer as informant —He is an interested witness and his tes­timony having not been corroborated by any independent and disinterested witness in material particulars, the prosecution case fails, Gazinur Rahman Vs. The State 11 BID (HCD) 11.

 

Smuggle goods — Whether contraband — Individual liability — Before convicting an accused on a charge of smuggling the prosecution is bound to prove that the seized goods are contraband goods and those were smuggled goods — It is also well-settled that before convicting any person in such a case, the individual liability of each of the accused must be considered Forkan Mondal and others Vs. The State 11 BLD (HCD) 231.

 

Innocence of accused — In a criminal trial the accused persons are not required to prove their innocence — It is the duty of the prosecution to prove the charge beyond reasonable doubt Abu Taker Chowdhury Vs. The State 11 BLD (AD) 2.

 

Background of Enmity — When there is a background of enmity it is unsafe to rely on the chance witnesses to connect the accused with the crime Nowsher Mollah and others Vs. The State 11 BLD (HCD) 295.

 

Natural probabilities — In a murder case the Court is to examine the circum­stances of the acts and their results for the purpose of gauging the natural prob­abilities when all the averments and the natural course of human conduct and probability of the case lead to the con­viction that the prosecution is not telling the truth, the benefit of doubt must be given to the accused.

 

Identification by torch light or hurricane light at dead of night is not sufficient unless the distance is a close proximity Nowsher Mollah and others Vs. The State 11 BLD (HCD) 295

 

Weight of witness — The weight to be attached to the testimony of a witness depends upon various considerations so that his evidence should not be inconsis­tent with probabilities and it must be consistent with other evidence and should generally fit in with material details of the case in order to carry conviction to a prudent mind — The test of his statement is its inherent worth in point of con­sistency, straight-forwardness as tested against physical probabilities and the circumstances surrounding the event to which the testimony relates. Nowsher Mollah and others Vs. The State 11 BLD (HCD) 295.

 

Single transaction — In order to de­termine   whether   the   offences were committed in the course of same trans­action, the Court will have to consider the questions of proximity of time, proximity of place continuity of action and community of purpose or design — Which factor or factors shall be given relative importance will depend on the facts of each case — The accusation must disclose the necessary facts upon which an inference as to the sameness of trans­action may be drawn. The Sate Vs. Constable Lai Miah and another 12 BLD (AD) 233

 

Stay of criminal case — Whether stay of a criminal case should be granted or not on the ground of pendency of a civil suit, the Court has to consider a number of things such as — in the two proceedings whether the parties are the same, whether the subject-matter is the same, whether there is likelyhood of prejudice to the accused and whether it is necessary or expedient in the interest of justice to grant such stay — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 344. Hussain Mohammad Ershad Vs. The State 12 BLD (AD) 67.

 

Judgment passed by criminal Court in the absence of accused — Such a judgment cartnot be deemed to be invalid because of the absence of the accused or his lawyer. Kazi Shaziruddin Ahmed Vs. Bangladesh 12 BLD (HCD) 49

 

Belated disclosure of the knowledge of the commission of the offence of murder — Worth of its credit and its impact on the prosecution case For the belated disclosure of the knowledge of the commission of offence, if fear or threat is easily accepted as an explanation, particularly in a case punish­able with death, then it will danger administration of criminal justice by open­ing up opportunities or concoctions and false implication, the impact of fear or threat will no doubt vary from person to person — In this case the spell of fear continued rather too long for PWs, to carry any conviction — In a gruesome murder like the present one when the accused cannot be brought to book for lack of evidence beyond reasonable doubt the prosecution instead of bewailing on an order of acquittal should take it and improve its quality of investigation in other cases. Abu Taker Chowdhury and others Vs. The State 11 BLD (AD) 2.

 

Conviction on a charge of murder —Nature of evidence required — In order to convict a person of a charge for which capital sentence is provided, there should be evidence of unimpeachable character bringing home the guilt of the accused beyond reasonable doubts. Suspicion, however strong it might be, is no substitute for legal evidence. Tareque Habihullah Vs. The State 11 BLD (HCD) 146.

Fugitive from Law — The appellants surrendered before the Sessions Judge upon conviction and obtained an interim bail — It is true that this order was illegal but the fact remains that they submitted themselves to the sentence passed and obtained bail albit wrongly — In the facts of the case it will be less than charitable to attribue to them that they were "fugitives from law" — Since the appellants were not covered by a legal order of bail, the proper order the High Court Division ought to have passed was to direct them to surrender within a certain time before taking up the petition of appeal for hearing — Since the appeal has not been heard on merit the case is remanded to the High Court Division for disposal in accordance with law — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) Ss. 410 and 426. Fugitive from justice — Sentence of death — In a reference under Section 474 Cr.P.C. for confirmation of death sentence,  irrespective of whether the convict is a fugitive from justiceand has not submitted to the due process of law or justice, the Court is under an obligation under Sections 375 and 376 Cr.P.C. to satisfy itself that the impugned order of conviction is justified on the evidence on record and that the sentence of death is justifiable under the facts and circums­tances of the case — In doing so, it may confirm the sentence or may pass any sentence warranted by law or may even annul the conviction or order a fresh trial — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) Ss. 374, 375 and 376. The State Vs. Kalu Bepari 10 BLD (HCD) 373.
