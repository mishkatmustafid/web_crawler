CRUELTY TO WOMEN (DETERRENT PUNISHMENT) ORDINANCE (LX OF 1983)

 

Cruelty to Women —When the offence is not constituted — It has not been alleged anywhere that the accused attempted to cause death or grievous hurt while demanding dowry — It has been simply alleged that accused beat the informant while demanding dowry — Such an allegation does not come within the mischief of the Ordinance — Cruelty to Women (Detterrent Punishment) Ordinance (LX of 1983) Ss 6 and 10 Appeal — Informant's competence to take an appeal in a case under the Special Powers Act — In the matter of filing a complaint before the Special Tribunal the informant has no locus stand and as such it follows that she has no locus stand in the matter of filing an appeal also — The Government or the accused alone can file an appeal against any order, judgment or sentence of the Special Tribunal — The Special Powers Act (XIV of 1974) Ss. 27 and 30. Firoza Begum Vs. Hor mux. AH and another 8 BLD (HCD) 122.

 

Power of Sessions Judge — In view of the case made in the FIR and the evidence of the PWs and the observation of the Sessions Judge himself that it is a devilish cruelty to a woman for dowry, the alleged offence is beyond his jurisdic­tion to be tried as an ordinary criminal case — The death reference is therefore rejected and the case is sent back to be registered as a Special Tribunal Case and tried as such — Cruelty to Women (Deterrent Punishment) Ordinance (LX of 1983) S 6 — Special Powers Act (XIV of 1974) Ss. 10 and 26 Para — 4B to Sch. of offences The State Vs. Khalilur Rahman 9 BLD (HCD) 43.

 

"Dowry" — Property or valuable security constitutes "dowry" only when it is demanded before or at the time of marriage — The husband of the victim woman or the husband's parents, guardian or any relation may commit the offence of demanding "dowry" as consideration for marriage at any time after the marriage — Cruelty to Women (Detterrent Punish­ment) Ordinance (LX 1983) S 6 Salema Khatun and others Vs. The State 9 BLD (HCD) 73.

 

Kidnapping a woman for unlawful purposes — When the charge is not sustainable — If the alleged victim is below 16 years her father can claim her custody but if she is above 16 she is entitled to go according to her free will — For the purpose of Section 366A of the Penal Code she must be between 16 and 18 years — If she is above 16 but below 18 and if no force is used to go with the accused, it will not be an offence under the Penal Code or the Ordinance — Penal Code (XLV of 1860) S 366A — Cruelty to Women (Deterrent Punishment) Ordi­nance (LX of 1983) S 4(b). Manindra Kumar Malakar Vs. The State 10 BLD (HCD) 119. Ref. AIR 1933 (Cal) 362; 23 DLR (DAC) 126; 35 DLR 127
