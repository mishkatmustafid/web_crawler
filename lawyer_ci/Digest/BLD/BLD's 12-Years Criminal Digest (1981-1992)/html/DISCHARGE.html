DISCHARGE

 

Prosecution of discharged accused —Whether without jurisdiction — If in fact it is a revival of the same proceeding, it is without jurisdiction but if the second prosecution is based on a fresh complaint it will not be without jurisdiction because the Magistrate does not preside over the same proceedings — He is functus officio in so far as the first proceedings are concerned but not in respect of the new proceeding — Code of Criminal Pro­cedure (V of 1898) S 250B Ahdus Salam Master and another Vs. The State 3 BLD (HCD) 267 Ref. 14 DLR (SC) 96; 30 DLR 344.

 

Further inquiry after discharge of the accused — When the order of discharge is by a Magistrate, one can not file before the Magistrate a petition for revival of the same case — The aggrieved party may move the superior Court for further inquiry — The superior court can merely order for further inquiry but neither the Court of Sessions nor the High Court Division can direct the subordinate court for taking of cognizance — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) S 436 Abdus Sal am Master and another Vs. The State 3 BLD (HCD) 267.

 

 

Discharge — Acceptance of Final Report necessarily results in the release of the accused form custody or from the bail bond, as the case may be, and the Magistrate's order releasing the accused is ordinarily referred to as an order of "discharge", although the term 'discharge' appears no where in Section 173 Cr.P C — This "discharge" is different from a "discharge" under Sections 253 or 209 Cr.P.C. where discharge takes place after cognizance is taken and process is issued against a person. Abdur Rashid Vs. The State 10 BLD (HCD) 407.

 

Discharge by the Sessions Judge —The Sessions Judge is empowered to discharge the accused persons when he finds no prima facie case against them — Statement of the witnesses recorded under Section 161 Cr.P.C. may be con­sidered for ascertaining a prima facie case against the accused persons — The material test is whether the fact alleged constitutes an offence or not — Whether the offence has been committed by the accused persons or not is a matter which can be decided at the trial on taking evi­dence — Section 265C Cr.P.C. authorises the Sessions Judge to discharge the accused persons if on the materials on record before him he finds no sufficient ground to proceed against them — Sec­tion 265D Cr.P.C. authorises the Court to frame charges against the accused persons if the Court is satisfied about the existence of a prima facie case against them — The Sessions Judge is to assign reasons based on materials on record for discharging the accused persons — Code of Criminal Procedure (V of 1898) Ss. 265 and 265D Md Kamiuddin Ahmed Vs. Abu Bakar and others 6 BLD (HCD) 96.
