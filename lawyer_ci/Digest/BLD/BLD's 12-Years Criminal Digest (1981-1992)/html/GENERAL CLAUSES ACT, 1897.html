GENERAL CLAUSES ACT(X OF 1897)

 

Repeal of Section 250(B) Cr.P.C. - Whether it affected the right of the accused to be discharged -— A privilege of an accused to obtain an order of discharge has been taken away by the repeal and as such the repeal would not affect the operation of the omitted provisions — The repealed provision is not a procedural one and it shall not affect the right of the accused or the power of the Magistrate to discharge accused persons — General Clauses Act (X of 1897) S 6(C) Didarul Alain and others Vs. The State and others. 3 BLD (HCD) 308 Ref. 20 DLR (SC) 315; 21 DLR (SC) 123.

 

Repeal of an enactment Us effect in a pending legal proceeding - In a pending proceeding the accused acquires a vested right to be tried under the law existing at the time when cognizance is taken — Even repeal of the law does not affect this right in the absence of a clear provision — The proceeding shall be continued as if the repealing Ordinance has not been made — General Clauses Act (X of 1897) S 6. The State Vs. Mokhul Hossain 6 BLD (HCD) 34.Ref. 30 DLR 49.

 

Words in the singular include the plural and vice versa — Section 13(2) of the General Clauses Act provides that in all enactments unless there is anything repugnant in the subject or context, words in the singular shall include the plural and the vice versa and as such the expression "an enquiry" in Section 202 Cr.P.C. includes more than one enquiry to ascertain the truth or falsehood of the complaint — Code of Criminal Procedure (Y of r«W) S 202 — General Clauses Act (X of 1897) S 13(2) Sarwar Kama! and others Vs.
