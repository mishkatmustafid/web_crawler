HEARSAY EVIDENCE

 

Hearsay Evidence — Generally speak­ing the statements or declarations of persons not called as witness but repeated by a third person are excluded as hearsay — But statement of a person not called as a witnesses is admissible when it is proposed to establish by evidence not the truth of the statement but the fact that it was made — Evidence Act (1 of 1872) S 60 S. M. Quamruzzaman Vs. The State 1 BLD (HCD) 107.Ref. AIR 1942 (Cal) 214; 1 (1956) WLR. 965; 97 IC 785.

 

Hearsay Evidence— When statement cannot be ruled out as hearsay — It is hearsay and inadmissible when the object of the evidence is to establish the truth of what is contained in the statement — It is not hearsay and is admissible when it is proposed to establish not the truth of the statement but the fact that it was madeEvidence Act (1 of 1872) S 60. Shafiullah alias Kala Mia Vs. The State 5 BLD (HCD) 129.Ref. 1 (1956) WLR (65 9970); 1 (1960) All ER331.

 

Hearsay Evidence — When it appears that all the witnesses heard about the occurrence, such kind of evidence is merely hearsay evidence and accused petitioners can not be convicted on the basis of such evidence — Evidence Act (1 of 1872) S 60 Yusuf Ali and others Vs. The State 8 BLD (HCD) 36.

 

Hearsay Evidence — Section 32 of the Evidence Act is an exception to the well known maxim 'hearsay evidence is no evidence' — The dying declaration though made to a police officer of a different police station is to be presumed to have been made to a private person and for non-examination of the person who recor­ded the declaration, no exception is legally sustainable as to the mode of proving the dying declaration — Evidence Act (1 of 1872) S 32. .V. K. Shamsur Rahman alias Shamsu Vs. The State 10 BLD (AD) 251.

 

Hearsay Evidence — When admissible — Statement of the deceased made before his murder as to the circumstances of the transaction resulting in his death is admissible in evidence — The witness stated that in the evening of the date of occurrence he heard from deceased Khalil that the two accused had hired his van and took him to Jessore to bring greencoconuts — Though this part of the evidence is hearsay from the deceased, it is admissible in evidence being the circumstances of the transaction which resulted in the death of the deceased — Evidence Act (1 of 1872) S 32. Abul Kashem and another Vs. The State 10 BLD (HCD) 309
