Citizenship Act, 1951

 

Citizenship does not evaporate with the passing of time but it clings to a person wherever he may roam. It cannot be taken away from a citizen unless he voluntarily renounces it or loses it in due course of law. The onus of proving that the citizen has lost his citizenship is on the Government who seeks to deprive him of his right as a citizen.

Ganga Narayan Roy Vs. Secretary, Ministry of Home Affairs, 14 BLD (HCD) 215.
