Declaration And Alteration Of Limits Of Union Rules, 1983

 

Rule—2(I)

Local Government (Union Parishad) Ordinance, 1983, Section—3

Section 3 of the Ordinance authorises the Deputy Commissioner to divide and reconstitute the rural areas into Unions in the prescribed manner while Rule 2(1) of the Declaration and Alteration of Limits of Union Rules, 1983 provides that the Government may also give necessary directions upon which the Deputy Commissioner has to act under the Rules. Such directions of the Government cannot be construed as infringement upon the authority of the Deputy Commissioner.

Abdul Maleque Vs. The Secretary, Ministry of Local Government and others, 15 BLD (AD) 155.

 

DEFENCE OF PAKISTAN ORDINANCE (23 OF 1965)

DEFENCE OF PAKISTAN RULES, 1965

ENEMY PROPERTY (LAND AND BUILDINGS) ADMINISTRATION AND DISPOSAL ORDER, 1966

 

Following the withdrawal of emergency in February, 1969 Enemy Property Ordinance (Emergency Provisions), 1969 came into force in which several provisions of the Emergency Powers Rules were kept alive. In 1974 the Emergency Property (Continuance of Emergency Provisions)(Repealed) Ordinance, 1974 was promulgated which was subsequently made Act No. XLV of 1974. This was followed by Act No. XLVI of 1974. Under the provisions of the aforesaid Ordinance the Vested and Non- resident properties were being administered. The Vested and Nonresident Property (Administration) (Repealed) Ordinance, 1974 was replaced by Ordinance No. 92 of 1976 and in that Ordinance there was a saving clause for protecting the existing proceedings and lists in respect of vested and non- resident properties. Excepting this saving clause the vested property law was totally repealed. In the circumstances, treating the suit property as a vested and non-resident property in the year 1979 by starting a new V. P. case is illegal and without jurisdiction.

Additional Deputy Commissioner (Revenue) Dhaka Vs. Mohammad Mostafa All Mridha and others, 15 BLD (HCD) 1.

Ref: Civil Revision No. 3375 of 1991 (Dhaka)—Cited.

 

 

 

DHAKA WATER SUPPLY AND SEWERAGE AUTHORITY ORDINANCE, 1963

 

Section—19

Dhaka Water Supply and Sewerage Authority (Sewerage Connection and Levy of Sewerage Rate) Rules, 1966, Rule— 6(3)

Section 19 of the Ordinance confers power on the authority to levy and collect water, sewer, and conservancy rate for its services in the prescribed manner. But sewer rate cannot be charged unless the owner of the holding actually uses the sewerage line. In other word, sewer rate cannot be charged against a holding in which no sewer connection has been taken.

Rule6(3) does not make any provision to charge sewer rate against a holding which does not have any sewer connection.

Mr. Yar Ahmed Vs. Dhaka Water Supply and Sewerage Authority, 14 BLD (HCD) 504.

 

Divorce Act, 1869 (Iv Of 1869)

 

Sections—1O and 7

It has been proved by admissible evidence that the mistress of the husband has given birth to a child and there has been irretrievable break down of the marriage on the proof of adultery committed by the husband. The result is that the decree passed by the District Court dissolving the marriage is confirmed.

Eva Sunanda Chowdhury Vs. Subir Sarker, 21 BLD (HCD) 8.

 

DISSOLUTION OF MUSLIM MARRIAGES ACT, 1939 (VIII OF 1939)

 

Section—2( VIII)

Grounds for dissolution of Marriage

For determining as to whether the wife is entitled to get her marriage dissolved through ‘Khula’, the question whether the husband treats his wife with cruelty or not, is not of much importance. If it appears that the husband and wife cannot live together in peace and amity and the wife offers considerations for the dissolution of the marriage, she is entitled to a decree for dissolution of marriage.

Sheerin Alam Chowdhury Vs. Captain Shamsul Alam Chowdhury, 16 BLD(HCD)24

 

DHAKA UNIVERSITY ORDER, 1973 (P.O. NO. 11 OF 1973)

 

Article—59(2)

The respondent, a Lower Division Assistant of the University of Dhaka, was removed from service for misconduct as he misconducted himself by violating the service discipline in refusing to vacate the staff quarter illegally occupied by him. Before removing him from service the University authority thrice asked him to show cause as to why he should not be removed from service for violation of service discipline. The respondent’s grievance that his removal from service offends against the principle of natural justice is without any substance.

Dhaka University and another Vs Syed Md. Ismail, 16 BLD (AD) 103.

 

 

 

EASEMENT ACT, 1882 (V OF 1882)

 

Section—44

Extinction of easement right

When the servient heritage of the subject- matter of the easement is so permanently altered by change of its character that the dominant owner can no longer enjoy the easement, the easement right in the property in question is totally extinguished.

Osi Meah Sowdager v. Tulsidham Akherar Madam Mahan Narasingha and others, 22 BLD (HCD) 492.

 

EAST PAKISTAN GOVERNMENT AND LOCAL AUTHORITY LAND AND BUILDINGS (RECOVERY OF POSSESSION) ORDINANCE, 1970 (XXIV OF 1970)

Sections — 2(b) and 5

After scrutiny of the relevant provisions of Ordinance No.24 of 1970 it appears that the Deputy Commissioner or Additional Deputy Commissioner or Joint Deputy Commissioner or a such other persons as may be appointed by the Government to perform any of the function of a Deputy Commissioner under the Ordinance is lawfully competent to pass an order of eviction of an unauthorised occupier of Government khas land. In view of the above the respondent No.2 was not competent to initiate the proceeding for eviction of the petitioner which is without any lawful authority and of no legal effect.

Abarak Ali Vs Mr.Shefaul Karim, Asstt. Commissioner, (Land) Jagannathpur, District Sunamganj, 19 BLD (HCD) 26.

 

EAST BENGAL (EMERGENCY) REQUISITION OF PROPERTY ACT, 1948 (XIII OF 1948)

Sections—3 and 5

Non utilization of the acquired land

Mere non-use of the acquired land for the purported propose for which it was acquired will not give any right to its previous owner to get return of the same. Once a property is validly acquired after meeting the legal formalities it vests in the Government and its previous owner does not have any right to ask return of the same for its non-utilization for the specific purpose for which it was acquired.

Al-Haj Abul Basher being dead his heirs (a)Hosne-Ara Begum and others Vs Bangladesh and others, 17 BLD (AD) 321.

 

Sections—5 and 8B

Acquisition of a property takes effect from the date of the gazette notification under section 5 of the Act.

Under Section 8B of the Act the Government is competent to release the land in question from acquisition before payment of compensation to its owner. Since in the instant case the petitioner’s property has been validly acquired, section 8B of the Act cannot be applied for releasing the land from acquisition.

Shah Ekramur Rahman Vs. Secretary, Ministry of Land, Dhaka and others, 14 BLD (HCD) 538.

 

Sections—5(7) and 8B

From the correspondences between the Government and the requiring body it is clear that the publication of the final notice in the Gazette under section 5(7) of the Act is in process. In the circumstances, the lawful order the High Court Division could pass under the law was to direct the appellants either to derequisition the case property under section 8B of the Act or to finally acquire the same by publishing in the Gazette a notification under section 5(7) of the Act. The High Court Division wrongly exceeded its jurisdiction in assuming the executive functions of the Government by officials directing release of the case property in favour of the writ petitioner.

Government of the People’s Republic of Bangladesh and others Vs Abdul Wahab Mia and others, 21 BLD (AD) 94.

 

Section—5(1)(7)

For assessment of compensation for the acquired property the period of “24 months” shall be the period preceding the date of notice under section 5(1). But there should be some proximity between notice under Section 5(1) and the notice of final acquisition under Section 5(7) of the Act.

Bangladesh Vs. Luxmi Bibi and others, 14 BLD (AD) 165.

Ref: 40 C.W.N. 83; 43 DLR (AD) 28; AIR. 1960 Cal. 258; A.I.R. 1921 Lah. 250; 42 DLR (AD) 100-Cited.

 

Section—8B

The Government’s choice to withdraw any property from acquisition as conferred by Section 8B of the Act must be exercised before the payment of compensation and not after it. Order of such withdrawal must be done by a notification published in the official gazette.

Bangladesh, represented by the Secretary, Ministry of L.A. & L.R. and others Vs. MIS. Commercial Trust of Bangladesh Ltd. and others, 14 BLD (AD) 72.

 

Section—7 (aaa)

Period of Limitation

The period of limitation for approaching the arbitrator under clause (aaa) of section 7 of the Act would be commuted from the date of receipt of the notice by awardee and not from the date of knowledge derived otherwise.

Md. Abdul Majid Dana Vs Bangladesh, 19 BLD (AD) 12.

 

Section—7 (aa)

After finalization of the proceeding for acquisition and notification thereof in the Gazette the Additional Land Acquisition Officer was required to obtain the approval of the Government before issuing the notice of the assessment of the compensation in Form J under clause (aa) of Section 7 of the Act. But in the instant case the records shows that no step for obtaining the approval of the Government was taken in the case of appellant.

The sequence of events shows that the appellant made application before the Additional Land Acquisition Officer claiming compensation when he had no title and interest in the case lands. As the notice of the assessment was collusively procured and as such it cannot be considered a notice under section 7 (aa) of the Act. Accordingly the arbitration proceeding was, therefore, without jurisdiction.

Md. Abdul Majid Dana Vs Bangladesh, 19 BLD (AD) 12.

 

Section—8B

There is no principle of law under which once a property has been validly acquired for a public purpose, as admitted by Government and has vested in the requiring body then even if the said property be utilized for a different purpose, the acquisition shall not stand void. A simple reading of section 8B of the Act shows that it does not come into operation in the circumstances of the case. So the order of withdrawal from acquisition in question cannot be sustained.

Brahamanbaria Pourashava Vs Secretary, Ministry of Land Reforms, Government of Bangladesh and others, 19 BLD (AD) 87.

 

 

Section—8B

After finality has reached to a proceeding for requisition and acquisition and compensation has been paid for the property, there can be no order for withdrawal from acquisition and return of the property to its former owner as section 8B of the Act stands as a bar against it.

Md. Matiur Rahman Vs. Bangladesh and others, 15 BLD (HCD) 331.

ELECTRICITY ACT, 1910 (IX OF 1910)

Section—24

The Power Development Board (PDB), whether commits any illegality in removing its transformer from the premises of defaulter customer.

The PDB committed no illegality in removing its transformer from the premises of the appellant’s rice mill since the appellant had been resorting to taking connection unauthorisedly and repeatedly without paying the bills due from him.

Md. Giasuddin Bhuiyan Vs. Chairman, Bangladesh Power Development Board, 13 BLD (AD) 72.

 

Section—26

Meters

Section 26 of the Act provides that in the absence of an agreement to the contrary the amount of energy supplied to a consumer or the electrical quantity contained in the supply shall be ascertained by means of a correct meter and the licensee shall if required by the consumer, cause the consumer to be supplied with such a meter.

Executive Engineer Sales and Distribution Division—1, Power Development Board, Circle—i, Shasangacha, Comilla and ors. Vs Messers Purba Espat Limited, 18 BLD (HCD) 627.
