Extradition Act, 1974 (Lviii Of 1974)

 

Section—4

Special Powers Act, 1974, Section—3(i ) and 2(t)(ii)

In a case where a citizen of a foreign country intends to stay in Bangladesh and if the Government is satisfied that the extradition of such person is called for in order to maintain friendly relations of Bangladesh with foreign States the Government can on the strength of section 3(i) and section 2(f)(ii) of the Special Powers Act extradite such citizen to his own country in absence of any extradition treaty between the Government of Bangladesh and such foreign States. The Government of Bangladesh has sufficient legal authority to extradite Anup Chetia to India in response to the right of India to demand extradition of Anup Chetia.

Saiful islam Dilder Vs Govt. of Bangladesh and others, 18 BLD (HCD) 615.

Ref: Ramos Vs. Diuz, 1498; M. Saleemullah Vs. Govt. of Bangladesh, 47 DLR 218—Cited.
