Pourasava Ordinance, 1977

(XXVI OF 1977)

 

Section—10(2)(g)

Rumu Thana Kalyan Bahini, a registered Co-Operative Society is a body corporate and loan taken by its Chairman on behalf of the samity does not render him disqualified for election under Section 10(2)(g) of the Pourashava Ordinance, 1977.

 

Section—10(2)(f)

This Section does not cover a case of defalcation, but it covers the case of pecuniary interest in-the affairs of the Pourashava to be a ground for qualification for election.

Nurul Absar Vs. A.K.M. Mozammel Haque and another, 14 BLD (HCD) 64.

 

Section—3(i) and Section—18B

After the Pourasava came into being there was no existence of Union Parishad and no election of Chairman and Members of the Union Parishad Could be held although the process of election was started before creation of the Pourasava.

Ataur Rahman Khan Vs. The Election Commission and others, 13 BLD (HCD) 494.

 

Section—149(6)

When two parts of the same Ordinance mean two things, the court is required to give that interpretation which carries out the intention of the law makers and that interpretation which makes the law unworkable is to be avoided. The Ordinance is self contained and all possible remedies for an aggrieved persons have been provided therein.

Where under an order of a Court of law, a vast multitude people will be deprived of the benefit of the utility service because of an order passed for the benefit of a few, such order should not be upheld.

Chowmuhani Paurashava Vs. Delta Jute Mills Ltd., 13 BLD (HCD) 256.

 

Section—152

The service of notice under section 152 of the Pourashava Ordinance as a condition precedent for institution of a suit—the provision of notice is mandatory, and not directory.

Md. Nurul Islam, Chairman, Kishurganj Paurashava and others v. M/S. Sheikh Enterprise and others, 21 BLD (HCD) 565.

Ref: Narayanganj Pourashava and others v. Abdur Razzaque 46DLR295; Sarder Uddin Chisty v. Bangladesh and others 50 DLR (AD)188; Md. Meher Uddin v. Gouripur Pourashava 2 BLC 88.
