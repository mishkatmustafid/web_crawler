Probate And Administration Act, 1881

 

Section—12

In every case where either the Will itself or anything done under it is challenged, whether proof of execution and capacity on the part of the testator and of the appointment of the executor, would be required.

In every case where either the Will itself, or anything done under it by the executor, is challenged, proof of execution and capacity on the part of the testator, and of the appointment of the executor, would be required. The object of the section is only to get rid of this multiplication of proofs. Probate once granted authenticates the Will against all the world; it affords a ready means of proof of the contents of the Will and it is a complete answer by the executor to any challenge of his authority as such.

Sree Jogendra Nath alias Govinda Sarker Vs Amulya Chandra Sarker and others, 13 BLD (AD) 160.
