Anti-Corruption Act, 1957

Section-3

Whether an officer of the Bureau of Anti-Corruption is competent to hold the investigation as the alleged offences under section 406/420/209 of the Penal Code are said to have been committed by private persons and not by public servants

There is nothing either in section 3 or in the schedule from which it can be said that an investigation to be made by an officer of the Bureau of Anti-Corruption in respect of the aforesaid offences must be alleged to have been committed by a public servant- this is not an authority for the proposition that the Bureau of Anti- Corruption has no authority to investigate the offences mentioned in the schedule of the Act unless they are committed by a public servant.

Sontosh Bhusan Das & Anr. Vs. The State & Ors. 4 BLT (AD)-58.

Section-3

We are conscious that no direction as prayed for by the petitioner for holding investigation can be given in this Writ petition, however, on perusal of the newspaper report, we agree with the learned Advocate for the petitioner that it contains some specific allegations against Respondent No. 1 about abuse of powers. The Respondent No. 1 at the relevant time held the responsible post of Secretary, Ministry of Works. Officers of the Bureau of Anti-Corruption are empowered under sub-section (2) of Section 3 of the Anti-Corruption Act, 1957 to hold any enquiry and investigation in respect of the offence specified in the schedule to that Act. We are of the view that though normally police cannot act on the basis of any report in the newspaper, as the matter has been brought to the notice of this court in the form of an application under Article 102 of the Constitution, this court to ensure transparency and accountability of public servants, especially those who hold responsible position, may give a direction to the Respondent No. 3 to take steps for holding enquiry into the allegations as contained in the report published in the daily "Dinkal" on 27.07.2001 so-' far the allegations relate to the Respondent Nos. 1 and 1A and thereafter to proceed according to law. We are inclined to issue such a direction in consideration of the fact that under Section 3 of the Anti-Corruption Act, 1957, an officer of the Bureau of Anti-Corruption is empowered to make such enquiry.

Mohammad Hossain Advocate Vs. Quamrul Islam Siddique & Ors. 10 BLT (HCD)-191

Section-3(2) read with

Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898 Sections-94 and 160

Whether the officer of the Bureau of Anti-Corruption has the power to issue the notices under Sections 94 and 160 of the Code of Criminal Procedure

Held: We hold that the officer of the Bureau Anti-Corruption has the power to] order and summon under Sections 94 and 160 Cr.P.C. for the purpose of making a preliminary enquiry— in the present cases the persons on whom the notices are served under Sections 94 and 160 should at least! have the right to know the nature of information and complaint the Bureau of Anti-Corruption has received so that he unknowingly does not make a statement to a question which might have a tendency to expose him to a criminal charge. So although an officer of the Anti Corruption Department is armed with power of a police officer under different section of the Criminal Procedure Code the person against whom an enquiry is being made ought to have protection provided by the Criminals procedure Code. Having found that the Bureau of Anti-Corruption has the power to issue orders and summons under Sections 94 and 160 CrPC.

Giasuddin Al-Mamun & Ors. Vs. Bangladesh & Ors. 9 BLT (HCD)-47

Section-3(3)

Under Sub-Section (3) of Section 3 of the Act, officers of the Bureau are authorized to exercise any of the powers of an officers-in-charge of the police station in the area in which he is for the time being enquiring into and investigation in the offence specified in the schedule and that while exercising the said powers he would be deemed to be an officer in-charge of a police station discharging the function of such an officer within the limit of his station.

Abu Siddique & Ors. Vs. Govt. of Bangladesh & Ors. 10 BLT (AD)-156

Section-4(2) and 5(1)

Whether the provisions of the Act are applicable only to 'Public Servant' and that have no application to an 'Ordinary Citizen'

Held: We are of the view that provision of the Act can be resorted to for enquire into or for investigation of pecuniary resources or property of any person whether he is a Public Servant or any other else or in other words, an ordinary citizen as the petitioner is claiming him.

A.K.M. Muhituddin Vs. The State 7 BLT (HCD)-148
