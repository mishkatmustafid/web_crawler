On the question of mens rea every case should be decided on the basis of particular facts involved in each case. Ordinarily, it be sufficient for the prosecution to prove that an incriminating fire-arm or ammunition was in possession of the accused and if such possession is established y the prosecution the Court will presume existence of mens rea in the mind of the subject to the right of the accused to this presumption and proved that he not possess the incriminating fire-arm ammunition consciously, knowingly or ally-  in  the  present  case, the t was holding. 3006 inch calibre rifle and same cartridges under the incriminating  cartridges under a valid licence and all these were kept at Sena Bhaban where he was residing. Thereafter on 24.12.90 the incriminating 11 pieces of cartridges found at Sena Bhaban when the Inventory Committee as making inventory of the articles left there. The appellant asserted that these 11 pieces of incriminating cartridges had been left at Sena Bhaban by the person sorted out the cartridges on account of inadvertence or having filed to identify them and that these had been left there until 24.12.90 without his knowledge, consent and intention - the assertion made by the appellant to be reasonable in all probability these cartridges were left behind at Sena Bhaban from 26.11.87 till 24.12.90.without his knowledge, consent and intention.
