Bengal Jail Code


Chapter XIV of Volume 1 (Part-1) Section-603

The jail authority must record that a convict has an inalienable right to avail all possible facilities for filing an appeal which is enshrined in chapter XIV of the Jail Code, which request should be promptly attended to so that his appeal can be filed in accordance with law. These less-privileged prisoners should be afforded all co-operations and facilities, which are legally available to them.

Ayar @ Ayaruddin & Ors Vs. The State 12 BLT (HCD) 434.

Section-5

Charge under Section 19(a) of the Arms Act- the accused petitioner is aged 14 years and as such the trial of the petitioner must be held by the juvenile Court and not by any other court-trial held by the Special Tribunal is without jurisdiction and as such the order of conviction and sentence passed by the said against the accused petitioner is liable be set aside.

Shamim Vs. The State 7 BLT (HCD) -305

Section-6 (1)

The trial of the minor accused appellant, together with adult accused is hit by want of jurisdiction and as such the trial was vitiated- conviction cannot be sustained.
