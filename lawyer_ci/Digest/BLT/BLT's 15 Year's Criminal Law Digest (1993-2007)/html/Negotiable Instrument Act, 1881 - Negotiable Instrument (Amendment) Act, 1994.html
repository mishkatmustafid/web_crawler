Negotiable Instrument Act, 1881

 

Section-138 and Penal Code, 1860 Section-406

Whether the case is filed under the provision of Negotiable Instrument Act, 1881 the court below is not competent to take cognizance under the provision of general criminal Law.

Negotiable Instrument Act, 1881 we  do not find any barring clause or outstare clause or non-obstante clause in the said Act and where there is no non-obstante clause the jurisdiction of the court, constituted under the Code of Criminal Procedure cannot be taken away or barred

Md. Moniruzzaman  Vs. ANM Deydar-e-Alam & Ors. 10 BLT (HCD)-415

Section - 138

The term "refer to drawer"- Criminal Liability

The term "refer to drawer" is a  courteous way. adopted by the bank to show it's inability to honour the cheqve for want of funds. It is also found under the scheme of the Act the offer punishable under section 138 would complete only upon the failure by drawer to pay within fortnight receipt of notice from the payee dishonour of the cheque. When fails to make such payment within period specified in clause(C) proviso the offence is complete.

Section-138

It appears that the accused petitioners are being Managing Director and Directors of a private company, named 'Salex Apparel Limited' issued 2 cheques of Taka 6,50,000/- in favour of the complainant opposite party No.2 and on presentation, those cheques in the bank for encashment, were bounced and then, after due compliance with the formalities, which are required under the law, the complainant opposite party No.2 filed the petition of complaint under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instrument Act against the accused petitioners and thereupon, the learned Magistrate took cognizance under Section 138 of the Act and therefore, we are of the view that the proceeding of the instant case does not suffer from any legal infirmity, rather it is well founded in law.

Negotiable Instrument (Amendment) Act, 1994

 

Section-138

If the cheque is presented to the bank for encashment more than once within a period of 6 months from the date on which it is drawn or within the period of its validity, whichever is earlier the notice as per clause (b) of the proviso to section 138 of the Negotiable Instrument Act demanding the amount of the cheque will require to be issued within 15 days of the receipt of the information by the payee or the holder in due course of the cheque from the bank regarding the last bouncing of the cheque.

Md. Anisuzzaman Vs. The State & Ors. 12 BLT (HCD) 22

Sections- 138 and 141 Read with The Penal Code, 1860 (XLV of 1860) Section- 420

We have carefully considered the provisions of section 141 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, which puts a bar on taking cognizance of an offence punishable under section 138 thereof except upon a complaint in writing made by the payee or, as the case may be, the holder in due course of the cheque. It is clear that an offence under section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act is for dishonour of a cheque simpliciter for insufficiency of fund, etc., whereas an offence under section 420 of the Penal Code for cheating is a distinct offence. The rule of law about the peremptory application of the special law in place of the general law for trial of an offence hardly applies when the offences are distinct under the two laws.

Md. Nurul Islam Vs. The State 5 BLT (HCD)-208.
