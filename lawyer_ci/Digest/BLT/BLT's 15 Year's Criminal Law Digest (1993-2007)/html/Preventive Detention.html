Preventive Detention

Citation: 6 BLT (AD)-216, 7 BLT (HCD)-122.

Subject: Preventive Detention

Delivery Date: 1970-01-01

 

 

Prejudicial Act

Whether the ground that on 10.12.90 the defence who was the President of the country in an interview with the BBC correspondent stated that he would start election campaign by holding a meeting of his party on 16.12.90 or on a near about date and he had nothing to repent or explain is valid.

The detenu in the present case committed no crime or illegality in telling the BBC that he would start the election campaign for his party soon and that he had no regrets or excuses- the ground was not pertinent and legally available to the authority for making an order of Preventive Detention against the detenu. There being thus no valid ground.

Mostafizur Rahman Vs. Ministry of Home Affairs 6 BLT (AD)-216

 

Preventive detention

Grounds- Vague and indefinite in nature

Grounds of detention are that there are various allegations against the detenu as to his criminal activities. It has been alleged that the detenu is a notorious terrorist and he is a criminal bent of mind. It has further been alleged in the ground of detention that he is an active member of gang of criminals within the area of Ranigaon Union Parishad under Chunarughat Police Station, and the detenu along with his group members has been committed train dacoity, high way robbery, the offence of killing, lifting of cows and stealing of trees of Forest Department within the area of B. baria, Chunarughat, Hobiganj Bahubal and Sree Mongal and by committing various crimes he has created a reign of terror in the area -Held: It appears to us that the grounds are to be vague and indefinite in nature as they back in material particulars, and therefore for the vagueness of the grounds the detenu has been deprived of making any effective representation to the detaining authority as completed under provision of law. In that view of the matter the order of detention is bad in law and as such the same should be declared being without any unlawful authority.

Md. Fatik Mia Vs. The State 7 BLT (HCD)-122.
