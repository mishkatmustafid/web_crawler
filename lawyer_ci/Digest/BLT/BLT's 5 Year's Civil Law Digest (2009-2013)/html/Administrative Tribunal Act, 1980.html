Administrative Tribunal Act, 1980

 

Section -4 read with Janata Bank (Employees) Service Rules, 1981 Regulation-29(l)(b) Second Show cause Notice

 

The  petitioner before the   Administrative Tribunal, he preferred departmental appeal against the order of dismissal and that as the appellate authority delayed disposal of the appeal he filed the case before the Administrative Tribunal in the light of the amended provision of section 4 of the Administrative Tribunal Act, 1980. -Held; we are of the view that the Administrative Appellate Tribunal (A.A.T.) and the Administrative Tribunal (A.T.) were not in error in setting aside the order of dismissal or in other words declaring the order of dismissal illegal. The direction given by the Administrative Tribunal in the background of the facts and circumstances of the case appears to be quite legal since the order of dismissal has been passed illegally and consequently he was at all time in service as order of dismissal was illegal. The Administrative Tribunal was quite correct in making the direction for payment of the arrear salary and other benefits as well as pension benefits.

 

Janata Bank & Anr Vs. Sheikh Shamsur Rahman & Ors 17 BLT (AD) 58.

 

Section -4(2)

 

The question of limitation In the instant case, the respondent's on 06.02.1995 informed that he had been dismissed from service on 21.11.1994. And thereafter, he preferred Departmental Appeal and the same was admitted but ultimately the appeal was dismissed on 06.04.1996 on merit and then on 24.06.1996 he preferred Administrative Tribunal Case. Administrative Tribunal disallowed the case not on merit but on the ground that the Departmental Appeal was barred by limitation. On the other hand the Administrative Appellate Tribunal held that the departmental appeal of the respondent herein was filed on 14.9.1995 and it was dismissed on 6.4.1996 on merit and not on the ground of limitation and thereafter, he filed Administrative Tribunal Case on 24.6.1996 and thus the case before the Administrative Tribunal was not barred by limitation. Held- it appears that a brilliant teacher was victimized by the Head Master of the School in connivance with Thana Education Officer, Hobigonj. The departmental appeal as well as the Administrative Tribunal case and also the appeal before Administrative Appellate Tribunal are not barred by limitation. The fraud caused by the Head Master in connivance with the Thana Education Officer vitiates everything. - we find that the Administrative Appellate Tribunal has considered each and every material and rightly found that miscarriage of justice has been done on the respondent herein.

 

Govt: of Bangladesh & Ors Vs. Himangshu Ranjan Pal 20 BLT (AD) 28.

 

Section - 4(2) read with Limitation Act, 1908(ActNo.IX of 1908) Section -14 read with Section - 29(2)Whether the law laid down on the construction of section 14 read with section 29(2) of the Limitation Act in the case of Abul Basher -vs- Investment Corporation of Bangladesh and another reported in 52 DLR (AD) 178 is the correct statement of law.

 

The law laid down on the construction of Section 14 read with Section 29(2) of the Limitation Act in the case of Abul Basher Vs. Investment Corporation of Bangladesh and another reported in 52 DLR (AD) 178 is not correct.

 

Abdul Yrahab Sheikh Vs. Md. Kamal Hossain & Ors 20 BLT (AD) 282.

 

Section -4 read with Section-6

 

Held; The Administrative Tribunal is not a Court proper although it has all the trappings of the Court and as such cannot exercise all the judicial powers of the Court provided under the Code of Civil Procedure. In the case of Kamrul Hasan Vs. Bangladesh and others reported in 49 DLR (AD) (1997)44 this Division held that sections 4 and 6 of the Administrative Tribunals Act do not provide jurisdiction to the Administrative Tribunal or the Appellate Tribunal to grant any interim relief in respect of a lis pending before it for final adjudication.

 

Project Director & Anr Vs. Ratan Kumar Das & others 17 BLT (AD) 338.
