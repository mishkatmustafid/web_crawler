The legislative intent of rule 83 is to facilitate, and not to obstruct, the satisfaction of the decree through private initiative of the judgment debtor instead of the court process. Order-26 Rule-1

 

Extraordinary power

 

The power of the Court to issue a commission to examine a witness, is an exceptional one and should be used carefully and only after being fully satisfied that there is no other way to examine the said witness whose evidence is necessary for proper disposal of the suit. An occasional illness or temporary infirmity is not enough to allow a commission in exercise of such an extraordinary power.

 

Sonali Bank Ltd. Ors Vs. M/s. Fair Chemicals Pvt. & Ors 19 BLT (AD) 52.

 

Order-26 Rule-9

 

In title execution case

 

It appears from the record that the suit land was specifically mentioned in the plaintiff of the suit and there was no uncertainty or vagueness in the land which has been decreed by the Court of Appeal below and maintained upto this Division. It further appears that the subject matter of the suit including the description of the property, as described in the schedule to the plaint, is sufficient to identify the suit land and the executing Court cannot go beyond the decree by allowing a local investigation to ascertain the suit land. During trial of the suit the contesting defendants could have raised the plea of identity of the suit land and could have made a prayer for local investigation but such prayer cannot be entertainable by the executing Court, as the executing Court cannot go behind the decree.

 

Md. Nurul Alain Miah & Ors Vs. Md. Idris Ali 17 BLT (AD) 296.

 

Order -26 Rule -9 Suit for Permanent injunction

 

The instant suit was for a decree of permanent injunction. In such suit the suit land must be specific and well identified as provided in Order VII Rule 3 of the Code. From the schedule appended to the plaint it appears the suit land measuring 11.50 decimals has been based on C.S. and S.A. Plot covering 6.46 acres of land situated within Dhaka City which has been devided into several plots and recorded in the Dhaka City survey khatians. Since the suit land covered by C.S. and S.A. Plots have not been identified by the R.S. and Dhaka City Survey khatians in the schedule of the plaint it is hardly possible to determine and ascertain the identity of the suit plot on the basis of oral and documentary evidence without holding local investigation by a survey knowing Advocate Commissioner. -It appears from the schedule of the plaint that the suit land has not been properly described and depicted and the same is not identifiable in the locality. Hence local investigation was essentially required for ascertaining the identity and location of the suit land by a survey knowing advocate Commissioner, otherwise the decree passed by the trial court would be infractous.

 

Order -26 Rule -9

 

Appointment of Advocate Commissioner -Available in appeal

 

A legal right for appointment of Advocate Commissioner for holding local investigation under Order XXVI Rule 9 of the Code available in a suit is also available in an appeal in appropriate case since the appeal is the continuation of the original suit. The appellate court having all the powers and trappings of the original court has discretion either to allow or disallow appointment of Advocate Commissioner for holding local investigation but the discretion should be exercised judiciously.

 

Md. Babul Islam Vs Md. Nasiruddin Sarder 17 BLT (HCD) 510.

Order XXVI Rule 10(2)

 

The report of the Advocate Commissioner shall go into evidence even if the Commissioner is not examined in Court and that the evidentiary value of the report of the Advocate Commissioner may vary depending upon the facts and circumstances of a particular case. Order -32 Rule- 6 &7 read with Constitution of Bangladesh, 1972 Article - 7, 8 and Article-10

 

Concept of Social Justice Held; On a close scrutiny of the mandate laid down in our constitution we find that the great people of this country through its constitution intended to lead our society to a welfare state and the concept of social justice would remain in oblivion unless social justice is dispensed. Dispensation of social justice and achieving the goals set forth in the Constitution are not possible without the active concerted and dynamic efforts made by the persons concerned with the justice dispensation system. Therefore, we are also of the considered opinion that without being confined to the four corners of the Code of civil Procedure and other laws relevant for the purpose, we must promote the concept of social justice so that life of the people living in a sovereign Nation become meaningful he or she be able to live with human dignity. We are also of the view that the concept of social justice is not fettered by any chain or shackles in order to do justice to the downtrodden in the society. Under the said concept the court must award appropriate relief to rehabilitate the person/person s in distress in proper social spectrum.

 

Tarik Hossain & Ors Vs. Dulari Begum & Ors 20 BLT (HCD) 521

By section 4(b) of the Ordinance, sub-section (2) and (3) of section 115 was omitted. Thereby the rivisional jurisdiction of the District Judge was withdrawn on and from 5.9.1983 -From section 4(b) of the Ordinance, 1983 it appears that sub-section (2) and (3) of section 115 of the Code was repealed /omitted. Section 6 of the said Ordinance provides that the pending proceedings shall be continued and disposed of as if the Ordinance, 1983 had not come into force, besides saving clause provided in section 6 of the Ordinance, 1983, section 6 of the General Clauses Act, 1879 also protects the aforementioned civil revision in its continuation and disposal by the Additional District Judge under the repealed section 115(2) of the Code as if the same was not repealed.
