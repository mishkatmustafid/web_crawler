Section 9 read with Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990 Section 6 read with Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003; Section 20: Whether the Civil Court has jurisdiction to entertain a suit challenging the judgment and decree passed by the Artha Rin Adalat

 

The instant suit was filed by the opposite party No.l as plaintiff, prayed for two fold declaration first is that the exparte decree obtained in Title Suit No.54 of 1998 is not binding upon him and a declaration that the registered Power of Attorney and Memo of Deposit of Title Deeds were forged, fabricated and false ones. - Held; Majority view Mr. Sheikh Abdul Awal, J. delivering the majority judgment and Mr. Nozrul Islam Chowdhury, J. concurring with him.

 

Per Mr. Sheikh Abdul Awal, J : Except the provisions of Artha Rin Adalat Ain, no question shall be raised before any court or authority about any pending proceeding in the Artha Rin Adalat, or its order, judgment or decree, and if any relief is claimed or prayer before any Court or authority ignoring the provisions of this Act, no Court or authority shall accept any such prayer, the judgment or decree of the Artha Rin Adalat is final and the same cannot be questioned in any other Court under any separate proceeding. - Therefore, I am led to hold that the suit filed by the plaintiff-opposite party No.l challenging the judgment and decree passed by the Artha Rin Adalat and some mortgaged deeds as produced/ used in the suit before the Artha Rin Adalat was impliedly barred by specific provisions of both old and new Artha Rin Adalat Ain. So, the suit is not maintainable.

 

Per Mr. Nozrul Islam Chowdhury, J : Section 6 of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990 or for that matter Section 20 of the Ain, 2003 create a bar in proceeding with a subsequent suit making a prayer for setting aside an ex-parte decree even on the ground of fraud or. even with a prayer for another declaration for the Power of Attorney and Memorandum of Deposit of Title Documents as forged, fabricated and false rather the only remedy available for the opposite party No.l was to go for an application under Order IX Rule 13 of the Code of Civil Procedure or an appeal under Section 7 or Section 41 of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990 and 2003 respectively, therefore, I find that the submissions made by the learned Advocate for the petitioner has got substance, as such accepted. Minority view.

 

Per Mr. M. Moazzam Husain, J: In the instant suit, in particular, no less an issue than one involving allegation of making the plaintiff a third party mortgagor by fraud and forgery has to be tried which, if proved, would render the decree against him a nullity and no decree in the eye of law. The issues cannot be decided except on evidence taken in a full fledged trial before a competent civil court. Since law does not intend any person to suffer for no fault of himself the opposite party No.l cannot be made to suffer the stringent conditions and procedure of special statute, even if available, for no fault of him and his property be taken away as is sought to be done by a decree of the Adalat. It follows, therefore, that the suit filed by the opposite party No.l is fairly maintainable.

 

Arab Bangladesh Bank Ltd. Vs Md. Salauddin & Ors 19 BLT (HCD) 244.

Section-9 read with Religious Endowment Act, 1863 (As Amendment Act XXI of 1925) Section-14 and 15: Whether an interested person or a worshipper can maintain a suit for protecting the deity's property

 

Section 14 lays down an important provision under which any person interested in the religious establishment may institute suit against the trustees for any misfeasance and empowers the Court to direct specific performance of act or remove the trustee. The nature of the interest entitling a person to institute such suit is described in section 15. It need not be a pecuniary or immediate interest or such an interest as would entitle the person receiving it to take part in the management. Any person having a right of attendance or partaking in the benefit of any distribution of along shall be a person interested within the meaning of the section. Under section 18 such suits could be instituted only after obtaining leave from the court. Section 18 further provides that if the Court is satisfied that the suit has been for the benefit of the trust and no party to the suit was to blame, it may direct the whole or any portion of the costs to be paid. Section 14 creates- a special jurisdiction in civil courts for the puipose of deciding matters relating to republic religious trusts and to that extent the jurisdiction is exclusive.

 

Paresh Chandra Biswas Vs. Sree Sree Shmashan Kalimata Idol & Ors 19 BLT (HCD) 474.

Section-11: Whether the suit was barred by Principle of resjudicata in view of the exparte decree in previous suit.

 

The suit was filed seeking declaration that the ex-parte decree dated 28.11.1981 obtained in Title Suit No.860 of 1981 was void, collusive and fraudulent and that the plaintiff of the said suit i.e. defendant in the instant suit (Title Suit No.22 of 1987) had no right, title and interest in the land of the said suit i.e. Title Suit No.860 of 1981 -the defendant in the previous suit had no knowledge about the ex-parte decree and that appellants came to know about the ex-parte decree in 1983 and thereupon filed Miscellaneous Case No.26 of 1983 but the same was dismissed on 28.3.1984. Thereafter the appellants filed the Title Suit No.22 of 1987 —The other ground on which leave was obtained was that the High Court Division was in error in holding that the Title Suit No.22 of 1987 was barred on the ground of principle of res-judicata in view of the exparte decree in the Title Suit No.860 of 1981 appears to be not well conceived one in that so long the ex-parte decree is not set aside the same being remaining the legal one the subsequent suit between the same parties, in respect of the matter which was the subject matter of the previous suit, may not be the position of the parties in the later suit similar to the earlier suit relating to the subject matter of the previous suit and as to which a competent Court has passed the decree, the later suit is certainly barred by the principle of res­judicata.

 

Govt: of Bangladesh & Ors Vs. Md. Osimuddin 19 BLT (AD) 119.

Section-20 C.P.C. provides the Provisions for institution of the suit, it clearly specified that where a person has a permanent dwelling at one place and also a temporary residence at another place, he shall be deemed to reside at both places in respect of any cause of action arising at the place where he has such temporary residence and a Corporation shall be deemed to carry on business at its sole or principal office in Bangladesh or, in respect of any cause of action arising at any place where it has also a subordinate office, at such place. Section-24 The ground for transfer: To sum up the grounds for transfer are:

 

(i)                 Reasonable apprehension in the mind of the litigant that he may not get justice in the Court where the suit is pending;

 

(ii)               Where the judge is interested to one party;

 

(iii)              Where the judge is prejudiced against the party;

 

(iv)             To avoid multiplicity of proceedings or conflicting decisions;

 

(v)               Where two persons have filed suits against each other on the same cause of action in different courts;

 

(vi)             Where common questions of law and fact arise in two suits;

 

(vii)           Where considerable section of public is interested in the litigation;

 

(viii)         Where     balance     of     convenience requires     considering     where     the witnesses   resides,   or   the   account books are kept.

 

 

Md. Enamul Hague Vs. Md. Ekramul Hague & Ors 19 BLT (HCD) 383

Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (As Amended) Section -44A District Court and District Judge -execution of a foreign decree.

 

There is a difference between District Court and District Judge. District Court means the principal civil court of a district and District Judge means the Judge of a principal civil court, not to any one inferior. When execution of a foreign decree is required, it must file before the District Judge, not to any other Judge. Here, we may refer to the decision of Sonali Bank-Vs- Abidur Rahman wherein it is held as 'The District Court' mentioned in Section 44A(I) of the Code means only the court of District Judge. In other words, the certified copy of decree under Section 44A(I) of the Code must be filed in the court of District Judge."

 

Nirman International Ltd. & Anr Vs. Janata Bank 20 BLT (HCD) 449.

years, he is entitled to get title even on the basis of invalid title thought the defendant No.l did not specifically claim title by adverse possession vide the decision reported in 7 DLR 94.-There is no illegality or infirmity in the above decision so as to call for any interference.

After closer of the plaintiff witness the defendant No.l did not examine any witness nor adduce any documentary evidence. Yet he participated in the hearing of the argument of the suit. The defendant No. 1 did not avail the opportunity to adduce evidence in support of his case and now after delivery of the judgment and decree if the suit is sent back on remand to the trial Court for fresh trial giving chance to the defendant No.l to adduce evidence it will give him an indulgence to fill up the lacuna. As the defendant No.l was negligent in bringing his evidence, I am of the view that the impugned judgment and decree passed by the learned judge of the appellate Court is not well founded both in law as well as in the facts and circumstances of the case.

Section 115

 

The opposite party No.l as plaintiff instituted Title Suit No.31 of 2002 before the Court of Joint District Judge, seeking a decree for two declarations one is that the judgment and decree in Title Suit No.54 of 1998 passed by the Artha Rin Adalat are illegal, collusive, fraudulent and not binding upon the plaintiff. In the said suit the defendant No.l entered appearance upon filing an application under Order 7 Rule 11 of Code Civil Procedure praying for rejection of the plaint on the ground that the proceedings in Title Suit No.54 of 1998 is governed by Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990 and Section 6 of the said Ain is a legal bar in proceeding with Title Suit No.31 of 2002. -After hearing the parties the learned Court of Joint District Judge rejected the application for rejection of the plaint holding that the contents of the plaint are related with some disputed deeds and judgment and decree which can be decided only at the trial on taking evidence. - Held;

 

Per Mr. Sheikh Abdul Awal, J : If any party aggrieved by judgment and decree of the Artha Rin Adalat he may prefer appeal against the same as per specific provisions of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain and in case of ex-parte decree, the judgment debtor may prefer an application under section 19 of the Artha Rin Ain, 2003 for setting-aside the ex-parte decree upon deposit of certain amount as per the said provisions of law. - I am inclined  to  hold   that   the   learned  Joint District Judge seriously erred in view in passing    the    impugned    order    without j properly applying his judicial mind into the I facts and circumstances of the case and law f bearing on the subject and the same has I resulted in an error in the impugned decision occasioning failure of justice. In any event I this is not a case in which the Court of Joint District Judge or the Court of District Judge established under the Civil Court Acts has jurisdiction to grant the relief asked for by the plaintiff.

 

Arab Bangladesh Bank Ltd. Vs Md. Salauddin & Ors 19 BLT (HCD) 244.

 

Per Mr. M. Moazzam Husain, J : I am of the opinion that the opposite party No.l, before  being  proved   as   such,   is   not  a 'judgment debtor' as contemplated under the 1 Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003: By the same token, the suit filed by him questioning the applicability of the same cannot be said to be barred by the ouster clause of the Ain for the precise reason that on the event of his success in his suit the decree passed against him will turn into a nullity and no 'decree' passed under the Act.
