Public Debuttor

 

A religious endowment which is known as Debuttor and which arise on dedication or gift of property to an idol , a Debuttor is managed and administered it is in an ideal sense that the dedicated property vests in an idol and in the nature of thinks the possession and management of it is entrusted to some person as shebait. He is the person entitled to speak on behalf of the deity on earth and is endowed with authority to deal with all its temporal affairs. In a Hindu religious endowment, the entire ownership of the dedicated property vests in the trustee who holds it for the benefit of cestui que trust. The entire ownership of the dedicated property is transferred to the deity. It is an admitted fact that the plaintiff is the shebait of the deity. Now the question is what is the status of the Shebait of a public debuttor property. It has been observed by the judicial committee in Prosonna Kumari Debya V. Golap Chand Baboo, (1875) L.R.2 I.A., 145 "that the person so entrusted must, of necessity, be empowered to do what ever may be required for the service of the idol and for the benefit and preservation of each property at least as great as the manager of an infant heir. If these were not so, the estate of the idol might be destroyed or wasted, and its worshi discontinued for want of necessary funds to preserve and maintain them".

 

Paresh Chandra Biswas Vs. Sree Sree Shmashan KaUmata Idol & Ors 19 BLT (HCD) 474.

 

Removal or a bhebait

 

Shebait is acting against the interest of the deity's property

 

There are unanimous views regarding the removal of a shebait by any interested person either a worshipper or a member of donor's family if the shebait is.misconducted himself in the management of the debuttor property or if he has misappropriated the property to his personal use and the mischief might be aggravated less likely to be rectified. The deity has also a right of its own to have a suit instituted by a next friend and anybody can act as next friend and if mischief is done by the shebait, such mischief would be aggravated unless the shebait is removed. The unscrupulous shebait would be encouraged to convert the entire Debuttor property to his personal use. Now the question is whether there is sufficient evidence in support of the plaintiff claim of mismanagement and/or misappropriation of the deity's property by the defendant -appellant. The deed of endowment clearly indicated that before the execution of the deed on Is1 September, 1947, the Hindu community had been performing shashan kalipuja in the stilt lands and the deed was executed with a view to remove future complications. This shows that there was image of the deity in the suit property from before 1947. - The learned Additional District Judge on a proper appreciation of the evidence on record rightly observed that there was no existence of the Mondir, that the defendant did not maintain any account of the debutter property; that he did not make any attempt for construction of the Mondir on the suit lands and that there is no proper committee for the management of the debuttor property. We find the learned Additional District Judge is perfectly justified in coming to such conclusions which are based on proper appreciation of the evidence on record.

 

Paresh  Chandra  Biswas  Vs.  Sree   Sree Shmashan Katimata Idol & Ors 19 BLT (HCD) 474

Hindu Personal Law Section -488 and 489

Requirements to the validity of an adoption

 

Mere expression of consent or the execution of a deed of adoption but not accompanied by an actual delivery of the boy does not operate as a valid adoption. To constitute giving and taking in adoption all that is necessary is that there should be some overt act to signify the delivery of the boy from one family to another.

 

Sree Bemol Chandra Mohanto Vs. Sreemoti Neoti Bala Dashsha & Ors. 17 BLT (HCD) 254.

Whether a believer of 'vishno' could perform 'Shashan Kalipuja'

 

Held; The High Court Division took note of the contention of defendant No.l that P.W.I admitted that he was a believer of 'Vishno' and therefore he could not perform Shashan Kalipuja. In order to resolve the contention, the High Court Division considered the evidence of P.W.I, who stated in cross-examination that his mother was cremated in the suit cremation ground. The High Court Division also repelled the contention that mere cremation of P.W.l's mother in the suit cremation ground would not confer him any

right to perform 'Shashan Kalipuja' since he was not a believer of the said rites as under: "The "Nitisare" tells, us "neither through colour nor through ancestor can the spirit worthy of Brahman be generated," Thus according to it cast division should not be by birth but by capacity." The High Court Division observed that a man could rise by capacity and merit; therefore, the High Court division found that the above contention could not hold good. -The findings arrived at and the decision made by the High Court Division having been made on proper appreciation of law and fact do not call for interference.

 

Paresh Chandra Biswas Vs. Shree Shree Shashan Kalimata Idol & Ors 21 BLT (AD) 339.

'Widow's estate' -right of enjoyment

 

A Hindu Widow takes only a limited estate in the property of her deceased husband, inherited by her. The estate taken by her in such property is called "widow's estate" or "woman's estate" —a widow shall have a right of enjoyment of her husband's property, during her life time but shall have to right to transfer or encumber the property in any way, and after her death, the property shall go to the heirs of her husband, and not to her own heirs, The injunctions contained in other Texts require the widow to enjoy with moderation and lead a life of austerity, and in that sense, she has not even a life interest. On the other hand, she is not a tenant for life, but the properly is vested in her and she is the owner of the property, subject to certain restrictions. She has the right to alienate the property absolutely for what is known as "legal necessity', namely, for payment of husband's debts, for performance of acts which conduce to the spiritual welfare of the husband, for discharging obligations of her husband, for her own maintenance which is also an obligation of the husband and for the preservation of the estate.

 

Hazi Md. Nazim Hossain & Ors Vs Hafiz Ullah & Ors 21 BLT (HCD) 386.

 

Hindu Women's Right to Separate Residence and Maintenance Act, 1946 Section – 2

 

According to Hindu Law, a Hindu wife is inseparable from her husband and they are obliged to live together as husband and wife till last day of their life. Any deviation of their relationship and conduct due to the behavior of the husband makes the husband liable to bear the consequences to the right accrued to the wife under the marriage.

 

Gopal Chandra Vs. Mitali Rani Chandra 20 BLT (AD) 279.
