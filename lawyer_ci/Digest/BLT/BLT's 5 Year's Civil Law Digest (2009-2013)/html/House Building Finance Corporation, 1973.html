Section -8(1) read with Code of Civil Procedure, 1908; Order-1 Rule 10(2) Whether a Share holder of a Company is a necessary Party

 

A Suit under the Provisions of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003 -A company incorporated under the Companies Act is a juristic person. A share-holder is not the owner of the company or its assets. The company itself owns its properties. A share-holder is only entitled to the dividends, if declared. On winding up however, after payment of its debts, he is entitled to participate in the distribution of its assets. The liability of a share-holder, whether he is the Chairman of the Board of Directors, or a director, is only to the extent of the face value of the shares he holds, nothing more than that. But if he guarantees repayment of the loan, enjoyed by the company or mortgages his property to the creditor, to ensure repayment of the loan by the company, then on the failure of the company to make such repayment, he becomes liable, not as a share-holder but as a guarantor or mortgagor or both as the case may be.

 

Mrs. Bakul Akter Vs. Bangladesh & ors 18 BLT (AD) 282.

On close analysis of the Provisions of section 34 (2) of the Ain 2003, we find that the substituted heirs of the principal debtors enjoy the right and privilege contemplated in the aforesaid provisions. The legislature promulgated Arlho rin Adalat Ain 2003 (Act VIII of 2003) for speed recovery of debts form the defaulting borrowers. The intention of the legislature is also to protect the right of substituted heirs of the principal debtors. Although such protection was not expressly provided in respect of the substituted heirs of mortgagor or guarantor in section 34(2) of the Ain 2003, but from the combined reading of the Ain 2003 as a whole, we find that the intention of the legislature is also to protect the right of the substituted heirs of mortgagor and guarantor. Therefore, we hold that the Provisions of section 34(2) of the Ain 2003 are squarely applicable in respect of the substituted heirs of mortgagor and guarantor.

 

Md. Akramuzzaman @ Babu Vs Artha Rin Adalat & Ors 17 BLT (HCD) 496.

Section-3(15)

 

The word "District Court" as used in section 44A(1) is primarily to be interpreted with reference to the definition of the " District Court" as given in section 2(4) of the Code. On consideration of the plain meaning of "Principal" as given in the Oxford Dictionary read with section 24 of the Code; and section 3(15) of the General Clauses Act, 1897 we are of the opinion that the "District Court" referred to in section 44A(1 ) of the Code means the Court of the District Judge under section 3(1) of the Act and no other Court though under section 18 of the Act concurrent jurisdiction had been given to the District Judge and the Joint District Judge in respect of all original suits cognizable by Civil Courts.
