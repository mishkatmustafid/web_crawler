Power of Attorney Act, 1882

 

Presumption-onus of proof

 

Whether the High Court has erred in rejecting the defendants' contention that the lease and the kabala dated 16-1-56 (which, he conceded, no doubt constituted a single transaction of sale) were executed by Abu Mohammad Kasem without authority, since the power of attorney in the latter's favour could not be brought on record.

 

Held; In view of the presumption attached to the said endorsements, it was for the defendants to prove that Abu Mohammad Kasem was not Sarat Shahi's constituted attorney. Mr. Behter AH could not dispute that the defendants made no attempt to discharge this onus.

 

Md. Shahed Faruque Vs. Mst. Matiunnessa Khatun & Ors 21 BLT (AD) 144.

 

 

Power of Attorney

 

On scrutiny of the power of attorney exhibit 28 it further reveals that one Nesar Hossain executed the same in favour of (a) Faizul Haque (b) Syed Mokarram Hossain (c) Sree Sushil Kumar Roy (d) Mazed Ali (e) Syed Jamshed Hossain Chowdhury. However, from the exhibit 16 deed in question it is crystal clear that out of 5 Attornee's only Mr. Sushil Kumar executed the alledged deed dated 28.8.65, not only that on scrutiny it further divulged from the power of attorney exhibit 28 that there is no schedule in the power of attorney regarding any land as described in the schedule of the suit as well in exhibit 16. So, we find that although the Court below in its judgment did not discuss on the merit about right and title of the plaintiff in respect of schedule Kha and Ga of the Other Suit No. 8 of 2003 but from the above discussions and considering the plaintiffs title deed exhibit 16 it manifest that the plaintiff has failed to prove his title by producing successive title deed as has been claimed in the plaint which was executed by Sushil Kumar Roy is nothing but a deed without sanction of law as because in the power of attorney there is no schedule of the land as described in exhibit 16, the alleged deed of the plaintiff. So, we can safely hold that the plaintiff has failed to prove his plaint case in respect of not only Ka schedule land but also Kha and Ga schedule land by producing valid documentary evidence.

 

Syed Jamshed Hussain Choudhury Vs Govt of Bangladesh & Ors 21 BLT (HCD) 303.
