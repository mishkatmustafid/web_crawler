Small Causes Act, 1887

 

Section -25 Bonafide requirement

 

On assessment of the evidence on record, appears that PW-1, PW-2 and PW-3 all of them in their respective testimony stated that in order to develop the suit shop room amongst other rooms of the suit holding which were let out to other tenants the plaintiff obtained a plan from RAJUK. -Held Bonafide requirement of the premises in suit by the owner for his own use or for the purpose of construction of building is a matter exclusively with landlord but still then be is under legal obligation to prove that he needs the premises for his own use or for construction of building through the evidence of reliable witness. In this case I have already indicated that there are sufficient corroborative evidence both oral and documentary available on record to arrive at a finding that the plaintiff petitioner has been able to prove his bonafide requirement of the suit shop premises for purpose of multistoried building.

 

Khandaker Mahmud Hasan Vs Anjir Asma 17 BLT (HCD) 423.

 

Section 15 read with Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 Order IX Rule 13

 

Suit for ejectment of ordinary ejectable monthly tenants, was filed in 1987 and that defendants were evicted from the suit premises on 5.11.1980 and the miscellaneous case was registered on 04.9.82

 

In the instant case as I find by execution of decree defendants were thrown out of the suit premises and when the miscellaneous case was filed, tenants were out of possession that means, decree of the suit or for the purpose the suit was filed has been satisfied and jurisdiction of Small Cause Courts has been totally ousted as a tenant can not fight any legal battle if he/she is out of possession as court cannot compelled the land lord to restore possession of an unwanted tenant.

 

Mrs. Rezia Sultan & Anr Vs. Md. Haroon Malik & Anr 20 BLT (HCD) 339.

 

Section-25

 

In any view, the scope of the scrutiny under Section 25 of the Small Causes Courts Act is much wider and the revisional Court is permitted to reassess the evidence in the light of the pleadings of the parties and also may consider all other evidence on record and is not restricted in exercise of its revisional power as is limited under Section 115( 1) of the Code of Civil Procedure.

 

Md. Nasiruddin Vs. Md. Mizanur Rahman 20 BLT (AD) 222.
