The contract must have the essentials of a contract and binding at law in order to be enforceable. The learned Advocate for the pre-emptee-petitioner is unable to dislodge the findings of the Appellate Court, the last Court of facts, save and except that after purchase, the petitioner made improvements on the land by erecting shops and houses which was noticed by the High Court Division. -Held; The petitioner-pre-emptee is entitled to the development cost of Tk. 10,0007.

 

Jahangir Alain Vs. Md. Joynal Abedin & Ors 18 BLT(AD) 305

 

Section -96 read with Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 Section-148

 

The Deposite not being made within time allowed.

 

The pre-emption law provides that four months time for filling an application for pre-emption from the date of knowledge and that any opposite party in the pre-emption case is permitted to file an application for adding him/her as an co-applicant with prayer for rateable pre-emption of the land within two months and is required to deposit the amount, as may be ordered, for, within the period allowed by the Court and such provision is mandatory inasmuch as this special provision of law provides special limitation and therefore the limitation as provided in the law itself shall prevail over the normal law of limitation.

 

Md. Chan Miah Vs. Md. Dabirul Islam Khan & Ors 18 BLT (AD) 462.

 

Section 96 read with Evidence Act, 1872 Section 102

 

Onus shifter on the pre-emptees

 

On perusal of the judgment of the High Court Division, it reveals that the High Court Division has categorically adverted to the evidence to the parties and held that the lower appellate Court considered the evidence of pre-emptor No.3 Abdur Rahman and P.Ws.2 and 3 who corroborated the pre-emptor and also considered the evidence of OPW Nos.l and 2 who proved that the purchaser did not take over the possession of the case land immediate after his purchase and that the seller remained in possession of the case land and drawn conclusion holding that the lower appellate Court correctly noticed that after the pre-emptors proved that they had no knowledge of the transfer in question prior to 01.09.1994, the onus shifted on the pre-emptees to prove that the pre-emptors had earlier knowledge of the transfer in question and that the pre-emptees in the present case failed to discharge their onus. - Upon over all security of the materials and the judgment and order of the High Court Division we find that the High Court Division affirmed of the judgment of the appellate Court on giving strong cogent reasons which calls for no interference by this Division.

 

Suratunnessa & Ors Vs. Nurjahan Bibi & Ors 20 BLT (AD) 74.

But the Appellate Court below has committed an error of law resulting in an error in the decision occasioning failure of justice in shifting the onus of proof of the same upon the petitioner.
