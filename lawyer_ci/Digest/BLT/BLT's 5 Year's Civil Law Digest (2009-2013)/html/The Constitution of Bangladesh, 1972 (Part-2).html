Article – 102

 

Public Interest Litigation

 

The petitioner has espoused his personal cause as well as the cause of the coterie of Marine Engineers only. Article-102 read with Article 83

 

Under art.83 not only the levy but also the collection of tax must be sanctioned by or under the authority of an Act of Parliament. The expression 'Levy' includes creation of liability realisation of tax. Not only a tax must be validly levied, its collection must also be in accordance with the Act of Parliament. Thus, when an Act of Parliament provides that a tax shall be collected in such manner as may be prescribed by rules, the tax cannot be collected until the rules are made. This article gives protection against arbitrary collection of tax. When an assessment is made in an arbitrary fashion, there is no collection of tax in accordance with an Act of Parliament. Apart from that the power of the Parliament in enacting legislations or ratification of certain action(s) is also subject to judicial review by the apex Country. If the Parliament enacts any law(s) inconsistent or ultra vires to the provision of the Constitution, the supreme law of the land, such enactment can also be interfered by the apex Court exercising the power conferred under Article 102 of the Constitution of the Republic.

 

S. Alam Steels Ltd. & Ors Vs Govt. of Bangladesh & Ors 19 BLT (HCD) 39.
