The Transfer of Property Act, 1882

 

Section 43

The principle of feeding the grant by estoppels

 

Held; there must be some evidence in taking recourse to the provision of Section 43 of the Transfer of Property Act. It must be in the pleadings of the parties that transferor or the vendor made a fraudulent representation of erroneous act by which the transferee or the vendee believed him and entered into the transaction. It might be gathered from the facts of the case but unless there is specific pleadings by either of the parties or there being no such intention disclosed in any of the document or any evidence, the court can not of its own, applied this provision in any case.

 

Eunus Mallik & Ors Vs. Khaleda Begum & others 20 BLT (HCD) 75

 

Section – 52

 

Title less person - Us pendensy In the instant case predecessor of the plaintiffs purchased land from Sree Sarnalata Dey when Title Suit No. 141 of 1945 was pending and this purchase is hit by lis pendensy. - title less person can not confer title but can give birth to litigation as possession of the plaintiff as claimed is totally unauthorized and illegal.

 

Md.   Faizur  Rahman   &   Anr   Vs.  Halima Khatun & Ors 21 BLT (HCD) 76.

 

Section 53A

The plaintiffs entered into bainapatra to purchase the suit land and got delivery of possession of the suit land immediately after execution of the bainapatra in their favour.

 

The provisions of Section 53A of the Transfer of Property Act have created a bar against enforcement of any right of the transferor in respect of the property in question which he would have apart from the agreement. In view of the provisions of Section 53A of the Act the vendor or his successor's in-interest are prevented from dealing with the property provided the vendee has performed his part of the contract and in pursuance thereof has taken possession of some immovable property.

 

Ayesha Khatun Vs. Nasiruddin Ahmed & Ors 21 BLT (HCD) 94.

 

Section-53A

 

In a suit for specific performance of contract court on applying judicial discretion can refuse specific performance of a contract even the agreement is found to be legal, lawful and genuine but under Section 53A of Transfer Property Act Court can not apply such discretion.

 

Sultan Miah Vs Mst. Roksana Begum & Ors 19 BLT (HCD) 37.

 

Section - 54

Biana or biana patra is agreement between two or more person and when it relating to transferring land, it is-a biana or biana patra.

 

As per section 54 of Transfer of Property Act sale is a transfer of ownership i.e. actual sale and actual sale means transfer by registered document and unless agreement bianama converted to a registered sale deed it merely an agreement binding the parties to the agreement, not a transfer of title to be used both as a weapon of offence or a shield of defense to all.

 

Most. Rahima Khatun & Anr Vs. Most. Sufia Begum & Ors 21 BLT (HCD) 39.

 

Section-53 A read with Specific Relief Act, 1877 Section-31 and 32

 

The concept of bona fide purchasers for value without notice is applicable only in respect of transfer of immoveable property and specific performance of contract for transfer of immoveable property and not in respect of use of immoveable property and it is an equitable principle which cannot override the bar placed by any statutory provision.

 

Metro Makers & Developers Ltd Vs. BELA 21 BLT (AD) 240

 

Section -106

Due service of Notice

 

In the present case it appears from the materials on record (Exhibits 4, 5, and 5-ka) that on several occasions the notice under section 106 of the Transfer of Property Act was posted in the address of the suit-shop but all the times the same was returned as undelivered. -Held in this case there was due service of notice under section 106 of the Transfer of Property Act inasmuch as I have already noticed that in the present case it appears from the materials on record on several occasions the notice under section 106 of the Transfer of Property Act was posted in the address of the suit-shop to the defendant-opposite party but all the times the same was returned as undelivered.

 

Khandaker Mahmud Hasan Vs Anjir Asma 77 BLT (HCD) 423.

 

Section-107

 

Admittedly the suit property was Non-Agricultural Property from before 1947 and the same is situated within Khulna Municipal Corporation. The defendants once claimed that they were monthly tenants under Srish Bhattacharjee and thereafter under his sons, Dhirendra and Birendra. They again claimed that they took settlement of the suit property by unregistered Amaldari from Dhirendra and Birendra on 17.05.1950. It is the settled principle of law that settlement of Non-Agricultural land within Municipality cannot be affected by unregistered document. The same must be affected by bilateral registered document executed by both the lessor and the lessee. Non-Agricultural Tenancy Act, 1949 came into force on 28th October, 1949 and according to defendants case they took settlement by unregistered amaldari on 17.05.1950 and as such the same is hit by section 107 of the Transfer of Property Act.

 

Khandoker Ansar Ahmed & Ors Vs. A.T.M. Monsur A U Mallik & Ors 19 BLT (AD) 129

 

Section-109

 

If the lessor transfers the property leased, the transferee acquires the right of the lessor and if the lessor so elects, the transferee shall be subject to all the liabilities of the lessor as to the property transferred. Since the plaintiffs acquired right, title and interest in the suit property through Bidyanath whose title was confirmed by sole decree passed in Title Suit No. 102 of 1958, it cannot be said that the defendants are not tenants under the plaintiffs in the face of Ext.7, 8(a), 9 & I2(b).

 

Khandoker Ansar Ahmed & Ors Vs. A.T.M. Monsur All Mallik & Ors 19 BLT (AD) 129

Since a resolution of no confidence against the petitioner was taken upon due compliance of law and the resolution has been approved by the competent authority, the order of removal in respect of the petitioner removing him from the office of Chairman do not suffer from any infirmity.
