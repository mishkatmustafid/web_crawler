Order-9 Rule-6( 1)(a)

The Latin expression “ex parte” meaning not defined in the Code of Civil Procedure— basic principles of adversary procedure followed in court system—exception to the principle—procedure under Order-9, Rule6(1) C.P.C.—no mandate as to passing an ex parte decree in C.P.C.

‘The Latin expression ‘ex parte’ meaning from one party or side, has not been defined in the Code of Civil Procedure. One of the basic principles of the adversary procedure followed in our court system is that as far as possible. No proceeding be conducted to the detriment of a person in his absence. One exception to this principle is that when the defendant, duly served with summons, does not avail himself of the opportunity to appear, the court may proceed in his absence under Rule-6(1)(a) of Order-IX. There being no mandate as such to make ex parte’ decree the court may order for fresh service of summons on the defendants or adjourn the matter suo motu. If the court is satisfied that the summons was duly served on the defendant may proceed to decide the matter. [Para- 11]

Order - 41 Rule - 31

The object of Rule 31 of Order 41 of the Code of Civil Procedure is to see that the appellate Court does not act mechanically in concurring with the findings of the trial Court. It requires that the appellate Court must discuss and consider the material evidence on record and come to its independent conclusions on all points at issue on the basis of the evidence. The appellate Court is also required to set aside the material findings of the trial Court with reference to the evidence on record adverting to the reasons assigned by it. In the case of affirmance general agreement of the appellate Court with the findings of the trial Court may be considered sufficient but in case of reversal the discussion of evidence must necessarily be fairly elaborate. [Para- 14]
