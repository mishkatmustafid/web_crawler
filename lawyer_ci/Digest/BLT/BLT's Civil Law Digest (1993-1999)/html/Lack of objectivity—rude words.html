Lack of objectivity—rude words

 

The contempt proceedings arose because respondent No. 2 was not allowed to join as secretary of BSC after the final disposal of his case before the Appellate Division by the appellant and thereby disobeyed the courts decree— The prayers made in the joining letter were beyond the terms of the decree obtained by the respondent—We have not been able to persuade ourselves to agree with the manner in which the matter has been dealt with, particularly the rude words used against the appellant. A high officer of the Government at the far end of his career with a long service record without any allegation of blemish prior to the present incident should have been dealt with more consideration and objectivity. An honest person would prefer dignity more than anything else. In the facts of the present case, it has appeared to us that these considerations have suffered a great deal because of the lack of objectivity. [Para-9]

S. A. M. Iqbal Vs. The State & Anr 6 BLT (AD)-198
