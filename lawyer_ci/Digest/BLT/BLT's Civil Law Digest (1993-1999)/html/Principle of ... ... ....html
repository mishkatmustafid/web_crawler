Principle of Promissory Estoppel

The project having been approved by the Investment Board earlier, the subsequent decision taken by the Government in the meeting of 1.8.94 to cancel the approval given earlier does not appear to be based on sound and proper reasonings. Principle of Natural Justice

Termination’s under Regulations (I) & (II) of the Bank Regulation cannot be termed as punishment and as such no notice need be served upon for passing the orders of termination. Termination simpliciter not being a punishment which is contemplated in Regulation 22, the question of giving notice or a pre-hearing does not arise. In such a case there is no scope for making any grievance on the ground of violation of the principle of natural justice either. [Para-7]

Principle Governing Interpretation of Instruments or Contracts

Encashment of the Bank Guarantee—in the instant case, it appears that the letter of credit in para 6 provides that the credit will be operative only on receipt of an agreement of reimbursement from Saudi Fund for Development, Riyadh, by the advising Bank. The letter of credit shall not be valid until there was “irrevocable agreement to reimburse’ from the Saudi Fund for Development. The contract itself clearly shows that the letter of credit opened by the plaintiff would be subject to approval by the Saudi Fund for Development and, such an approval having not come from the Saudi Fund for Development, the letter of credit and also the contract could not be operative in consequence of which the bank guarantee, in the facts and circumstances of the case, was not encashable. [Para- 13]

Principle regarding the evidence of the witnesses of a party

Law requires that when the contending parties adduce evidence in support of their respective cases, whether oral or documentary, a duty is cast upon the Court to consider the same and thereafter to arrive at its conclusions with reference thereto but it has no right tq ignore the evidence of a party irrespective of the value that it may carry. Applying the correct principle regarding the appreciation of the evidence the Court has the right to believe or disbelieve a witness or a number of witnesses but it has no right to ignore the evidence of a party in coming to his decision. Non-consideration of a material witness or a material piece of evidence of the witnesses of a party or any important document is a serious legal infirmity which renders the impugned decision unsustainable in law. Principle—Power of Review

The Court, the tribunal or the body performing function of judicial or quasi judicial nature is not vested with inherent or implied power of review or revision of its judgment or order. The power of review or revision can only be vested in the Court, the tribunal or the body discharging function(s) of judicial or quasi judicial nature by the statute under which the said body acts or discharges function(s) of judicial or quasi judicial kind or nature. The Court, the Tribunal and the body discharging function(s) of judicial or quasi judicial nature shall have to have sanction of law in the exercise of power of revision or review of its judgment or order. More so while the judgment or order of an appellate court, tribunal or body has been made final by the statute under which the appellate body exercises its appellate power. If the Court, the Tribunal or the body performing function(s) of judicial or quasi judicial nature exercises power of revision or review or in other words revise or review its order in the absence of being not vested with the same it acts without jurisdiction or in other words in a manner not authorised by law. [Para- 10]

Chittagong Port Authority Vs. Mst. Khadiza Khatoon & Ors. 6 BLT (HCD)-69


Principle—use the expressions of “Interest of justice” or “ends of justice”

An applicant can succeed in his case only on the merit of his own case, and not an any other extraneous considerations. When there is a specific provision of law for giving relief to an aggrieved party but the requirements of law are not met, the Court has no power to doll out gratuitous relief in the name of “interest of justice” or “ends of justice”. The Court must not use these expressions lightly and capriciously as it would tend to defeat justice but use the same accordingly to sound judicial principles for ensuring real justice. [Para-8]

Principle to the award of interest

It appears that the Managing Director of the defendant-company died on 3-5-83 and after his death the company failed to run their business and were thus unable to pay any interest, Even then the decretal amount remains fully paid in 1990. After a passage of 7 years from the satisfaction of the decree we are not inclined to impose the contractual rate of interest upon the judgment-debtor. We are of the opinion that the interest ofjustice will be sewed if the defendants are directed to pay interest at the rate of 6% on the decretal sum with yearly rests from the date of institution of the suit till the date of decree in two equal yearly installments—With regard to interest from the date of decree till realisation we find that the discretion of the Court is even wider in this area and considering the facts and circumstances of this particular case as already noticed we think that no interest should be paid on this score. [Paras-22 & 23]

Principle on the selection of Bolunong Chief

The selection of Bohmong Chief is no doubt an executive selection and the judiciary will not substitute its own selection in place of the executive selection because it is not the function of the judiciary to select a Bohmong Chief. The selection will have to be made by the executive only. The Court will only inquire whether the selection has been made following the tradition, custom and usage which govern such selection. If so made the judiciary will not intervene. If extraneous considerations have influenced the executive decision the court has certainly the power to declare the selection to have been made without lawful authority. [Para-30]

Aung Shwe Prue Chowdhury Vs Kyaw Ala Pnie Chowdhury & Ors. 6 BLT (AD)-109

 

Principle of Administration of Civil Justice

It is a cardinal principle of administration of justice that no result of any judicial proceeding should be allowed to receive judicial approval from any court of law whenever it is obtained by practicing fraud upon the court; reason being fraud demolishes the very foundation of sanctity of such judicial proceeding. It is also well established principle of law that fraud vitiates all judicial proceeding. Thus contravention of the provision of law, cannot be a valid ground for allowing an order obtaining by fraud to stand. When the trial court itself on consideration of the materials on record was satisfied that a fraud had been committed in obtaining the ex parte decree it was the duty of the trial court to set aside the ex parte decree. The failure of the trial court in the performance of its legal obligations ought not to have been maintained by the High Court Division in affirming the finding of the trial court. [Para-13]

Govt. of Bangladesh & Anr Vs. Mashtur Rahman & Ors. 6 BLT (A.D)-73

 

Principle on Facts by Oral Agreement

So far as the oral agreement is concerned it should always be very closely scrutinized and taken with a grain of salt. Although oral agreement is not barred by any law it has to be looked at with some suspicion unless proved by very reliable evidence and circumstances. In Ouseph Varghese V. Joseph Aley, (1969) 2 SCWR 347 the Supreme Court of India discouraged a decree for specific performance of a contract on the basis of an agreement supported solely by oral evidence. [Para-7]

Principle for Analogous Trial

The principle of consolidation of suits for analogous trial necessarily implies that similarity or identity of the matter in issue in different suits by the same party should be decided by the court once and for all, the object being to avoid multiplicity of litigations between the same parties involving matter in issue which are substantially or directly the same. [Para – 7]

Abdul Wahab & Ors. Vs. Md. Jahur Ali 5 BLT(HCD)-85

 

Principle of Mohammedan Law on the Point of Revocation of Gifts

It is an established Principle of Mohammedan Law that a gift is not liable to be revoked by the donor after delivery of possession, if it is made to a donee who is related to the donor within the prohibited degree and once possession is delivered nothing short of a decree of Court is sufficient for the revocation of a gift.
