The Notaries Ordinance, 1961

 

Under the Notaries Ordinance, 1961 the General Power of Attorney dated 20.2.65 on which the plaintiff bases his claim is not admissible in evidence owing to lack of a reciprocal agreement between India and Bangladesh contended by the learned petitioner’s counsel—Held In the instant case no objection was taken to the plaintiffs power of attorney dated 20.2.65 in any of the courts below. Being a mixed question of fact and law we are not inclined to examine this question at this stage. [Para – 5]

A. Bewa & Ors. Vs. Ekramul Hoque & Ors. 4 BLT (AD)-147

Section-14

In the present case the original Affidavit was filed in the court of Bangladesh and marked as exhibit without validating with local stamp though it was duly sworn before a Magistrate in India. The plaintiff having duly proved the same as an exhibit without any objection whatsoever had discharged the onus of proof and as such it would be wrong to hold that the document is not admissible in evidence. [Para-22]

Abdul Karim & Ors. Vs. Md Serajuddin Ahmed & Ors 7BLT (AD)-160.
