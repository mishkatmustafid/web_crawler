Order 1 Rule 1

The plaintiff claiming himself as the Vice Chairman of the Nalchity Girls High School and he has figured as plaintiff on being authorized by the Managing Committee —The view so taken by the High Court Division as to the matter of admission of girl students into Nalchity Girls High School as the exclusive right thereof in our view is not well founded since a particular school imparting education to the girl students of the locality cannot have the exclusive claim to have the girl students of the girls to decide the matter of admission in the institution or school wherein girls would have better education and as such the action of the High school in admitting girl students in our view has in no way invaded or threatening proprietary right or any kind of right of the School, and in that view of the matter the Nalchity Girls High School was not on correct legal footing in going to the Court for having an order restraining the High School from admitting girl students in it.

Whether the petitioners are entitled to he added as parties to safeguard their interest

Sushil Chandra Das Vs. Nironjan Paul & Ors. 6BLT (HCD)-174

Order-1 Rule-10

From the petition under Order 1 Rule 10 of the Code of Civil Procedure dated 2.5.87, marked Annexure “A” to the petition, it appears that the petitioners claimed the suit property as heirs and heiresses of the original tenants Poyer Mamud Mondal and his sister Powchhari Bibi. The case of the plaintiff opposite party is that he got the suit property from his father Poyer Mamud Mondal and fufu Powchhari Bibi by way of settlement on obtaining dakhilas in the year of 1345 B.B., which facts are stoutly denied by the present petitioners. It has been contended in the application under Order I Rule 10 of the Code of Civil Procedure that the original tenants Poyer Mamud Mondal and Powchhari Bibi never settled the suit land with the plaintiff and the plaintiffs claim of title by way of settlement is false. It has further been asserted that as co-owners of the suit property by inheritance like the plaintiff the petitions have been owning and possessing the suit property in ejmali. It was also contended in no uncertain terms that the plaintiffs’ papers in respect of the alleged settlement are totally false and fabricated. It thus appears that vital interests of the petitioners are involvement in the suit and they have definite say in the adjudication of the suit and they are interested in the result of the suit for declaration of title in the suit land. In the face of the clear and specific assertions made by the petitioners it is evident that vital interests of the petitioner are involved in the suit and as such their presence in the suit is necessary for proper adjudication of the controversies between the contending parties. The order of the learned Munsif, rejecting the petitioners’ prayer under Order 1 Rule 10 of the Code of Civil Procedure is thus found to be illegal and arbitrary occasioning failure of justice and as such is same is liable to be set aside in the interest of justice.

Whether a stranger to the contract, to be added as a proper party

Abdul Hye & Ors Vs. Khetra Mohon Bawaii 7BLT (HCD)-345

Order-1 Rule-10

It was also found that in such. a suit only consideration is whether the vendors have executed ‘Bainapatra’ or not. Md. Abdul Hye Vs. Md. Rafiqul Islam Talukder & Ors 8BLT (AD)-301.

Principle-addition of a party

Addition of Parties

In a suit for specific performance of contract the Court is called upon to determine whether the disputed contract is a lawful one and if it is legally enforceable. Court’s Power to add or strike out parties

Order 1 Rule 10 of the Code of Civil Procedure empowers the Court to add any person to a suit or to strike out the names of any person improperly impleaded therein at any stage of the proceeding if the Court considers the presence of such a party necessary or unnecessary, as the case may be, for the effective adjudication of the proceedings before it. Atar Ali & Ors Vs. Hasan Ali 13BLT(HCD)469

Order-6 Rule-17

Whether in making the prayer for partition by amendment, would change the nature and character of the suit.

(a) The principle is that the nature and character of a suit do not change so long as the fundamental character of the suit remains the same. A suit for declaration of title recovery of possession and/or for partition is essentially based on a common claim of the plaintiff which may call for aforesaid different relief or reliefs in different situations—by adding a relief for partition in a suit for declaration of title and possession there was no alteration to the foundation of the suit and as such the fundamental character of the suit remained the same, there being thus no question of changing the nature and character of the suit appeal allowed.

Whether the amendment cannot allow

Order-6 Rule-17

The provisions embodied in Order 6, Rule 17 of The Code constitute a Rule of Procedure advancing a remedy to rectify an error, Mistake, omission or default in pleadings of parties. The consideration is to minimize the litigation and to advance cause of justice instead to stifle it. Prayer for amendment is to be liberally allowed so that rights of the parties can be fairly and conclusively determined and multiplicity of suits avoided.
