Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Order-40 Rule-1

Simply because the property in suit was under the management of a receiver for a long period of time it cannot be regarded as a just and convenient ground for appointment of a receiver as Order XL Rule (1) does not authorise court to do so on such a ground.

Kamiruddin & Ors Vs Md. Mokshed Ali Biswas & Ors. 4 BLT (AD)-4.

Order-40 Rule-1

Appointment of receiver

It appears that the trial court with a view to saving the property of the suit from being sold in auction due to non-payment of tax and duties passed the order for appointment of receiver with an observation that whether the plaintiffs have acquired any right, title in the suit property will be decided in the suit itself. Upon such finding of the trial court and in the facts and circumstances of the case it cannot be said that the learned Judge of the High Court Division has committed an error of law in upholding the impugned order of the trial court.

Mohammad Mosharef Hossain & Ors. Vs. Jalal Uddin Ahmed & Ors. 7BLT (AD)-211

Order 40 Rule—1

Appointment of Receiver

In the instant case, the application made under Order XL Rule-1 simply discloses that the plaintiff is going to let out some shops in favour of some other persons and is going to realize salami from the shopkeepers. Save and except the said assertion there is no other case brought before the Court for getting an order for appointment of receiver. The order of status quo in respect of possession was passed on contest on 13.4.1999 and that after that order the plaintiff being in possession was receiving rent from the shop keepers and that he took an attempt to repair shops. The defendant-opposite parties being not in physical possession and having accepted the order of status quo in respect of possession in the suit shops were not entitled to make application for appointment of receiver long after about 7 years of the status quo order was passed. Order-40 Rules 1(2) is clear bar in allowing the prayer for appointment of receiver.

Md. Matiur Rahman Vs Dr. Fayez Uddin & Ors. 16 BLT (HCD)303

Order 40 Rule—1

The receiver should not remove any person from the possession or custody of the suit property unless a compelling circumstance is appearing for doing so.

Md. Matiur Rahman Vs Dr. Fayez Uddin & Ors. 16 BLT (HCD)303

Order 40 Rule—1

The object and purpose of appointment of receiver may generally be stated to be the preservation of the subject matter of the litigation pending judicial determination of the rights of the parties and it must be with the dominant object to prevent the ends of justice from being defeated. Before allowing an application it should be the duty of the court to see that the rights of the parties are not jeopardized and the ends of justice not defeated.

Md. Matiur Rahman Vs Dr. Fayez Uddin & Ors. 16 BLT (HCD)303

Shamsuddin Ahmed and Ors. Vs Bangladesh 1BLT (HCD)-1

Order-40 Rule-1(a)

Principles —appointment of a receiver

Suit for partition — in the instant case, the parties are in separate possession of the suit property left by their father on the basis of a registered partition amongst them since 1991. Out of six sisters and two brothers, Plaintiff No. 1 only wants appointment of a receiver. And her success in the suit mainly depends on the proof that the registered deed of partition was forged and void, and/or not acted upon. In the circumstances, introduction of a third party as a receiver of the valuable property cannot be surely said would not result in Mismanagement of or harm to the property — there should be no appointment of a receiver.

Order-41 Rule 11(1)

A close reading of Order 41 Rule 11(1) read with Rules 12 and 13 leads to the irresistible conclusion that the impugned judgment and order is not an order of dismissal of the appeal. It is an order finally disposing the appeal without admission of the appeal and without serving notice upon the respondent. Such order as has been passed under the impugned judgment and order is not an order of dismissal of appeal rather it is a final order setting aside the order of status quo passed by the trial court without giving an opportunity to the respondent to which she was entitled to. The judgment and order impugned has no legal sanction in that it is not an order within the meaning of order 41 Rule 11(1) C.P.O.

Order - 41 Rule – 31

 The object of Rule 31 of Order 41 of the Code of Civil Procedure is to see that the appellate Court does not act mechanically in concurring with the findings of the trial Court. It requires that the appellate Court must discuss and consider the material evidence on record and come to its independent conclusions on all points at issue on the basis of the evidence. The appellate Court is also required to set aside the material findings of the trial Court with reference to the evidence on record adverting to the reasons assigned by it. In the case of affirmance general agreement of the appellate Court with the findings of the trial Court may be considered sufficient but in case of reversal the discussion of evidence must necessarily be fairly elaborate.
