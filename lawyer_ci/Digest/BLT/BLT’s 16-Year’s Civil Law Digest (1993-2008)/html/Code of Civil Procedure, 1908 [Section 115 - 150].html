Section-115

Period of limitation—Section 115 C.P.C. is an empowering provision granting discretionary jurisdiction to the High Court Division to correct any error of law committed by any subordinate Court resulting in an error in the decision occasioning failure of justice. In appropriate cases High Court Division may suo-motu exercise such jurisdiction to rectify failure of justice. But as a matter of long standing practice one has to exercise vigilance and promptness in invoking the revisional jurisdiction at the earliest opportunity without causing any inordinate delay. Preferably within 90 days from the decree or order complained against as has been prescribed for preferring an appeal to the High Court Division from any decree or order of a court subordinate to it — relied on 39 DLR (AD) 205.

Section-115

In exercising revisional jurisdiction under Section- 115 of the Code of Civil Procedure this Court can interfere with the finding of fact only when it can be shown that the finding is based on cross Misreading of evidence or it has been founded on a Misconception or Misapplication of law or Misinterpretation of any material document or otherwise it is perverse being contrary to law and evidence brought on record—it is also to be borne in mind that mere erroneous decision is not a ground for interference in the exercise of revisional power under section 115 of the Code. Interference may be called for if the judgment appears to have suffered from a patent illegality, legal infirmity and flagrant error of law occasioning a failure of justice.
