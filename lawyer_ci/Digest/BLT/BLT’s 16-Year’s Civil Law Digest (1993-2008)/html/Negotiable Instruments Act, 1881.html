Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881

Sections-79 & 80

Section 79 provides for interest when it is expressly made payable on a promissory note or a bill of exchange, and section 80 provides that the rate of interest would be 6% per annum, when no rate of interest is specified in the instrument.

Sonali Bank Vs. M/S. Karnaphuli Works Ltd. 2BLT (AD)-78

Section-138

In order to make a person liable under section 138 the payee of a cheque has to give a notice in writing to the drawer of the cheque within 15 days of the receipt of information by him from the Bank regarding the return of the cheque as unpaid in terms of clause (b) to the proviso. The drawer of the cheque is obliged to make the payment within 15 days of the receipt of the said notice failing which the cause of action for prosecution will arise under clause (c).

S.M. Anwar Hossain Vs. Md. Shafiul Alam Alom & Anr. 7BLT (AD) -218

Section- 138

In the instant case the cheque was presented to the bank after expiry of 6 months from the date of drawing of the cheque. So, obviously this case under Section 138 of Negotiable Instruments Act is not maintainable in view of the restriction imposed by proviso (a) to the said section.

Alhaj M.A. Majid Vs. Md. Abdul Motaleb 10 BLT (HCD)-459

Section- 138 and Penal Code, 1860 Section-406

Whether the case is filed under the provision of Negotiable Instrument Act, 1881 the court below is not competent to take cognizance under the provision of general criminal law.

Negotiable Instrument Act, 1881 we do not find any barring clause or outstare clause or non-obstante clause in the said Act and where there is no non-obstante clause the jurisdiction of the court, constituted under the Code of Criminal Procedure cannot be taken away or barred.

Md. Moniruzzaman Vs. ANM Deydar-E-Alam & Ors. 10 BLT (HCD)-415

Section—138(b)

Though the appellant presented the cheque on four dates but after the cheque was dishonoured for the last time on 26.10.2000, he served the required notice on 06.11.2000, well within, statutory period and as such filing of the instant complaint on 11.12.2000 cannot be regarded as illegal.

Munshi Md. Rashed Kamal Vs. Abdus Salam and Anr. 13BLT (AD)158

Section-141(b)

The subsequent allegations will not save the limitation—the requirement under the law is that the complaint has to be filed within one month of the date on which the cause of action arises under clause(c) of the proviso to section 138.

S. M. Anwar Hossain Vs. Md. Shafiqul Alam & Anr. 7BLT (AD)-218

Negotiable Instrument Act (as amendment), 2006

Section- 138A read with General Clauses Act, 1897

Section-6

Whether amendment of section 138 of the Negotiable Instrument Act, 1881 wil1 have retrospective effect on all cases including those even filed before coming into force of above amendment or will have prospective effect on all cases filed after above amendment having coming in to force.

This Court is of the view that said amendment dated 9.2.2006 will have prospective effect on all cases to be filed after the amendment came into force as in the amending enactment as above no intention was expressly stated about retrospective effect of the same. Accordingly there was no need for deposit of 50% of the amount of dishonoured cheque before filing of the concerned appeal.

Md. Akhter Hossain Vs. Hasmat Ali & The State 16 BLT (HCD)80

Section —138

Alleged occurrence took place prior to coming into force of the Act No. III of 2006 on 9.2 .2006

Since the trial of the case already commenced with the framing of the charge by the learned Metropolitan Magistrate, Dhaka on 12.12.2005, he should not have sent the case to the learned Metropolitan Sessions Judge, Dhaka for trial under the amended provisions by the Act. No.111 of 2006.

Arif Sultana Mahmud Vs Hazi Imam Hossain & Anr 16 BLT (HCD)263

Section- 138

If the cheque is presented to the bank for encashment more than once within a period of 6 months from the date on which it is drawn or within the period of its validity, whichever is earlier the notice as per clause (b) 01 the proviso to section 138 of the Negotiable Instrument Act demanding the amount of the cheque will require to be issued within 15 days office receipt of the information by the payee or the holder in due course of the cheque From the bank regarding the last bouncing of the cheque.

Md. Anisuzzaman Vs. The State & Ors. 12 BLT (HCD-)229.
