The Court of Wards Act, 1879

(As Amended) Section-8A read with Bangladesh Laws (Amendment)

Ordinance 1976 (Ordinance No.IX of 1976) Section 2A

In view of section 8A of the Act and section 2A of said Ordinance, the retainable Khas land of a ward of the erstwhile proprietors vested in the Government and the Government shall possess and manage such land on behalf of such ward and no person save the Government can be allowed to represent any of the wards of the Bhawal Raj Estate, if any survived, in respect of any retainable khas land only. How much retainable khas land still belongs to which of the wards of said estate cou1d not be ascertained, we understand such survey was never made. It may be noted that it is misleading to say Bhawal Raj Court of Wards Estate since no such Court of Wards finds its existence in the Act. Similarly, whereabouts of the manager could not be ascertained. Who made whom manager could not be made clear. Nor any authority could be placed before us on behalf of the manager to represent the estate.

Mrs. Romisa Khanam & Ors Vs Govt. of Bangladesh & Ors 16 BLT (HCD)461
