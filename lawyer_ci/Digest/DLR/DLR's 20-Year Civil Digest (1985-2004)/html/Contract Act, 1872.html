Section 28–

This principle of common lawhas now found statutory recognition in section 28 of the Contract Act. So,condition No. 18 of the policies has the full backing of explanation (1) ofsection 28 of the Contract Act. It would be a clear violation of the law ofcontract and terms and conditions of the policy to saddle the insurers with theliability to pay the loss though they never acknowledged the liability and hada right to postpone a decision on liability until arbitrator had fixed theamount of loss or damage.
