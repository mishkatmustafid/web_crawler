Order XXVII (CPC)

 

Suits by or against the Government or Public Officers in their official capacity.

Or. 27, r. 1—Government’s responsibility when acting as a litigant to a suit—In a suit against Government the latter is not expected to raise all sorts of defences in a spirit of sheer litigiousness— Must not fight shy of admitting patent facts.

Muhammad Akbar Khan Vs. Province of West Pakistan (1961) 13 DLR (SC) 4.

 

Or. 27, r. 2—An Advocate when authorized by the Government to conduct case on its behalf becomes a recognized agent for purpose of appearance, act and applications under the CP Code.

Messrs. Nabi Baksh Vs. Pak. Secy. Ministry of Industries, Karachi (1970) 22 DLR (WP) 99.
