Order XXXII (CPC)

 

Suits by or against Minors and Persons of Unsound Mind.

Or. 32, r. 1—The guardian or the next friend of the minor can bring a suit on behalf of the minor at any time during the minority of the ward.

Aalaz Mia Vs. Mohammad Mostafa (1965) 17 DLR 112.

Debutter property—Maintainability of suit— Non-joinder of deity as a party in a suit in respect of debutter property is fatal for maintenance of the suit.

Tejendra Chandra Dhar Vs. Rohini Ranjan Saha (1971) 23 DLR 45.

 

Suit by or against minor, procedure in—

It is the duty of the court to appoint a minor’s guardian 39A 8.26 CLJ 258.

—One of two joint promissors cannot plead the minority of the other and his consequent immunity is a bar to the promissee’s claim against him. 24 CLI 74 PC 34 M314 affirmed.

—When minor is sued as major, the decree is a nullity, but if the minor has paid rent under the decree he cannot recover it. 27 CWN 549.
