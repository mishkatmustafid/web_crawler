Passport Act

(XXXIV of 1920)

 

Read with Passport Rules, 1955 Passport does not clothe its bearer with any vested right to leave native country at pleasure.

Syed Abul A‘ala Maududi Vs. The State Bank of Pakistan, (1970) 22 DLR (WP) 57

 

S. 3—Issue of passport—Object of—No citizen has a right to issue of passport.

A passport is not a document under which a citizen is authorized to leave that country but it is a document issued by a sovereign State requesting the other sovereign State or its functionary to give certain facilities to one of its citizens. The rights to issue passport to a citizen arc recognized in a sovereign State under the well—established rule of International Law and the issuing of passport is a part of the State Department’s Foreign Affairs function, which is not justifiable. In other words, Pakistani citizen has no legal right, much less Constitutional right, to be issued a passport.

Manzoor Khan vs. Government of Pakistan, PLD 1966 (W.P) Peshawar 29 (D.B.) (Faizullah, J.)
