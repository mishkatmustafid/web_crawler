Patents & Designs Act (II of 1911)

 

S. 43(6): Right as to design operates from the date of its registration.

Md. Abdul Latif Vs. Md. Harunur Rashid (1984) 36 DLR 59.

 

Secs—47 and 52: Registration of the design ensures the right of the design. So long as the registration certificate (regarding a design) exists the plaintiff gets protection under sections 47 and 52 of the Patent and Designs Act to guard against piracy of his registered design. Ibid.

 

S. 47 (1)—Registered design—Copyright is granted in favor of owner—Length of time of existence of copyright or doubts as to its validity not sufficient reason for not protecting copyright.

Registration of a design creates copyright which is protected by section 53(1) of the Act “during the existence of copyright”. In the presence of these statutory provisions it is difficult to see how the length of time in which registration may exist, or mere doubts, whether substantial or otherwise, as to its validity which may be entertained can be given weight for not protecting copyright in a registered design, irrespective of whether the protection that is needed is temporary or otherwise. Section 53 (1) is categorical when it says that “(luring the existence of copyright in any design it shell not be lawful for any person” other than the registered proprietor to use it for the purpose of selling his goods.

Silver Cotton Textile Mills Ltd. Vs. Bawany Violin Textile Mills Ltd., PLD 1963 (W.P) Karachi 79=PLR 1963 (1) W.P. 71. (Qadeeruddin, J.).

 

Ss. 47(I) and 83 (I): Copyright over the registered design extends for 5 years when no imitation is permissible.

Md. Abdul Latif Vs. Md. Harunur Rashid (1984) 36 DLR 59.

S. 51(6): If a registration in regard to a particular design is obtained by fraud the remedy is to move the Controller of the Patents and Designs or the High Court Division for cancellation of registration of design. Ibid.
