Absence ofofficial signature—in thegazette is at best an irregularity and does not render the notification invalidif the original contains the signature.

In the present case the area in questionwhich was formerly a rural area has been converted into an urban area bynotification published in official Gazette. The notification has however beenattacked on the ground that it does not contain any signature of any Governmentofficer to give the publication an official authorize.

The question is: Will this absence ofsignature in the Gazette make it illegal?

Held: Since from the records we are satisfiedthat this notification was signed by the Joint Secretary to the Govt. of EastPakistan, we do not think that this notification as published in the DaccaGazette is without any authority.

At the most, it may be said to be anirregularity, but this mere irregularity will not affect the validity of thisnotification.

M/S Amin Jute Mills Ltd. Vs. The ChittagongMunicipality, (1968) 20 DLR 1040.

 

Addressingthe Chief Justice or a Judge of the High Court directly on a point of law by asubordinate court— not proper.

If on the ground of urgency the attention ofHigh Court is sought to be drawn directly, the Registrar of the High Courtshould be addressed by the subordinate court and it is not the practice towrite a personal letter addressed to the Chief Justice or any other Judge ofthe High Court.

Normally such matters should be brought tothe notice of the High Court by way of a proper application moved through theLegal Remembrancer.

HarmuzMondal Vs. State (1961) 13 DLR 405.

Administrationof justice.

Court’s function is to administer law and notto look into the propriety of the legislation—no deviation from its strictapplication by the court—stricter vigilance necessary in cases wheremisapplication of law apprehended. (1956) 8 DLR 526.

 

—Administrativedecision said to have been arrived at togive a fishery right to a particular party unless expressed by a written ordercannot create any right to that party in whose favor the decision is said tohave been made and therefore creates no legal right vested in that party.

The petitioner claims that the Board ofRevenue could not cancel the settlement made in its favor for the year 1375B.S. and this cancellation and order of settlement of the fishery for the year1375 B.S. with Abu Bakkar Siddique are without any legal authority.

Held: The correspondence between the DeputyCommissioner, Rajshahi and the Board of Revenue is internal correspondencewhich does not create any actionable claim in the petitioner Co—operativeSociety. Had it been the case that the settlement for the year 1375 B. S., hadbeen made with it and if it had been put into possession of the fishery thenpossibly the petitioner could have said that the lease could not be cancelledwithout giving it a prior hearing.

NawabganjThana Fishermen Co-operative Society Ltd. Vs. Member, Board of Revenue, Govt.of East Pakistan, (1968) 20 DLR 992.
