Court notbound to take notice of events subsequent to the institution of a suit, thoughit has the discretions.

A Court is not bound to take notice of eventsubsequent to the institution of the suit though has discretion to do so. Itcan adjudicate up rights of parties as they existed at the date of institutionof the suit leaving the question arising out of subsequent events to be decidedin fresh proceedings.

Delay inrectification of Clerical Mistake in the Judgment, when can be condoned:

It appears that on 30.3.1964 the trial Courtaccepting the Pleader Commissioner’s report passed the following order:

Petitioner is directed to deposit Rs.12201—within one month from this date.

In fact, the proper words should be not “thepetitioner” but the Opposite party and the amount is not Rs. 1220/- but Rs.1160/-. For correction, of these mistakes an application was moved on 16.9.68but it is objected to that it is too late to have this mistake rectified.

Held: Delay alone is not sufficient to disentitlea party from securing rectification of a clerical or arithmetical mistake in anorder or a decree provided the delay is not intentional and based on mala fidemotives or because of such delay the property itself has passed into the handsof a bona fide third party.

Ramjan Vs.Abdul Gani Mistry, (1970) 22 DLR 61.
