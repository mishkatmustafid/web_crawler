Practice and Procedure

 

Judge’s previous decision.

Judge’s previous decision on specific law point ought to be accepted as binding on him.

 

Judgment—Presumptions that on which judgment is silent were not pressed—Cannot always be made.

There can be no presumption that the Court will always be correct in its decision on points of law or that it will raise and decide the necessary issues every case. It is not always necessary to file an affidavit in support of the ground that all the points which were pressed in the lower appellate Court were not considered.

Asan Uddin Vs. Abed Ali (1962) 14 DLR 279.

 

Judicial functions – union of, with executive functions.

Union of judicial functions with executives, administrative and arbitral functions in the same court unconstitutional and ultra vires. A body whose primary or only object is administrative, executive or arbitral cannot be converted into a court merely by being called a court—Essential difference between judicial power and arbitral power— whether a tribunal is a court depends upon the substance— Incidents of a court.

Attorney General Vs. The Queen (1958) 10 DLR 9 (PC)

 

Jurisdiction and exercise of jurisdiction —Distinction

Distinction between jurisdiction and exercise of jurisdiction – Civil Courts not to correct errors of judgments of Revenue Officers far less the Executive officers.

Golam Hossain Vs. Abdul Manna (1983)35 DLR 331.

 

Laches defeats claim for relief

In refusing the petitioner’s petition for relief under Art. 98 the court in this circumstances of this case, made the following observations:—

On the one hand, we are faced with facts which leave no room for any doubt that the respondents not only acted illegally but also in a most high— handed manner, on the other hand, we are also faced with the proposition that, if we allow the petitioner the relief prayed for, we would be encouraging petitioner to come to this considerable lapse of time. The relief which this Court can give under Art. 98 must be given having regard to considerations of laches and delay on similar principles upon which prerogative writs were issued

Legislation where offending the constitution.

There may be circumstances in which legislation though framed so as not to offend directly against a constitutional limitation of the power of the legislature may indirectly achieve the same result and that in such circumstances the legislation would be ultra vires.

Govindan Silappa Vs. Punche Banda. (1953) 5 DLR 521 (PC).

—It must be shown affirmatively by the party challenging a statute which is upon its face intra vires that it was enacted as part of a plan to effect indirectly something which the legislature had no power to achieve directly. Ibid.

 

Legislation—principle of

The principle that a legislature cannot do indirectly what it cannot do directly has always been recognized by the Privy Council and a legislature must of course be assumed to intend the necessary effect of its statutes. But the maxim omnia pracsumuntur rite esse acta is at least as applicable to the Act of a legislature as to any other acts and the court will not be astute to attribute to any legislature motives or purposes or objects which are beyond its power.

Govindan Silappa Vs. Punchi Banda (1953) 5 DLR 521 (PC)

 

Legislature to determine the composition of its nationals.

It is a perfectly natural and legitimate function of the legislature of a country to determine the com— position of its nationals. Standards of literacy, of property, of birth or of residence are standards which a legislature may think it right to adopt in legislation on citizenship and it is clear that such standard though they operate to exclude the illiterate, poor an the immigrant to greater degree than they may ex., elude other people do not create disabilities in a community by its literacy, its poverty or its migratory character but by its race or its religion. (1953) 5 DLR 521 (PC).

 

Legislature illegally adding to its members—The general rule is that if a legislature illegally adds to its members and the persons so added take part in discussion and voting the laws passed by ii are void.

Federation of Pakistan Vs. A. Ahmed Hossain Sah (1955) 7 DLR (FC) 279 (281 r-h.col).

 

Liability arising out of debt—Liability arising out of debt and the manner of payment of debt under D.S. Board’s direction by in statements are different. If the manner of payment can’ not be given effect to the liability Continues to exist.

Siddik Ahmed Khan Vs. Gura Mia Khan (1956) 8 DLR 417.

 

Letter put in the process of transmission by posting it cannot be held to beyond recall if the post office is instructed to withhold it or a quicker means of transport is used before the letter reached the addressee.

It was suggested that once the letter of resignation had been put in transmission so as to go beyond recall of the writer, it could not have been withdrawn.

held: If such a letter is sent by post, the possibility is still open that, before it is delivered to the addressee, it may be withheld at the instance of the sender, by the Postal Authority, or the latter being satisfied that the sender did not want it to be delivered at its destination. The Post Office acts in such a case as an agent of the sender and not of the addressee. Again, if the letter is sent through a messenger and overtakes him by a quicker means of transport, so as to prevent him reaching his destination, the resignation, would not take effect.

Fazlul Quader Chowdhury Vs. Shah Nawaz (1966) 18 DLR (SC) 62

 

Matter not raised in the original Court—should not ordinarily be allowed to be raised in appeal.

Allahdino Vs. Fakir Mohammad, (1969) 21 DLR (SC) 382.

 

Members wrongly elected or nominated —acts done by,

Acts done by wrongly elected or nominated members do not become invalid, before the member’s election or nomination is declared invalid, nor the Board’s proceedings are affected.

S.M. Wali Ahmed Chowdhury Vs. Mahfuzul Haq Chowdhury (1956) 8 DLR 429.

 

Made of determining the nature of claim sought to be assigned

The true test for determining the nature of a claim sought to be assigned should be to see that the claim when it does accrue partakes of the character of a debt beneficial interest in moveable property not in the possession, either actual or constructive, of the claimant which can be recovered by an action.

Kazi Abdul Ali Vs. Nurul Amin (1955) 7 DLR 406 (411 r-h. col)

Natural justice

An application or substitution of the heirs of a deceased opposite party was made before the trial court (in this particular case) on 9.8.58 when it was contended on behalf of other opposite parties that death actually took place much earlier and therefore the case had abated for non—substitution of the heirs of the said deceased opposite party. On a direction by the Appellate Court to Trial Court (which dismissed the applicant’s cast,) for giving opportunity to the applicant to get the abatement set aside, the applicant made an application on 26.8.61 for treating the application made on 9.8.58 as one under Order 22, r. 9, C.P.Code for setting aside the abatement consequent on the death of the opposite party No.1. The Court granted the prayer and set aside the abatement of the case but on appeal, the High Court held that the application filed on 9.8.58 could not be treated as one made under Order 22, r. 9, of the Code. On appeal by special leave to the Appellate Division of the Supreme Court, it has been held: It is true that there was no prayer for setting aside abatement in the application filed on 9.8.58 and there could not be any such prayer in the said petition since the death of opposite party No. I was shown as within time. But in the application filed on 26.8.61 the appellant stated:

“Before the date of 9.8.58, this petitioner was not aware of the death. Therefore, this petitioner is entitled to get extension under section 5 of the Limitation Act.”

And thereafter made the following prayer: “The application filed on 9.8.58 by this petitioner for the substitution of the heirs may be treated as an application under Order 22, r. 9 C.P.C.

Technically it can be said that the petition filed on 9.8.58 cannot be treated as an application for setting aside abatement since there was no such prayer in the said petition. But when the applicant for setting aside abatement filed a petition on 26.8.61 stating that he had no knowledge of the death earlier and is entitled to get extension under section 5 of the Limitation Act and the learned Munsif, for ends of justice, treated the petition as a proper one for disposal of the issue and disposed it accordingly.

The technicality of form, if adhered to, as the High Court took to be so, and then there would be no proper dispensation of justice in the facts and circumstances of the case. The substance of the prayer should be looked into taking an overall view of the facts and circumstances of the case, ignoring the mere technicality of form of the petition, and it is not desired that the technicality of form should prevail causing impediment of dispensation of justice.

Abdul Hamid Kazi Vs. Abdul Jabbar (1974) 26 DLR (SC) 68.

—When an order has taken effect and in pursuance of that order certain right has been created in favor of any person that order cannot be withdrawn or rescinded to the detriment of that person.

When a right has accrued to a person by virtue of an order that order cannot be rescinded by the person who made it. Again, when it is not incumbent upon the Government to punish a 3overnment servant merely because he has been convicted by a court a fresh order of punishment after the petitioner will be reinstated is wholly un-warrantal.

A.H.M. Abdul Hai Vs. Bangladesh (1979) 31 DLR 88.

Order for Appointment of next friend of a deity can be made in the Appellate stage.

New trial when should not be asked—Their Lordships of the Privy Council cannot concede to the application made by counsel on behalf of the defendant that the case should be remitted for a new trial to enable him to prove the existence of an agreement in writing which was essential to his case and which he failed to do during hearing which had with numerous adjournments covered a period from March, 1947 to February, 1949.

Emmanuele Grech Vs. Antonio Grech (1954) 6 DLR 598 (PC) 603.

 

Notice of requisition issued without any lawful authority—Impugned notices of requisition in these cases were issued without any lawful authority and of no legal effect.

The main respondents could not say whether there was any public purpose of public interest in the matter or any form of propriety, as they appeared to be hell—bent in getting the lands for a Society which was not even registered. In their anxiety they even forgot the law that the authority requisitioning or acquiring any property, under the compulsive power of the act, must indicate definitely what part of the land is required by them.

Chandi Kaibarta Das Vs. Bangladesh. (1982) 34 DLR 227.

 

Notice for particular relief not available to plaintiff—Particular relief asked for in the notice not being available to the plaintiff, the court can substitute appropriate relief, without the necessity of a fresh notice under section 80 C.P.C.

Federation of Pak. Vs. Mrs. A.V. Isaacs. (1957) 9 DLR (SC) 16.

 

No Court can pass a decree in favor of a defendant which is not sought—The contention was that the plaintiffs have come to Court for relief but not the defendants but the lower Court passed orders in favour of the defendants though not claimed. The portion of the order objected to, runs thus: The defendant Government and the lessees under the Government arc quite entitled to enter into the Suit properties by removing there from all the plaintiffs of the suits aforesaid’ and the same has been made part of the decree i.e, a decree has been passed in favour of the defendants.

Held: No Court can pass a decree in favour of a defendant which is not sought for by him legally.

Jadu Nath vs. Province of East Bengal (1961) 13 DLR 496.
