Practice and Procedure

 

Temporary suspension order—Does not require a prior show cause notice.

No judicial decision is available in support of proposition that an interim administrative order for temporary suspension of any person in any office, required any prior show cause notice, particularly when such order is followed by a quasi—judicial proceeding where the aggrieved person is enabled to defend oneself before any final penal action is taken.

MR. Khan Vs. Bangladesh (1975) 27 DLR 603.

 

—Time allowed by Court to do an act by a certain date—whole of that date available for performance of that act.

When time is allowed by the Court upto a certain date for performance of an act the whole of that date will be available to the person to whom the time is allowed for compliance with the order. (Chowdhury, C.J.)

Md. Ramjan Vs. Faziul lluq (2963) 25 DLR 99.

 

Transfer of a case—Friendship with a judge.

Friendship with a judge is not a factor to disqualify a judge to hear a case.

Mirza Akbar Ali Vs. Mrza Jftekhar Ali, (1956) 8 DLR (FC) 15.

 

Transposition takes place—between two different persons and not between the same people.

Madan Chand Gouthy Vs. Mohammad Nurul Huda, (1979) 31 DLR 372.

 

Trial Court’s decisions—Trials court’s decision on fact—advantages which it enjoys—when cannot be disturbed and when can be disturbed. (1957) 9 DLR 682.

 

—It can only be on the rarest occasions, and in circumstances where the appellate court is convinced by the plainest considerations, that it would be justified in finding that the trial judge had formed a wrong opinion. But when the court is so convinced, it is entitled and indeed bound to give effect to its conviction.

An appellate court is not justified in reversing the trial Judge on what is in truth his perception of the facts not his evaluation of them.

K.M. Parira Vs. H.G. Martin Dias, (1958) 10 DLR (PC).

 

—However excellent a judge’s note of evidence may be, the cases must be rare where, no transcript being available, the appellate court in a case involving the veracity of a witness can properly disturb the finding of fact of the trial judge who made the note.

Md. Akbar Abdul Sattar Vs. W.L. Bogtstra (1956)8 DLR (PC) 19.

 

Trial Court’s order when irregular and illegal—Trial courts order irregular or illegal if it passes an order in contravention of the appellate court’s stay order.

Zillur Rahman Sarkar Vs. Abu Bakkar Siddique  @ Siddique Ali Pk. (1980) 32 DLR 292.

 

Trial Court’s reasoning and findings—when agreed to by 1st appellate Court.

When the 1st. appellate court agrees with the reasoning and findings of the Trial Court it need not re—slate effect of evidence or repeat reasons of the Trial Court.

Abdus Salam Vs. Md. Ustar (1983) 35 DLR 65.

Of an intending employee is not a punitive measure and question of giving opportunity to show cause does not arise.

There is no doubt that the service of a temporary employee can be terminated according to the terms and conditions of the contract. In the instant case, the Government could have terminated the case without showing any cause.

Voting paper— casting of

Voting paper taken out from one booth and cast into box in another booth, invalid.

Md. Mohsin Siddiqui vs. Chief Judg Karachi. (1956) 8 DLR (WP) 66.

 

What an appellate or revisional authority is competent to do.

The revisional authority not empowered to pass an order which the original Court could not have passed.

The conception of an appellate or revisional authority is that it examines the original order for determining whether it ought to have been passed in accordance with the law and even though in some exceptional case an order may be passed by the revisional authority in view of some facts which had occurred after the original order was passed, it is not empowered to pass an order on any ground on the basis of which the original Court could not have proceeded.

M.M. Ispahani Ltd. vs. Md. Sultan (1961) 13 DLR (SC) 47.
