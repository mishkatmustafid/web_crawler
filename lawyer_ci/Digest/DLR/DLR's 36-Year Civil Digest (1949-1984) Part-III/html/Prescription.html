Prescription

(See also under Easement and ‘Right of way’)

 

Prescriptive right of way—A prescriptive right of way cannot be acquired by mere enjoyment for the statutory period. The enjoyment must be as of right, i.e. not attributable to permission implied or express.

Abdul Hafiz Vs. Mafizuddin, (1955) 7 DLR 577.

 

—The mere fact that one person walks over the land of another does not raise any presumption that he has a right to do so. Numerous people pass over the lands of their neighbors, friends and relations with their tacit permission. Such user though continuous and long is seldom understood as being assertion of any right. Ibid.

—In a suit for prescriptive right of way the plaintiff must show that user was such as was sufficient to put servient owner on notice that the enjoyment was not attributable to his tacit permission or favour. Ibid.

 

Prescriptive right—acquisition of—limited interest by.

A limited interest of a lessee may be acquired by prescriptive right.

Hajari Sardar Vs. Mozam Molla. (1956) 8 DLR 640.
