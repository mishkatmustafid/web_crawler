President’s Order No. 4 of 1962

 

Art. 76. Application of the not limited only to the holding 1st election to the National and provincial Assemblies.

The contention was that Article 76 of the President’s Order No.4 of 1962 which enacted that the wife of a person in the service of Pakistan is qualified from being elected as a member of the Assembly was limited to the holding of the first election and secondly that the President acted beyond his constitutional powers by prescribing a further disqualification in the said Article 76 in addition to those already specified in Article 103 of the Constitution.

Held: Reading Article 230 of the Constitution with the preamble of Order 4 of 1962 which sets Out in clear terms that it is necessary to provide for the conduct of the elections to the National d Provincial Assemblies and for matters ancillary thereto or connected therewith one cannot escape conclusion that the above Order No. 4 was not confined to the very first election.

Begum Shamsunnehar Vs. Speaker, East Pak. Assembly (1965) 17 DLR (SC) 21.
