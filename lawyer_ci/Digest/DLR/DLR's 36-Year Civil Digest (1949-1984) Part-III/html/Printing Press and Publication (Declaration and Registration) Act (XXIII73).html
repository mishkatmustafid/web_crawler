Printing Press and Publication (Declaration and Registration) Act (XXIII/73)

 

Secs. 7 & 12 District Magistrate given power to authenticate a newspaper declaration-In case of refusal an appeal lies with the Government —Proceeding for authentication is a quasi-judicial act.

Sk. Faziul Karim Salim Vs. Bangladesh ors. (1981) 33 DLR 406.

—When conditions embodied in clauses (a) of s.12 (2) of Act XXIII/73 are fulfilled authentication of declaration must he given. Ibid.

—Referring the matter of authentication of declaration to the Government by the Dy. Commissioner was wrong.   Ibid.

S. 12 (1) (2)— Memo of the Deputy Commissioner was made by virtue of a colourable exercise of authority vested in him under sub-sections (1) & (2) of section 12 of the Act XXIII of 1973. On that account the impugned memo must be held to have been made illegally without any lawful authority, with no legal effect and must be set aside. Ibid.

 

S. 12(2) clauses (a) to (h) The District Magistrate in dealing with authentication matters acts in quasi-judicial capacity—

—Personal hearing to the person making the declaration.

Motahar Hossain Siddiqui, Vs. Bangladesh & another. (1981)33 DLR 238.

—Requirements of clauses (a) to (h) of section 12 (2) of Act XXIII of 1973 once being complied with the District Magistrate has no option but to authenticate the declaration.   Ibid.

—When a declaration has fulfilled all the requirements of clauses (a) to (h) of section 12(2) of Act XXIII/73, the Deputy Commissioner is under an obligation to authenticate the declaration without referring the matter to the Government. Ibid.
