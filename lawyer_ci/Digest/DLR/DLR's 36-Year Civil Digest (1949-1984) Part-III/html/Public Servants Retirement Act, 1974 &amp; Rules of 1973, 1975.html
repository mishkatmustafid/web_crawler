Ss. 7 and 9(2)—The authority while passing order directed that the petitioner would not be entitled to leave preparatory to retirement. This direction not permissible in a case of compulsory retirement. This part of the impugned order, that is, “that he shall not be entitled to leave preparatory to retirement” is bad in law, and as such it cannot be sustained.

—Absence of Principle of guideline in section 9(2) of Act XII 011974 as to how description is to be exercised—scope for arbitrary Act exists.

Since neither the impugned provisions of the Act nor the rules provide any principle or guideline for the exercise of discretion by the Government when it proposes to retire a Government servant form amongst a group of Government servants similarly situated and holding similar jobs, there invariably exists the scope for arbitrary exercise of such discretion.

Discretion of the relevant authority must be seen to have been exercised on some such principles as arc discernible in Fundamental Rule 56, Note 2 of the East Bengal Service Rule and Rule 3 of the Bangladesh Public Servants Retirement Rules, 1973. Where such basis has been provided the concerned authority before exercising its discretion, has to conform to the principles so provided therein.

Dr.Nurul Islam, Vs. Bangladesh represented by the Secretary, Ministry of health. (1981) 33 DLR (AD) 201.

—Section 9(2) not ultravires of the Constitution.

The petitioner being in service of the Republic immediately before the commencement of the situation is entitled to continue in that service on same terms and conditions as were applicable to r immediately before such commencement. This section does not divest the Parliament or the prime Law-making body to regulate the condition of service, as in the instant case, fixing age of retirement. However, clause (b) sub-paragraph(2) of paragraph 10 makes it clear that the protection as mentioned in sub-paragraph (1) shall not prevent making of any law varying or revoking the cc lions of service (including remuneration, leave, pension rights and rights relating to disciplinary matters) of person employed at any time before commencement of this Constitution or of persons continuing in the service of the Republic under the p visions of paragraph 10. In view of the above note provisions in the Constitution, we are of the c ion that section 9(2) of Act XII of 1974 is not ultravires of the Constitution.

Abdur Rashid, Vs. The Govt. of the People’s Republic of Bangladesh. (1979) 31 DLR 233.

 

—Section 9(2) is not violative of Art. 135 of The Bangladesh constitution, 1972.

Section 9(2) is not violative of Article 135 for die simple reason that protection under article 135 is strictly limited to dismissal, removal or reduction in rank, whereas premature retirement on completion of 25 years’ service with full pensioner benefits, does, under no circumstances, amount to dismissal or removal.

Dr. Nurul Islam, Vs. Bangladesh (1981) 33 DLR (AD) 201.

PUBLIC SERVANT’S RETIREMENT RULES OF 1973

 

Enacted that Retirement of the public servant could be ordered where it is necessary for the interest of public service or in public interest thereby offering a basis for a retirement order.

Dr. Nurul Islam, Vs. Bangladesh (1981) 33 DLR (AD) 201.

—Such a basis if could be found in Act XII of 1974 as was in the rules of 1973 that would have enabled the authority to show the basis of its action.   Ibid.

 

Public Servants Retirement Rules, 1975

No Guideline Provided.

No principle or guideline for the exercise of discretion by the Government in the matter of retiring a Government servant on the completion of 25 years of service is provided in the Public Servants (Retirement) Rules, 1975.

Dr. Nurul Islam, Vs. Bangladesh represented by the Secretary, Ministry of Health. (1981) 33 DLR (AD) 201.
