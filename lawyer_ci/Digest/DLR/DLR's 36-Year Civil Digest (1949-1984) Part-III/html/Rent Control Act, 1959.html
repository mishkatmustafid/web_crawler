Rent Control Act

(XVI of 1959)

 

S. 17(e): Bonafide requirement for personal use may arise subsequently.

A landlord or an owner of a house may have family members or other dependants who might grow in number and might also require more accommodation from time to time.

It is true that the owner concerned must have a bonafide necessity for using and occupying the entire house but while determining the question as to bonafide necessity, the desirability of allowing the land- lord for having better accommodation should not be ignored or overlooked.

Fazlur Rahman Vs. Nayeema Khantun (1961) 13 DLR 869.
