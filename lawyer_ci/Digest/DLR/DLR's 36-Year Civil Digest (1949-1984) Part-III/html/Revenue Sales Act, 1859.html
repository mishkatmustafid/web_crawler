Revenue Sales Act (XI of 1859)

 

Ss. 29, 37. Sale of an estate for arrears of revenue does not annul under tenures automatically but renders them voidable at the option of the purchaser.

Purchaser can exercise his option of annulment by institution of suit, service of notice upon the under-tenure holders, by collecting rent from tenants or by any other overt act indicating a clear intention to annul and the intention must be made known to the under-tenure holders.

Santa Gopal Roy Choudhary vs. Md. Mohashwaruddin, (1964) 16 DLR 580.

—The under- tenure holder or his successor in interest may succeed on ground of adverse possession even inspite of annulment. Ibid.

 

S. 37 (4th exception): Dwelling house—For availing protection under 4th exception of section 37 of the Revenue Sales Act a dwelling house should be of permanent character but that does not mean that it must be a building or a structure of permanent build—Dwelling house includes cowshed.

Rahman Ali Vs. Upendra Lal Sarkar (1972) 24 DLR 24.

—Tanks and panborj, i.e., betel leaves plantation arc ‘protected interest’ within the meaning of 4th exception of section 37 of the Revenue Sales Act.   Ibid.

—S. 37. Encumbrances, annulment of—No formal notice by the purchaser is required under the Revenue Sales Act to annual encumbrances. Ibid.
