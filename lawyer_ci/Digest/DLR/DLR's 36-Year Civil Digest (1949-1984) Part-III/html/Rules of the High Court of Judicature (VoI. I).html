Rules of the High Court of Judicature (VoI. I)

 

Chap. A r. 9 (Appendix IV).

Rule 9 of Chapter A in Appendix IV to the Rules of the High Court of Judicature Vol. I pros that if at the hearing of the application the t is of opinion that any person who ought to been served with notice of the application has been so served, the Court may order that notice also be served on such person and adjourn the hearing upon such terms as the Court may consider.

Habibullah Khan Vs. Shah Azharuddin Ahmed and ors. (1983) 35 DLR (AD) 72.

 

Rule 9—Rule 9 clearly provides that if in the opinion of the Court the appellant ought to have been served with notice of the writ petition, and since no notice was served upon him, only course open to the Court was to adjourn the hearing and issue direction for service of the writ petition upon appellant.
