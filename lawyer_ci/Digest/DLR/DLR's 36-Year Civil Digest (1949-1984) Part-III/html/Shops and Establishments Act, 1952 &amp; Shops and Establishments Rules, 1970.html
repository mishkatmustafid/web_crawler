S. 9—Leave of absence of an employee for 14 days with full pay.

It was argued that a piece-rated worker is not entitled to the benefit of leave. But this argument is not valid, because nowhere in the Act, a distinction has been made between a salaried employees and a piece- rated employee.

The East Bengal Shops and Establishments Act is mainly for the benefit of the employee and , if possible, ii should be given the widest construction so that the employee’s case may not be jeopardized.

A. Sakur Vs. Chairman, (1968) 20 DLR 520.

 

S. 11—Words “discharge” and “dismissed” explained.

The words “discharged” and “dismissed” found in section 11 of 1952 must be construed to mean “a discharge’ or “a dismissal” at the instance of the employer. By way of reference, we may quote the definition of the word “discharge” as given in section 2(f) of the East Pakistan Employment of Labour (Standing Orders) Act of 1965 (East Pakistan Act No. VIII of 1965) which means the termination of services of worker by the employer for reasons of physical or mental incapacity or continued ill-health of the worker or such other similar reasons not amounting to misconduct. Ibid

If the employer discharges an employee even on grounds of physical incapacity, that too, would amount to a “discharge” which will entitle the employees to the benefits contemplated by section 11 of East Bengal Act, I of 1952.

—Tribunals (as the Labour) Court are to act in accordance with judicial principles.   Ibid

 

S. 11— Does not empower the employers to dismiss employees.

Section 11 of the Act is intended to provide benefits and relief’s to employees even in cases of dismissal. It cannot be construed as a source of power for employers to exercise their right of dismissal of their employees.

River Steam Navigation Co. Vs. A.B.S. Chowdhury (1959) 11 DLR (SC) 108.

 

Shops and Establishments Rules, 1970

 

Rules 10, 12-14—Failure to produce Registers and particulars as required by the rules on the instant demand by a visiting Inspector does not attract any penal provision. —Proper course would be to demand their production by service of notice. —It is on failure thereof that action can be taken.
