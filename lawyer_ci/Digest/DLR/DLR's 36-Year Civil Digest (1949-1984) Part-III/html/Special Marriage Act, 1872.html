Special Marriage Act

(III of 1872)

 

S. 2—Renunciation of religion is necessary for a marriage under the Act—Marriage invalid if religion is not renounced by the parties to the marriage.

The provisions of the Act itself make any false declaration punishable. If the petitioner has not in fact renounced his religion he has made a false declaration.

In the present case she has categorically stated that she is a Christian, and she never renounced her faith and, therefore, she in fact has given a false declaration in the declaration form exhibit 2. Unless one is not a Christian or not a Muslim, as far as this case is concerned, one cannot contract a marriage under this Act. Therefore, on the evidence of respondent herself and on the admission of the petitioner neither of them were competent to contract a marriage under the Special Marriage Act and as such the whole proceedings before the Marriage Registrar, was void.

The marriage purported td have been performed on the 27th February 1964 between the petitioner Mustafizur Rahman Khan and the respondent No. 1 Rina Ghosh is null and void because, firstly, the petitioner was a Muslim before the marriage and is continuing to be a Muslim after the marriage and the declaration made by him on the 22nd February, 1964 was a false declaration and respondent No. 1 Rina Ghosh, as clearly testified by herself, was at the time of the alleged marriage and till this date a Christian of the Roman Catholic sect and she also made a false declaration on 22nd February, 1964, and as such no marriage under the Special Marriage Act (III of 1872) was possible to be contracted between the parties and secondly, because the essential formalities laid down in section 11 of the Special Marriage Act were not performed .

Md. Mustafizur Rahman Vs. Rina Khan (1966) 18 DLR 509.

S. 11—Formalities not having been observed, marriage is void.

The provisions of section 11 are mandatory provisions and this formality was not in fact performed. On this ground also the marriage is void.

Md. Mustafizur Rahman Vs. Rina Khan (1966) 18 DLR 509.
