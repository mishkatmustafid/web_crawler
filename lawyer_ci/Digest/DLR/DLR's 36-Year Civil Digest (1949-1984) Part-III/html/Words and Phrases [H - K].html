Hire-purchase agreement.

There is a distinction between hiring and acquisition of goods uponpayment of installments and each has different legal consequence.

Where the transaction is a sale, a third party bonafide purchaseracquires a good title to the goods against the owner notwithstanding theprovision that the ownership in the goods would remain with the original owneruntil the entire purchase-price was paid; whereas in a hire-purchase agreementsuch third party cannot acquire a better title against the owner.

The main distinguishing feature of a hire- purchase agreement is that ahirer has a right to terminate the agreement for hire at his pleasure and isnot bound to pay the value of the goods. In a contract of sale for pricepayable by installments, the purchaser has no option of terminating thecontract and returning the goods.

Arabindra Poddar Vs. Shew Ram Das Agarwalla. (1960) 12 DLR 229.

 

Holding over.

Upon the death of an original tenant in respect of an immovableproperty, the tenancy being one from month to month, the tenancy does not ceaseto exist and the heirs and legal representatives of the original tenant do notbecome trespassers but having continued in possession they become tenants byholding over and have an accruing interest out of the original contract.

Gouri Bala Pal Vs. Kunja Lal Saha (1960) 12 DLR 37.

 

—The right of holding over, unless conferred by a statute, cannot beimplied or inferred by analogy.
