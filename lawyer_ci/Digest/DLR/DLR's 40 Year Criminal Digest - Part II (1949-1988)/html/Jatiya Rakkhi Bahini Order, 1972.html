                                  Jatiya Rakkhi Bahini Order, 1972

 

Art. 17—Heightened tragedy of the whole matter is that though a law called the Jatiya Rakkhi Bahini Order, 1972 has been enacted under which the Bahini has been constituted, no rules as required by Article 17 has been framed to serve as guidelines to the Bahini without which it is impossible for them to carry on their functions which arc similar to those of the police force who are guided and controlled by very elaborate set of regulations and whose activities are subject to meticulous rules of the law—R. Bahi-ni's activities being such which affect the rights and liberties of the people at large it discloses a serious state of affairs to allow them to function in any manner they like and as such what they may do may not have the proper legal protection for the function they have been discharging. S. Mohsin Sharif Vs. Govt, of Bangladesh. (1975) 27 DLR 189.

 

—Grave allegation, in the present case, is Shah-jahan has been killed by Rakkhi Bahini men— Defence is Shahjahan escaped on the night of 28th December. Petitioner says he met Shahjahan on 30-12-73 as well as on 2-1-74 at RB Hd. Quarters when Shahjahan complained of physical torture—This, if true, contradicts the respondent's version that Shah­jahan escaped on 28lh December.

 

The petitioner moved the S.D.O. on 21-2-74 for Shahjahan's production in Court. That application with the Courts order on it as also a forwarding let­ter to the Officcr-in-Chargc, Ramna P.S. were deliv­ered by the petitioner to the person addressed. These two documents arc very material to show whether Shahjahan was in possession of Rakkhi Bahini on 30-12-73 and 2-1-74. In the affidavit-in-opposition it is averred that this is not true. But the only person who could deny the petitioner's statement arc the S.D.O. and the Officcr-in-Chargc of Ramna P.S. who have been made respondents Nos. 3 & 8 in this case but they have not come forward to deny this. Thus the whole situation reveals an extra-ordinary state of things. S. Mohsin Sharif Vs. Govt, of Ban­gladesh. (1975) 27 DLR 189.

 

—Rakkhi Bahini, Armed Forces and the Police, three important institutions of the country. There is imperative necessity to demarcate the respective spheres of action of these 3 bodies in accordance with Constitution and the general law. Authority's attention invited towards remedying present unsatis­factory state of affairs, such as by setting up a Spe­cial Commission or the like with a view to put mat­ters on even keel—Special attention has further been drawn to call out the whereabouts of Shahjahan who is alleged by the petitioners to have been killed, by setting up an enquiry committee. S. Mohsin Sharif Vs. Govt, of Bangladesh. (1975) 27 DLR 189.
