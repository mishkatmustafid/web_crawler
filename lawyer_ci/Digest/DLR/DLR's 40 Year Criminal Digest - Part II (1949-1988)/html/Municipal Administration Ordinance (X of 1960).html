S. 116—Magistrate convicted and sentenced the accused to pay a fine of Rs. 20/- each day so long as he goes on running his saw mill—The sen­tence is illegal.

There is no doubt that the Criminal Procedure Code would apply to the case in the matter of proce­dure as no particular procedure has been laid down by the Ordinance in question. Under the section 234 of the Code, not more than three offences can be tried at one trial if they are committed within one year. But here in the present case, there is no knowing for how many fresh offences the trial took place and the petitioner has been convicted and punished. All it says is from the date of his last conviction up to the end of his continuation of the running of the Mill. On the face it, it is vague, indefinite and without any limit of time. It cannot be the intention of the said Ordinance to punish a man without any limit of time and with complete disregard to the rules of pro­cedure prescribed for criminal trials. For any offence under the criminal law, there must be some definite mode of trial, definite accusations and all necessary particulars of the offences.

Since no definite accusations has been made as to the offences and other essential particulars so it is illegal under the provisions of section 234 of the Criminal Procedure Code.

There must be some limit to any punishment for any kind of offences and that limit of punish­ment at least must be upto the limit of the maxi­mum powers of a Court. Therefore, the punishment awarded in the present case is obviously illegal be­cause the Magistrate has no jurisdiction to impose such amount of fines as he has done in the present case. Amanullah Molla Vs. Dhaka Municipality, (1967) 19 DLR 684.
