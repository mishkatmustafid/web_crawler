Jail Code

[IX of 1894]

 

Paragraph 910—

The Chief Metropolitan Magistrate has avoided passing of orders on flimsy grounds and as such the question of approval of the District Magistrate for such permission does not arise. In such a case, the under-trial prisoner could invoke the inherent jurisdiction of the court for ends of justice. The under-trial prisoner in this case is entitled to Class I status in the jail under the provisions of Paragraph 910 of the Jail Code and the authority is directed to allow the status due to him.

Major (Retd) M Khairuzzaman vs State 50 DLR 283
