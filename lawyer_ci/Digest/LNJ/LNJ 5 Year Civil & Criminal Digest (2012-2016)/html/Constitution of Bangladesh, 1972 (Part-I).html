Article 102

Article 102(1) and (2) of the Constitution of Bangladesh confers an extraordinary power to the High Court Division and it can exercise such power in its own discretion. An aggrieved person can directly make out a case of infringement of fundamental rights under Article 102(1) whereas in order to qualify to be an applicant under Article 102(2) the aggrieved person must satisfy the Court that 'there is no other equally efficacious remedy. Because of its extraordinary nature the jurisdiction under Article 102(2) cannot be invoked if there is other equal efficacious remedy available. The words 'if satisfied' in Article 102(2) mandates the High Court Division to think about the competency of invoking the Special Original Jurisdiction before issuance of any Rule as such rule may cause sufferings for the parties of the case as well as wasting valuable time of the Court. Concord Pragatee Consortium Ltd. Vs. PBDB and others, (Spl. Original), 3 LNJ 634

Article 102

(Per M. Husain, J, delivered the main judgment): 'Person aggrieved'-      

A person annoyed or hurt or even shocked by any act of an authority may not be a 'person aggrieved' in the technical sense. Although conceptually the expression 'person aggrieved' cannot be fit in a straight jacket there has been a loose but consistently followed parameter of the phrase. A 'person aggrieved' must be a  person whose legal right has been infringed or who has suffered legal wrong or injury in the sense that his interest recognized by law has been prejudicially and directly affected by an act or omission of the authority.  Mere protest raised against a possible decision of a public authority does not ipso facto qualify the protesters as persons aggrieved. Maulana Syed Rezaul Haque Vs. Jamat-E-Islam & ors, (Spl. Original), 3 LNJ 25
