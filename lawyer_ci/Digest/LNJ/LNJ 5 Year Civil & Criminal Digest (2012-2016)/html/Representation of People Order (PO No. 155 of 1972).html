Representation of People Order (PO No. 155 of 1972)

Articles 12 (3a) and 26

The provisions contained in clause (3a) of Article 12 of the Order of 1972 is a pre-election requirement of an independent candidate or any candidate nominated by a Political Party which is not in any way contradictory to the provisions of Article 26 of the said RPO because Article 26 conceived of an election process which requires an election under the RPO, 1972 shall be decided by secret ballot. In order to decide the eligibility or competency of a candidate seeking election to the Parliament for conducting free and fair election through a democratic process Article 12 of the RPO, 1972 was amended by inserting clause (3a) which is not at all ultra vires the provisions of the said Order of 1972 or any of the provisions of the Constitution of the People's Republic of Bangladesh. Mahbub Ahmed Chowdhury Vs. CEC and others, (Civil), 4 LNJ 1

Article 12(3a)

In the instant case we have seen that the provisions contained clause (3a) of Article 12 of the Representation of People Order, 1972 have not been enacted without any legislative competence nor is it in conflict with the other provisions of RPO, 1972 or any other provisions of law.  Mahbub Ahmed Chowdhury Vs. CEC and others, (Civil), 4 LNJ 1
