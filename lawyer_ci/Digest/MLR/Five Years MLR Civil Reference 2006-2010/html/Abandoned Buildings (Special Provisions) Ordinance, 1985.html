Abandoned Buildings (Special Provisions) Ordinance, 1985

Abandoned Buildings (Special Provisions) Ordinance, 1985— (Ord No. 54 of 1985)— Section  7— Release  of abandoned buildings from the "Ka" list-Section     5(2)—     Presumption     of correctness of the list— its rebuttal—

The owner of the building in question was althrpugh in Bangladesh and as a retired government servant drew his pension from the exchequer of the Government till before his death in August, 1981 and as such it can not be said that his whereabouts were not known. In that view of the matter the presumption of correctness of the "Ka" list stands rebutted. The apex court declared the enlistment of the, said building as abandoned illegal and directed to release the same in favour of the writ petitioners. Sirajul Hue] and others Vs. Bangladesh, represented by the Secretary, Ministry of Works and others 14 MLR (2009) (AD) 191.

 

Abandoned Buildings (Supple­mentary Provisions) Ordinance, 1985— Section 5— The abandoned character of the property is the determining factor as to whether the same is abandoned or not— Enlistment of the property either in the 'Ka' or 'Kha' list is immaterial— Fraud vitiates everything—

Decree for specific performance of contract has no bearing, on the question of determination of the disputed property as to whether the same is abandoned property or not. The petitioner who claims the suit property has to prove by valid documents that it is not abandoned property. Golam Rabbani Vs. Chairman, Court of Settlement and others 13 MLR (2008) (AD) 345.

 

Abandoned Buildings (Supple­mentary Provisions) Ordinance, 1985— Section 5(1)— List of abandoned buildings has presumption as to its correctness   until   rebutted   by   the claimant—

Decree of specific performance of contract does not confer any title to the suit property. Such decree is no bar against inclusion of the property in the list of abandoned properties. The onus to rebut the presumption lies on the person who claims the property. Government has no obligation. Moreover transfer of ownership of the abandoned building through court is null and void as per provision of article 6 of P.O. No. 16 of 1972. Amena Kha tun (Mrs.) Vs. Chairman, Court of Settlement and others 15 MLR (2010) (AD) 438.

 

Abandoned Buildings (Supple­mentary Provisions) Ordinance, 1985— Section 7(1)— Release of abandoned buildings from the Ka list—

List of abandoned buildings prepared and published under section 5(2) of the Ordinance, 1985 in the Official Gazette has the presumption of correctness and is a conclusive evidence that the property included in the list is abandoned property and vested in the Government. Government is not required to disclose the basis of the list. When it is established that the Government has once taken over the control and management of the abandoned property, the onus lies upon the  claimant  that  it  was  never     an abandoned property. AH AshrafMia Vs. Chairman, First Court of Settlement and others 11 MLR (2006) (HC) 313.
