Abandoned Property (Manage­ment, Control and Disposal) Order, 1972 (P.O No. 16 of 1972)

Abandoned Property (Management, Control and Disposal) Order 1972 (P.O. No. 16 of 1972) Enlistment of abandoned building in 'Kha' list-When it is found that  the original lessee left Bangladesh during the war of liberation leaving behind the building in question uncared for and thereafter did not return back, the said property has rightly been enlisted in the 'Kha' list of abandoned property. Creation of spurious  documents  and  the exparte decree not in respect of the enlistment can not be bar to the inclusion of the building in the 'Kha' list. Abul Kalam Khan (Md.) Vs. Government of Bangladesh represented by the Secretary, Ministry of Works and others 13 MLR (2008) (HC) 82.

 

Section 2— Property can not be held to be abandoned by reason of temporary absence of its owner—

It has been consistently held by the Appellate Division of the Supreme Court that a property can not be treated as abandoned by reason of temporary absence of its owner and staying in a country or when he was not national of the country with which Bangladesh was at war and his such absence was not prejudical to the interest of Bangladesh. In that view the High Court Division directed the Respondents to release the shares of the petitioners in the Eastern Tubes Ltd. after proper verification which the apex court held perfectly justified. Arif A. Shekha and others Vs. Secretary, Ministry of Industries and another 13 MLR (2008) (AD) 229.

 

Section 5-— Presumption of correctness of the list of abandoned property—

The list of abandoned property duly prepared has a presumption of correctness until such presumption is rebutted by showing the contrary. The Appellate Division found the petitioner bonafide purchaser of the disputed property for valuable consideration without notice of the defect in the title of the transferor and got over possession of the same. Since the said property is abandoned and duly included in the list as such, the title did not pass to the petitioner. However the apex court observed the petitioner should be given preference as and when the respondents dispose of the said property and meanwhile directed the respondent no 4 not to disturb the possession of the petitioner therein. Shadharan Bima Corporation Vs. First Court of Settlement and. others 13 MLR (2008) (AD) 242.
