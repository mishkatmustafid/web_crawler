Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990

 

Definition of loan — Loan includes the principal and interest etc —

The controversy as to the definition of the term "loan" has been settled by the Appellate Division which includes the principal, interest and penal interest etc. Fazle Kader Mukul (Md.) Vs. Bangladesh and others 13 MLR (2008) (HC) 198.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990 Section 4(2)(3)— Held by the Apex Court— Not ultravires the constitution—

The legislature in its wisdom made provisions in section 4(2) and (3) of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990 for the establishment of the Artha Rin Adalat and appointment of the judge thereof which are not inconsistent with and violative of the provisions of article 55(2), 115 and 116 of the Constitution and as such the suit filed in the Artha Rin Adalat for recovery of loan money of the financial institution is quite maintainable. A.R.A Jute Mills Ltd. Vs. Janata Bank and others 11 MLR (2006) (AD) 194.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990— Section 5 and 6— Imposes restriction against appeal  and revision against interlocutory   order   of   Artha    Rin Adalat— Section   7—   Provides   for   appeal against order and decree of Artha Rin Adalat—

Constitution of Bangladesh—

Article 102 and 109— Extra ordinary power of the High Court Division of judicial review— Under article 102 High Court Division is invested with power of judicial review of certain orders against which there is no provision of appeal or revision. Power of High Court Division under article 109 is of administrative nature.

The apex court held the power of High Court Division under article 102 of the Constitution can not be taken away or curtailed by subordinate legislation i.e. the Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990 and as such the writ petition against interlocutory order passed by Artha Rin Adalat quite maintainable. Harun-or-Rashid (Md.) Vs. Pubali Bank Ltd. and others 12 MLR (2007) (AD) 343.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990— Section 6— Provides for bar against filing separate suit for setting aside exparte  decree passed  by Artha  Rin Adalat—

Code of Civil Procedure, 1908— Order 7 rule 11— Rejection of plaint in suit barred by law—

It is well settled that no separate suit shall lie against exparte decree passed by Artha Rin Adalat as provided under section 6 of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990. The remedy against exparte decree is available under Order 9 Rule 13 of the Code of Civil Procedure upon deposit of 50% decretal dues. The rejection of plaint in the suit is held perfectly justified. M. Tariqullah Sikder Vs. Sonali Bank 12 MLR (2007) (HC) 73.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990— Section      6(ka)—      Excludes      the application of rule 3, 5, 6, 7, 8 and 9 of Order 21 but does not exclude the application of section 65 of the Code of Civil Procedure— Code of Civil Procedure, 1908— Section 56— Applies against arrest and   detention   of   woman   in   civil prison—

Since the application of section 56 of the Code of Civil Procedure has not been excluded by section 6(ka) of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990, the prohibition againt arrest and detention of woman in civil prison in execution of decree of Artha Rin Adalat shall apply. Hazera Begum Vs. Artha Rin Adalat and others 12 MLR (2007) (AD) 281.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990— Section  6(2)— Provides  for setting aside exparte decree—

Since specific provisions are there under section 6(2) of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990 for setting aside ex­parte decree, inherent jurisdiction of the court under section 151 of the Code of Civil Procedure can not be invoked for setting aide the same. Goden Re-Rolling Industries Ltd. Vs. Subordinate Judge, Artha. Rin Adalat No. 1 Dhaka and another 12 MLR (2007) (AD) 234.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990— Section   7—   Provides   for   appeal against the judgment  and  decree  of Artha  Rin  Adalat  and  as  such Writ Petition does not lie thereagainst— Constitution of Bangladesh— Article  102— Writ Petition  against judgment and  decree  of Artha  Rin Adalat is not maintainable—

Alternative efficacious remedy is available against the judgment and decree of the Artha Rin Adalat by way of appeal as provided under section 7 of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain 1990 and as such Writ Petition under article 102 of the constitution does not lie against such judgment and decree. Therefore the law on this point is well settled. Bangladesh Agricultural Development Corporation (BADC) represented by Chairman Vs. Artha Rin Adalat, Court No. 3 Dhaka and others 11 MLR (2006) (AD) 308.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990—

Section 7— Order, judgment and decree passed by Artha Rin Adalat are appealable against which no revision lies—

Law is well settled that appeal lies to the High Court Division against an order, judgment and decree passed by the Artha Rin Adalat as provided under sections 6 and 7 of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990 and as such no revision thereagainst under section 115 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 is maintainable. Bulbul Electric Market (M/S) and others Vs. Rupali Bank Ltd. and another 11 MLR (2006) (AD) 409.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990— Section 41— Provides for an appeal against an order or decree passed by Artha Rin Adalat to be filed within 30 days of the date thereof together with deposit of 50% decretal amount—

Requirement of the deposit of 50% decretal amount as provided under section 41 of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990 is mandatory prerequisite and the statutory period of 30 days for filing appeal is special limitation which cannot be extended. Memorandum of appeal accompanied with the document showing deposit of 50% decretal amount must be filed within 30 days of the date of an order or decree appealed against. An appeal filed within 30 days without the deposit of 50% decretal amount but the deposit is made subsequently after the statutory period though under the direction of the High Court Division is held by the apex court not entertainable. Peninsular Shipping Service Ltd. Vs. M/S Faruque Paint and Varnish Manufacturing Co. Ltd. and others 12 MLR (2007) (AD) 1.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 1990—

Section 60(3)— Transfer of Execution case from one Artha Rin Adalat to another Artha Rin Adalat in the same district— held not illegal—

The Appellate Division found nothing wrong with the transfer of the Money Execution case from one Artha Rin Adalat to another Artha Rin Adalat of the same district and refrained from interfering with the impugned order. M/S. Llttara Steel Corporation Limited and another Vs. judge, Artha Rin Adalat and others 13 MLR (2008) (AD) 266.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003 Constitution of Bangladesh— Article 102— Writ petition is held not maintainable against stay of all actions for registration of two buses purchased by the loan of the bank— The impugned action of BRTA cropped up out of the proceedings relating to suit for recovery of loan. The High Court Division held the writ petition not maintainable. The Appellate Division also concurred with the impugned decision of the High Court Division. Monir Hossain Vs. The Artha Rin Adalat No. 4, Dhaka and others 15 MLR (2010) (AD) 236.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003— Execution proceedings ends with the completion of the auction process—

Auction of the mortgaged property was held at the instance of the decree-holder bank. Auction sale was confirmed by the court and the decree-holder bank had withdrawn the bid money in consequence whereof the execution proceedings came to an end. Thereafter the continuation of the proceedings shall be redundant. Sonali Bank, Shilpa Bhaban Corporate Branch Dhaka Vs. Artha Rin Adalat Nami/anganj and others 15 MLR (2010) (AD) 20.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003— Remission of interest—Writ petitioner can not get the advantage of wrong committed by bank official in calculation of interest. The writ petitioner cannot get the benefit of remission in respect of the interest paid before grant of such remission. The interest so paid shall be adjusted with the principal. Ebrahim Steel Re-Rolling Industries Pvt Ltd Vs. Bangladesh and others 15 MLR (2010) (AD) 190.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003— Necessary parties in suit for recovery of loan—

Shareholder of a company is not liable for repayment of loan and as such is not necessary party. The Chairman or the directors who executed the charge documents and mortgaged property as security against the loan are necessary parties in a suit for recovery of the loan. The chairman if did not execute any charge document or stood as guarantor, is not necessary party merely by reason of his being the chairman. Bakul Akter (Mrs.) Vs. Bangladesh and others 15 MLR (2010) (AD) 225.

 

 Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003— Section 6— Provisions of the Code of Civil   Procedure,   1908   applicable   to proceedings of the Artha Rin Adalat so far not inconsistent therewith— Code of Civil Procedure, 1908— Section 148 and 149— Power of the Adalat to enlarge the time for putting in deficit court fee—Order 7 rule ll(c) Rejection of plaint for non-payment of deficit court fee within specified time—

To put in deficit court fee within 21 days is not the mandatory requirement of law and as such the Adalat can enlarge the time under section 148 and 149 of the Code of Civil Procedure. When the deficit court fee is subsequently paid and the Adalat accepted the same there is no ground for rejection of the plaint. Apparels Limited and another Vs. Government of Bangladesh and others 12 MLR (2007) (HC) 149.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003—

Section 12(8)— Deposit of the rest of the auction money within next 10 (ten) days is not mandatory and the provision of sub-section (2) of section 33 is not applicable to the auction sale held under section 12—

The provision of section 33(2) is applicable to the cases of auction sale held in pursuance of the execution of decree of the court and not to the cases of auction sale held under section 12. Zinnatul Ara and others Vs. Government of Bangladesh and others 15 MLR (2010) (AD) 185.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003— Section 33(5)—

Issuance of certificate of sale of the mortgaged property in favour of the decree-holder when the auction sale could not be held due to low price offered by the bidders, terminates the execution proceedings leaving behind no scope of stay of further proceedings and to grant instalment in payment of decretal amount. Mohiuddin (Sk.) Vs. Joint District Judge and Artha Rin Adalat No. 3, Dhaka and others 13 MLR (2008) (AD) 356.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003—

Section 33(7)— Conferment of title of the mortgaged property to the decree holder bank when auction sale could not be held despite taking all necessary steps—

In exercise of the power vested in it under section 33(7) of the Artha  Rin Adalat Ain, 2003, the Artha Rin Adalat upon an application can pass an order conferring    title    of    the    mortgaged property upon the decree holder bank and issue a certificate to that effect when after taking all the necessary steps the auction sale could not be held due to no bidder coming up  to  participate. The j learned   judges   of   the   High   Court Division   held   the   impugned   order perfectly justified. Setara Begum (Mosammat) Vs. Al-Arafa I Islami Bank Ltd. and others 14 MLR (2009) (HC) 480.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003—

Section 34— Empowers the Artha Rin Adalat to pass order for civil imprisonment against the judgment-debtor—

Section 34 of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003 empowers the Artha Rin Adalat to pass order for civil imprisonment against the judgment-debtor to compel payment of decretal dues, section 34 is not dependent on section 33. When inspite of steps taken by the decree-holder, no auction purchaser came up the Artha Rin Adalat was competent to pass order for civil imprisonment. The civil imprisonment is not the alternative provision and as such it does not absolve the liability of paying the decretal dues. The Appellate Division held the High Court Division was perfectly justified in passing the impugned judgment and order. Proval Kumar Das Vs. Manager Agrani Bank, Main Branch Shahcb Bazar, Rajshahi and another 15 MLR (2010) (AD) 96.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003— Section 34— Empowers the Artha Rin Adalat to pass order for civil imprisonment against judgment-debtor when auction sale of the mortgaged property could not be held for any reason—

In the instant case the Artha Rin Adalat on the prayer of the decree- holder, directly passed order for civil imprisonment against the judgment-debtor to compel him to pay the decretal amount when inspite of steps taken, auction sale of the mortgaged property could not be held due to non-availability of bidders. The learned judges of the High Court Division in view of the position of law held the impugned order passed by the Artha Rin Adalat is perfectly justified. Pravat Kumar Das Vs. Manager, Agrani Bank, Main Branch, Shaheb Bazar, Rajshahi and others 15 MLR (2010) (HO 122.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003— Section 34(1)— Issue of warrant to compel the judgment-debtor to pay the outstanding decretal dues—

In the execution proceeding all the provisions relating to auction sale of the mortgaged property were exhausted but without any positive result. Thereafter on the prayer of the decree-holder, the Adalat issued warrant u/s 34(1) of the Ain to compel payment of decretal dues with which the learned judges of the High Court Division found no fault. M.A Jaher and others Vs. Sonali Bank and others 13 MLR (2008) (HC) 283.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003— Section 34(1)— Issue of warrant of arrest to compel the judgment-debtor to    pay    the    decretal    dues    is    not improper—

As the land under mortgage was diluvated into the river, there was no scope for the decree-holder bank to sell the same. In such situation on the prayer of the decree-holder the Artha Rin Adalat issued warrant of arrest against the judgment-debtor to compel to pay the decretal dues as provided under section 34(1) of the Ain with which the learned judges of the High Court Division found nothing wrong. Bashirullali Master Vs. Government of Bangladesh and others 14 MLR (2009) (HC) 269.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003— Section 34(1)— Provisions are held not ultravires of the Constitution—

Issuance of warrant of arrest against the judgment-debtor before compliance with the provisions of section 34(9)(10) held illegal. It has already been decided in a number of cases that the provision of sub-section (1) of section 34 of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003 since amended are not inconsistent or ultravires of the Constitution. Before issue of warrant of arrest the decree-holder bank is required to take steps under section 34(9)(10) of the Ain for sale of mortgaged property. In the instant case the learned judges of the High Court Division having found the impugned order violative of the provisions of section 34(9) and (10) set aside the same. Jaliangir Chowdhun/ (Mohd.) alias Jahangir Alain Chowdhun/ Vs'. Judge, Artha Rin Adalat No. 1, Chittagong and another 14 MLR (2009) (HO 293.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003—

Section 34(1)— Issuance of warrant of arrest against judgment-debtor is not dependent on the prior compliance with the provisions of sub-sections (5), (6) and (7) of section 33—

In the instant case warrant of arrest was issued against the judgment-debtor in execution proceedings under section 34(1) of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003 in a situation when no intending purchaser was forth coming pursuant to the attempts to sell the pledged property, the learned judges of the High Court Division found nothing wrong with the impugned order and held that the issuance of warrant of arrest against judgment-debtor to compel him to pay the decretal dues is not dependent on the compliance with the provisions of sub-sections (5), (6), and (7) of section 33. M.A Jaher and others Vs. Sonali Bank and others 13 MLR (2008) (HC) 64.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003—

Section 34(2) Exemption of the substituted heirs of the mortgagor and guarantor from arrest and civil imprisonment—

Sub-section (2) of section 34 of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain provides for the exemption of the substituted heirs of the judgment-debtor from arrest and civil imprisonment in connection with the execution of the decree of Artha Rin Adalat. The learned judges of the High Court Division held the provisions of sub-section (2) of section 34 are equally applicable to the substituted heirs of the mortgagor and the guarantor. Akramuzzaman (Md.) alias Babu Vs. Artha Rin Adalat No.l, Rajbari and others 15 MLR (2010) (HC) 1.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003— Section 37— Time limit for disposal of Artha Rin Execution case is directory and not mandatory—

Since section 37 of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain 2003 does not provide for any consequence in the event the execution case is not disposed of within the time limit, the learned judges of the High Court Division held the provisions of section 37 as directory and not mandatory. Abul Basher (Md.) Vs. Judge 1st Artha Rin Adalat, Chittagong and others 13 MLR (2008) (HO 208.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003

Section 41 — Appeal against order passed by Artha Rin Adalat in Execution case without deposit of 50% decretal money is not maintainable —

Artha Rin Adalat Ain being a special statute shall prevail over all other laws including the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908. Appeal against order of rejection of application for setting aside auction sale on ground of low valuation without deposit of 50% decretal amount is held by the Appellate Division as no appeal in the eye of law. Korea-Bangladesh Food Products Ltd. Vs. National Bank Limited and others 13 MLR (2008) (AD) 253.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003 — Section 41 — Held not ultravires of the    constitution    and    violative    of fundamental rights — Constitution of Bangladesh — Article 102 — Writ petition does not lie  against judgment  and  decree  of Artha Rin Adalat. As there is specific provision of appeal under section 41 of the Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003 Writ Petition under article 102 of the Constitution does not lie against judgment   and   decree   of   Artha   Rin Adalat. Anisur Rahman @ K.M. Ziaul. Hague Vs. Government of Bangladesh and others 12 MLR (2007) (HC) 75.

 

Artha Rin Adalat Am, 2003— Section 44— Provides that no appeal or revision lies against interlocutory order passed by Artha Rin Adalat— Code of Civil Procedure, 1908— Order XXI, rule 90-— Application for setting aside auction sale—

In the instant case the appellant filed the miscellaneous appeal against an interlocutory order passed by the Artha Rin Adalat rejecting an application for setting aside auction sale in execution case without complying with the requirements of section 41 and as such the learned judges of the High Court Division dismissed the appeal as being one not maintainable. Awlad Hossain Vs. Bangladesh Shilpa Bank, Maijd.ee Branch and others 15 MLR (2010) (HO 444.
