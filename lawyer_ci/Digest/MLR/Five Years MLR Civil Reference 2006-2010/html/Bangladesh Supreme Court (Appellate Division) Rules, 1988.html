Bangladesh Supreme Court (Appellate Division) Rules, 1988

 

Rule  26—  Grounds  for review  of judgment—

Unless there is ground of error of law apparent on the face of the record occasioning failure of justice, Review Petition cannot be entertained. Despite the ouster clause of jurisdiction Civil Court can scrutinise the impugned order to see as to whether the same is passed in accordance with law. An issue which was never raised earlier cannot be raised for the first time at the stage of review before the Appellate Division. Sena Kalyan Sangstha and another Vs. Hazi Sufi Fazal Ahmed being dead his heirs Nazir Mohiuddin and others 13 MLR (2008) (AD) 296.

 

Rule 26— Review of judgment does not mean rehearing of the matter already decided on merit—

Where the issues were finally decided upon consideration of evidence on record, the review of the judgment in such a case can not be allowed. Review of judgment does not mean rehearing of the matter already finally decided on merit. Shamima Akhtar Vs. Md. Tofazzal Hossain and others 14 MLR (2009) (AD) 38.

 

Section 26— Review of judgment— scope and ground—

Review of judgment can only be made on the ground of error apparent on the face of the judgment or on the discovery of important evidence which could not be produced inspite of exercise of due diligence. When the grounds raised were already answered in the judgment the review thereof is not maintainable. Mozaffar Ahmed Miah (Mohd.) Vs. The State and another 14 MLR (2009) (AD) 167.

 

 Rule 26 — Review of judgment —

Review is not meant for rehearing the matter already finally decided by pronouncement of judgment. Except in exceptional circumstances on ground of error aparent on the face of the record or discovery of new and material evidence and error in interpretation of law, review of judgment can not be entertained. General Manager, Postal Insurance and another Vs. A.B.M Abu Taher 14 MLR (2009) (AD) 239.
