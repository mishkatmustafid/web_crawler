Bank Companies Act, 1991

 

Section 2— Other existing laws are not affected— Local Government (Union Parishads) Ordinance, 1983— Section 7(2)(Chha)— Bank loan defaulter— failure to repay any of the instalments makes the loanee a defaulter—

Section 2 of the Bank Companies Act, 1991 provides that the other provisions of the existing laws are not affected by the provisions of Act, 1991 and as such a loanee who defaulted in the repayment of any of the instalments of the loan money within specified date is a defaulter and disqualified to contest in election as provided under section 7(2)(Chha) of the Local Government (Union Parishads) Ordinance, 1983. Belal Hossain Vs. Kazi jane Alain and others 13 MLR (2008) (AD) 74.

 

Section 5(gaga) and section 27kaka— Guarantor is not a loan defaulter and as such his name can not be included in the CIB list—

Although the financial institution can pursue against the guarantor when the principal borrower fails to repay the loan money for recovery of the same, he is not a loan defaulter as defined under the Bank Companies Act, 1991. Therefore the learned judges of the High Court Division held the name of the guarantor cein not be included in the CIB list and as such declared the impugned CIB list as illegal and of no legal effect. Mahmudur Rahman and another Vs. Bangladesh Bank and others 13 MLR (2008) (HC) 353.

 

Bank Companies Act, 1991— Section   5(GaGa) and 27 KaKa— the names of defaulter  borrowers   are required to be included in the report of the Credit Information Bureau— Bangladesh Bank Order, 1972— Article   42(b)—   defines   the   term borrower—

The petitioners in the instant writ petitions are held by the learned judges of the High Court Division not defaulter borrowers within the meaning of article 42(b) of the Bangladesh Bank Order, 1972 read with section 5(GaGa) and 27 KaKa of the Bank Companies Act, 1991. Since the petitioners transferred their shares in the companies in question with the consent of the lending financial institutions and ceased to be directors of the said companies, they are not liable to be included in the report of defaulters of the Credit information Bureau of the Bangladesh Bank merely by reason of their being guarantors in the capacity of directors at the time when the companies obtained the loan and subsequently defaulted in the payment thereof. The companies which are defaulter barrowers are liable to be included in the list of defaulters but not the petitioners who have no subsisting interest therein. Mostafa Kamal (Md.) and others Vs. Bangladesh Bank represented by its Governor and others 11 MLR (2006) (HC) 377.

 

Bank Companies Act, 1991— Section 27 kaka— Inclusion of the name of Bank loan defaulter in the CIB Report—

The petitioner took loan from the Bank and thereafter failed to repay the same in terms thereof. The Bank concerned instituted suit in the Artha Rin Adalat for recovery of the loan against which the petitioner resorted to various litigations. The Bank in turn furnished information to the Bangladesh Bank whereupon the petitioner's name was included in the CIB Report. The High Court Division found nothing wrong with such inclusion. Sultana Jute Mills Ltd and another Vs. Bangladesh Bank and others 14 MLR (2009) (HC) 434.
