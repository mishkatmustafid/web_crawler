Building Construction Act, 1952

Section 3B— Construction of building without approved plan of the RAJUK is illegal-Town Improvement Act, 1953—Master plan   of  the   city   is   made keeping  in  view  regard  to  healthy environment—

Constitution of Bangladesh— Article   21—   Right   to   life   means meaningful life in healthy environment—

Construction of multi-storied building on the space kept earmarked for open car parking by the Dhaka City Corporation in violation of the master plan and without approval of the plan by the RAJUK is held by the apex court illegal and as such directed demolision of the said construction as being opposed to the spirit of article 21 of the constitution. Sharif Nuntl Ambia Vs. Dhaka City Corporation represented by the Mayor and others 11 MLR (2006) (AD) 288.

 

Building Construction Act, 1952— Section 3— Provides for granting sanction/approval of plan by the RAJUK for construction of building in the Metropolitan City subject to certain terms and conditions—

 

Section 3B— Prescribes the procedure of removal of unauthorized construction on the cancellation of sanction on the ground of breach of any of the terms and conditions specified therein—

 

Section 9— Empowers the authorised officer/committee of the RAJUK to cancel the sanction only on the ground of violation of any of the terms and conditions subject to which the sanction/approval of plan was accorded and or on the making of statements which are incorrect—

 

Section 15— Provides for appeal to specified authority against cancellation of sanction—

Cancellation of sanction by the authorised officer is a quasi-judicial function which has to be exercised judiciously and fairly strictly in accordance with law. As provided under section 9 of the Building Construction Act, 1952 the sanction can only be cancelled on the ground of violation of the terms and conditions subject to which the sanction was granted and or on the ground of making statements which are incorrect. In the instant case there is no allegation of violation of any terms and conditions and or making any incorrect statements. In that view of the matter the learned judges of the High Court Division held there was no valid ground for cancellation of sanction and for directing removal/demolition of the disputed Rangs Bhaban from above the 6th level. The learned judges further held RAJUK can cancel sanction only on the ground of breach of the terms and conditions thereof or making of incorrect statements in obtaining the sanction and on no other consideration. RAJUK must exercise its statutory powers independently and its decision must not be influenced by outside interference or dictation of any other authority whatsoever. The learned judges upon an elaborate discussion of all the aspects agitated held the cancellation of the sanction and the order directing removal/demolition of the building above the 6th floor illegal and arbitrary and declared the same to have been passed without any lawful authority and of no legal effect.
