Section 5— Liability of the husband to pay the dower and maintenance to his wife on the dissolution of marriage—

When the marriage is admittedly dissolved by talak at the instance of the husband and the husband failed to establish the payment of the dower as claimed, the wife is legally entitled to realise the dower money as well as the maintenance during the period of her iddat. Shahid Ha mid Vs. Niliifar Momtaz 14 MLR (2009) (AD) 33.

 

Section 5— Custody of minor— Best welfare of the child shall be the paramount consideration—

According to the Mohamedan Law and also, the Guardian and Wards Act, 1890, the father is entitled to the custody of minor son after he has attained the age of 7(seven) years. But this 7 years factor as held, is not the only criteria to determine the custody of a minor boy. it has now been consistently held by the apex courts, that in deciding the custody of the minor boy the seven years age factor shall not be the only criteria. The welfare and best interest of the child shall be the paramount consideration. In that view of the matter the learned judges of the High Court Division directed the boy though aged 10 years to be restored to the custody of the mother during the pendency of the Family Court Suit. Zahida Ahmed (Liza) Vs. Si/ed Nooruddin Ahmed and another 14 MLR (2009) (HC) 465.

 

Section 5— Suit for maintenance of wife during subsistence of marriage—

Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961— Section 7(1)— Notice of divorce shall be given to the chairman in whose jurisdiction the wife resides at the relevant time—

As required under section 7(1) of the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1,961 the notice of divorce shall be sent to the chairman in whose jurisdiction the wife was residing at the time of pronouncement of the divorce.- When the notice is not properly served the divorce does not become valid and the marriage between the husband and wife subsists and as such the wife so long remains loyal is entitled to maintenance. Kazi Rashed Akhter Shahid (Prince) Vs. Most Rokshana Chowdhury (Sanda) 11 MLR (2006) (HC) 213.

 

Section 5— Custody of minor child— Entitlement of divorced mother when she is remarried to a second husband—

Law is well settled that a mother even after her divorce, is entitled to the custody of her male child until he completes the age of seven years and a female child till she attains her puberty. Mother loses her right to the custody of such child when she is remarried to a second husband. In determining the custody of a minor child, the best interest and welfare of the child shall be the paramount consideration. Sefina Ferdousi @ Shimla and another Vs. Jaohar Kabir and others 13 MLR (2008) (HC) 434.

 

Section 5— Dispute as to solemnisation of marriage can well be decided by the Family Court—

Evidence Act, 1872—

Section 35— Presumption of public documents as to their correctness—

Voter list and Kabinnama duly registered under the Muslim (Marriages and Divorce) Registration Act, 1974 are public documents and have presumption of their correctness so long such presumption is not rebutted, these are admissible in evidence. From these public documents it is found that the age of the defendant was about 22 years at the time of the disputed marriage and as such the defendant can not repudiate his marriage with the plaintiff on ground of minority and accordingly the learned judge of the High Court Division upheld the impugned judgment of the appellate court below which decreed the suit by reversing the judgment and decree of the trial court. Azizur Rahman (Md.) Vs. Mosammat Nnzmun Nnhar 13 MLR (2008) (HC) 246.

 

Section 5— The Family Court is required to decide whether there is any valid marriage when it is flatly denied by the defendant—

Evidence Act, 1872—

Section 101— Burden of proof lies upon the person who asserts the existence of certain thing—

Mollah's Mohammedan Law—

Section 252—Prescribes certain essential requirements of a valid Muslim Marriage—

Muslim marriage is a socio-religious contract which the party claiming must prove by cogent evidence. In the instant case the plaintiff claims for decree for realisation of dower money and maintenance. But the defendant flatly denied the existence of any marriage of the plaintiff with him. The plaintiff could not produce the original kabinnama of the marriage and she could not also produce any witness of the marriage nor even the Nikah Registrar to prove the marriage. Since the plaintiff miserably failed to establish her marriage with the defendant the learned judge of the High Court Davison held there was no valid marriage in between the parties and accordingly set aside the impugned judgment and decree of the courts below. 'Shahldnl Islam (Md.) Vs. M.ost Amvara Begum 15 MLR (2010) (HC) 100.

 

Section 17— Appeal against the judgment and  decree  of  the  Family Court-Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961—

Section 10— When mode of payment of dower is not specifically mentioned in the Kabinnama, the entire dower is presumed to be payable on demand—

 

Marriage between the plaintiff and the defendant is admitted. The wife though not taken to the house of the husband, claims to have their marriage consummated which is supported  by consistent evidence. There is presumption to the effect that the marriage was consummated and the wife is entitled to the entire dower. Wahcd AH (Md.) Vs. Moshma Khatun 14 MLR (2009) (HC) 427.

 

Section 17— Appeal against judgment, decree or order of the family court — is a statutory right-Appeal shall lie to the District Judge against  judgment,   decree   or order passed by the Family Court which is a statutory right and as such no precondition for admission  of appeal can be attached. Arif Hossain (Md.) Vs. Mosammat Binci Akhtar 15 MLR (2010) (HC) 51.
