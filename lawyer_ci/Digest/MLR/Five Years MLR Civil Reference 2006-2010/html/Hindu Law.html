Hindu Law

 

Diety has its perpetual entity and an idol is always a minor and should be represented by shebayet—

A property once made a debattor property and is dedicated to a diety it remains as such and never can be turned into a secular property. Record of rights of the C.S. Khatian have the presumption of correctness. Debattar property cannot be treated as enemy property. Abdul Aziz and another Vs. Hindu Diety Luxmi Gobinda Jew and others 11 MLR (2006) (AD) 22.

 

Transfer of ejmali property by a member of Hindu joint family—

A member of Hindu joint family governed by Dayabhaga school can dispose of his undivided ancestral property by sale, will or gift even though the same were not partitioned by metes and bounds. No vested property case with regard to the Hindu property can be started after 23.03.1974 on which date the Ordinance No.l of 1969 was repealed. A lessee on year to year basis lease of vested property has no locus-standi to challenge the impugned decree or prefer an appeal thereagainst. Aroti Rani Paul (Sreetnati) Vs. Sree Shudarshan Kumar Paul and others 11 MLR (2006) (AD) 43.

 

Shebayet—  Rights and responsibilities—

Shebayet of a Hindu deity is a manager of the debattar property and he performs the sheba puja. He has no title or heritable right to the said property. When the Shebayet left for India he ceases to be Shebayet and he has no authority to appoint Ramesh Chandra Barman as Shebayat by a power of attorney which is of no legal effect. Ramesh Chandra Barman Vs. Sree Sree Kala Charan Jieu TJwkur and another 13 MLR (2008) (AD) 8.

 

Right of Hindu widow to transfer immovable property on ground of legal necessity—

In the instant suit a Hindu widow transferred her husband's property by registered deed for some legal necessity some 30 years before the reversioners transferred the suit land. The learned judge of the High Court Division held the transfer made by the widow for legal necessity perfectly valid where-by the transferee acquired good title, interest and possession. The subsequent transfers by the reversioners did not create any title and interest in the suit land. Rahima Khatun (Mst.) and another Vs. Sree Monoranjan Saha and others 13 MLR (2008) (HC) 334.

 

An idol is considered minor and has to be always represented by next friend— Religious Endowment Act, 1863— Section 14—Removal of Trustee—

Any person in the Hindu community having interest in the worship of the diety can bring suit for the removal of the trustee or the shebayet on ground of misconduct in the management of the debattar property. Pores 7 Chandra Biswas Vs. Srec Sree Shashan Kali ma ta Idol, represented by Hindu Inhabitants 14 MLR (2009) (HC) 507.
