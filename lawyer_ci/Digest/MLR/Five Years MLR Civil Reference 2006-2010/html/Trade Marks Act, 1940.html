Trade Marks Act, 1940

 

Section 70(c)— Registrar of Trade Marks is mandatorily required to decide the application for registration of Trade Marks and the opposition there against upon affording opportunity of hearing to both the parties—

Registrar of Trade Marks is under the legal obligation to decide the application for registration of Trade Marks and the Opposition thereagainst after hearing both the parties. He can not follow any short cut procedure in granting registration of Trade Marks other than that provided under the law. Sony Kabushika Kaisha Vs. Sanyo Industrial Co. Pvt Ltd. 12 MLR (2007) (HC) 123.

 

 

Section 46(4)— Cancellation of registration of trade mark obtained by practice of fraud—

In the agreement it was clearly mentioned that the agent shall have no right to the ownership of the trade mark "Diploma". Inspire of that the appellant obtained registration of the trade mark "Diploma" by misrepresentation and practice of fraud which the High Court Division cancelled. The apex court held the impugned judgment and order passed by High Court Division perfectly justified. Unilac Sanowara (BD) Lid. Vs. Bonlnc Foods Ltd. and another 14 MLR (2009) (AD) 91.

 

Sections 76 and 8 and 10— Registration of trade marks made in violation of the prohibition of sections 8 and 10 is liable to be struck down—

Registration of the Trade Mark of the respondent which is identical to that of the appellant as MK registered long ago having been violative of the provisions of sections 8 and 10 of the Trade Marks Act, 1940 is struck down and the learned judges of the High Court Division directed the Registrar, respondent No.2 to recall the certificate and remove the entry from the Registry. MK Electric Limited Shrubbery Road, Edmonton, London N9 OP/3 (U.K.) Vs. Mil. Mozamrnel Flaque Trading as MK Electrical Industries, Wari, Dhaka and others 15 MLR (2010) (HO) 11.

 

Section 76 and 80—Trade Mark agent can represent a person in a Trade Mark case for  registration of Trade Mark—

As provided under section 80 of the Trade Marks Act, 1940 Trade Mark agent is entitled to represent a person in a Trade Mark case. The learned judges of the High Court Division held the Registrar of Trade Marks wrong in rejecting the prayer for time to file notice of opposition and direct the registration of trade mark straight way without following the procedure of law prescribed therefor. General Biscuits Belgie. Belgium Vs. Prince Hotel Bakery and Food Product and another 12 MLR (2007) (HC) 51.
