Anti-Corruption Act, 1957

 

Section 3— Notice during preliminary enquiry—

Constitution of Bangladesh— Article 35(4)— Notice given by DACO is not violative of article 35(4)-

A District Anti-Corruption officer is deemed to be an officer-in-charge of a police-station under section 3 of the Anti-Corruption Act, 1957 and as such he can very well exercise his powers under section 94 and 160 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. Giving notice by District Anti-Corruption officer to a person to give statement in preliminary enquiry which is of fact-finding nature at the stage when there is no criminal proceedings started against him, is not violative of the provision of article 35(4) of the Constitution.

 

Abdul Hafiz (Md) and others Vs. Director General, Bureau, of Anti-Corruption and others- 3, MLR (1998) (HC) 130.

 

 

Section 4(1)— Notice calling for Statement of property— during preliminary enquiry—Not violative of fundamental rights—

There is nothing wrong in the order passed in pursuance of the satisfaction of the Government directing to submit statement of property under section 4(1) of the Anti-Corruption Act and as such this does not offend fundamental right guaranteed under article 35(4) of the Constitution as the petitioners are not accused in any criminal case following FIR or complaint.

 

Mustajizur Rahman and others Vs. Director General, Bureau of Anti-Corruption- 4, MLR (1999) (HC) 138.
