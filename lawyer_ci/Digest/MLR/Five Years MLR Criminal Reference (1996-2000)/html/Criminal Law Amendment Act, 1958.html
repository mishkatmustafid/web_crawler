Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1958

 

Section 6(5)-Sanction for prosecution—Duty of Special Judge—

Whether a public servant should be prosecuted or not is entirely within the jurisdiction of the Government. Sanction of the Government is a pre­condition for the prosecution of a public servant. Special judge has to write to the Government for sanction upon receipt of a complaint or a police report not accompanied with sanction before any step is taken.

 

Abdur Rahim @ Md Abdur Rahim Vs. The State—1, MLR (1996) (AD) 315.

 

The Criminal Law Amendment (Amendment) Act 1987 (XIII of 1987)

Section 8 (a) - Section 339C of Cr.p.c- Time schedule for trial-Enforceability of—

Proceeding of a special case can not be stopped under section 8 (a) of Act XIII of 1987 after the expiry of two years from 20-1-1987 the date of its coining into force when trial of the case did not commence or 270 working days did not expire after framing of the charge and before coming into force of the said Act.

 

Mojibul Haque Vs. The State— 1, MLR (1996) (HC) 33.

 

Section  5(1)— Offences exclusively triable by Special Judge.

Offences shown in paragraphs (b) and (c) of the schedule of the Act are triable by the Special Judge.

 

Mohor RanjanPal and others Vs. The State-2, MLRU997) (HC) 391.

 

Section 6(5)— Sanction is precondition- Not necessary where offence committed prior to his appointment as public servant—

Sanction of the Government for prosecution of a public servant is a pre-condition without which trial of a public servant is illegal and of no legal effect. Sanction for prosecution of a public servant is not necessary in respect of offence committed prior to his appointment as public servant. Evidence recorded by Magistrate during erroneous trial cannot be used by the Special Judge in fresh trial.

 

Azam Shah Alamgir and others Vs. The State—2, MLR(1997) (HC) 391.

 

Section 6(5)— Sanction of the Government for prosecution— Prerequisite—

Special Judge must write to the Government for sanction for prosecution of a Government servant upon receipt of the complaint before passing any order thereon.

 

Tarani Mohon Ghosh and others Vs. The State and another— 3, MLR (1998) (HC) 97.

 

 

Section 8— conviction after expiry of time limit—

When the trial could not be concluded within the time limit, there was, in fact, no proceeding after the expiry of the period. Trial held and conviction given after the expiry of the time limit is illegal.

 

The State Vs. Md. Minhazuddin Khan— 4, MLR (1999) (AD) 147

 

Sectioa   494—  Withdrawal   from prosecution—

 

Section 10(4)- Withdrawal from prosecution-Role of the Special Judge—

Both section 494 of the Code of Criminal Procedure and section 10(4) of the Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1958 shall have to be read together while allowing or refusing withdrawal from the prosecution. Special Judge is required to exercise this power judicially upon consideration of the materials on record. He should not record withdrawal from prosecution mechanically or as a matter of course simply on the filing of an application therefor.

 

Sreemoti Prativa Rani Dey (Tirtha) VS. Dr. Mohd. Yousuf and others— 5, MLR (2000) (AD) 33.
