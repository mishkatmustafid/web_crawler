INTERPRETATION OF THE LEGAL TERMS

 

Absconsion—

Absconsion of an accused immediately after occurrence is relevant. Absconsion in legal sense means hiding from ordinary residence to avoid the process of justice. 4, MLR (1999) (HC)   20.

 

Alibi—

Alibi as plea of defence can only be taken during trial before the Court. Section 169 of the Code of Criminal Procedure does not empower the Investigating officer to decide the plea of alibi during investigation and submit final report on the basis of alibi. ................ 4, MLR (1999) (AD)   29.

 

Apology—

In a case of contempt of court apology must be unconditional and the contemner must be genuinely penitent and overtaken with remorse for his indiscreet act. . . . . . . . . . . . 4 MLR (1999) (AD) 22.

 

Chance witness:

Chance witness is he who is found in a place of occurrence where he is not normally expected to be there. A Rickshaw puller waiting for passenger is not a chance witness. . . . . . . . . 4. MLR (1999) (HC).

 

Efficacious alternative remedy:

When alternative efficacious remedy is available in any particular forum, writ jurisdiction of the High Court Division can not be invoked. But if such remedy is saddled with non-relax able pre-condition application under article 102 of the Constitution is maintainable.

 

Hartal

Hartal being an unlawful assembly is an offence under section 141 punishable under section 143 of the Penal Code. . . . . . . . . . . . .4, MLR (1999) (HC) 182.

 

Hostile witness

There is no provision in the Evidence Act for declaring a witness hostile who does not support the case in favour of the person who called his as witness. Section 154 of the Evidence Act provides t5hat the Court may permit the party calling such witness to put questions which could be put to him in cross examination by the adverse party. 4, MLR (1999) (HC) 243.

 

Licence

Licence in commercial sense is a legal privilege and not a charity which cannot be cancelled without show cause notice. Cancellation of licence without show cuase notice is arbitrary and is not sustainable in law. 4, MLR (1999) (AD) 11.

 

Natural Justice

Natural justice means no person should be condemned unheard and without giving opportunity of defence. 4, MLR (1999) (AD) 10.

 

Show cause notice

Show cause notice is not a mere technicality or idle ceremony. Show cause notice must not be vague. When it is vague, it is not a show cause notice in the eye of law. 4, MLR (1999) (AD) 10.
