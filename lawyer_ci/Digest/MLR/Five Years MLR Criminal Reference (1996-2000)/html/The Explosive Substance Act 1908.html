The Explosive Substance Act 1908

 

 Section 3—

The Special Powers Act, 1974—

Section 27(b)— Absentia trial-Remedy when not appealed against—

Constitution of Bangladesh-Article 102—

An absconding accused may be tried in his absence after publication of proclamation in two daily news papers. Where the case is triable by Special Tribunal publication should be made by the Tribunal and not by Magistrate. Under section 340 of the Code of Criminal Procedure an accused facing capital charge even if absconding is entitled to state defence. When not provided with state defence the trial is vitiated. Merely because the convict-appellant failed to avail of the provision of appeal under section 30 of the Special Powers Act, 1974 an application under article 102 of the Constitution is not barred.

 

Seritu Vs. Government of Bangadesh and others— 3, MLR (1998) (HC) 271.

 

Section 3 and 4 - When accused may be granted bail—

When allegations are not specific and the accused is in custody for long time knowing not when the trial will be concluded and the co-accused on same footing are granted bail, the accused petitioner in the circumstances may also be granted bail.

 

Abdus Sattor Vs. The State— 4. MLR (1999) (HC) 375.

 

Code of Criminal Procedure 1898— Section 561A- Quashment of proceedings or sentence-Explosive Substance Act, 1908— Possession of explosive substance must be exclusive and not constructive— In order to sustain a conviction for possession of explosive substance, the possession  must be  exclusive.   No conviction can be based on constructive possession of explosive substance. Proceedings in a criminal case or a sentence may be quashed when the offence alleged does not constitute any offence or on ground of coram-non-judice or otherwise for securing the ends of justice.

 

Shahidul Vs. The State— 5, MLR (2000) (HC) 107.

 

Section 4- Punishment for possession of explosive substance—

In order to bring the offence of explosive substance within the mischief of the penal provision of the Act, the incriminating materials, alleged to be bomb or explosive substance shall be examined by Explosive Expert. Otherwise the charge does not stand proved to the hilt.

 

Harun Bepair (Md). Vs. The State — 5, MLR (2000) (HC) 395.
