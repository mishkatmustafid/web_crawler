The Nari O Shishu Nirjaton

(Beshesh Bidhan) Ain 1995

 

 

Section 10(1)- Punishment for murder of wife for dowry-Circumstantial evidence can well be relied upon—

Husband when living with wife in the same house has to explain as to how his wife is murdered. Failure to offer satisfactory explanation points to the guilt of the accused husband. When inmates of the house of the accused do not come to support the prosecution case, circumstantial evidence can well be relied upon to base the conviction. In the case of murder of wife for dowry sentence of death is the appropriate sentence.

 

Abdul Matleb Howlader Vs. The State— 5 MLR(2000) (AD) 362.

 

Tribunal can try only offences under the Ain-

The tribunal established under the Nari-o-Shishu Nirjatan (Bishesh Bidhan) Ain, 1995 is empowered to try offences under the Am only. It cannot tiy offence under section 304 of the Penal Code. Appellate Court is. always competent to direct retrial by appropriate court where there are sufficient evidence on record warranting conviction. But where there is no sufficient evidence on record, retrial will be meaningless exercise of jurisdiction. Retrial cannot be ordered to provide opportunity for the Prosecution to fill up lacuna by bringing fresh evidence which could not be produced during trial at the 1st instance.

 

Asiman   Begum Vs. The  State represented by the Deputy Commissioner Patuakhali— 3, MLR (1998) (AD) 286.

 

Section 10(1)— Tribunal judge's jurisdiction—

Tribunal constituted under the Act has exclusive jurisdiction to try offences under the Act. The Tribunal Judge cannot try offences as Court of Sessions under the Code of Criminal Procedure. Conviction and sentence passed under section 304 part I of the Penal Code in a case where charge under sections 4 and 10(1) of Nari-o-Shishu Nirjatan (Bishes Bidhan) Ain, 1995 was framed is illegal for want of jurisdiction.

 

Asiman Begum Vs. The State— 2, MLR (1997) (HC) 398.

 

 

Section 11- Charge for causing grievous hurt to wife for dowry-It is now well-settled that the term "Dowry" includes property or valuable security paid or agreed to be paid at the time of marriage, or before or after the marriage as consideration of marriage. Causing death or grievous hurt to the wife by the husband or his relation in demand for dowry for continuing the marriage falls within the mischeif of section 10 and 11 respectivly of the Nari-o-Shishu Nirjaton (Beshesh Bidhan) Ain, 1995. The proceeding under section 11 of the Ain for causing grievous hurt for dowry is quite maintainable and cannot be quashed under section 561ACr. P.C.

 

Dipak Kumar Roy alias Kazal Vs. the State and another— 4, MLR (1999) (HC) 70.

 

 

Section 17— Taking coginzance on FRT where permissible—

The Tribunal judge has the power to take cognizance of an offence under the Nari O Shishu Nirjaton Daman Ain on the basis of a report even though a final report submitted by a police officer not below the rank of Sub-Inspector if the materials contained therein disclose the offence.

 

Jarina Khatoon Vs. The State— 1, MLR (1996) (HC) 317.

 

 

Sections 6 and 14— Offence of kidnapping and committing rape- bail of the accused— against whom no allegation of rape or overt act is made—

Where in the F.I.R. the accused petitioner's name is mentioned as one with the help of whom the principal accused kidnapped the victim but in the statement of the victim recorded under section 164 Gr.P.C. she did not allege any overt act on the part of the accused petitioner in connection with kidnapping and committing rape on her the accused petitioner in the circumstances may be granted bail.

 

Farid Ahmed Vs. The State— 4, MLR (1999) (HC) 332.

 

 

Section 20- Bail can be granted when trial is inordinately delayed—

When the accused is in custody for long time and the trial could not be concluded within the time schedule and the trial is delayed inordinately without sufficient reasons, the accused may be granted bail in such circumstances under section 339C.Cr.P.C.

 

Sohel Vs. the State— 5, MLR(2000) (HC) 380.
