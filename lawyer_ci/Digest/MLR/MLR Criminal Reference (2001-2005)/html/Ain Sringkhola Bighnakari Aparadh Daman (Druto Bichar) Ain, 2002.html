Ain Sringkhola Bighnakari Aparadh Daman (Druto Bichar) Ain, 2002

Section HKM and 4(1)- Extortion of money under threat to life at gun point—

When the charge brought against the convict-petitioner was duly established by consistent and reliable evidence together with long absconsion of the together porting at the guilt at the convict-petitioner and the trial court as well as the appellate court concurrently having found the convict-petitioner guilty of the commission of the offence convicting and awarding the sentence which was duly affirmed by the appellate court, the Revisional court found nothing to interfere therewith.

Although absconsion by itself is no evidence and it can not be the sole basis of conviction, long absconsion from immediately after the commission of the offence offers sufficiant corroboration to other evidence forming the basis of conviction. Rom Bepari Vs. The State, 10 MLR (2005) (HC) 381.

Ain Srinkhola Bignakari Aparadh Daman (Druta Bicher) Ain 2002—

Section-4 Offence Kidnapping and putting the victim in illegal confinement for realisation of ransom under thereat of death—

Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898— Section 154— FIR is no substantive piece of evidence-

Evidence of related witness cannot be discarded when they appear to be natural—

It is well settled that FIR is not a substantive piece of evidence. It is important that it gives the information about the occurrence first in point of time and can be taken into consideration to check subsequent embellishment of the prosecution case.

When the witnesses who are related to each other appear to be natural to the occurrence and their evidences appear to be reliable, such evidence can well form the basis of conviction. When the prosecution appears to have proved the charge by consistent and reliable evidence the conviction and sentence passed by the trial court thereon and affirmed by the lower appellate court are held perfectly justified by the High Court Division.Sohel (Md.) Vs. The State, 10 MLR (2005) (HC) 239.

Ain Srinkhola Bighnakari Aparadh Daman (Druto Bicher) Ain, 2002—

Section 4(1)— Charge of the offence can well be framed when primafacie case is found established on the materials on record—

Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898—

Section 241A— Accused can not be discharged when the allegations are supported by materials on record—

Trial Court, is quite justified to frame charge under section 4(1) of the Ain Srinkhola Bignakari Aparadh (Druta Bicher) Ain, 2002 when the materials on record prima facie support the case to go for trial. Court is not required to scrutinize the adequacy or inadequacy of the evidence at this stage which can only be done during trial. Having regard to the position of law as above the High Court Division summarily rejected the application under section 435 and 439 Cr.P.C. seeking to interfere with the rejection of the application under section 241A Cr.P.C.
