Anti-Corruption Act, 1957

Section 3(3)— Officer of the Bureau of Anti-Corruption enjoys the powers of the police officer in-charge of a Police Station—

                                            Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898

Sections 94 and 160— Power to direct production of document and appear to give statement—

Anti-Corruption officer as envisaged under section 3(3) of the Anti-Corruption Act, 1957 is empowered to exercise power of Officer-in-charge of the Police Station under sections 94 and 160 of the Code of Criminal Procedure 1898 to direct production of  document and appear to give  statement in connection of enquiry. In a preliminary enquiry for ascertaining the prima-facie truth or otherwise of an allegation when the person so directed is not an accused, such direction is not volatile of the provision of article 35(4) of the Constitution. Abu Siddique and another Vs.  Government of Bangladesh represented by the Secretary Ministry of Defence and others, 7 MLR (2002) (AD) 212.

 

 

 

                                     Anti-Corruption Act, 1957

Section 4(1)— Notice calling for property statement   pursuant   to satisfaction of the Government—

Notice calling for statement of properties believed to have been acquired disproportionate to known sources of income as envisaged under section 4(1) of the Anti-Corruption Act, 1957 can only be issued pursuant to the satisfaction of the Government and not of the Bureau of Anti-Corruption. Proceedings drawn following the notice not based on satisfaction of the Government are held to be illegal and without jurisdiction and accordingly the conviction and sentence passed therein are set aside. Saidur Rahman Vs. The State, 7 MLR (2002) (HC) 260.
