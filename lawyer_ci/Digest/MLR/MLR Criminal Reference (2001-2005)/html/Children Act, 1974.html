                                                     Children Act, 1974

Section 66(1)(2)— Presumption as to the age of the accused—

When enquiry to ascertain age of a child can be resorted to—

Section 66(2) of the Children Act, 1974 empowers the court to presume as to the age of the accused. If the court draws presumption by physical appearance of the accused that he is not a child below 16 years of age, the court is not required to resort to enquiry for ascertaining the age of the accused. Only when the court finds the accused to be child below 16 years it shall then resort to an enquiry to ascertain the age of the child accused as contemplated under sub-section (1) of section 66 of the Children Act, 1974.Anwarul Islam Vs. The State, 8 MLR (2003) (HC) 18.

                                               Children Act, 1974

 

Trial of Juvenile offender—

When the offender is a child below 16 years of age he has to be tried under the Children Act, 1974. Court is under a duty in such situation to undertake enquiry to ascertain the age of the accused, otherwise the trial of a child Linder the common law will be without jurisdiction rendering the conviction and sentence a nullity. Monir Hossain (Md.) Vs. The State, 6 MLR (2001) (HC) 275.
