Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898

Section 526 - Transfer of case cannot be allowed on unspecified allegations.

The allegations as to threat were omnibus andunspecified and were not tested by calling police report. More over the accusedand the witnesses reside within the jurisdiction of Druta Bichar Tribunal atBarisal. The apex court held the grounds for transfer of the case from Barisalto Khulna not justified.

The State Vs. Omar Ahmed 13 MLR (2008) (AD) 30.

 

Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898

Section 526 - Transfer of case on ground of apprehension of notgetting fair and impartial trial and security of life.

When there is apprehension of not getting fair andimpartial trial and also of security of life and harassment in the hands of theopposite party in the courts of a particular district and the materialsproduced in support of such apprehension, the case in question can well betransferred from one district to the other. Accordingly the High Court Divisionupon consideration of the situation directed the case to be transferred fromthe district of Magura to that of Dhaka for disposal in accordance with law.

Mahfuz Anarn, and others Vs. The State representedby the Deputy Commissioner, Magura and others 11 MLR (2006) (HC) 112.

Section 526 -Transfer of criminal case

Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898

Section 561A - Proceedings drawn up on complaintdisclosing some offence and charge framed there under cannot be quashed.

The truth or otherwise of the allegations made in the complaint can be Decided after taking evidence at the time oftrial. When upon disclosure of certain offence in the complaint cognizance wastaken and charge was framed the proceedings cannot be quashed at this stage.

Sujit Chandra Roy and others Vs. Abdur Rouf andanother 11 MLR (2006) (AD) 125.

 

Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898

Section 540 - Recalling witness for re-examinationin relation to omission and contradictions in the evidence of other P.W.S notpermissible in law.

A prosecution witness who has already been examinedand cross- examined cannot be re-called for re-examination in relation to theomission and contradictions in the evidence of other P.W.S as provided undersection 540 of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

Rana Das Gupta and another Vs. The State 12 MLR(2007) (HC) 199.
