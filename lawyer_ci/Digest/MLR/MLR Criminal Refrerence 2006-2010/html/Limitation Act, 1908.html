Limitation Act, 1908

 

Section 5 - Applicability to criminalappeal Delay to be satisfactorily explained
Judgment in appeal against conviction and sentence was passed in presence ofthe Convict appellant who was on bail. He did not surrender nor he took anystep there against and after 1710 days filed the revision which has beenrejected on ground of limitation. Since no satisfactory explanation was offered the rule wasdischarged. Apex court held it was perfectly justified.

Khalid Alom Chowdhury Vs. The State 13MLR (2008) (HD) 57.

 

Limitation Act, 1908

Section 5 - Condonation of delay in preferring by convict appellant tried in absentia
When the convict petitioner was tried in absentia without taking proper stepsto secure his arrest and the trial was held beyond is knowledge; the learned judge of theHigh Court Division condoned the delay of days inpreferring the appeal and directed the office to registerthe case.

Jahangir Alam (Mohd.) Vs. The State 11MLR (2006) (HC) 403.
