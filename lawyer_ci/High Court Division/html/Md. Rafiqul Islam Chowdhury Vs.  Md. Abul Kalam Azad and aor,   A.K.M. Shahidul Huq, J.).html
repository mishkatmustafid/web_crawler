Code of Civil Procedure (V of 1908)

Section 115(1)

Since both the Courts below concurrently decreed the suit in favor of the opposite parties under section 115(1) of the Code of Civil Procedure there is very little scope to interfere with the concurrent findings of facts which are immune from attack under the revisional jurisdiction. As such I am constrained not to interfere with the concurrent findings of the Courts below. Consequently the instant Civil Revision filed under section 115(1) of the Code of Civil Procedure is devoid of any substance.            ...(46)

JUDGMENT

 

A.K.M. Shahidul Huq, J. This Rule was issued calling upon the opposite parties to show cause as to why the impugned judgment and decree dated 09.3.2014 (decree signed on 16.3.2014) passed by the learned Special District Judge, Dinajpur in Other Class Appeal No. 134 of 2009 disallowing the appeal and thereby  affirming the judgment and decree dated 23.7.2009 passed by the learned Assistant Judge, Nowabgonj, Dinajpur in Other Class Suit No. 95 of 2004 decreeing the suit should not be set-aside and/or pass such other or further order or orders as to this Court may seem fit and proper.

 

2.            The short facts for disposal of the instant Rule is that the plaintiff filed an Other Class Suit No. 95 of 2004 in the Court of Assistant Judge, Nawabpur, Dinajpur against the petitioner for declaration of title over the suit land contending inter alia that the defendant No. 1 purchased the suit land and was in possession. In need of money the defendant offered to sell the suit land. the plaintiff agreed to purchase the suit land and the consideration money was fixed at Tk. 94,000/- and an unregistered bainapatra was executed on 18.07.1997 in the presence of the witnesses and the defendant No. 1 received Tk. 50,000/- cash and promised to execute and register a sale deed whence he will get the rest Tk. 44,000/- from the plaintiff. The plaintiff was an illiterate and stupid and the defendant No. 1 was a B.Sc Teacher. The plaintiff gave the rest Tk. 44,000/- to the defendant No. 1 on 02.12.1997 and went to the scribe Shahajahan Ali of sub-registrar officer who is nephew of the defendant No. 1 and had his well known scribe written the suit sale deed and took Tk. 12,000/- as stamp fee cost from the plaintiff and then he informed him that the stamp was not available and for that the parties should go to the sub-registrar  office after some days. The plaintiff asked the scribe to return  his money but the defendant No. 1 assured him that the scribe was his nephew and nothing to worry. On 31.12.1997 the parties to the suit went to the sub-registrar office and registered the sale deed but the same was not read over to the plaintiff. The next day the defendant No. 1 delivered the possession of the suit land to the plaintiff. The plaintiff got the original deed on 01.01.2002 and the witness, Khaleque told  him that the suit deed not contained the entire suit land, it contained 2.26 acres of land out of 5.63 acres. Which make the plaintiff astonished. On 17.03.2002 the plaintiff along with the witnesses went to the house of the defendant No. 1 and asked him about the matter and asked him to return the rest of the money but the defendant No. 1 refused to return and for that the plaintiff filed a criminal case being No. 317C of 2002 under section 406/420 of the Penal Code. In that case the defendant No. 1 promised to return the money before bail but after enlarged on bail he refused to return the money. On 17.04.2004 the Court to framed charge against the defendant No. 1 under section 420/406 of the Penal code and that time he promised to register the suit land if the plaintiff withdrawn the criminal case. Finally on 15.07.2004 the plaintiff along with the witnesses went to the defendant No. 1 to register the suit land but the defendant No. 1 also refused to do so and hence the suit of the plaintiff.

3.            The defendant No. 1 contested the suit by filing written statement, denying all the material allegation of the plaint and contending inter alia that the suit of the plaintiff is not maintainable by law as because the plaintiff filed the suit for declaration of title instead of Specific Performance of contract and as such the suit is liable to be dismissed. The defendant No. 1 purchased 6.23 acres of land through 7(seven) different deeds and while owning and possessing of his purchased land, proposed to sell 1.70 acre of land the plaintiff agreed to purchase the same that later the plaintiff purchased  the proposed land by registered sale deed. Subsequently the defendant No 1 in  need of money sold 2.26 acres of land by a registered sale deed No. 7663 dated 31.12.1997 in favour of the plaintiff. The defendant No. 1 also executed a baina deed by a registered deed being No. 5062 dated 22.07.1999 to the plaintiff. The plaintiff breach the terms and condition of contract and for that the defendant No. 1 did not register the land to  the plaintiff. The plaintiff after purchasing 3.96 acres of land from the defendant No. 1 trying to acquire other property by filing criminal case being No. 78C of 2002 under section 420/406 of the Penal code which was dismissed and after that the plaintiff also filed another criminal Case No. 371C of 2002 in which charge was framed against the defendant No. 1 and against that order the defendant No.1 filed a criminal Revision No. 147 of 2004 which is now pending in the learned Additional Sessions Judge, Court No. 3, Pabna. The defendant No. 1 never transferred the possession of 5.63 acres of land to the plaintiff. 1.67 acres of land out of 3.96 acres is in possession through adhiar and the suit of the plaintiff.

 

4.            After conclusion of the trial the learned Assistant Judge, Nawabganj Upazilla, Dinajpur was pleased to decree the suit in favour of the plaintiff by its judgment and decree dated 23.07.2009 (decree signed on 30.07.2009).

 

5.            Being aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the judgment and decree passed by the learned Assistant Judge, Nawabganj, Dinajpur the defendant petitioner as appellant institute an appeal before the learned District Judge, Dinajpur which was registered as Other class Appeal No. 134 of 2009 and on transferred it was heard by the learned Special District Judge, Dinajpur and after hearing was pleased to disallow the appeal and thereby  affirming the judgment and decree dated 23.07.2009 and 30.07.2009 by the Assistant Judge court, Nawabganj, Dinajpur in Other Class Suit No. 95 of 2004 by its judgment and decree dated 09.03.2014 (decree signed on 16.03.2014).

18.        The learned Advocate for the opposite parties further submits that both the Courts below, after considering the facts and circumstances of the case and the evidence on records and having found title and exclusive possession of the plaintiffs in the suit land decreed the suit and as such this Hon’ble court has nothing to interfere under it’s revisional jurisdiction and as such the Rule is liable to be discharged.

34.        I have perused the impugned judgment and decree of the last court of fact considered the submissions as advanced by the learned Advocate appearing for the petitioner as well as the learned Advocate for the opposite parties on due and proper consideration of documents on record and the citations referred and on due consideration of the submissions as advanced by the learned Advocate for the petitioner as well as the learned Advocate for the opposite parties.

 

35.        Before arriving at a proper finding it is utmost important to go through the citations as referred by the learned Advocate for the opposite parties in the case of Md. Habibullah Vs. Mr. Sher Ali Khan and others reported in 11 ADC where our Apex court held that;

 

“Since the Court of fact on detail discussion of the evidence on record held that the plaintiffs failed to prove their exclusive possession in respect of the land in suit and that the last Court of fact also held that the land in respect whereof decree for  permanent injunction has been sought is unspecified and as the said finding did not suffer from any mis-reading or non-consideration of the evidence or in other words any legal evidence was not left out in making the decision in the case by the Court of fact the High Court Division was in serious error in reversing the concurrent finding of fact of the courts below……..”

 

36.        In the case of Shambhu Nath Poddar -Vs. Bangladesh Railway and others reported in 43 DLR (AD) 82 where our Apex court held that;

 

“A revisional Court acts beyond its jurisdiction in setting aside concurrent finding of fact, when there is no misreading and misappreciation of the evidence on record.”

 

37.        Now I go through the next citation as referred by the learned Advocate in course of his submissions is the case of Abdul Gafur and others Vs. Md. Abdur Razzak and others reported in 62 DLR (AD) 242 where our Apex court held that;

 

“Courts below also concurrently found that the defendants have failed to prove their right title, interest and possession in the suit lands by adducing oral and documentary evidence. These findings of fact are not immune from interference by the High Court Division in exercise of revisional jurisdiction in the absence of misreading or non-consideration of the evidence on record. The learned Judge of the High Court Division has exceeded its jurisdiction in interfering with the concurrent findings of fact, which is liable to be interfered with.”

 

38.        Now I go through the next citation as referred by the learned Advocate in course of his submissions is the  case of Sakina Khatoon and others Vs. Ibrahim Ahmed Khan and others reported in 2 MLR (AD) where our Apex court held that;

 

“The finding of fact arrived at by the trial Court as well as the appellate Court on proper appreciation of the evidence on record cannot be interfered with under section 115 of the Code of Civil Procedure by the High Court Division.”

 

39.        Now I go through the next citation as referred by the learned Advocate in course of his submissions is the  case of Additional Deputy Commissioner (Revenue), Jhalakathi Vs. Syed Nuruzzaman and others reported in 6 MLR (AD) where our Apex court held that;

 

“Unless there is a case of non-consideration or misreading of any material evidence on record resulting in an error in the decision occasioning failure of justice concurrent finding of fact cannot be interfered with”

 

40.        Now I go through the next citation as referred by the learned Advocate in course of his submissions is the  case of Mahmud Ali and another Vs. Bangladesh and others reported in 1986 BLD (AD) where our Apex court held that;

 

“Whether appellate Court is to find out the points for determination and give its judgment in an appeal if the absence of any defect in the judgment of the trial Court pointed out by the appellant-When, the appellate Court does not differ with the findings and conclusions of the trial Court it should express its concurrence with them” It is not the duty of the appellate Court when it  agrees with  the views of the trial Court to restate the effect of evidence or to reiterate the reasons given by the trial court-Expression of general agreement with reasons given by the Court decision of which is under appeal would ordinarily suffice.”

 

41.        Now I go through the next citation as referred by the learned Advocate in course of his submissions is the case of Md. Shah Alam Vs. Mosammat Farida Begum reported in 17 BLD (AD) where our Apex court held that;

 

“Without reversing the findings of facts concurrently arrived at by the Courts below on the grounds covered by section 115 C.P.C. the High Court Division has no jurisdiction to disturb the findings of facts. It cannot superimpose itself as a third Court for fresh appreciation of the evidence on record this being not the function of a Court of revision.”

 

42.        Now I go through the next citation as referred by the learned Advocate in course of his submissions is the case of Chairman, Mongla Port Authority Vs. Kazi Brothers and others reported in 59 DLR (AD) where our Apex court held that;

 

“When the appellate Court agrees with the views of the trial Court it is not required to restate the effect of evidence or to reiterate the reasons given by the trial Court and expression of general agreement with reasons given by the Court, decision of which is under appeal would ordinarily suffice.”

 

43.        I find since both the Courts below on due and proper consideration of both oral and documentary evidences concurrently passed the decree in favour of the plaintiff as such the same is immune from attack under the revisional jurisdiction of this Hon’ble Court.

 

44.        The submissions as advanced by the learned Advocate for the petitioner I do not find any merit in the Rule.

 

45.        On the contrary, the submissions as advanced by the learned Advocate for the opposite parties I find merit and the citation as referred by the learned Advocate for the opposite parties in support of his contention are fully applicable in the facts and circumstances of the present case.

 

46.        I find that since both the Courts below concurrently decreed the suit in favour of the opposite parties under section 115(1) of the Code of Civil Procedure  there is very little scope to interfere with the concurrent findings of facts which are immune from attack under the revisional jurisdiction. As such I am constraint to interfere with the concurrent findings of the Courts below. Consequently the instant Civil Revision filed under section 115(1) of the Code of Civil Procedure is devoid of any substance.

 

47.        In result, the Rule is discharged without any order as to cost.

 

48.        The impugned judgment and decree dated 09.03.2014 and 16.03.2014 passed by the learned Special District Judge, Dinajpur in Other Class Appeal No. 134 of 2009 disallowing the Appeal and thereby affirming the judgment and decree dated 23.07.2009 decree signed on 30.07.2009 passed by the learned Assistant Judge, Nawabgonj, Dinajpur in Other Class Suit No. 95 of 2004 decreeing the suit is hereby affirmed.

 

49.        The order of stay granted earlier at the time issuance of the Rule is hereby recalled and vacated.

 

50.        The office is hereby directed to transmit a copy of this judgment to the concerned Court forthwith for information and necessary action.
