Interpretation of Statutes

 

Where a statute requires something to be done or to be done in a particular manner and the consequences of failure to do so are also provided, no difficulty arises and the provision is construed as mandatory. But where the statute requires something to be done or to be done in a particular manner without specifying the consequences of non-compliance, a question arises as to whether the requirement is to be construed as merely directory, or as imperative or mandatory. The simple answer is that when a consequence is attached the provision is directory and not mandatory.                                 … (18)

 

Interpretation of Statutes

 

A provision is not mandatory in spite of the use of the word “shall” and inability to comply with its provision is not attended with any legal consequence whatsoever.    ...(23)

 

Bangladesh Water Development Board (Employees) Service Rules, 1982

 

Rule 143 (4)

 

In the provision to sub-rule(4) of rule 143, it has been provided that the inquiry officer shall submit a report by such date within two months from the date of his appointment as may be specified in this behalf. Is the provision mandatory? There is no consequence for not concluding the inquiry within two months from the date of appointment of the inquiry officer and as such, the word “shall” has been used implying that the framer of the rule meant the same to be directory.       … (24)

 

Bangladesh Water Development Board (Employees) Service Rules, 1982

 

Rule 143 (4)

 

The proviso to sub-rule (4) of rule 143 provides that the proceeding shall be completed within three months and no consequence is provided for not completing the proceeding within three months. Therefore, the time frame given in the proviso is also directory.        … (25)

JUDGMENT

 

Syed Mahmud Hossain, J: This appeal by leave is directed against the judgment and order dated 08.04.2010 passed by the High Court Division in Writ Petition No.6023 of 2008 making the Rule absolute.

 

2.            The facts leading to the filing of this appeal, in brief, are:

 

         The case of the writ-petitioner, in brief, is that the writ-petitioner joined Bangladesh Water Development Board (hereinafter referred to as the Board) on 05.11.1981 as Assistant Engineer. He was promoted to the post of Sub-Divisional Engineer after 3 years. Since the date of joining, the writ-petitioner had served with honesty and sincerity and got an unblemished service carrier. Lastly, the writ-petitioner had been serving as a Sub-Divisional Engineer, Narayangonj, Dredger Sub-Division-2, Narayangonj. At that time, the writ-petitioner got a foreign service and applied to the authority for joining the said foreign service. After fulfilling the formalities, the authority gave permission to the writ-petitioner for foreign employment to serve as transport and vehicle support officer in Onion Vancouver Enterprises Inc. United Kingdom Building, 1402-409 Granvile Street Vancouver B.C Canada for a period of 3(three) years with effect from 30.07.2002 to 29.07.2005, vide memo dated 21.07.2002. Accordingly, the writ-petitioner joined his new foreign service. The writ-petitioner filed an application to the lien authority of the Board for extension of his lien period as per regulation (in the writ petition wrongly written as section) 6(Kha) of the Regulation of the Establishment for further 2(two) years. The authority received the application on 05.03.2005, but did not inform the writ-petitioner regarding his application. On being asked the authority verbally gave him assurance as to the extension of the period as per regulation 6(Kha) of the Regulation and by that time his foreign employer extended his service for a period of 2(two) years. When the writ-petitioner was in Canada, the authority issued a show cause notice to him vide memo dated 30.01.2006, but the same did not reach at the last address of the writ-petitioner or his permanent address. After some time, the lien authority without receiving any answer from the writ-petitioner, appointed a one-member enquiry committee to investigate into the charges brought against him, vide memo dated 20.04.2006. After investigation, the committee submitted its report to the authority, vide memo dated 09.08.2006. The Board issued a second show cause notice to the writ-petitioner, vide memo dated 05.10.2006. The writ-petitioner, through his brother, deposited all of his foreign service contributions, such as:  General Contributory Provident Fund, Group Insurance Premium, Benevolent Fund and others on 24.07.2006 those were accepted by the authority which was informed to him, vide memo dated 22.08.2006. The foreign employer of the writ-petitioner issued a General Notice declaring all services related to technical, administration and financial as essential and further announced that no leave shall be granted to those services up to December, 2006 or until further notice. So, the writ-petitioner failed to communicate personally to the Board. The Board dismissed the writ-petitioner from his service, vide memo dated 30.11.2006.

 

3.            In the instant case, the proceedings were initiated through charge sheet under the signature of writ-respondent No.6 on 20.04.2006 and the inquiry officer sent his report on 09.08.2006, that is, after expiry of 3(three) months though he was supposed to send the report within 2(two) months as specified in rule 143(3) of the Rules, 1982. But writ-respondent No.6 passed the impugned order on 30.11.2006, after 7(seven) months of the framing of charge, that is, much beyond 3(three) months in violation of the proviso to rule 143(4) of the Rules,1982. The proceedings initiated in the present case died its natural death after the expiry of three months as per the above provision of law. As the action of the respondents is ex-facie illegal, the writ-petitioner through his learned Advocate served a notice upon the writ-respondents demanding justice by cancelling the impugned action, but the respondents did not comply with the notice. 

 

4.            Being aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the office order dated 30.11.2006 issued under the signature of writ-respondent No.6, the writ-petitioner, filed a writ petition before the High Court Division  and obtained Rule Nisi in Writ Petition No.6023 of 2008.

 

5.            Writ-respondent Nos.2-6 contested the Rule by filing an affidavit-in-opposition controverting the material statements made in the writ petition. Their case, in short, is that after receiving application of the writ-petitioner for extension of his lien period, the lien authority i.e. the Training Directorate referred the application to the Ministry of Water Resources (hereinafter referred to as the Ministry) on 24.04.2006. But the Ministry informed the Board on 25.05.2006 that there was no scope for extension of the lien period of the writ-petitioner and the Board might take necessary action against him for not joining its service after expiry of the period of lien. Necessary action for initiating departmental proceedings against the writ-petitioner was taken as per the Rules,1982 as he did not join the Board’s service after expiry of the approved lien period. A show cause notice was served on 30.01.2006 at the permanent address, Canadian address and also the official address of the writ-petitioner asking him to explain as to why disciplinary action shall not be taken against him for not joining the Board’s service after expiry of the approved lien period. The writ-petitioner did not submit any reply to the charge sheet. Mr. Ranjit Kumar Das, Deputy Secretary (Finance) was appointed as an inquiry officer on 20.04.2006 to inquire into the matter. A copy of the said letter was forwarded to the writ-petitioner’s permanent address, Canadian address and official address. The writ-petitioner submitted a reply to the inquiry officer on 27.04.2006. The inquiry officer submitted his report on 09.08.2006. The charges levelled against the writ-petitioner were found established as reported by the inquiry officer. The writ-petitioner was served with the second show cause notice on 05.10.2006 asking him to show cause as to why he shall not be dismissed from service and this copy was forwarded to his permanent address, Canadian address and official address. The writ-petitioner did not give reply to the second show cause notice and as such, he was dismissed from service by letter dated 30.11.2006. Copy of the said letter was forwarded to the petitioner’s permanent address, Canadian address and official address. The formalities as laid down in the Rules,1982 were duly observed in the departmental proceedings against the writ-petitioner and he was afforded all opportunities to defend himself. There being no merit in the writ petition, the Rule was liable to be discharged.

 

6.            The learned Judges of the High Court Division, upon hearing the Rule, by the judgment and order dated 08.04.2010 made the Rule absolute. 

 

7.            Feeling aggrieved by and dissatisfied with the judgment and order passed by the High Court Division, the writ-respondents as the leave-petitioners have filed Civil Petition for Leave to Appeal No. 2296 of 2010  before this Division, in which, leave was granted on 24.05.2012, resulting in Civil Appeal No.130 of 2012. 

 

8.            Mr. Md. Abdun Nur, learned Advocate-on-Record, appearing on behalf of the appellants, submits that the time frame as mentioned in Rule 143 of Bangladesh Water Development Board (Employees) Service Rules,1982 is directory and not that of mandatory because there is no provision in the Bangladesh Water Development Board (Employees) Service Rules,1982 that the proceedings drawn up would be treated as invalid or unlawful if the proceedings cannot be completed within the time schedule mentioned in it. 

 

9.            Mr. Sheikh Mohammad Morshed, learned Advocate, appearing on behalf of the respondents, on the other hand, supports the impugned judgment delivered by the High Court Division.

 

10.        We have considered the submissions of the learned Advocate-on-Record for the appellants and the learned Advocate for the respondents, perused the impugned judgment and the materials on record.

 

11.        Respondent No.1 joined the Bangladesh Water Development Board as Assistant Engineer. Subsequently, he was promoted to the post of Sub-Divisional Engineer, which position he held till he was dismissed from service. While respondent No.1 had been serving as a Sub-Divisional Engineer, Narayangonj, Dragger Sub-Division-2, Narayangonj, he got a foreign service and applied to the authority for joining there. After fulfilling the formalities, the authority gave him permission for foreign employment. Petitioner-respondent No.1 filed an application for lien to the authority for extension. After expiry of the period of the lien, petitioner-respondent No.1 prayed for extension of the lien as per regulation No.6(Kha) of the Regulations of the Establishment for further two years. The authority received the application on 05.03.2005 but did not inform him regarding the application. When petitioner-respondent No.1 was in Canada, the authority issued a notice dated 30.01.2006 for showing cause upon him but according to petitioner-respondent No.1 the same did not reach at his last address or his permanent address. Subsequently, the authority appointed a one-member inquiry committee to investigate into the charges brought against him. After investigation, the committee submitted its report to the authority on 09.08.2006. The Board issued a second show cause notice to petitioner-respondent No.1 on 05.01.2006 but petitioner-respondent No.1 failed to communicate personally to the Board. The Board dismissed petitioner-respondent N.1 from service on 30.11.2006.

 

12.        The High Court Division made the Rule absolute and directed the leave-petitioners to reinstate petitioner-respondent No.1 to his position where he had been with due seniority forthwith. The High Court Division also directed to pay all wages and other benefits to petitioner-respondent No.1.

 

13.        The High Court Division has come to a finding that Sub-rule(3) of Rule 143 of the Rules warrants that the inquiry officer shall submit a report by such date within two months from the date of his appointment as may be specified in this behalf. The High Court Division has held that it is true that the consequence of non-compliance has not been specified, but the word “shall” rather than “may” has been used implying that the framers of the rule meant the same to be mandatory. The High Court Division has noticed that proviso to Sub-rule (4) of Rule 143 lays down three months deadline during which the proceeding “shall” be completed and that again, the word used is “shall”. Therefore, the High Court Division came to the conclusion that the time frame stipulated in the proviso to Sub-rule(4) of Rule 143 is mandatory.

 

14.        In order to resolve the issue, Sub-rule (3) of Rule 143 of Bangladesh Water Development Board (Employees) Service Rules,1982 should be looked into. The same is quoted as under:

 

“The authority competent to impose the penalty shall appoint an officer to conduct an inquiry who shall submit a report and giving the employee a personal hearing, if so desired by him, by such date within two months from the date of his appointment as may be specified in this behalf.”

 

15.        The proviso to Sub-rule (4) of Rule 143 is also quoted below:

 

“Provided that before imposition of penalty of compulsory retirement, dismissal, removal or reduction to a lower post, the delinquent employee shall be given second opportunity to show cause against the penalty proposed to be imposed. The decision of the employee concerned, the proceedings under this rule shall be completed within three months from the date of framing charge against an employee.”

 

16.        Having gone through Sub-rule (3) of Rule 143, it appears that the officer appointed to conduct the inquiry shall submit a report after giving the employee a personal hearing, if so desired by him, within two months from the date of his appointment. The enquiry officer could not complete the enquiry within two months.

 

17.        The proviso to sub-rule (4) of Rule 143 provides that the proceeding under this rule shall be completed within three months from the date of framing charge against an employee. Emphasis has been given by using the word “shall” in order to complete the proceeding within three months of framing of the charge against an employee. In the case in hand, the proceeding could not be completed within three months as stipulated in the proviso to sub-rule (3) of Rule 143. It is to be seen whether it is mandatory to complete the proceeding within three months. 

 

18.        Where a statute requires something to be done or to be done in a particular manner and the consequences of failure to do so are also provided, no difficulty arises and the provision is construed as mandatory. But where the statute requires something to be done or to be done in a particular manner without specifying the consequences of non-compliance, a question arises as to whether the requirement is to be construed as merely directory, or as imperative or mandatory. The simple answer is that when a consequence is attached the provision is directory and not mandatory.

 

19.        In Maxwell’s Book ‘On Interpretation of Statutes’ 11th Edition, page 364, it has been propounded as follows:

 

“When a public duty is imposed and the statute requires that it shall be performed in a certain manner, or within a certain time, or under other specified conditions such prescriptions may well be regarded as intended to be directory only in cases when injustice or inconvenience to others who have no control over those exercising the duty would result if such requirements were essential and imperative.”

 

20.        The above principle of Maxwell was approved in the case of Aminul Islam and others vs. M/s. James Finlay & Co. Ltd., Khulna (1974) 26 DLR (SC)33.

 

21.        In the case of Md. Delwar Hossain vs. The State (2006) 26 BLD (AD)109, this Division affirmed the finding of the High Court Division that although section 20 of Nari-O-Shishu Nirjatan Daman Ain,1995 laid down a period of 90 days for conclusion of trial with a further provision in sub-section (3) of section 20 to conclude the trial with an extended period of 30 more days for reasons to be recorded in case of failure of the trial Court to conclude the trial within the first 90 days as fixed in sub-section (2), the Ain does not provide for any consequence for failure of the trial Court to conclude the trial within 120 days. Therefore, the High Court Division held that the provision is not mandatory rather directory.

 

22.        In the case of A. H. M. Mustafa Kamal @ Lotus Kamal vs. Government of People’s Republic of Bangladesh, represented by the Secretary, Ministry of Justice and Parliamentary Affairs, Bangladesh Secretariat, Ramna, Dhaka and others, (2009) 14 MLR (AD)45, it has been held as under:

 

“We have also perused section 6A of the Criminal Law Amendment Act,1958 and Rule 19Ka of the EP Rules,2007 and it appears that no consequence has been proved for in the event of failure to conclude the trial within the stipulated period.

 

Since no consequence has been provided for the provisions both in section 6A of the Criminal Law Amendment Act and rule 19Ka of the EP Rules,2007 are directory in nature and the Court shall not become functus officio even after the expiry of stipulated period.”

 

It has been further held as under:

 

“In such view of the matter there is no hesitation in holding that section 6A of the Criminal Law Amendment Act,1958 and Rule 19Ka of the EP Rules,2007 having not provided for any consequence for their non-compliance are directory and not mandatory.”

 

Craies on Statute Law, 6th ed. opined:-

 

“The terms “mandatory” and “directory” refer to the method by which the legislature sets about attaining its object. When a statute is passed for the purpose of enabling something to be done and prescribes the formalities which are to attend its performance, those prescribed formalities which are essential to the validity of the thing when done are called imperative (or mandatory); but those which are not essential, and may be disregarded without invalidating the thing to be done, are called directory.”

 

23.        From the citations made above, we find that a provision is not mandatory in spite of the use of the word “shall” and inability to comply with its provision is not attended with any legal consequence whatsoever.

 

24.        In the provision to sub-rule(4) of rule 143, it has been provided that the inquiry officer shall submit a report by such date within two months from the date of his appointment as may be specified in this behalf. Is the provision mandatory? There is no consequence for not concluding the inquiry within two months from the date of appointment of the inquiry officer and as such, the word “shall” has been used implying that the framer of the rule meant the same to be directory. 

 

25.        Again the proviso to sub-rule(4) of rule 143 provides that the proceeding shall be completed within three months and no consequence is not provided for not completing the proceeding within three months. Therefore, the time frame given in the proviso is also directory.

 

26.        In the light of the findings made before, we find substance in this appeal. Accordingly this appeal is allowed and the impugned judgment is set aside.
