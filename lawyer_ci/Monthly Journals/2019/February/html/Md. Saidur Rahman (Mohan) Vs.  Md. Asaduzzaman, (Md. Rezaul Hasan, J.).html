JUDGMENT

Md. Rezaul Hasan, J: Instant Rule has been issued under section 115 (1) of the Code of Civil Procedure calling upon the opposite parties to show cause as to why the impugned judgment and order dated 01.11.2016 passed by the learned Joint District Judge, 2nd Court, Dhaka, in Title Suit No.339 of 2013 rejecting the application for allowing the time in submitting the written statement should not be set-aside and/or such other or further order or orders passed as to this court may seem fit and proper.

2.             Facts relevant for disposal of this Rule, in short are that, the opposite party No.1 Md. Asaduzzaman had filed an application, in title suit No.339 of 2013 before  the Joint District Judge, 2nd Court, Dhaka, for declaration of title and recovery of khash possession alleging, amongst other, that the property belong to the National Housing Authority, which is opposite party No.5 (proforma defendant), that the original allotee of the said plot, one Sufia Khatun, who being sick and otherwise unable to look after and manage the suit land had appointed on Feroza Begum as her attorney, who looked after the said property. Said Feroza Begum (attorney of the Sufia Khatun) having died, her sons (also defendant Nos.2, 3 and 4 in the suit) were looking after the said properties. Be that as it may, the original allotee Sufia Khatun has transferred her 16 anas of title in the suit properties to the plaintiff-opposite party No.1, after complying with all formalities, as specifically stated in the plaint, with reference to the memo numbers and dates of all allotment letters and the mutation case, that the plaintiff has been regularly paying the land development tax (khajna) of the suit properties, that on 01.11.2016 this suit was fixed for further hearing, but the defendant Nos. 2, 3 and 4 (the petitioners) had filed an application, on that date, for withdrawal of the suit from hearing argument and to allow them time to file and written statement. The learned Joint District Judge, having recorded, amongst other, that it has not been stated in the petition of these defendants that they did not receive any summons and as such, there is no reason to withdraw the case from the list for further hearing or argument, accordingly, the Court has fixed 28.11.2016 for argument.

3.             Being aggrieved by the said order dated 01.11.2016, the said defendants-petitioners have filed this petition and obtained the instant Rule.

4.             Mr. M.A. Yousuf, Learned Advocate appeared for the petitioners. He submits that, the petitioners have come to know about this suit and accordingly filed an application on 01.11.2016 to withdraw the case from further hearing or argument, but the trial court has rejected their application for allowing them time to submit the written statements. Hence, the impugned judgment and order dated 01.11.2016 is liable to be set-aside and the lower court may be directed to allow the application for time for submitting the written statements by these defendant-petitioners. When attention of the learned Advocate for the petitioners (defendant Nos.2, 3 and 4) were drawn, from the Bench, to their application dated 01.11.2016 (Annexure-B), the learned Advocate for the petitioner has admitted that, no date has been mentioned as to when the defendants have come to know about the said suit. The learned Advocate also submits that the defendants did not receive, nor signed the summons issued from the Court. Thereby, the said court has committed error of law, resulting in error in the decision and occasioning failure of justice. Accordingly, he has prayed that the Rule may be absolute.

5.             Learned Advocate Mr. Md. Serajul Hoque, appeared for the opposite party Nos.1 and 2, having placed the petition and other materials on records, submits that, this suit property is Government property. The petitioner is owner (99 years lessee) of the said property acquired, by way of transfer, from the previous allottee Sufia Khatun, upon complying with all legal formalities with the NHA and that these defendants-petitioners are forcefully occupying the said properties since the death of their mother Feroza Begum (who was the then attorney of Ms. Sufia Khatun) and these defendants have been demanding chada (coercive payment) to vacate the said property. They have also drawn attention of this Court to the orders dated 28.11.2016 and dated 17.01.2017, whereby the Court below has allowed time twice, on these dates,  and as the last chance for filing written statements. But, the petitioners (defendant Nos.2-4) have deliberately suppressed these facts in their petition and obtained this rule by suppressing these facts and by misleading the Court. He lastly submits the impugned decision is just and proper and that the Rule may be discharged with exemplary cost.

6.             I have heard the learned Advocates for both sides and perused the petition alongwith the documentary evidences annexed therewith as well as the materials on record.

7.             I prima facie that, this suit is property of the National Housing Authority (NHA), and this property was previously allotted to one Feroza Begum, who had then appointed one Sufia Khatun, as her attorney, to look after the suit property. Said attorney died and after her death these defendant-petitioners were looking after the suit property. At this stage, the previous allottee Ms. Feroza Begum has transferred the suit property to the plaintiff, following all formalities as prescribed by the NHA and the plaintiff has acquired title in the suit property, got his name mutationed, but could not recovered possession from the sons of deceased attorney, i.e. the defendant-petitioners. As such, prima facie, these defendants-petitioners are forcefully occupying the said properties and demanding chada to give up possession to the plaintiff.

8.             I also find from the orders dated 28.11.2016 and dated 17.01.2017, that the Court below had allowed time on both these dated and as the last chance to file written statement. But the petitioners (defendant Nos.2-4) have deliberately suppressed these facts from this petition and obtained this rule by suppressing this fact and miss leading the Court.

9.             Besides, attention of the learned Advocate for the petitioners were drawn from the Bench, to summons issued from the Court (Annexure-D series), wherein it has been recorded that the summons was duly served upon the family member’s (Liza Islam, wife of one of the defendants), at about 2.00 p.m., on 10.06.2013), the learned Advocate for the petition could not give any proper reply to deny the same.

10.         I also find that, nor could he deny the fact that, the trail court, even also had twice allowed time to these defendant-petitioners as well as the last chance for filing written statements, but they could not.

11.         Therefore, I am of the opinion that the rule was obtained by the petitioners (defendants Nos.2-4) by deliberately suppressing the facts and by misleading the Court for which exemplary cost should be awarded against the petitioners. I also draw an inference, from the materials on record that, these petitioners (defendants Nos.2-4) had no defense to offer, by filing written statement, since the property belongs to the NHA and the plaintiff is their allottee, who is possessing all required documents in favour of his title. 

12.         I am also of the further opinion that the proforma opposite party No.3 being a Government authority (NHA) a presumption is attached to the accuracy of their acts and deeds, in allowing transfer of suit property to the plaintiff.

13.         Therefore, in the aforesaid situation, I do not find any reason to interfere with the impugned judgment and order passed by the courts below.

14.         The learned Advocate for the petitioner was not found at the time of the delivery of the judgment.

15.         Before, parting of, it should be kept on record that, this court has judicial notice of many reports on incidents of either forceful occupation of the landed properties or forceful eviction of the lawful owners from the landed  properties, and then to demand a huge amount of chada, (demand of money by coercion) and this trend is spreading like epidemic, even to the extent that a group of people have adopted this as their defecto profession, publicly, and have been defying the law and order in the broad day light. This should be discouraged, before going too far and causing utmost mental and financial sufferings of the helpless citizen and dragging them to court’s lengthy process of litigation, against their means and will. Therefore, exemplary cost should be awarded against these mastan’s and  land grabbers.

16.         In the result, the rule is discharged with direction about payment of cost.

17.         Accordingly, the trial court is directed to award Tk. Tk.5,00,000/- (five lacs) as cost, if the suit is decreed and to ensure recovery of the same though execution proceedings, as it is bound to follow this direction as per Article 111 of the Constitution of the Peoples Republic of Bangladesh.

18.         The order of stay granted at the time of issuing of the Rule is hereby recalled and vacated.

19.         Send a copy of this judgment to the Court below for record and compliance.
