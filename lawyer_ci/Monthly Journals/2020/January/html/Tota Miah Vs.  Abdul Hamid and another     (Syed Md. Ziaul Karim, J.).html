JUDGMENT

 

Syed Md. Ziaul Karim, J. By this appeal, the informant-appellant has challenged the legality and propriety of the order dated 18-06-2001 passed by the learned Judge of Nari-O-Shishu Nirjatan Daman Bishes Tribunal, Habigonj (briefly as Tribunal) accepting the Police report and directing to prosecute the informant under section 17 of the Nari-O-Shishu Nirjatan Daman Ain, 2000(briefly as Ain 2000)

 

2.            Facts in brief, are that on 07-11-2000 the appellant as complainant filed a petition of complaint in the Court of Magistrate, First Class, Habigonj against the accused respondents alleging that on 14-10-2000 the accused respondents forcibly entered into the dwelling hut of the informant and attempted to abduct his daughter Minara Begum failing which the accused assaulted the victim. With this allegations the complainant filed the instant case.

 

3.            On receipt of the complaint the learned Magistrate referred the case to the local Police Station wherein it was recorded as Habigonj P.S. Case no. 07 dated 05-01-2001 under Sections 10, 30  of the Ain, 2000.

 

4.            The Police after investigation submitted final report in favour of the accused respondents and sought for permission to prosecute the informant under section 17 of the Ain, 2000.

 

5.            On receipt of the Police report the learned Magistrate referred the case to the Tribunal wherein the learned Judge by the impugned order dated 18-06-2000 accepted the Police report and discharged the accused and also permitted to prosecute the respondents under Section 17 of the Ain,2000.

 

6.            Feeling aggrieved the informant preferred the instant appeal.

 

7.            No one appears on behalf of the appellant to support the appeal. In view of the facts this is an old appeal of 2001 we are inclined to take it up for disposal on merit considering the materials on record.

 

8.            No one appears on behalf of the accused-respondent nos.1-2 to oppose the appeal.

 

9.            The learned Deputy Attorney General appearing for the State-respondent no.3 supports the appeal and submits that the learned Judge of the Tribunal after considering the materials on record rightly accepted the Police report and discharged the accused which calls for no interference by this Court.

 

10.        In order to appreciate her submissions we have gone through the record and given our anxious consideration to her submissions.

 

11.        On going to the materials on record, it transpires that although the informant made allegation under sections 10, 30 of the Ain, 2000 but the Police during investigation tried to collect evidence to that effect. But none of the local witnesses supported the allegations made by the informant. Therefore the Police submitted final report. On receipt of the Police report the learned Judge of the Tribunal after considering the materials on record rightly discharged the accused. It is pertinent to point out that challenging the Police report the informant did not file any narajee petition to that effect. Therefore, we are at one with the learned Judge of the Tribunal. Thus the appeal having no merit fails.

 

12.        In view of foregoing narrative, the appeal is dismissed. The order of stay granted earlier stands vacated.

 

13.        The Office is directed to send down the record (if arrived) or to communicate the order at once.
